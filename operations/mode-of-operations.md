# Mode of Operations

## Purpose
A Growth Operator's job changes shape at each revenue threshold. Running a $1M playbook at $3M breaks the team. Running a $3M playbook at $500k burns the cash. This doc is the decision tree: what mode are you in, what do you do, what do you stop doing, and what are the traps at each stage. Use `team-leadership.md` for the leadership mechanics; use this doc for the mode decision.

## The Five Modes

| Mode | Revenue Band | Primary Question | Time Horizon |
|---|---|---|---|
| Build | $0 – $500k | Does this offer work, and can I sell it? | Weekly |
| Systemize | $500k – $1M | Can I write down what I'm doing and hand pieces off? | Monthly |
| Delegate | $1M – $3M | Do my managers own outcomes I used to own? | Quarterly |
| Scale | $3M – $10M | Is the leadership layer stronger than the Operator? | Half-yearly |
| Refactor | $10M+ | What do we need to rebuild — the offer, the team, or the structure? | Yearly |

## Mode 1 — BUILD ($0 – $500k)

**Operator's job:** Do the work. Sell, deliver, answer every ticket.

### Do
- Talk to every customer personally
- Sell every deal personally
- Write every piece of content personally (or co-create 1:1 with 1 contractor)
- Spend 80%+ of waking hours on lead-gen + sales + delivery
- Obsess over the 10 customers, not the 1,000
- Ship a v1 of every system at 60% quality, don't polish
- Hire: 1 VA, 1 Setter — no one else

### Stop
- Don't hire managers
- Don't build dashboards beyond a Google Sheet
- Don't run weekly all-hands (too few people)
- Don't buy tools over $200/mo
- Don't write a "brand guide" — write landing pages that convert

### Common Traps
- **Premature hire.** Hiring a Closer at $200k revenue when you have 10 calls/month. Fix: own the sales seat past 100 closed deals; the Closer hires themselves when your calendar is full.
- **Systemizing too early.** Writing SOPs for processes you've done twice. Fix: do it 3x, then SOP it.
- **Tool-shopping as procrastination.** Fix: sheets + email + 1 CRM is enough.
- **Vanity audience.** Growing followers, not customers. Fix: one metric (closed revenue/month) on the fridge.

### Exit Trigger → Move to Systemize
- Consistent $40-50k/mo revenue
- Same offer repeatable
- Operator working 70+ hrs/week and capacity-capped
- OR: Operator has done any 1 task 3+ times and knows the exact reps

## Mode 2 — SYSTEMIZE ($500k – $1M)

**Operator's job:** Write down what you've been doing. Hand off the first pieces.

### Do
- Block 4 hrs/week for SOP writing (non-negotiable)
- Hire: Closer, Content Mgr — in that order
- Move sales onto a Closer's calendar, gradually (transfer pipeline over 60-90 days)
- Build: a real CRM (GHL or HubSpot), a real dashboard (Sheets + Supermetrics or Databox starter), a Notion workspace
- Record yourself on every call, every call-prep, every delivery touch — turn into Looms → SOPs
- Set up first weekly cadences (sales weekly, all-hands)
- Start tracking: MRR, CAC, churn — even roughly

### Stop
- Stop running every sales call yourself after 60 days with a Closer
- Stop owning content calendar personally
- Stop answering every CS ticket personally (if offer has delivery)
- Stop saying yes to every opportunity — start picking

### Common Traps
- **Keeping the closer seat.** You stay the best closer, so you keep closing. Fix: hard deadline (day 90) to transfer; your time is more expensive elsewhere.
- **Premature leadership layer.** Hiring a "COO" at $750k. Fix: not yet. Ops Mgr comes at $1.5M+.
- **SOP perfectionism.** Polishing SOPs instead of shipping them. Fix: 60% Loom + transcript is an SOP.
- **Dashboard theater.** Fancy dashboards, no action. Fix: 3 numbers on a daily dashboard is enough.

### Exit Trigger → Move to Delegate
- Closer closing at or above your close rate
- Content Mgr running the calendar
- Revenue consistent $80-100k/mo
- Operator time <60% on IC (individual contributor) work
- At least 12 core SOPs written

## Mode 3 — DELEGATE ($1M – $3M)

**Operator's job:** Hand ownership to managers. You set the what; they own the how.

### Do
- Hire: Ops Mgr, CS Mgr, Ad Buyer, Video Editor (in that order, staged over 6-12 months)
- Write role charters: outcomes owned, decisions owned, metrics reported
- Install the full weekly cadence (leadership, sales, marketing, CS, content, all-hands)
- Install the full monthly cadence (MBR, content retro, strategic priorities)
- Start quarterly planning (OKRs) — see `cadences-quarterly.md`
- Part-time CFO at $1.5M+
- Hold skip-levels (your reports' reports talk to you quarterly)
- Your calendar: 30% doing, 70% leading

### Stop
- Stop running the calendar (Ops Mgr does it)
- Stop approving every marketing spend under $5k
- Stop editing every piece of content personally
- Stop answering DMs from customers (route to CS)
- Stop being in every meeting — delegate meeting attendance

### Common Traps
- **Delegating without authority.** Naming a manager but holding all decisions. Fix: every manager has a decision ledger (what they own vs what escalates).
- **Loneliness.** You're not in the sales calls anymore; you miss the market. Fix: 1 call/week listen-in; 1 customer conversation/week.
- **Politics emerge.** Managers competing for resources. Fix: clear OKRs + a single RACI document per decision type.
- **Middle-manager overload.** Ops Mgr has 8 direct reports. Fix: add team leads between Ops Mgr and ICs.
- **Revenue plateau.** Team of 8, still $1.2M. Usually: sales team weak OR offer weak. Diagnose honestly.

### Exit Trigger → Move to Scale
- 4-5 direct reports all running their divisions independently
- Revenue $250-300k/mo consistently
- Operator time <10% on IC work
- OKRs shipping (>60% hit rate) at quarterly level
- Each division has written scorecards for their roles

## Mode 4 — SCALE ($3M – $10M)

**Operator's job:** Build the leadership layer. Stop building the thing; build the builders.

### Do
- Promote or hire: a number-two (VP/Chief of Staff), specialized VPs (Marketing, Sales, CS) as revenue warrants
- Your calendar: 90%+ leading + strategy, 0-10% doing
- Go deep on: comp structure, equity plan, leadership development
- Build: annual planning rhythm, board-like quarterly reviews, investor-grade reporting
- 1:1 with your #2 every week; skip-levels with every director quarterly
- Customer visit or call once a week minimum — to keep market tuning
- Start capturing IP: playbooks, training materials, internal case studies
- Consider: brand PR, speaking, category leadership

### Stop
- Stop approving individual hires under director level
- Stop running any weekly operational meeting personally (attend, don't drive)
- Stop being the smartest person in every room — develop your seconds
- Stop reacting to dashboard noise — look at weekly and monthly, not hourly

### Common Traps
- **Founder-CEO debt.** Still approving everything because "no one's as fast." Fix: systems + trust. Expensive mistakes are cheaper than bottlenecking.
- **Second-layer weakness.** Directors who were great ICs but weak managers. Fix: hire experienced managers externally for the leadership layer, or invest heavily in coaching.
- **Culture drift.** From 10 to 40 people, the founder's values no longer transmit by osmosis. Fix: codify values, teach them, hire/fire on them.
- **Wrong co-founders in wrong seats.** Original partner made Operator for $800k is not a VP at $5M. Fix: honest conversation, new role or exit.
- **Customer distance.** You haven't talked to a customer in 3 months. Fix: protected customer time weekly.

### Exit Trigger → Move to Refactor
- Revenue >$8M trending $10M+
- Leadership team of 5-8 directors running independently
- Operator working on 3-year, not 90-day, horizons
- Offer/model showing scale limits (unit economics or team size suggests refactor)

## Mode 5 — REFACTOR ($10M+)

**Operator's job:** Work on the business, not in it. Decide what to rebuild.

### Do
- Strategic-level work only: M&A, category positioning, major offer overhaul, executive team refresh
- Refactor candidates: offer structure, pricing model, org design, tech stack, geography
- Install a real exec team (CEO role you play, or hire a CEO and become Chairman)
- Board / advisors installed for governance
- Invest in your own leadership development (coaching, peer groups, education)
- Prepare for options: acquisition, further growth, platform expansion
- Customer focus shifts to strategic accounts and new segments

### Stop
- Stop touching operations (even weekly) — the exec team owns it
- Stop being the face of every external thing (train a bench)
- Stop shipping tactical work personally

### Common Traps
- **Couldn't let go.** Still running sales calls at $12M revenue. Fix: the exit is mental first. Work with a coach.
- **Forgot to refactor.** Running a $3M playbook on a $15M team. Everything feels slow. Fix: a structured refactor project every 12-18 months.
- **Built the wrong team for the next stage.** Your $3M team isn't your $30M team. Fix: leadership refresh is not failure, it's the job.
- **Operator burnout.** Running on fumes at the hardest stage. Fix: sabbatical, coach, or bring in a President/CEO.

## Mode Diagnostic — 10 Questions

Answer honestly. Your score tells you which mode you're in (not which mode you should be in — those can differ, and the gap is the most important signal).

1. Annual revenue run-rate: <$500k / $500k-1M / $1M-3M / $3M-10M / $10M+
2. Team size (FTE + regular contractors): 1-2 / 3-5 / 6-15 / 16-40 / 40+
3. Operator IC hours / week: 50+ / 30-40 / 10-20 / 0-5 / 0
4. Named managers reporting to Operator: 0 / 0-2 / 3-5 / 5-8 / 8+
5. Does Operator run weekly sales meeting? Yes / Yes / Attends only / No / No
6. Does Operator know every customer name? Yes / Most / Top 20% / Top strategic / No
7. Written OKRs at quarterly level? No / Informal / Yes, 1 yr / Yes, multi-yr / Yes, 3 yr
8. Dashboard freshness: N/A / Weekly / Daily / Real-time / Real-time +alerting
9. Retained advisors (coach, CFO, lawyer, etc.): 0 / 1-2 / 3-4 / Full roster / Board
10. Exit readiness (docs, numbers, team): No / Partial / In progress / Yes, on hold / Yes, optional

Score: each answer scores 1-5 by column; average → mode.

**If Operator's actual mode < team size / revenue:** under-managing, will cap growth
**If Operator's actual mode > team size / revenue:** over-building, will burn cash
**If matched:** on-mode — execute the do/stop list for this mode

## Quarterly Mode Check

Every quarter, Operator and Ops Mgr (once hired) run the Mode Diagnostic together. Take 90 minutes. Answer honestly. Adjust the next quarter's plan to match.

Mode drift is the most expensive mistake an Operator makes. Name it quarterly and you catch it before it's a crisis.

## Metrics / Success Criteria
- Operator IC hours aligned to mode ±10%
- Mode diagnostic completed quarterly
- Mode change communicated to team within 14 days of Operator recognizing it
- "Stop doing" list for the current mode is visible on Operator's desk
- 1 sabbatical or real break every 18-24 months at Scale+ modes

## Failure Modes
- **Denial about mode.** Running Build playbook at $1M. Fix: diagnostic quarterly, honest peer / coach / CFO feedback.
- **Skipping Systemize.** Trying to go Build → Delegate. Team can't execute on un-codified knowledge. Fix: never skip.
- **Premature Refactor.** Thinking strategically at $600k while the sales seat is open. Fix: match mode to revenue.
- **Stuck in Build.** Addicted to doing. Fix: coach, sabbatical, or forcing function (hire the Closer on Day 1 of Systemize).
- **No "stop" list.** Doing everything of every mode you've been in. Fix: writing down what you're stopping is the act.
