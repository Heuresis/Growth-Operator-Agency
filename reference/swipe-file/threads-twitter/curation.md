# Curation Thread — X/Twitter

> **Hook → hand-picked resources + notes.** 5-10 tweets. Editorial commentary. Community generosity signal.

---

## The Skeleton

```
Tweet 1 — HOOK
"N [resources / tools / ideas] I've used to [OUTCOME] this [TIMEFRAME]"

Tweets 2-N — RESOURCE + COMMENTARY
Per resource:
— What it is (1 line)
— Why it's on the list (1-2 lines)
— How to use it (1 line)

Final tweet — CTA
Follow / DM / reply with your additions.
```

Target: 5-10 items + hook + CTA = 7-12 tweets.

## Worked Example 2 — Ideas / Principles Curation

```
[Tweet 1 — HOOK]
7 ideas I keep returning to this year.

Not quotes. Not tweets. Frames I actually use weekly to make decisions.

🧵

[Tweet 2]
1/ "Most problems are upstream of where the symptom shows up."

Use: when I see a [SURFACE SYMPTOM], I spend the first 15 minutes asking "what upstream system produced this?" before trying to fix the symptom itself.

Saves hours of downstream whack-a-mole.

[Tweet 3]
2/ "Attention is the scarcer resource, not time."

Use: I budget my week by attention blocks, not hours.

Three blocks of real attention a day beats 10 hours of shallow switching.

[Tweet 4]
3/ "The system that runs on your best day doesn't matter. The one that runs on your worst day does."

Use: when I build any operation, I ask "does this still work when I'm at 60%?"

If no, it's fragile. Redesign.

[Tweet 5]
4/ "If you can't write it in a paragraph, you don't understand it."

Use: before I commit to a strategy, I write it in a single paragraph. If it takes a page, I don't understand it well enough yet.

Paragraph-clarity is the gate.

[Tweet 6]
5/ "The price you charge is a signal to yourself about the value you believe you produce."

Use: when I'm undercharging, I ask why. The answer is usually about my own belief, not the market.

Fix the belief first, price follows.

[Tweet 7]
6/ "Decisions compound, not actions."

Use: I spend 2× as much time on weekly decisions as I do on daily actions.

One good decision saves months of wrong action.

[Tweet 8]
7/ "Replaceable work kills businesses. Irreplaceable work scales them."

Use: every Friday I audit the week — which hours were replaceable, which were irreplaceable.

Goal: move the ratio toward irreplaceable each quarter.

[Tweet 9 — CTA]
Seven ideas I'd tattoo if I were the tattoo type.

Which ones do you disagree with? Which do you run? Reply with yours.

Follow for more operator-level ideas.
```

## Slot-Fill Rules

### Hook tweet
- Number + outcome
- "This year / this month / in the last N" timeframe
- Promises real commentary, not just a list

### Resource tweets
- "N/" prefix
- Three components: what it is, why it's here, how to use it
- Honest weight (time required, effort, skip-worthy parts)
- No real brand names without permission (use archetype: "a newsletter on X" rather than a specific name)

### CTA tweet
- Invitation to reply (curation threads get strong reply rates)
- Follow for more curation
- Community-building frame

---

## Curation Integrity

- Actually used / read / applied (not a list of things you've heard about)
- Honest weight and caveats
- No affiliate links without clear disclosure
- No resources you haven't personally engaged with

---

## Common Failure Modes

- List is too long (drop-off after 8 items)
- Items without commentary (just names)
- Fake curation (things you haven't actually used)
- Missing "how to use" on each item
- No reply invitation (misses engagement compounding)
