# Step-by-Step / How-To Thread — X/Twitter

> **Hook → numbered steps → close.** 7-12 tweets. Tactical. Reader should be able to execute after reading.

---

## The Skeleton

```
Tweet 1 — HOOK
Outcome + timeframe + "here's the exact sequence"

Tweet 2 — FRAMING
Prerequisites / stake / "who this is for"

Tweets 3-N — STEPS
One step per tweet.
Step structure: what to do + how long it takes + what success looks like.

Final tweet — CLOSE + CTA
Save-trigger close + single action.
```

Target: 7-12 tweets = 5-10 steps + hook + close.

## Worked Example 1 — "Validate an Offer in 2 Weeks" How-To

```
[Tweet 1 — HOOK]
How to validate a new offer in 14 days for under $500.

The exact sequence I use before I build anything:

🧵

[Tweet 2]
This is for [ICP] who've ever built something, launched it, and watched it not sell.

Almost always: validation was skipped because the pain felt obvious.

Pain being obvious ≠ willingness to pay being obvious. Validate willingness before building.

[Tweet 3]
Step 1 — Write the offer as a one-paragraph promise. Day 1.

Format: "I help [SPECIFIC ICP] get [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] in [SPECIFIC TIMEFRAME] via [SPECIFIC MECHANISM]. Price: $[NUMBER]."

If you can't write this crisply, you can't validate it. Sharpen first.

[Tweet 4]
Step 2 — Identify 20 people who match the ICP. Day 2.

Not "my network." Actual people who match the profile you wrote in Step 1.

List them. Name, context, why they're a fit. No fit = skip them.

[Tweet 5]
Step 3 — Book 10 of them for a 25-min conversation. Days 3-5.

Cold or warm. Pitch: "I'm building something for [ICP]. I'd value 25 minutes of your perspective. No pitch. Just listening."

Book 10 to account for no-shows.

[Tweet 6]
Step 4 — Run the conversations. Days 6-9.

Question structure:
— "Walk me through the last time you tried [SOLVING THIS PROBLEM]."
— "What did you spend / what would you spend?"
— "What would make you say no to [MY OFFER]?"

Take notes verbatim.

[Tweet 7]
Step 5 — Build the smoke test page. Day 10.

One page. Hook. Promise. Price. Buy button.

Button goes to a "we're opening applications / pre-orders soon" form. Collect email + reason.

Deploy.

[Tweet 8]
Step 6 — Drive 50-100 visitors. Days 11-13.

Pay for it. $200-$400 in targeted [PLATFORM] ads to the ICP. 

Metric: conversion rate on the form.

[Tweet 9]
Step 7 — Read the signal. Day 14.

Thresholds (rough):
— >5% form conversion: demand exists, build it
— 2-5%: offer needs sharpening — run variant with different promise
— <2%: offer is wrong; don't build; listen more

[Tweet 10 — CLOSE + CTA]
14 days. Under $500. Real signal on whether to build.

Save this when you're about to build. Come back before you start.

DM "validate" for the full worksheet (scripts, page template, ad settings).
```

Tweet count: 10. Type: How-To. Steps: 7.

## Worked Example 2 — "Land Your First High-Ticket Client" How-To

```
[Tweet 1 — HOOK]
How to land your first high-ticket client in 6 weeks.

No audience required. No existing case studies.

Here's the sequence that's worked for every [ICP: consultant/coach] I've run it with:

🧵

[Tweet 2]
"High-ticket" = $[THRESHOLD]+ for a single engagement.

This sequence bypasses the chicken-and-egg problem: "need case studies to get clients / need clients to get case studies."

Step 1.

[Tweet 3]
Step 1 — Pick one outcome. Week 1.

Not one service. One outcome.

Example: "I help [ICP] reach [SPECIFIC METRIC] in [TIMEFRAME]."

The outcome has to be specific enough that the client imagines their own version clearly. "Growth" isn't an outcome. "Move from $15k to $30k/mo" is.

[Tweet 4]
Step 2 — Write the problem essay. Week 1.

1500-2000 words. Public. On LinkedIn or your site.

Structure: the problem you solve → why it persists → the mechanism that solves it → what most get wrong.

This is your positioning, not your sales pitch.

[Tweet 5]
Step 3 — Identify 30 ICPs who have the problem. Week 2.

Specific. Named. Searchable.

For each: write a 2-sentence note on why they specifically have this problem now.

Generic lists don't convert. Specific homework does.

[Tweet 6]
Step 4 — Send 30 hand-written first messages. Week 2-3.

Each message: references their specific situation + shares the problem essay + asks one diagnostic question.

Not a pitch. A conversation opener.

Expected reply rate: 25-40%.

[Tweet 7]
Step 5 — Run discovery calls (not sales calls). Week 3-4.

First 20 minutes: diagnose their specific version of the problem.

Next 10 minutes: if they're qualified and motivated, outline the mechanism.

Next 5 minutes: if they ask, walk them through the engagement.

[Tweet 8]
Step 6 — Present the engagement as a risk-transfer container. Week 4-5.

Structure: "You're hiring the outcome, not the hours. Here's the timeline, the deliverables, the guarantee."

Price: first engagement priced 30% below the long-run target to offset lack of case study.

[Tweet 9]
Step 7 — Close 1 of the first 10 qualified conversations. Week 5-6.

Expected close rate on qualified calls: 15-25% for a new operator.

10 conversations → 1-2 closes is the realistic base case.

That's your first high-ticket client.

[Tweet 10]
Step 8 — Deliver + document. Post-engagement.

The first client is also your first case study.

Document the before state, the process, the outcome. Everything compounds from this.

[Tweet 11 — CLOSE + CTA]
6 weeks. 30 outreach messages. 1 high-ticket client. No audience, no case studies, no ads.

Save this if you're at Week 0. DM "first" and I'll send the full scripts.
```

Tweet count: 11. Type: How-To. Steps: 8.

## Slot-Fill Rules

### Hook tweet
- Outcome + timeframe + promise of the sequence
- Specificity ≥ 8

### Framing tweet
- Who the thread is for (filter out wrong readers)
- Prerequisites (if any)
- Tone: "this is tactical, not theory"

### Step tweets
- Numbered (Step 1, Step 2, etc.)
- What to do (action in imperative mood)
- When (day or week)
- What success looks like (signal or metric)

### Close + CTA tweet
- Save-trigger ("save this when...")
- One CTA

---

## Executability Test

After reading the thread, can the reader take the first step within 24 hours?

If yes: executable. Ship.
If no: too abstract. Rewrite each step with specific actions.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Steps too abstract ("clarify your offer")
- Steps too many (15+ = overwhelming)
- Steps without timeframes (reader loses urgency)
- Missing save-trigger close
- Thread skews theory when it should stay tactical
