# PIER Structure — LinkedIn

> **Problem → Insight → Example → Recommendation.** Authority-pillar default. Save-optimized. Works when the creator has a clean teaching frame.

---

## The Structure

```
[P] — PROBLEM
Hook line that names a specific problem the ICP has.
1-2 sentences of setup that prove you know the shape of the pain.

[I] — INSIGHT
One-sentence reframe or mechanism that the reader hasn't seen before.
This is the load-bearing idea. Everything hangs on it.

[E] — EXAMPLE
Specific, dated, named. Numbers. Outcome. Receipt.
1-3 short paragraphs or a bullet list.

[R] — RECOMMENDATION
One to three concrete moves the reader should make this week.
Followed by a single CTA.
```

### Character Target
1300-2200 chars. Aim for 1500-1800 sweet spot (save rate + dwell).

### Pillar
Authority 50%. Teaching frame. Works for How-To, Myth-Bust, Case-Study pillars from `/content-calendar`.

## Worked Example 1 — B2B Consultant ICP

```
[P]
[ICP: B2B consultants] can't scale past [PLATEAU: $30k/month] for one reason most of them never name.

It's not leads. It's not pricing. It's not delivery.

[I]
It's that they sell "[COMMODITIZED SERVICE NAME]" when they should be selling "[NAMED OUTCOME + TIMEFRAME]."

The buyer for the first is comparing you to 40 alternatives on price. The buyer for the second has one question: does this work?

[E]
Last [QUARTER], I repositioned [N] consultants from service-category selling to outcome-category selling.

Before:
— Avg deal: $[X,XXX]
— Close rate: [X]%
— Pipeline cycle: [X] weeks

After [TIMEFRAME]:
— Avg deal: $[2-5× X,XXX]
— Close rate: [1.5-2× X]%
— Pipeline cycle: [halved]

Same consultants. Same skills. Same market. One variable: what they put at the top of the sales page.

[R]
Three moves this week:
1. Write down the [NAMED OUTCOME] your best client got. Dollar figure + timeframe + before state.
2. Delete every instance of [COMMODITIZED SERVICE NAME] from your website and replace with the outcome.
3. Re-send your last three cold pitches with the outcome as the first line. Track reply rate vs. prior.

If the reply rate doubles, the repositioning is real. If not, DM me and we'll look at why.

Follow me for more frames like this — I'm writing through the repositioning playbook over the next 8 weeks.
```

Character count: ~1550. Post type: Authority. Specificity: hook 8/10 (ICP + plateau), example 10/10 (delta math), recommendation 9/10 (3 specific moves).

## Worked Example 2 — Fitness Coach ICP

```
[P]
Most online [ICP: fitness coaches] stop growing at [PLATEAU: 20 clients].

They blame their ads. Their funnel. Their offer. Their market.

It's none of those.

[I]
The ceiling isn't demand. It's the fact that their offer is structured for 1:1 delivery at a price point that can only scale linearly.

Twenty clients × 5 hours/week each = 100 hours. That's the ceiling of a human.

[E]
I worked with a [ICP: coach] stuck at 23 clients for 14 months.

We didn't add ads. We didn't redo the funnel. We restructured the offer from 1:1 weekly calls to:
— Weekly group coaching (1 hour, 10 clients per group)
— Asynchronous check-ins (Slack, 15 min/client/week)
— One personal 30-min call per month

Same promise. Same outcome. Different delivery shape.

90 days later: 67 clients. Same 40 hours/week. Revenue 2.9×.

The unlock wasn't more. It was differently structured.

[R]
If you're capped at [20-30 CLIENTS]:
1. Map your current hours per client. Multiply by your client count.
2. Identify which activities need to be 1:1 and which can be group or async.
3. Redesign the delivery for a 2-3× client ceiling at the same hours.

Delivery design is the offer. The people who scale past the ceiling fix delivery before everything else.

I'm running through the full delivery redesign sequence in my [WEEKLY NEWSLETTER / COMMUNITY]. Link in comments.
```

Character count: ~1580. Post type: Authority + soft conversion.

## Worked Example 3 — SaaS Founder ICP

```
[P]
[ICP: SaaS founders] at [STAGE: $30-100k MRR] miss their growth targets not because of acquisition.

They miss them because of a retention problem they've misdiagnosed as an acquisition problem.

[I]
If your [SPECIFIC METRIC: 3-month retention] is below [THRESHOLD: 70%], adding more leads just accelerates the leak.

Every new cohort fills the top of a bucket with a hole in it. The bucket never fills. You just run the pump harder.

[E]
One [ICP] came in at $[MRR]. Growth had stalled for 5 months despite tripling ad spend.

Their 90-day retention: 52%.

We stopped acquisition for 30 days. Built:
— Onboarding checkpoints at day 1, 3, 7, 14, 30
— An activation metric (specific product use) we could measure per user
— A human touchpoint from the founder at day 7

Ninety-day retention climbed from 52% to 78% in one cohort.

Then we turned ads back on. MRR grew 3.4× in the next 120 days — with the same ad spend as the stalled months.

[R]
Before adding another dollar of acquisition:
1. Measure 30/60/90 retention honestly
2. Build an activation metric tied to the core value prop
3. Add a human check at the point most users churn

Fix the leak, then turn the pump back on. Otherwise you're buying churn.

Want the full retention diagnostic checklist? Comment "retention" and I'll DM it.
```

Character count: ~1520. Post type: Authority + Lead Magnet.

## Slot-Fill Rules

When filling the PIER template:

### The P slot
- Name the **specific** ICP (age, stage, role, constraint)
- Name the **specific** problem (not "growth" — "stuck at $30k for 6 months")
- 2-4 lines max. Longer = diluted.

### The I slot
- One sentence. Never more.
- Must be **falsifiable** — the insight either holds or it doesn't
- Must **explain** the problem, not just restate it
- Should rhyme with the mechanism from `/build-positioning`

### The E slot
- Dated
- Named (ICP type; no real client names unless permission)
- Specific numbers (not rounded)
- Delta: before → after
- Time-bounded outcome

### The R slot
- 2-4 concrete moves (not "mindset shifts")
- Verbs in imperative mood
- Ends with **one** CTA — follow / DM / comment / link-in-comments

---

## Common Failure Modes

- P too vague: "Entrepreneurs struggle with marketing." Reject and sharpen.
- I restates the problem: "The issue is that marketing is hard." Reject — no insight.
- E missing numbers: "One of my clients saw great results." Reject — no proof.
- R is abstract: "Focus on what matters." Reject — no action.
- Multiple CTAs at end: Reject — one CTA per post.

---

## First Comment Plan

For every PIER post, schedule a first comment 60-90 min after publish:
- Expand on the I (insight) with one more proof point
- OR link to a related post / lead magnet (in comment, not body)
- OR ask a specific question to drive replies
