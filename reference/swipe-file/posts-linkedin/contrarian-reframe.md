# Contrarian-Reframe Structure — LinkedIn

> **Category belief → Reversal → Reframe → Proof.** Engagement pillar. Comment-driven. Designed for long replies (Dec 2025 Knowledge Graph signal).

---

## The Structure

```
[CATEGORY BELIEF]
Name the default belief the ICP's category carries. One sentence.

[REVERSAL]
Flip it. State the opposite with conviction. One or two sentences.

[REFRAME]
Explain the underlying mechanism that makes the reversal true.
This is where the post earns its authority.

[PROOF]
Dated, specific evidence that the reversal holds.

[CTA]
Usually "what's your take?" or "comment disagree / agree" to drive long comments.
```

### When To Use
- Engagement pillar (30% of weekly mix)
- You have a real Big Enemy (from `/build-positioning`)
- You want long comments + quote-tweet-style reposts
- You're willing to lose the wrong readers

## Worked Example 1 — Paid Ads Contrarian

```
[CATEGORY BELIEF]
The standard advice for [ICP: coaches] starting paid ads is "start small, test, and scale what works."

[REVERSAL]
That advice is wrong. It's how most [ICP] burn 6 months and a 4-figure ad budget learning nothing.

The right move: spend the first month's entire ad budget on a single creative + a single offer + a single audience. Don't split-test anything.

[REFRAME]
Split-testing assumes you already have a working baseline. You don't — you're pre-baseline. Splitting $30/day across 4 ad sets means every data point is statistically useless.

A $900 concentration on one creative produces a real data point. A $900 split across 10 variants produces noise that looks like data but isn't.

The sequence is:
1. One creative. One offer. One audience. One month. Read the result.
2. If it worked: scale the concentration, then begin variants.
3. If it didn't work: you know something concrete is wrong (not vague). Swap one variable and repeat.

This is statistics, not opinion. Under-funded split-testing is the single biggest ad-budget waste in creator businesses.

[PROOF]
I ran this with [N] [ICP] over [TIMEFRAME]. 

Split group (standard advice, $30/day × 4 variants):
— Spent $3,600 over 30 days
— Best-performing variant: 0.8× return
— Decision confidence: low (still "testing")

Concentration group ($120/day × 1 variant):
— Spent $3,600 over 30 days
— First variant: 1.9× return
— Decision confidence: high (real data)

Same budget. Different structure. Different outcome.

[CTA]
Curious what you'd push back on. Where does this break down in your experience?
```

Character count: ~1620. Post type: Contrarian-Reframe.

## Worked Example 2 — Content Cadence Contrarian

```
[CATEGORY BELIEF]
"Post every day. Consistency wins. Volume beats perfection."

[REVERSAL]
Post less. Most [ICP] get more from 3 posts a week than 7 — and the data says so at the platform level.

[REFRAME]
Two things changed in 2025 that broke the "daily" advice:

1. LinkedIn's Dec 2025 Knowledge Graph update discounts high-frequency single-creator posting to prevent topic-graph saturation. Your 5th post this week competes against your 1st, not against other creators.

2. The quality-per-post bar went up because reader feeds are denser than ever. A mediocre post in 2022 could ride a topic wave. A mediocre post in 2026 doesn't get out of its first 10 minutes.

The structural math: if post quality scales non-linearly with preparation time, and reach scales sublinearly with frequency, the right move is to reduce frequency and pour the time into quality.

[PROOF]
Same account. Same ICP. Two 8-week phases.

Phase 1: 7 posts/week. 
— Avg impressions/post: [NUMBER]
— Avg saves/post: [NUMBER]
— Avg long comments/post: [NUMBER]
— Total reach (8 weeks): [TOTAL]

Phase 2: 3 posts/week.
— Avg impressions/post: [2-3× Phase 1]
— Avg saves/post: [2×]
— Avg long comments/post: [2-3×]
— Total reach (8 weeks): [HIGHER THAN PHASE 1]

Fewer posts. Higher total reach. Higher quality signals per post. Less burnout.

The operators still winning on daily cadence are outliers with very specific voice + format advantages. The median creator is better off at 3-4x/week.

[CTA]
What's your cadence right now — daily, 3-4×/week, weekly, sporadic? Curious what's actually working for people.
```

Character count: ~1680. Post type: Contrarian-Reframe.

## Worked Example 3 — Niche Contrarian

```
[CATEGORY BELIEF]
Everyone says "niche down."

[REVERSAL]
Niching down too early is how most [ICP] starve.

The right sequence is: go broad enough to test, then niche to the pocket that actually converts. Not the pocket you assumed would.

[REFRAME]
"Niche down" is good advice for someone who already has data. You don't have data when you're starting — you have hypotheses. Niching on a hypothesis locks you to an unproven audience.

The sequence:
1. Start with a broader topic surface that covers 3-5 adjacent niches
2. Run content + offers against that surface for [TIMEFRAME]
3. Read the data: which niche actually engaged, bought, and referred?
4. Then narrow to the data-confirmed niche

Most early [ICP] skip steps 2-3 and niche on step 0 — the niche that sounded right, the niche their coach told them to pick, the niche that matched their identity. That niche often isn't the niche with actual demand.

Narrowing too early = zero signal. You can't tell if the niche is wrong or your execution is wrong.

[PROOF]
Tracked [N] [ICP] over their first [TIMEFRAME] in business:

Group A: niched on day 1 (before data).
— Time to $10k/mo: [X] months median
— Pivoted niche within first year: [Y]%
— Still operating at 24 months: [Z]%

Group B: broad-to-narrow sequence.
— Time to $10k/mo: [2/3 X] months median
— Pivoted niche within first year: [1/3 Y]%
— Still operating at 24 months: [1.5× Z]%

The broad-to-narrow group didn't get to $10k faster because they stayed broad. They got there faster because they niched on the right niche, once.

[CTA]
Curious where this hits a nerve. If you niched too early, what did you wish you'd tested first?
```

## Slot-Fill Rules

### The Category Belief slot
- Quote it as if the reader heard it this week (because they did)
- One sentence
- Verbatim tone the category uses

### The Reversal slot
- Sharp — mild reversals get zero engagement
- Specific — not "this is wrong" but "this is wrong because X"
- Concise — if it takes 4 sentences to reverse, the reversal is muddy

### The Reframe slot
- Mechanism-explained — why is the reversal true?
- Structural, not vibes-based
- Numbered or step-structured to drive saves

### The Proof slot
- Dated
- Comparative (reversal vs. category default)
- Numbers, not anecdotes

### The CTA slot
- Debate-inviting for engagement-pillar use
- "Curious where you'd push back" > "what do you think"
- Ask for a specific contrasting experience, not a vague opinion

## Polarization Ethics

Per `hooks/contrarian-hooks.md`:
- Attack methods, beliefs, structures — not people
- Use archetypes, not named individuals
- Be willing to be wrong — invite the pushback, read it, update

---

## First Comment Plan

- 60-90 min post-publish
- Drop the #1 objection you expect and answer it
- OR tag no one but reply to every long comment
- Reply to disagreements with curiosity, not defense

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Reversal is mild ("maybe it's not always true")
- Reframe is abstract (no mechanism)
- Proof is a single anecdote
- CTA is defensive ("tell me why I'm right")
- Attacks a named person or company — never
