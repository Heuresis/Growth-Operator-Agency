# Thought-Proof-CTA Structure — LinkedIn

> **Thought → Proof → CTA.** Opinion-forward. Receipts-backed. Works when you have a sharp point of view and dated evidence.

---

## The Structure

```
[THOUGHT]
Strong opinion. Falsifiable. One paragraph max.
The thought must be the creator's actual point of view — not a borrowed take.

[PROOF]
Dated, specific, named evidence that backs the thought.
Numbers, receipts, before/after deltas, case walkthroughs.
Target: 60-70% of the post's total words.

[CTA]
One action. Often tied to "follow to see the rest of the series" or "DM to discuss."
```

### When To Use
- You have a sharp opinion + receipts
- Authority pillar
- Building a series (point of view compounds over posts)
- Content-to-conversion bridge toward a consultative offer

## Worked Example 1 — Operator Opinion + Portfolio Data

```
[THOUGHT]
The reason most [ICP: solo consultants] never scale past [PLATEAU: $15k/mo] is that they price by deliverable instead of by outcome.

It's not about pricing higher. It's about pricing to a different thing entirely.

[PROOF]
I've worked with [N] [ICP] over [TIMEFRAME]. Same base skill level. Split them by pricing model:

Group A — deliverable pricing ($X per deck, per site, per report):
— Median revenue: $[AMOUNT]/mo
— Median engagement length: [N] weeks
— Median % of revenue from referrals: [X]%

Group B — outcome pricing (flat fee tied to a specific outcome in a specific window):
— Median revenue: $[3-5× AMOUNT]/mo
— Median engagement length: [2-3×] weeks
— Median % of revenue from referrals: [2×]%

Same market. Same skills. Different pricing frame.

The deliverable-priced consultant is selling time. The outcome-priced consultant is selling risk transfer. Buyers pay exponentially more for risk transfer — because the whole point of hiring a consultant is to transfer risk.

If you're still pricing by deliverable past $15k/mo, the ceiling isn't your market. It's the frame you're selling inside.

[CTA]
I'm writing through the full outcome-pricing shift in a weekly series. Follow to see the next piece — next post is how to write the outcome-based contract.
```

Character count: ~1380. Post type: Thought-Proof-CTA.

The play is fewer, better posts — with real engagement replies 60-90 minutes after publishing to deepen the first-hour signal.

## Worked Example 3 — Portfolio Opinion

```
[THOUGHT]
Most creator businesses I audit are running three offers when they should be running one.

Multiple offers at different price points dilute attention, confuse the pipeline, and cap the LTV of the core product.

[PROOF]
Over the last [TIMEFRAME], I audited [N] [ICP: creator businesses] between $[RANGE] MRR.

The split:
— Running 3+ offers: [N] businesses
— Running 1 offer: [N] businesses

The single-offer group outperformed on every metric:
— Average revenue: [X]% higher
— Average profit margin: [X]% higher
— Average hours worked: [X]% lower
— Client referral rate: [X]× higher

Why: attention is the constraint in a creator business, not demand. Splitting attention across 3 offers means:
— 3 sales pages to maintain
— 3 nurture sequences
— 3 onboarding flows

The operators running 1 offer can get every element to "great" because every element has their full attention. The operators running 3 have every element at "fine" because attention is divided.

If you're running 3 offers and plateaued — the plateau isn't your market. It's your attention budget.

[CTA]

```

## Slot-Fill Rules

### The Thought slot
- One paragraph max
- Falsifiable — could be wrong; isn't
- Specific enough that smart readers nod or push back
- Avoid hedges ("maybe," "sort of," "I think")
- Use present tense active voice

### The Proof slot
- Dated (month or quarter level specificity)
- Named by ICP type (not real client names without permission)
- Before / after or comparative math
- 60-70% of the post's word budget
- Structured (columns, bullets, paragraphs) to maximize save rate

### The CTA slot
- Usually "follow" for Thought-Proof-CTA because the opinion has to compound
- Alternative: "DM me [word] to discuss" if post is debate-inviting
- Never stack

---

## Opinion-Proof Coherence Test

The proof must actually prove the thought. Failure mode: thought says X, proof shows Y, reader mentally connects them but the connection isn't airtight.

Test: write the proof section first. Does it support the thought? If it only half-supports, sharpen the thought.

---

## First Comment Plan

- 60-90 min post-publish
- Add one more proof point the body didn't include
- OR invite debate: "curious where people disagree with this"
- Reply to every long comment

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Thought is hedged: "maybe most people X" → Reject, sharpen
- Proof isn't dated: no timeframe = no proof
- Proof is anecdotal: one story isn't a pattern
- CTA asks too much: "book a call + DM me + follow + comment"
- Borrowed opinion: reads as recycled — must be the creator's actual take
