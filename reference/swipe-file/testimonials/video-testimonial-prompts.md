# Video Testimonial Prompts

> **10 prompts for collecting video testimonials.** Ordered to produce natural, specific, usable material. Replaces the generic "say something nice about me" ask.

---

## How To Use These

Send the client a document with the prompts, along with:
- Context (what you'll use it for, where it'll appear)
- Permissions (what they're approving)
- Technical ask (5-10 minutes of video, quiet room, ring light if possible, horizontal framing)

Ask them to record the prompts in order. They don't need to answer all of them. 6-8 answered well is plenty.

---

## The Prompts

### Prompt 1 — Starting State (30-60 sec)
```
"Walk me back to where you were before we started working together. 
What was your business looking like? What was frustrating you? 
What specifically made you pick up the phone or book the call?"
```

Produces: setup context. Pain-surface. Starting-state snapshot.

### Prompt 2 — The Decision (30-60 sec)
```
"What made you decide to actually engage, vs keep looking? 
What tipped it from 'maybe' to 'yes'? 
What were you uncertain about even as you said yes?"
```

Produces: decision frame. Objections they overcame. Why-you-not-others.

### Prompt 3 — The Process (60-90 sec)
```
"Describe what the work was actually like. 
What did we do together in the first few weeks? 
What was harder than you expected? What was easier?"
```

Produces: process credibility. Texture of the engagement.

### Prompt 4 — The Turn (30-60 sec)
```
"Was there a specific moment where you knew this was going to work? 
What was it? What had just happened?"
```

Produces: turn moment. Pivot. Belief-shift point.

### Prompt 5 — The Specific Result (60-90 sec)
```
"Walk me through the specific results. 
Numbers, metrics, outcomes — whatever you're comfortable sharing publicly. 
Before / after math is especially useful."
```

Produces: proof data. Dated metrics. Delta.

### Prompt 6 — The Unexpected Outcome (30-60 sec)
```
"What result did you get that you weren't expecting? 
Something that surprised you, either in kind or in magnitude?"
```

Produces: secondary benefits. Asymmetric outcomes. Honest surprise.

### Prompt 7 — The Honest Caveat (30-60 sec)
```
"What's been hard about it? Where have you hit friction? 
What hasn't worked as well as you'd hoped?"
```

Produces: honesty credibility. Avoids hype tone. Increases trust in everything else.

### Prompt 8 — The Recommendation Filter (30-60 sec)
```
"Who would you recommend this to specifically? 
And — importantly — who wouldn't you recommend it to? 
Who shouldn't engage?"
```

Produces: ICP definition by social proof. Anti-ICP signal. Filter credibility.

### Prompt 9 — The Imagined Future (30-60 sec)
```
"Where do you see this going over the next year? 
What's the next thing you want to unlock?"
```

Produces: forward momentum. Signals that the engagement produced a trajectory, not a one-off.

### Prompt 10 — The Raw Sentence (open)
```
"If someone asked you at a party what working with me was actually like, what would you tell them? 
Don't think — just say the first honest version."
```

Produces: the verbatim quote that often becomes the banner line. This one alone is worth the shoot.

## Editing Guidance

Once you have the raw video:

1. Watch it once, no editing, no notes
2. Identify the 3-5 strongest 20-second clips
3. Transcribe those clips verbatim
4. Cut each into a standalone testimonial asset (for different channels)
5. Keep one 90-120 second "full testimonial" version for the sales page
6. Extract 2-3 quote cards (static image + text) for social

One video = 5-7 testimonial assets across channels.

---

## Technical Ask

What to tell the client:
- 5-10 minutes of their time
- Quiet room (no background music / noise)
- Horizontal framing (16:9) — easier to crop for vertical later
- Good natural light or a simple ring light
- Phone is fine; DSLR better
- No pressure to be polished — raw + honest > over-produced

## Permissions Document

Include with the prompts:

```
Permission grant:

I, [NAME], authorize [YOUR NAME / BRAND] to use the video recording 
made on [DATE] for the following purposes:

[ ] Website / sales pages
[ ] Social media (LinkedIn, IG, X, etc.)
[ ] Email marketing
[ ] Paid advertising
[ ] Internal materials only

Identifying details that may be shared:
[ ] Name
[ ] Business name  
[ ] Revenue figures
[ ] Industry / role
[ ] Likeness (face in video)

Duration of grant: [indefinite | N years]
Revocation: [terms]

Signature + date
```

---

## Compensation Disclosure

If the client is compensated in any way (discount, free service, referral fee, etc.), the testimonial must disclose it per FTC guidelines. Example:

> "Disclosure: I received a discounted engagement in exchange for this testimonial."

Uncompensated testimonials don't need this disclosure but should still be accurate.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Over-leading the prompts ("tell me how I changed your life")
- Skipping the caveat prompt (loses credibility)
- Skipping the anti-ICP prompt (reduces filter value)
- Editing in ways that change meaning
- Publishing without written permission
- Missing compensation disclosure
