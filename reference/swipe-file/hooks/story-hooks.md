# Story Hooks

> **20 story-opener hooks.** First line of a narrative — earns line 2. Works when the body is storytelling. Fails when the body is a list.

---

## How Story Hooks Work

A story hook opens a narrative loop the reader must see closed. The opener promises tension, stakes, or setup.

Three story patterns:
1. **In Medias Res** — drop the reader into the middle of a scene
2. **The Precipitating Event** — the moment something changed
3. **The Contradiction** — a setup that doesn't add up, forcing resolution

---

## Variant Structure Template

```
[SETTING + STAKES + TENSION — one line]
[OPTIONAL: second line that raises the stakes or deepens the tension]
```

Story hooks fail by over-explaining. Drop detail and trust the reader's imagination.

## The Hooks

### Pattern: In Medias Res

**1. The Middle-Of-Scene Open**
```
[SPECIFIC TIME + SPECIFIC PLACE]. [SPECIFIC ACTION HAPPENING].
The [LARGER CONTEXT] — nobody knew about yet.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**2. The Physical Sensation Open**
```
My hands were shaking when I [ACTION].
It wasn't fear. It was the exact moment I realized [REALIZATION].
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube.

**3. The Conversation Fragment**
```
"[QUOTED LINE]," he said.
I sat with it for three days before I understood what he was really saying.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**4. The Decision Scene**
```
I was [MUNDANE ACTION] when the [SPECIFIC NOTIFICATION / EVENT] came in.
I had maybe 90 seconds to make the decision. The decision was [HIGH-STAKES DECISION].
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**5. The Environment Open**
```
[SPECIFIC SENSORY DETAIL — coffee shop noise, 3am screen glow, quiet empty office].
I remember thinking [SPECIFIC THOUGHT]. Then the email arrived.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube.

**6. The Room Scene**
```
The room: [N] people. [ONE SPECIFIC DETAIL ABOUT THE PEOPLE].
I was the [N+1]th person and I had [STAKE]. Here's what happened next.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

### Pattern: The Precipitating Event

**7. The Day That Changed Everything**
```
[SPECIFIC DATE]. The day the [CORE NARRATIVE] began — even though I didn't know it for [TIMEFRAME].
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**8. The Phone Call**
```
I got the phone call [SPECIFIC TIME]. The voice on the other end said exactly [WORDS].
I hung up and stared at the wall for [TIME PERIOD]. Here's what happened next.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**9. The Moment Of Decision**
```
The decision took 11 seconds.
The consequences took [TIMEFRAME]. The lesson took [LONGER TIMEFRAME].
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube.

**10. The Email I Almost Didn't Open**
```
I almost archived the email. The subject line looked like [BORING/GENERIC DESCRIPTION].
Glad I opened it. It [CHANGED CONDITION].
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**11. The Stranger's Sentence**
```
A stranger said [SPECIFIC SENTENCE] to me on [SPECIFIC DAY].
I've built every decision since on that sentence.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**12. The Rejection Hour**
```
[SPECIFIC TIME]. [SPECIFIC REJECTION RECEIVED].
Usually this post would be about overcoming. It's actually about something else.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

### Pattern: The Contradiction

**13. The Seems-Contradictory Open**
```
[RESULT A] and [RESULT B, WHICH SHOULDN'T COEXIST WITH A] — both true, same [TIMEFRAME].
The explanation is counterintuitive.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, X.

**14. The Win-That-Felt-Like-Loss**
```
I [WON SOMETHING OBJECTIVELY].
I cried in the [LOCATION] afterward. Not about the win — about [SURPRISING REASON].
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**15. The Loss-That-Won**
```
I lost [SPECIFIC THING] on [SPECIFIC DATE].
Best financial outcome of my year. Here's the math.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**16. The Unexpected Teacher**
```
The [SURPRISING SOURCE — a 23-year-old client, a taxi driver, an intern] taught me the most important [DOMAIN] lesson I know.
They didn't mean to. That's the lesson too.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**17. The Smallest Thing**
```
The smallest thing changed the largest outcome.
[SMALL DETAIL]. [LARGE OUTCOME]. The connection is the post.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**18. The Quiet Moment**
```
Nothing happened that day. I was [ORDINARY ACTIVITY].
A thought passed through that changed what I'd do the next [TIMEFRAME]. This is the thought.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

**19. The Thing I Didn't Tell You**
```
Three weeks ago, I posted about [EARLIER EVENT].
What I didn't say in that post: [OMITTED DETAIL]. Here's why it mattered, and why I'm saying it now.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube.

**20. The Chapter I Skipped**
```
Every time I tell this story, I skip [SPECIFIC PART].
I'm not going to skip it this time.
```
Platform fit: LinkedIn, YouTube, email SL.

## Story-Hook Integrity

Stories in creator content have to be:
1. **True** — actual event, possibly edited for clarity, never fabricated
2. **Recent enough** — if it's 10+ years old, say so
3. **The creator's own** — not a second-hand story passed off as first-hand
4. **Transferable** — ties to a lesson or mechanism the reader can use

Violations (from `INVARIANTS.md`):
- Never invent events
- Never borrow a story without attribution
- Never manufacture a narrative arc where one didn't exist

---

## The Body Architecture After A Story Hook

Story hooks commit the body to a narrative shape. Best structure:

```
HOOK         — in medias res / precipitating event / contradiction
SETUP        — who, where, stakes (2-4 sentences)
RISING       — what made it harder (2-4 sentences)
TURN         — the pivot / decision / realization
AFTERMATH    — what happened next
LESSON       — the mechanism or takeaway (1-3 sentences)
CTA or CLOSE — invitation to act / reflect / DM
```

This is the 3-A story (Arrival / Adversity / Aftermath) applied to the hook.

---

## Combination Rule

Story hooks work best with:
- Insight hooks in the body (narrative → earned mechanism)
- Specific-result hooks as the aftermath payoff
- Identity hooks as the closing frame ("if you're [ICP], this story is your story")

Never pair with another story hook. One narrative per asset.
