# Isomorphic Case Study Structure

> **Same shape + similar situation + same outcome.** Per `INVARIANTS.md` Isomorphic Story Principle. Readers see themselves in the client = association = conversion.

---

## The Structure

```
[OPENING MIRROR]
Describe the client's starting state in a way the reader recognizes as their own.

[SITUATIONAL MATCH]
Specific details that rhyme with the reader's life (role, stage, constraints).

[SAME STRUCTURAL PATH]
The process they ran — described as a pattern, not as unique.

[SAME OUTCOME SHAPE]
The result — described so the reader imagines their version.

[ASSOCIATION CLOSE]
Not a CTA. An invitation: "if this story rhymes, your version is closer than it feels."
```

## Worked Template — The Consultant Who Became An Operator

```
[OPENING MIRROR]
Picture a [ICP TYPE]: 3-7 years into their consulting practice, running at $[REVENUE BAND], mostly through referrals, stuck between "want to grow" and "can't grow without breaking the parts that work."

Their weeks look like: 2-3 client calls a day, async work between calls, writing a few posts a week, hoping the pipeline fills itself. Not broke. Not scaling. Running.

If that's a familiar shape, the rest of this is for you.

[SITUATIONAL MATCH]
The specific texture:

- Revenue around $[BAND] with high variance month to month
- 6-12 clients at a time, all 1:1 engagements
- Pipeline is 70%+ referrals
- Content inconsistent — posts when inspired, silent when slammed
- Offer is "consulting" or "advising" or "coaching" — category words, not outcome words
- Most hours of the week are spent delivering, not building

[SAME STRUCTURAL PATH]
The path ran in phases:

Phase 1 — name the constraint.
The constraint was never "need more leads." It was "the offer's shape caps the business, not the market."

Phase 2 — rewrite the offer.
From "hourly consulting on X" to "90-day outcome engagement for [ICP] facing [PAIN]."
Specific outcome. Time-bounded. Priced 3-4× the hourly equivalent.

Phase 3 — rebuild delivery.
Structured around the outcome, not the hours. Group elements for the non-personal work. Async for the between-meeting moves.

Phase 4 — repeat.
Same outreach. New offer. New economics. Pipeline shifted shape.

[SAME OUTCOME SHAPE]
The outcome:
- Revenue 2-3× prior, same or fewer hours
- Pipeline less referral-dependent (new offer travels better)
- Content cadence stabilized (weekly, not whenever)
- Position in the market clearer — they became known for a specific outcome, not a generic category
- Next step options opened: productize the outcome further, hire delivery, add a higher tier — options that weren't visible before

The specific numbers vary by [ICP]. The shape doesn't.

[ASSOCIATION CLOSE]
If you're reading this and the mirror landed on Phase 0 — the texture I described, not the outcome — your Phase 1 probably starts with the same audit: what's the actual shape of the ceiling?

The audit takes a weekend. The answer usually surprises people. Once it's named, the path is clearer than it seems.

I've run this path with [N] [ICP] over [TIMEFRAME]. Different domains, different personalities, different starting revenues. The shape is remarkably consistent.

If your version feels close, you can DM me "audit" and I'll send the worksheet. Or do it alone. Either works.
```

## Slot-Fill Rules

### The Opening Mirror slot
- Starting state described generically enough to apply to many readers
- Specific enough to be credible (not too generic)
- Uses language the reader uses about themselves

### The Situational Match slot
- Bullet list of 4-7 specific texture details
- Each one should make the reader nod ("yes, that's me")
- Shorthand — don't over-explain

### The Same Structural Path slot
- Phases, not unique steps
- The reader should feel "this is a path, not a one-off"
- Specific enough to be real, general enough to transfer

### The Same Outcome Shape slot
- Outcomes as patterns, not exact numbers
- "Revenue 2-3× prior" > "$47,200 to $142,600"
- Named "the specific numbers vary; the shape doesn't"

### The Association Close slot
- No hard CTA
- Invitation
- Honest about what the reader would have to do

## Isomorphic Principle

Per `INVARIANTS.md` Isomorphic Story Principle: same structure + similar situation + same outcome → association.

The reader's brain fills in: "if they got there and we started in the same place, I can get there."

The mechanism is matching, not persuading. Don't persuade — describe. The reader persuades themselves.

---

## Difference From Before-After

- Before-After is data-forward, specific numbers, one specific client
- Isomorphic is pattern-forward, described as a type, many clients aggregated into a shape

Use Isomorphic when you have multiple similar outcomes and want to show pattern.
Use Before-After when you have one strong specific case with clean delta math.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Opening mirror too generic (reader doesn't recognize themselves)
- Opening mirror too specific (reader thinks "that's not me")
- Situational match missing (reader doesn't feel the rhyme)
- Outcome described in absolute numbers (breaks pattern association)
- Hard CTA in the close (breaks the association frame)
