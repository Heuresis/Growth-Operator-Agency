# Reverse-Order Case Study Structure

> **Start with outcome. Walk backward.** Hook-first. Works when the delta is dramatic enough to open with.

---

## The Structure

```
[OUTCOME FIRST]
Lead with the end state. Specific numbers. Dated.

[THE DISBELIEF HOOK]
The counter-intuitive or unlikely part of the outcome.

[THE IMMEDIATE BEFORE]
The final step that produced the outcome.

[WALKING BACKWARD]
Go backward through the arc — step by step, in reverse.

[THE STARTING POINT]
End at where it all started.

[THE MECHANISM + INVITATION]
Why it worked. Open door for the reader.
```

---

## Worked Template — The Reverse-Engineered Arc

```
[OUTCOME FIRST]
$[AMOUNT]/month in recurring revenue.
[HOURS]/week of founder time.
[TEAM SIZE] team members.
92% client retention at 12 months.

As of last month.

[THE DISBELIEF HOOK]
Two years ago the same business was:
$[STARTING AMOUNT]/month
[HIGH HOURS]/week founder time
Same team size
54% client retention at 12 months

Same market. Same founder. Same team. Different shape.

[THE IMMEDIATE BEFORE]
The final move that closed the arc: implementing a quarterly pricing and delivery refresh built into the engagement structure. Clients stopped churning at the 6-month mark because the engagement refreshed before the refresh question came up internally.

That move alone didn't do it. It landed because of everything that came before.

[WALKING BACKWARD]

Six months before the final move: added a second delivery specialist to the team so founder wasn't the bottleneck on client feedback cycles.

Twelve months before: renegotiated offer structure from 3-month contracts to 12-month with built-in opt-out clauses — clients who'd have left at month 6 under old structure renewed at month 12 under new structure at ~70%.

Eighteen months before: pruned lowest-margin service line (represented 22% of revenue, 45% of delivery hours). Revenue dropped 22% in the month of the prune. Recovered and passed prior levels within 4 months because the remaining services got better.

Twenty-four months before: redesigned onboarding to hit an activation metric in the first 14 days. Clients who activated retained at 85%+; those who didn't retained at 30%. The onboarding redesign pushed activation rate from 41% to 73%.

Twenty-seven months before: defined the activation metric. Before this, "retention" was measured as "clients who stayed." After this, retention was measurable as a function of early behavior — actionable, not just reportable.

Thirty months before: the diagnostic. Sat with the pattern that 46% of clients were leaving at or before month 12. Named it as "retention problem masquerading as acquisition problem."

[THE STARTING POINT]
Thirty months ago, the founder was convinced the business needed more leads.

It didn't. It needed fewer clients who stayed longer.

The entire arc is downstream of that diagnostic reframe.

[THE MECHANISM + INVITATION]
The mechanism isn't the onboarding redesign or the pricing refresh or the hire. It's the ordering. Solving retention before scaling acquisition changes what each downstream move is worth.

More leads into a leaky bucket = faster churn. Fewer leads into a tight bucket = compounding revenue.

The reader's version: if your retention numbers at month 6 or 12 are below 70%, the "grow" work probably isn't growth work. It's retention work. The arc is available. It takes 24-30 months, roughly in the order above.

If the mirror hit, the first move is the same as the one here: define the activation metric. You can do that alone this weekend. Everything else downstream follows.
```

## Slot-Fill Rules

### The Outcome First slot
- Specific metrics
- Dated (so reader can't dismiss as lifetime numbers)
- Lead with the most dramatic delta

### The Disbelief Hook slot
- Starting state for contrast
- Makes the reader ask "how"
- Concise (3-5 lines)

### The Immediate Before slot
- The last move that produced the outcome
- Named specifically
- Hint that it only worked because of prior moves

### The Walking Backward slot
- Reverse chronology, step by step
- Specific timeframes ("six months before," "twelve months before")
- Each step builds the case for the prior step

### The Starting Point slot
- The originating diagnostic or moment
- The frame shift that made the whole arc possible

### The Mechanism + Invitation slot
- Why the ordering mattered
- The transferable principle
- Soft invitation for the reader's version

---

## When Reverse-Order Wins

- Outcome is dramatic and specific
- Multiple intermediate steps the reader needs to see
- Hook-first platform (Reels, X thread, LinkedIn hook)
- Audience is sophisticated (can handle non-linear)

When it loses:
- Simple before-after (no intermediate steps to walk through)
- Audience prefers linear storytelling
- Outcome isn't dramatic enough to carry the lead

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Outcome isn't dramatic enough to hook with
- Walking backward feels mechanical (lose narrative pull)
- Too many steps (6+ backward steps = loses reader)
- Starting point is generic (undermines the arc)
- Missing mechanism (the ordering is the point)
