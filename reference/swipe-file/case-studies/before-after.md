# Before-After Case Study Structure

> **Classic delta.** Starting state → ending state. Numbers-forward. Proof-dominant.

---

## The Structure

```
[CLIENT INTRO]
1-2 sentences. ICP type, stage, situation.

[BEFORE STATE]
Specific, dated snapshot of starting conditions.
Numbers: revenue, hours, audience, retention, margin, etc.
Pain: what wasn't working.

[WHAT CHANGED]
The specific intervention. Dated timeline.
Not abstract — specific moves.

[AFTER STATE]
Dated snapshot of current conditions.
Same metrics as Before, with deltas.

[MECHANISM]
Why it worked. Transferable principle.

[REPLICABILITY CAVEATS]
Honest conditions under which this outcome is reproducible vs isn't.
```

## Worked Template — B2B Consultant Delta

```
[CLIENT INTRO]
[ICP TYPE]: senior consultant, 8 years in domain, pre-engagement working solo.

[BEFORE STATE]
As of [SPECIFIC MONTH]:
— Monthly revenue: $[AMOUNT]
— Hours worked: [NUMBER]/week
— Engagement type: hourly, 1-off
— Pipeline: 90% inbound referrals, 0 outbound
— Close rate on qualified calls: [%]
— Deal size average: $[AMOUNT]
— Client retention at 6 months: [%]

Stated pain: "I'm busy, making decent money, and stuck. Adding hours doesn't help because I'm already maxed. Adding rate feels like hitting a market ceiling."

[WHAT CHANGED]
11-week engagement. Three structural changes:

Week 1-3: Repositioned the offer from hourly consulting to a flat-fee 90-day outcome engagement.
— New offer written as 1-page contract
— Price: 4× equivalent hourly math
— Outcome: specific, measurable, time-bounded

Week 4-6: Rebuilt delivery to be outcome-accountable.
— Milestones at day 15, 30, 60, 90
— Async + weekly call cadence
— Guarantee: continue past 90 days free if outcome not hit

Week 7-11: Ran same outreach sequence, new offer.
— Pipeline velocity doubled (specific offers attract decisive buyers)
— Close rate on qualified calls increased
— Deal size increased 3-4×

[AFTER STATE]
As of [SPECIFIC MONTH, 3 months post-engagement start]:
— Monthly revenue: $[AMOUNT × 3.1]
— Hours worked: [NUMBER]/week (roughly similar)
— Engagement type: 90-day outcome, flat fee
— Pipeline: 60% inbound, 40% referral-nurture outbound
— Close rate on qualified calls: [% × 1.6]
— Deal size average: $[AMOUNT × 4]
— Client retention at 6 months: data incomplete (90 days post-engagement)

Stated shift: "The business feels structured for the first time. Hourly was making me choose between hours and revenue. This doesn't."

[MECHANISM]
Hourly billing sells time (commodity). Outcome billing sells risk transfer (premium).

Buyers pay exponentially more for risk transfer because that's the whole point of hiring a consultant. Hourly billing puts the buyer in charge of the outcome; outcome billing puts the consultant in charge.

Transferable principle: **price the outcome, not the input**. Structurally changes both the close rate and the deal size simultaneously.

[REPLICABILITY CAVEATS]
This result reproduces when:
- The consultant has a genuinely measurable outcome to sell (not all services do)
- The consultant has 12+ months of case evidence to back the outcome
- The market's buyers are organized enough to assess outcome-based contracts
- The consultant has the discipline to say no to hourly work during the transition

It doesn't reproduce when:
- The consultant doesn't have outcome evidence
- The market is trained to buy hours (some verticals)
- The consultant lacks the runway to wait 2-3 months for the pipeline to rebuild
```

## Slot-Fill Rules

### The Client Intro slot
- Anonymized unless explicit permission
- Specific enough to be credible (years in domain, stage, situation)
- Not generic ("a client of mine")

### The Before State slot
- Dated (month / quarter precision)
- Specific metrics (same metrics that appear in After)
- Pain in client's own words (paraphrased if not exact quote)

### The What Changed slot
- Sequential (week 1-N)
- Specific actions (not "we worked on positioning")
- Time-bounded steps

### The After State slot
- Same metrics as Before
- Dated
- Delta math visible

### The Mechanism slot
- Transferable principle
- Falsifiable (could be wrong, isn't in this case)
- Named if possible

### The Replicability Caveats slot
- Honest: not every client hits this delta
- Conditions under which the result holds
- Conditions under which it wouldn't

---

## Proof Integrity

Per `INVARIANTS.md`:
- Real numbers only (never manufactured)
- Permission required for identifying
- Mention anonymization explicitly if applied
- Don't round in ways that inflate

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Numbers too round ($50k → $150k feels invented; $47,200 → $148,900 feels real)
- Before/After metrics don't match (measuring different things)
- No timeline (reader can't assess reasonableness)
- Mechanism missing (reader sees result, doesn't know why)
- No caveats (reads as guaranteed replication)
