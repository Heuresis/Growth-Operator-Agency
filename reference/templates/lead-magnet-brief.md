## 9 Lead Magnet Types

| Type | Best for | Consumption | Conversion mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Checklist | Problem-aware audience needing structure | 5-10 min | Simple opt-in → email sequence |
| Cheatsheet | Reference-style quick-win | 3-5 min | Opt-in → webinar or VSL |
| Template | "Give me the frame, I'll fill it in" | 10-20 min | Opt-in → tripwire or core offer |
| Swipe-file | Proof-driven audiences (copywriters, marketers) | 10-30 min | Opt-in → nurture → VSL |
| Mini-course | Solution-aware audience willing to commit time | 20-60 min | Opt-in → sequence → application/book-a-call |
| Quiz | Unaware or problem-aware discovery | 2-5 min | Opt-in → personalized outcome → segmented sequence |
| Challenge | Community + commitment-based lead gen | 3-7 days | Opt-in → challenge → pitch |
| Toolkit | Done-with-you style bundling multiple assets | 15-30 min | Opt-in → nurture → mid-ticket or application |
| Case-study pack | Proof-hungry, later-stage audience | 15-30 min | Opt-in → application / book-a-call |

## Appendix B — Type Decision Tree

- Audience is problem-aware and wants structure → Checklist
- Audience wants quick reference → Cheatsheet
- Audience wants to fill-in-the-blank → Template
- Audience loves proof and sees many examples → Swipe-file or Case-study pack
- Audience wants to commit time to learn → Mini-course
- Audience is unaware or segmenting-friendly → Quiz
- Audience responds to community + commitment → Challenge
- Audience wants a bundled done-with-you experience → Toolkit

## Appendix C — Bridge Design

A lead magnet's job is not to teach everything. Its job is to:
1. Deliver one real micro-win.
2. Surface the next problem that only the paid offer solves.
3. Position the paid offer as the obvious next step.

If steps 2 and 3 are weak, conversion stays at the opt-in layer.
