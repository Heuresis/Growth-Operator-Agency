## 1. Pre-Capture Prep

### 1.1 Review client's outcome

Before the capture, review:

- Their before state (from CRM / onboarding notes)
- Their specific outcome (numbers + timeframe)
- Specific mechanism sub-steps they applied
- Common objection they had that they overcame

### 1.2 Release form

Confirm the release form covers:

- [ ] Name usage (full / first only / archetype only)
- [ ] Image and likeness
- [ ] Channels (sales page / ads / social / website / internal)
- [ ] Duration (perpetual or time-bound)
- [ ] Geography
- [ ] Modification rights (can creator edit for length?)

Reference `reference/legal-templates/photo-video-release.md`.

### 1.3 Capture environment setup

**For video:**
- Quiet room, no echo
- Natural light from front or good key light
- Camera at eye level
- Phone or DSLR — vertical (9:16) for social, horizontal (16:9) for sales page
- Mic (lavalier or USB) — don't rely on laptop mic
- Test recording before live capture

**For written:**
- Google Doc or email thread
- Set expectation: "30-60 minutes to answer, specific answers > long answers"

## 2. Video Capture Prompts (in order)

### 2.1 Opening (establishes the archetype)

**Prompt 1:**
> "Quick intro — tell me who you are and what you do."

*Target answer: 20-30 sec. Sets archetype for viewer.*

**Prompt 2:**
> "Before you started working with [creator] / before [Offer Name], where were you in [relevant area]?"

*Target: 30-60 sec. Specific before state, not vague.*

**Follow-up if answer is generic:**
> "Can you give me a specific example? Like what were you actually doing week-to-week?"

### 2.2 The problem (the stuck point)

**Prompt 3:**
> "What was the one thing that was most frustrating about [area]?"

*Target: 30-60 sec. Surface Layer 2-3 pain.*

**Prompt 4:**
> "What had you already tried? What didn't work?"

*Target: 30-60 sec. Establishes credibility — you've heard my attempts.*

### 2.3 The decision

**Prompt 5:**
> "What made you decide to work with [creator] / try [Offer Name]?"

*Target: 30-60 sec.*

**Prompt 6:**
> "Was there a moment or piece of content where you thought, 'okay this person knows what they're talking about'?"

*Target: 30-60 sec. Gives specific decision-anchor.*

**Prompt 7:**
> "What were you worried about before joining? What almost stopped you?"

*Target: 30-60 sec. This becomes objection-handling material.*

### 2.4 The experience

**Prompt 8:**
> "Walk me through what you actually did once you got inside. What was the first thing you applied?"

*Target: 60-90 sec. Mechanism-specific.*

**Prompt 9:**
> "What was the specific move or insight that unlocked the biggest result?"

*Target: 60-90 sec. The one thing — often different per client.*

### 2.5 The outcome

**Prompt 10:**
> "Where are you now? What's specifically changed?"

*Target: 60-90 sec. Numbers + timeframe + identity shift.*

**Prompt 11:**
> "Give me a specific example — like what happened last week that wouldn't have happened six months ago?"

*Target: 30-60 sec. Proof via concrete example.*

### 2.6 The recommendation

**Prompt 12:**
> "Who is this for? Who should work with [creator] / join [Offer Name]?"

*Target: 30 sec. Creates avatar recognition for next buyer.*

**Prompt 13:**
> "Who is it NOT for? Who should skip it?"

*Target: 30 sec. Trust-building — honest disqualification.*

**Prompt 14:**
> "What would you say to someone who's on the fence right now?"

*Target: 30-60 sec. Peer-to-peer objection handling.*

### 2.7 Close

**Prompt 15:**
> "If you had to sum up your experience in one sentence, what would you say?"

*Target: 15-30 sec. Pull-quote for ads and pages.*

## 3. Written Capture Prompts

Send as a structured form (Typeform / Google Form / doc). Instructions:

> "The more specific you are, the more useful this will be — for [creator] and for others thinking about joining. Bullet points are fine. Aim for concrete examples, not general praise."

### 3.1 Identity + before

**Q1:** Who are you and what do you do (1-2 sentences)?

**Q2:** Before [Offer Name], where were you in [area]? Describe the specific situation.

**Q3:** What was the most frustrating part of that situation?

**Q4:** What had you already tried that didn't work?

### 3.2 Decision

**Q5:** What made you decide to try [Offer Name]?

**Q6:** What were you worried about?

**Q7:** What finally pushed you past the hesitation?

### 3.3 Experience

**Q8:** What did you actually do inside [Offer Name] that moved the needle most?

**Q9:** What was the specific insight or move that unlocked the biggest change?

### 3.4 Outcome

**Q10:** Where are you now? Include any specific numbers or milestones.

**Q11:** What's one thing you're doing now that you weren't doing before?

**Q12:** What's one thing you're NOT doing now that you were doing before?

### 3.5 Recommendation

**Q13:** Who would you recommend this to?

**Q14:** Who should probably skip it?

**Q15:** If you had to sum up your experience in one sentence, what would you say?

### 3.6 Closing

**Q16:** Anything else you want to say?

**Q17:** [Permissions check] Can we use your first name and the results you've shared in marketing materials (sales page, ads, social)? Yes / Full name / First name only / Archetype only

## 4. Interview Best Practices

### 4.1 For the interviewer

- **Let silence work.** After they answer, pause 3-5 seconds before the next prompt. Often the best material comes in the silence.
- **Ask "can you give me a specific example?"** whenever an answer is general.
- **Avoid leading.** Don't say "would you agree that [creator] transformed your business?" Ask "what changed?" and let them tell you.
- **Don't interrupt.** Even if they pause, let them finish.
- **Re-ask if needed.** If an answer is too short, "Say more about that?"

### 4.2 For the subject

Brief them:
- "Speak as if you're talking to a friend who's considering this."
- "Specific stories matter more than big claims."
- "If you mess up a sentence, just restart — we'll edit."
- "There are no right answers."

### 4.3 Duration

- Video interview: 30-45 min, produces 5-15 min raw
- Written: 30-60 min on their side, comes back usable

## 7. Hard Rules

- **NEVER** capture without signed release.
- **NEVER** use testimonial outside the scope approved in the release.
- **NEVER** edit a testimonial to change meaning — only for length and clarity.
- **NEVER** use archetype anonymization as a way around missing release.
- **ALWAYS** verify numbers / timeframes before using them in marketing.
- **ALWAYS** include disclaimers about atypical results if the outcome is exceptional.
- **ALWAYS** honor withdrawal requests if the subject asks to revoke use.

## Appendix A — Common Answer Patterns to Watch For

Generic (flag these and re-prompt for specifics):
- "It changed my life" → re-prompt: "What specifically changed?"
- "[Creator] is amazing" → re-prompt: "What did they do that was different?"
- "Best decision I ever made" → re-prompt: "What made it worth it?"

Specific (keep these):
- "I went from [number] to [number] in [timeframe]."
- "The thing that unlocked it was when [specific move]."
- "I was worried about [specific objection] but [how it was resolved]."

## Appendix B — Ethical Guidelines

- Compensating for testimonials is allowed in most jurisdictions if disclosed. Check local rules (FTC in US, ASA in UK, similar in EU).
- Exceptional results require disclaimer ("Results not typical").
- Health / income claims require extra scrutiny.
- Subject can withdraw consent at any time — honor promptly.
