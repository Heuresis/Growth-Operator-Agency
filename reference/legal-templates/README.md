# reference/legal-templates/

Starter legal templates for a high-ticket creator business. Drop-in skeletons you can tailor to your jurisdiction, then have a licensed attorney sign off before use.

> **DISCLAIMER — read before editing.** Every file in this directory is a template only. It is **not legal advice** and **does not create an attorney-client relationship**. Jurisdictional rules vary by state, province, country, industry, and buyer class. Before you ship any version of any of these documents to prospects, clients, contractors, affiliates, or employees, review with a licensed attorney who practices in your operating jurisdiction and knows your business model. Treat these templates as a 70% head-start on the drafting, not the final artifact.

## What's here

| File | Use when |
|---|---|
| `privacy-policy.md` | You collect any personal data (email opt-ins, checkout, analytics). Required on marketing pages under GDPR / CCPA. |
| `terms-of-service.md` | You run a website, course platform, or any digital product where users can register, purchase, or access content. |
| `refund-policy.md` | You sell any digital product or coaching engagement. Three variants (strict / reasonable / extended-guarantee). |
| `client-agreement.md` | High-ticket coaching or consulting engagement ($3K–$50K+). Scope, deliverables, payment, IP, termination. |
| `affiliate-agreement.md` | You run an affiliate or referral program. Commission structure, attribution, prohibited practices, clawback. |
| `contractor-agreement.md` | You hire independent contractors (VAs, editors, writers, ad buyers). IP ownership, payment terms, confidentiality. |
| `nda.md` | You expose proprietary assets (client lists, SOPs, offer math) to a contractor or partner. Mutual non-disclosure. |
| `ip-assignment.md` | A contractor creates deliverables (copy, design, code) you want to own. Work-for-hire + moral-rights waiver. |
| `photo-video-release.md` | You capture a client's testimonial on video, use their likeness in ads, or feature them in a case study. |
| `saas-terms.md` | You sell a software product or SaaS subscription. Uptime, data handling, termination, liability. |
| `aup.md` | Acceptable Use Policy. Pairs with Terms of Service; covers what users cannot do on your platform. |

## Usage pattern

1. Copy the file into `/your-private-company/legal/` (outside the repo).
2. Fill in every `{{BRACKETED_FIELD}}`.
3. Red-pen the template — cross out clauses that do not apply to your model, mark sections that need more detail.
4. Send to your attorney for jurisdiction review + structural rewrite where needed.
5. Version the approved final document. Treat later changes as amendments.

## Non-negotiables (things no template can substitute for)

- A **registered business entity** in your jurisdiction (LLC / Ltd / GmbH / equivalent). Templates assume you are contracting as an entity, not a person.
- **Business insurance** — general liability + errors-and-omissions (E&O) for coaching / consulting, plus product liability if you sell physical goods.
- **Trademark + domain ownership** registered under the entity name.
- A **registered agent** for service of process where applicable.

These sit upstream of the templates. The templates assume they exist.

## Jurisdictional notes

The templates are drafted in US English with generic US-state neutrality. Notes inline where you must adjust for:
- **EU / UK** — GDPR data-handling, right-to-erasure, DPA, cookie consent specifics
- **California** — CCPA / CPRA consumer-rights additions
- **Canada** — PIPEDA + CASL for marketing consent
- **Australia** — Privacy Act 1988 + Australian Consumer Law
- **Rest of world** — local consumer-protection law applies; consult counsel

## What these templates do NOT cover

- Employment law (W-2 / PAYE / equivalents) — talk to a payroll specialist and employment attorney
- Securities or investment offerings — separate regulatory regime, not a template problem
- Fiduciary / advisory services (financial advice, therapy, medical) — heavily regulated, not suitable for templating
- International tax structuring — country-specific, talk to a cross-border CPA
- Class-action liability caps — jurisdiction-specific, attorney only
