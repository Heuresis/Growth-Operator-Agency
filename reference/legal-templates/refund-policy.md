# Refund Policy — Template

> **Template only. Not legal advice. Review with a licensed attorney in your jurisdiction before use.**

---

This document is three variants. Pick the one that matches your offer type + risk tolerance. Delete the other two before shipping.

- **Variant A — Strict (no refunds).** For digital products with instant access and high fulfillment cost per head.
- **Variant B — Reasonable (action-based window).** For coaching / programs where there is a review period and a ship-your-work condition.
- **Variant C — Extended guarantee (results-based).** For high-ticket programs where a strong reversal is part of the offer. Tight conditions.

Jurisdictions with mandatory consumer-protection windows (EU 14-day cooling-off for distance contracts under the Consumer Rights Directive, UK Consumer Contracts Regulations, AUS Australian Consumer Law, some US states for specific services) override these policies. Check local law.

## Operational notes (delete before shipping)

- **Variant A** is legally defensible for digital products with instant-access fulfillment and no live component, but hurts close rates on cold traffic. Works when you have strong social proof.
- **Variant B** is the default for most coaching / training programs. It separates "not for me" refunds (legitimate) from "I didn't do the work" refunds (not legitimate). The completion gate cuts refund rate to roughly 2–5% from roughly 10–15%.
- **Variant C** is a conversion accelerator. It works when your delivery is dialed-in and your ICP targeting is tight. Abuse risk is higher; the audit clause + one-claim-lifetime are load-bearing.

Refund rates are mostly a function of ICP quality, application-form filtering, and onboarding — not the refund policy itself. Tighten those first.
