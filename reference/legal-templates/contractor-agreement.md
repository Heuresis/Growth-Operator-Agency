## 3. Independent contractor relationship

Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or partner.

- Contractor sets own hours, methods, and workspace, subject to agreed deadlines and deliverable specs.
- Contractor provides own tools and equipment unless Schedule A specifies otherwise.
- Contractor may work with other clients provided no conflict (Section 8).
- Contractor receives no employee benefits (health insurance, retirement, paid leave).
- Company does not withhold payroll taxes; Contractor is responsible for self-reporting.

If the relationship is later reclassified by a tax or labor authority, Contractor's liability for employment taxes, penalties, or reclassified benefits is Contractor's alone, unless the reclassification resulted from Company's misrepresentation.

## 4. Work product & intellectual property

### 4.1 Work made for hire

All deliverables produced by Contractor in connection with the Services (code, copy, design, video, audio, strategies, frameworks documented for Company) are "**Work Product**" and constitute "**work made for hire**" to the maximum extent permitted by law.

### 4.2 Assignment

To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as work made for hire, Contractor hereby assigns to Company, exclusively and irrevocably, all right, title, and interest in the Work Product, including all intellectual property rights, waiving any moral rights to the extent permitted.

### 4.3 Pre-existing IP

Contractor retains ownership of tools, processes, or content created before engagement or outside the scope of Services ("**Pre-Existing IP**"). Where Pre-Existing IP is incorporated into Work Product, Contractor grants Company a worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, sublicensable license to use it in the Work Product's context.

### 4.4 Portfolio use

Contractor may reference the engagement in Contractor's portfolio (client name, deliverable type, anonymized metrics) unless Company marks an asset as confidential (Section 5). Specific metrics, numbers, or revenue figures must be pre-approved by Company before portfolio use.

## 5. Confidentiality

Contractor will keep confidential all non-public information learned during the engagement, including but not limited to:

- Client lists, customer data, prospect lists
- Revenue numbers, unit economics, pricing, forecasts
- Offer construction, funnel architecture, scripts, ad creative, email sequences
- Strategic plans, launch calendars, personnel matters
- Source code, credentials, API keys

Confidentiality survives for {{CONFIDENTIALITY_YEARS}} years after termination (perpetually for trade secrets under applicable law).

Exceptions: information that is (a) public without Contractor's fault, (b) lawfully in Contractor's possession before engagement, (c) independently developed, or (d) required to be disclosed by law.

## 6. Security & data handling

Contractor will:

- Use Company-provided credentials only for the engagement; not share, store in plaintext, or transmit over insecure channels
- Use multi-factor authentication on every shared account
- Store client data only on encrypted drives / approved cloud locations
- Return or destroy Company data on termination within 7 days and certify destruction on request
- Report any suspected security incident within 24 hours to {{SECURITY_CONTACT}}

## 7. Conduct

Contractor will:

- Treat all Company staff, clients, and community members with respect
- Disclose conflicts of interest immediately
- Not engage in harassment, discrimination, or hostile conduct
- Comply with applicable laws (anti-bribery, anti-spam, data protection, advertising standards)

## 8. Non-solicitation & non-compete

### 8.1 Non-solicitation of clients

During the Term and for {{NON_SOLICIT_MONTHS}} months after, Contractor will not solicit Company's clients, prospects, or affiliates Contractor was introduced to through the engagement, to divert business from Company or compete with Company's products.

### 8.2 Non-solicitation of staff

During the Term and for {{NON_SOLICIT_MONTHS}} months after, Contractor will not recruit or hire Company's employees or contractors.

### 8.3 Limited non-compete

During the Term only, Contractor will not work for a direct competitor offering substantially identical services to substantially the same market defined as {{COMPETITIVE_MARKET_DEFINITION}}. After termination, no general non-compete applies except the non-solicit above.

(Check enforceability in jurisdiction — non-competes are unenforceable in California and several other US states. Non-solicits are generally enforceable if narrowly tailored.)

## 9. Term & termination

### 9.1 Term

Begins {{EFFECTIVE_DATE}} and continues until terminated.

### 9.2 Termination for convenience

Either Party may terminate with {{TERMINATION_NOTICE_DAYS}} days' written notice. Company pays for completed work through termination.

### 9.3 Termination for cause

Either Party may terminate immediately for material breach, dishonesty, security incident, harassment, or non-payment (after a {{CURE_DAYS}}-day cure notice where applicable).

### 9.4 Effect

On termination:
- Contractor stops work and submits a final invoice within 14 days
- Contractor returns all Company property and data (Section 6)
- Sections 4, 5, 7, 8, 10, 11 survive

## 10. Representations & warranties

Contractor represents that:

- Contractor has authority to enter this Agreement
- Contractor's work will be original or properly licensed
- Work Product will not infringe a third party's rights
- Contractor will perform with reasonable skill and care consistent with industry standards
- Contractor has no agreement with another party that conflicts with this engagement

## 11. Liability & indemnity

### 11.1 Cap

Each Party's aggregate liability under this Agreement is limited to the fees paid in the 12 months before the event giving rise to the claim, except for confidentiality breach, IP infringement, and indemnity obligations under 11.2.

### 11.2 Contractor indemnity

Contractor indemnifies Company against claims arising from Contractor's infringement of a third party's IP, confidentiality breach, or willful misconduct.

### 11.3 No consequential

Neither Party is liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages, or lost profits.
