# The 3-Tier YouTube Funnel

> **Why this matters:** Most high-ticket creators build only one tier and wonder why growth stalls. The 3-tier funnel — Top (reach), Middle (nurture), Bottom (convert) — compounds because each tier feeds the next, and they require different content formats, not different channels.

## The 3 Tiers

| Tier | Format | View Target (per video) | Role |
|------|--------|------------------------|------|
| Top | Shorts + Listicle long-form | 500K–2M+ | Reach + cold awareness |
| Middle | Educational Deep-Dive + Tutorial | 100K–500K | Belief + mechanism-teaching |
| Bottom | VSSL + Case Study (w/ CTA) | 10K–50K | Conversion + book-a-call |

Each tier has a different purpose and is measured differently.

---

## Tier 1 — Top (Reach)

### Goal

Maximum reach. Introduce the channel to the broadest possible audience that matches your ICP. Build a top-of-funnel population the algorithm recognizes as "belongs to this channel."

### Content formats

- Shorts (primary — see `shorts-mrbeast-strategy.md`)
- Listicles (6–12 min long-form)
- Occasional hot-take / reaction content
- Trending-topic educational takes

### Metrics that matter

- Views (raw count)
- CTR (click-through rate from impressions)
- Reach (unique viewers)
- Shares (the virality proxy)

### Metrics that DON'T matter at this tier

- Watch time per view
- Conversion
- Book-a-call rate

### View target

- Shorts: 500K–2M views is the target band for compounding. Below 100K per Short = poor top-of-funnel fuel.
- Listicles: 50K–300K views in 30 days = healthy

### Publishing cadence

- Shorts: 3–5 per week
- Listicles: 1 per 2–3 weeks

### Why this tier exists

The algorithm needs a high-volume top to pattern-match "this channel's audience is X." Without Tier 1, the algorithm doesn't know where to place your Tier 2 videos. Channels that skip Tier 1 grow 3–5x slower than channels that use it to feed the system.

### The trap at Tier 1

Optimizing for views in a vacuum. If your Shorts get 2M views but none of them match your ICP, it's vanity traffic that actually HURTS the channel by confusing the algorithm.

Filter: if a top-tier video gets >500K views but <2% end-screen click-through, the audience is wrong for your funnel. Scrap the concept.

---

## Tier 2 — Middle (Nurture)

### Goal

Teach the mechanism. Move viewers from "this channel exists" to "this person is the expert on X." Build belief in the specific methodology that sells the offer.

### Content formats

- Educational Deep-Dive (15–30 min)
- Tutorial (8–15 min, search-optimized)
- Case Study (12–20 min, without hard CTA)
- Session-Starter videos (see `session-starter-strategy.md`)

### Metrics that matter

- Average view duration (AVD)
- Audience retention curve (smooth vs. cliffs)
- Session time generated
- End-screen click-through rate
- Playlist engagement
- Subscriber conversion rate

### View target

- 100K–500K views per video in the first 90 days
- Retention >45% at 50% mark
- End-screen CTR >8%

### Publishing cadence

- 1–2 per week

### Why this tier exists

Tier 1 reach without Tier 2 depth = viewers who never trust you enough to buy. Tier 2 is where authority is manufactured. Viewers who've watched 3+ Tier 2 videos are 6–12 times more likely to eventually book a call than viewers who've only watched Tier 1.

### The trap at Tier 2

Skipping the CTA entirely. Many creators treat Tier 2 as pure education and don't signal at all toward the bottom of the funnel. Result: nurture works, but the viewer doesn't know the next step. Fix: end every Tier 2 video with a SOFT CTA — a playlist, a lead magnet, a next video — even though Tier 2 isn't the conversion tier.

---

## Tier 3 — Bottom (Convert)

### Goal

Book calls. Convert warm viewers into applications, sales, or direct customers.

### Content formats

- VSSL (5-chapter or 15-step — see `vssl-5-chapter-youtube.md`, `vssl-15-step.md`)
- Case Study with hard CTA
- "How to work with me" content
- Launch videos

### Metrics that matter

- Book-a-call rate (applications per 1000 views)
- Show-up rate (for booked calls)
- Close rate
- Revenue attributed to the video
- Cost-per-acquisition (compared to paid ads)

### Metrics that DON'T matter at this tier

- Raw view count (if 10K views produces 300 calls, that's better than 500K views producing 5 calls)
- CTR from browse (Tier 3 is typically reached from pinned placements, mid-tier end-cards, and direct subscriber traffic)

### View target

- 10K–50K views per video
- Book-a-call rate: 0.5%–2% of viewers
- For a $10K offer: 1 booked call per $50–$200 of "earned media value"

### Publishing cadence

- 1 VSSL per quarter
- 1 Case-study-with-CTA per month

### Why this tier exists

Without Tier 3, the entire funnel generates attention but no revenue. The VSSL is the conversion engine. One well-built VSSL can produce 6–18 months of inbound when pinned + promoted in end-cards from Tier 1 and Tier 2 videos.

### The trap at Tier 3

Making Tier 3 content that's also Tier 2 (soft education with a CTA tacked on). VSSLs that don't commit to the sales structure convert at 10–20% of what a properly-built VSSL converts at.

## How the Tiers Compound

The flywheel:
1. Tier 1 Shorts/Listicles attract cold viewers
2. Strong Shorts route to Tier 2 deep-dives via descriptions + end-cards
3. Tier 2 videos build belief + subscribe the viewer
4. Subscribers who've watched 2–3 Tier 2 videos eventually see Tier 3
5. Tier 3 converts them into calls/applications
6. Revenue from Tier 3 funds more Tier 1 production (often via paid promotion of top Shorts/Listicles)

Each tier's strength amplifies the others. A weak tier bottlenecks the whole funnel.

---

## Diagnostic: Which Tier Is Broken?

If the funnel isn't compounding, find the bottleneck:

### Tier 1 broken
- Symptoms: low total views, low subscriber growth, no algorithm pickup
- Fix: more Shorts, better hooks, stronger listicle titles/thumbnails

### Tier 2 broken
- Symptoms: Tier 1 getting views but subscribers don't retain, no repeat viewers, low session time
- Fix: stronger mechanism teaching, better worked examples, session-starter design

### Tier 3 broken
- Symptoms: lots of subscribers, lots of views, no book-a-calls
- Fix: actual VSSL, clearer offer, stronger proof stack, single CTA

## 3-Tier Budget Allocation

For an operator spending 10 hours/week on YouTube:
- Tier 1: 3 hours (scripting + shooting Shorts, listicle writing)
- Tier 2: 5 hours (deep-dive scripting, shooting, editing)
- Tier 3: 2 hours (VSSL work — concentrated in 1 week per quarter, otherwise 0)

For outsourced teams (operator + 1–2 editors):
- Tier 1: 30% of editing capacity
- Tier 2: 50% of editing capacity
- Tier 3: 20% (burst load quarterly)

## Tier Misalignment (common mistakes)

### "All Tier 2, No Tier 1"
Creator publishes only deep-dives. Channel has high retention per video but doesn't reach new audiences. Growth stalls at 5K–15K subscribers. Fix: add Shorts.

### "All Tier 1, No Tier 2"
Creator publishes only Shorts. Big view counts, no authority. Subscribers don't retain. Fix: add monthly deep-dives.

### "No Tier 3"
Creator has 50K subscribers and no VSSL. Traffic exists but there's no conversion mechanism. Fix: build one VSSL in the next 60 days. Pin it. Link from every other video's end-card.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- Newsletter/blog operators using YouTube as a secondary channel — only need Tier 2 and Tier 3
- Pure paid-ad YouTube (only Tier 3 matters; Tier 1–2 irrelevant)
- B2B SaaS demo content — flatter funnel, no Tier 1 needed
