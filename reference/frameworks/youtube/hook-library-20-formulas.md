# Hook Library — 20 Proven Formulas for High-Ticket YouTube

> **Why this matters:** The first 15 seconds decide whether the algorithm ever shows your video again. For high-ticket, a weak hook doesn't just lose a view — it loses a $5K–$50K buyer who would have stayed for the mechanism.

## Hook Grammar (the invariants)

Every hook below, regardless of formula, must do four things in the first 15 seconds:
1. **State stakes** (what's at risk if you don't watch)
2. **Pattern interrupt** (break the expected open)
3. **Forward promise** (what they'll know/see/get by minute X)
4. **Credential flag** (why this person, not another)

If any one of the four is missing, the hook leaks retention by minute 2.

## The 20 Formulas

### 1. Curiosity Gap

Pattern: "The [specific noun] that [specific audience] [specific transformation] — and [counterintuitive element]."

Examples:
- "The 3-page document that closed $1.2M in 90 days — and why most agencies would refuse to send it."
- "The one question I ask every prospect that triples close rate — and why it sounds rude."

### 2. Specific Promise

Pattern: "In the next [X minutes], you'll [specific skill/outcome] — [credibility marker]."

Examples:
- "In the next 12 minutes you'll write a cold-email sequence that books 3–5 sales calls a week — I've sent this template 40,000 times."
- "By the end of this video you'll have a complete pricing page — we tested this structure against 11 variants and it won every time."

### 3. Contrarian

Pattern: "[Widely held belief] is wrong. Here's [the real thing]."

Examples:
- "Posting every day on LinkedIn is the fastest way to kill your personal brand. Here's what to do instead."
- "$100K months are ruining founders. Here's the revenue number you actually want."

### 4. Pattern Interrupt

Pattern: Open with an action, image, or statement the viewer was not expecting to see on this channel.

Examples:
- "I just fired my best client. $28K a month. Here's why." (on a channel about scaling agencies)
- "[Silent 3 seconds, holds up a ripped contract] This is what a $500K deal looks like when the founder doesn't read the fine print."

### 5. Story Open

Pattern: "[Specific date/place]. I was [specific scene]. Then [inciting event]."

Examples:
- "March 2023. I'm sitting in a Starbucks in Austin with $47 in my account. My phone buzzes — it's a Stripe notification for $24,500."
- "Last Tuesday a client emailed me at 11pm. The subject line was four words: 'I'm pulling the offer.'"

### 6. Stat Open

Pattern: "[Surprising statistic]. Here's what that means for [audience] and what to do about it."

Examples:
- "87% of high-ticket coaches will be gone in 18 months. Here's the 3 things the surviving 13% are doing."
- "The average cold DM gets a 0.4% reply rate. Mine gets 14%. The difference is one sentence."

### 7. Problem-Agitate

Pattern: "[Specific painful symptom]. [Deeper painful cause]. [It's about to get worse because X]."

Examples:
- "You're posting 5x a week and getting 80 views per post. It's not the content. It's not the algorithm. It's that you're writing for the wrong awareness level — and Meta's 2026 ad changes are about to make this 3x more expensive."
- "Your sales calls feel like interviews. The prospect asks questions, you answer, they say 'let me think about it.' You're not losing on price. You're losing on frame."

### 8. Challenge

Pattern: "I gave [myself/someone] [constraint + timeframe]. Here's what happened."

Examples:
- "I had 30 days to take a cold niche from zero to $50K in signed contracts. Here's the exact daily playbook."
- "I challenged my team to book 100 sales calls with no ad spend in 14 days. Day-by-day."

### 9. Behind-the-Scenes

Pattern: "[Insider scene the viewer would never normally see]."

Examples:
- "This is the private Slack channel where my team plans $50K launches. I'm going to walk you through this week's thread."
- "Here's the actual P&L of a $4M consulting business. Line by line."

### 10. Authority Co-Sign

Pattern: "[Credible third party] said [specific claim]. Here's why I think [stance] and what it means for [audience]."

Examples:
- "Naval said 'specific knowledge compounds.' Here's the exact 3-step system I used to turn one piece of specific knowledge into $380K in 8 months."
- "the acquisition economist posted last week that most offers are priced an order of magnitude too low. I ran the math on 47 of my clients. He's right — and here's the exact method to reprice without losing deals."

### 11. Question Hook

Pattern: "[Provocative question the viewer has asked themselves but never answered]."

Examples:
- "Why do your best leads ghost after the proposal? It's not follow-up. It's not price. It's something you said in the first 4 minutes of the call."
- "Why does the same offer sell at $2K and flop at $10K? It's not the price. It's the three sentences before the price."

### 12. Reveal/Teardown

Pattern: "[Famous example]. Here's what's actually happening under the hood."

Examples:
- "Apple's Vision Pro landing page. I'm going to read it line by line and tell you which 6 sentences are doing 80% of the selling."
- "This is the acquisition economist's $2M webinar funnel. Here's the 4 things he does that nobody copies."

### 13. Before-After-Bridge

Pattern: "[Before state — specific]. [After state — specific]. [Bridge — the mechanism]."

Examples:
- "6 months ago this client was posting 3x a day and making $8K. Today they post once a week and just had their first $180K month. Here's exactly what changed."
- "I was booking 2 sales calls a month. I changed one variable. Now I book 2 a day. The variable isn't what you think."

### 14. Failure / Loss Open

Pattern: "I lost [specific thing]. Here's what it taught me."

Examples:
- "I lost $340K on a launch last year. Here's the 3 assumptions I made that anyone scaling past $1M needs to check."
- "I fired 60% of my agency clients in one week. Revenue doubled in 90 days. Here's the filter I used."

### 15. The List Tease

Pattern: "[Number] [specific items] that [specific audience] [specific outcome] — and #[middle number] is the one almost nobody does."

Examples:
- "7 emails that generate 90% of my client revenue — and #4 is the one almost nobody sends."
- "5 frames that eliminate price objections — and #3 is uncomfortable to use, which is why it works."

### 16. Forbidden Knowledge

Pattern: "[Thing you 'shouldn't' say]. But after [experience], I think it's the most important thing I've learned."

Examples:
- "My lawyer told me not to publish this. I'm publishing it anyway because it's the single biggest reason high-ticket offers fail."
- "I'm about to say something that will make half my industry hate me. Here it is."

### 17. Big Idea / Thesis

Pattern: "I believe [claim that reframes the entire category]. Here's why — and what it means for you."

Examples:
- "I believe the agency model is dead for anyone under $5M/year. Here's the replacement model — and how to move over in 90 days."
- "Content is no longer marketing. Content is product. If you still treat it as marketing, you'll lose the next five years."

### 18. The Mistake Confession

Pattern: "I made [specific mistake] that cost [specific amount/time]. Here's exactly what I'd do differently."

Examples:
- "I spent $87K on ads in 2023 chasing a broken offer. If I could restart, here's the 5-step validation I'd run before spending a dollar."
- "I hired 4 sales reps in 60 days. All 4 quit or got fired. Here's the hiring system I use now that's kept the last 7 in seat for 18+ months."

### 19. Comparison / Versus

Pattern: "[Option A] vs. [Option B] — which one wins for [specific audience/use case]?"

Examples:
- "Webinar vs. VSL for $10K offers — I ran both for 90 days and the winner surprised me."
- "Paid ads vs. organic YouTube for coaches — which one actually scales past $100K/month?"

### 20. Ancient Story / Timeless Lesson

Pattern: "[Historical figure, story, or analog]. Here's what they knew about [modern problem] that we forgot."

Examples:
- "In 1923, Claude Hopkins wrote a book that predicted every trick TikTok creators use today. Page 47 is the one that changes everything."
- "Napoleon's generals had a rule about when to attack. It's the same rule that separates $100K and $1M offers."

## Hook Testing Protocol

- Record 3 hook options per video before scripting anything else
- Test the top 2 in Shorts first — which hook makes viewers stay past 3s?
- The winning hook becomes the long-form cold open
- Retention at 30s is the truth metric. Target: >70% of viewers still watching at 0:30

## Hook Failure Modes (what to avoid)

- Generic "Hey guys, today we're talking about…" — 40–60% drop in first 15s
- Over-promising what the video doesn't deliver — kills AVD and session time
- Hook that doesn't match the title — CTR collapses as viewers realize and bounce
- Hook longer than 20 seconds — modern YouTube audiences expect payoff in <15s
