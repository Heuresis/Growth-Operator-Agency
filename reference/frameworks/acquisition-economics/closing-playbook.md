# Closing Playbook - Objection Handling by Blame Source

> **Source:** the acquisition economist, *the closing canon - How to Turn Nos into Sales* (Bumble IP / a portfolio holding company, 2025)
> **Frame:** "The money is in the maybes."

## The Origin: a fitness-operator rollup, January 2017

the acquisition economist opens the playbook with the lowest point of his operating life. His business partner drained the accounts. He got a DUI. He was living at his girlfriend's parents' house. He launched six gyms simultaneously on $3,300/day in ads he didn't have the money for - going into debt at "$412 dollars per working hour."

Day 3: $10K in the hole. He hopped on the sales huddle: reps were 2-for-14, 3-for-11, 2-for-8, 0-for-7, 0-for-18. Diagnosis after someone finally raised their hand and asked "Can you teach us this stuff?": the reps weren't lazy or dumb. They'd never been taught to sell. the acquisition economist wrote the first version of this playbook that night and drilled objections every day. By month-end, break-even. The 13-month processing record in the PDF shows the ascent from $100K in January 2017 to $1.5M in January 2018.

The playbook is, literally, the document that closing team used.

## The Three-Bucket Model

Advertising splits any audience into three buckets:

1. **YES (Lay-downs)** - the ad already closed them. You just have to not mess it up.
2. **NO (Unqualified)** - bad fit. Usually pulled in by confusion about what you sell.
3. **MAYBE (On the fence)** - this is where the closing work happens. Most people who are interested in your thing are here. Without a closer, they go to the NO bucket by default.

"The money is in the maybes." Closing is the skill that converts bucket 3, which is always larger than bucket 1.

## The Core Thesis: Closing Gives Decision-Making Power

"People on the fence struggle over a buying decision. They have too little power to influence their own decision one way or another - so they waffle."

The goal of closing is not to get the prospect to buy. The goal is to give the prospect enough power to make a real decision - yes or no. A genuine no is acceptable. An avoidance-masquerading-as-no is the profit destroyer.

## The Onion of Blame

When a qualified prospect avoids a decision, they point their finger at one of three blame targets, in concentric layers:

1. **Circumstances** (outermost) - "I must get what I want when I want it. I'm not getting it. XYZ circumstance is to blame."
2. **Other People** (middle) - "I must be treated fairly. XYZ person is to blame."
3. **Self** (innermost) - "I must be perfect. XYZ character trait of mine is to blame."

Expect the prospect to move through multiple layers during one call. Rule: "When that happens, it means you're closing correctly. Just keep peeling back the layers until you've empowered the prospect to make a decision."

## The 28 Rules of Closing

Exact verbatim from the playbook. These govern every interaction.

1. When in doubt, repeat back what they said.
2. Acknowledge or agree, but never disagree.
3. Before getting real, get permission ("Can I be a coach instead of a friend for a second?").
4. Learn to stack closes. If one works well, a second works even better.
5. You don't need to memorize. You need to understand.
6. You only need two or three closes per obstacle.
7. Use nudges when someone is right on the edge.
8. Yellows are golds. Practice on unqualified leads.
9. Volume negates luck.
10. Don't assume objections mean they won't buy.
11. Never change your price on the spot to get a sale.
12. If you feel like you're always in a battle, look at what happens before they show up.
13. Only ask for the sale when you think you've got 'em.
14. People want to believe you. They want to buy.
15. Selling happens before you ask for the sale. Closing happens after.
16. It's easier to handle problems before you ask for money than after.
17. Expect and plan for no. It's not failure; it's expected.
18. Price shock isn't a no. Unless they tell you there's a problem, assume they're processing out loud.
19. Selling properly is the first step to becoming a coach.
20. The person who cares the most about the prospect wins the sale.
21. Seek to understand, not to argue.
22. Selling is a transference of belief over a bridge of trust.
23. Belief and trust are a continuum, not binaries.
24. Closers ask hard questions because they genuinely care. Be kind, not nice.
25. If you're going to say something offensive, don't say it about them - say it about someone like them or your past self.
26. Once the prospect says yes, shut up.
27. Record all your sales to learn from them.
28. Power is the ability to direct or influence people. If you want to be powerful, understand closing.

## The Five All-Purpose Closes

These work on any objection that's not "yes." the acquisition economist's "80/20 closes."

### 1. "What's Your Main Concern"
> "What's your main concern? What are you afraid of happening?"

If they give fluff, escalate: "So you're afraid of spending money and me stealing it and spending it in Vegas?" They'll laugh and give you a real answer.

### 2. "The Reason"
> "The reason you are telling yourself not to do this is the reason you should do it."

Applied to each category:
- Money: "The fact that you don't have money is the very reason you need to do this more than anyone."
- Time: "The fact that you don't have time is the very reason you need to do this."
- Authority: "The fact that you're so dependent on your spouse is the reason you need to take this decision and own it."

### 3. "Hypothetical"
> "If this were perfect, would you do it? Then what's the difference between perfect and what we've got?"

If they give a real detail, fix it and close. If they can't, pivot: "Right, well because it sounds like it's nothing to do with the program; it's something else. What are you afraid of happening?"

### 4. "Zoom Out"
> "You want [X]. We sell [X]. Do you think overanalyzing the details might be the reason you haven't made it happen yet?"

### 5. "1 to 10"
> "On a scale of 1 to 10, where 1 is not interested and 10 is ready to start right now, where are you?"

9 or less → "What would it take to get you to a 10?" → they tell you the real objection.

## Closes by Blame Source

### Circumstances - Time

- **"Better To Start When You're Busy"**: "The busier you are, the better time it is to start. Because life is always going to have busy seasons, and if you can't figure out how to do it during busy seasons, then this isn't going to last."
- **"You're Gonna Get Busy Again"**: "Even if you waited until it wasn't busy, you'd get busy again. So you'd stop again the moment you were busy."
- **"It's Not About Timing, It's About Priorities"**: "Either [goal] is more important than these interruptions, or it's not."
- **"The Smartphone Close"** (the acquisition economist's personal story): his partner caught him whining about being too busy, pulled out his phone, showed him 20 hours/week on social media. "At the end of the day, you, me, and the wealthiest entrepreneurs all had the same 24 hours when we started out."
- **"When/Then"**: "Saying 'I'll do it when I have more time' is like 'When I get healthy, then I'll go to the doctor.' It flips the order."

### Circumstances - Money (Not Enough Value)

- **"It's Good That It's A Lot"**: "The fact that this feels like a big investment means you're more likely to take it seriously. If this were just $1, it might be easy to overlook."
- **"Good Things Aren't Cheap - Cheap Things Aren't Good"**: "How many free PDFs have you downloaded and made no more money?"
- **"Would You Even Believe Me If It Were Less?"**: "Would you even believe it could do what I say if it were one third the price? ... Then what are we talking about?"
- **"It's Not A Lot When You Think About It"**: "Hypothetically, if this got you an extra $10,000/mo, would it be worth it? Then it's not the price, it's whether you believe you can do it."
- **"You're Gonna Spend The Money Either Way"**: "In the next 12 months, you're gonna spend this amount of money either way. The only decision is where it's going."
- **"You Pay The Price Either Way"**: "With years of your own life to figure it out, or with a payment today to use the time other people have already put in for you."
- **"Some Now or More Later"**: "Doing this costs some now. Not doing this will cost more later."

### Circumstances - Money (Can't Afford)

- **"Resourcefulness Not Resources"**: "Every self-made millionaire started with nothing. The only difference is how resourceful they were." Phil Knight/Nike example: went to his vendors and said "You need to pay my payroll for me."
- **"Had It Worse And Done Better"** - reference to someone with worse starting conditions who made it.
- **"Everyone Starts At Zero"** - all self-made millionaires had no resources at their start.

### Circumstances - Money (Competitor Does It Less)

- **"Cheap Comparison Close"**: "If we were the same price, which one would you pick? (they say you) Why? (they list reasons)... So now you understand why we aren't the same price, right?"
- **"Good Fast Cheap"** - pick two.
- **"Cheap Or What You Need"**: "Buy something cheap, or what you need. Another thing that doesn't hurt to buy because we both know it won't do anything, or invest in yourself and solve this problem for good."

### Circumstances - Money (Haggling)

- **"We Could Do It For More"**: "We could do it for $XXX (higher than quote)." Works when someone asks for a discount.
- **"It's Not, Not The Money"**: "If this were free, would you do it? Yes. OK, well... if you're gonna do it either way, lock in the savings now."

### Other People (Decision Makers)

The 4-step sequence:
1. What if they say no? → "I'd probably do it anyways" → close now.
2. What would they object to? → pivot to the actual objection.
3. If they know you're not happy, why would they stop you? → remind them their spouse wants their happiness.
4. Ask for support, not permission.

Best closes:
- **"Support Not Permission"**: "It's your life, not a copresidency. If you give that power to them and then two years from now you look back and you haven't made progress, who are you going to blame? Them. That's not fair."
- **"Three-Day No-Sweat Guarantee"** (downsell form): "Sign up. I'll delay the payment. If your partner says no within 72 hours, call me, I'll tear up the contract. This way you lock in your spot but still get time to talk it over."
- **Humorous hypothetical** - a joke about the partner wanting them to stay broke/unhealthy. Breaks the tension.

### Other People (Been Burned Before)

- **"Don't Let It Burn You Twice"**: "It burned you once when you made the poor investment. But it'll burn you twice if that bad decision prevents you from making a good one. Like having a bad eighth-grade boyfriend and saying 'men aren't for me.'"

### Self (Own Preferences - "not right for me")

- **"Isolate And Solve"**: "What specifically would make it for you vs. not for you?"
- **"We Vote With Our Dollars About The Things That We Care About"**: the partner's beauty-retailer story about 13-year-old girls being told "women budget for this stuff every month" - the identity-shift reframe.
- **"Pain Of Change Is Less Than The Pain Of Staying The Same"**: "We change when the pain of staying the same is greater than the pain of change."
- **"You Gotta Change To Change"**: "What you have been doing has been getting you what you've been getting, and you gotta change to change."
- **"Change The Variables - Change The Outcome"**: "We know these variables, in this order, create this outcome. If you change the variables, you change the outcome."
- **"The Mechanic Close"**: For ultra-specific questions you can't answer upfront - "It'd be like asking a mechanic what's wrong with your car before bringing it in. The only way we'd know is after we look."

### Self (Rushed Decisions / "Need to think about it")

- **"What Would Make It A No?"**: "If you don't mind my asking - what would make this a no?" Easier to poke holes in weak no-reasons than to pull a yes.
- **"Don't Repeat The Same Mistake That Got You Here"**: "Do you think maybe you're in this situation because you've delayed making the decision?"
- **"How Much Has Not Making This Decision Cost You?"**: "The cost of inaction. We never regret what we did; we regret what we didn't do."
- **"Another Year of Almost"**: "Another year of almost hitting your goals, of almost getting to 10K a month, of almost retiring your partner... If you're tired of another year of almost..."
- **"It Doesn't Take Time To Make Decisions, It Takes Information"**: "You're not going to sit in a rocking chair and think about this. You'll get in your car, go to Timmy's soccer practice, make dinner, and by day 5 you won't have made the decision. It doesn't take time. It takes information. I'm here to help."
- **"The Gameplan" Close**: Three questions:
  1. Do you think this will help you get closer to your goals? (They say yes.)
  2. Do you have access to the money? (They say yes. If no, resourcefulness close.)
  3. Do you think we can help you do this better than on your own? (They say yes.) → close.
- **"This Isn't A Fast Decision"**: "You made this decision years ago. You saw the ad. You clicked. You opted in. You set time aside. You showed up. You've been talking to me for X minutes. You're six inches from gold - don't stop digging now."

### Self (Guarantee closes)

- **"Both Options Are Risk Free"**: "The bad news is you can't make an informed decision until you've tried it. The good news: you can try it risk-free."
- **"Decadere"**: "The word decide comes from Latin de-cadere - to kill off. Doing this is a decision. Not doing this is a decision. Which future are you cutting off?"

## The "When They Take The Blame" Philosophy

When the prospect blames themselves - their fear, their preference, their past - the close is not "convince them they're wrong." The close is: "Show them that avoiding the decision is the biggest mistake of all. And more, that it does the opposite of what they want."

## Ethical Assumption (Author Note)

"The product will get them to their goal the way they want to go. You, and others will support them. It will work for them, not just everyone else. The difference between help and manipulation is intention."

If you cannot meet these three conditions, don't apply the closes.
