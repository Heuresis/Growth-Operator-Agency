# Hooks Playbook - Distilled

> **Source:** the acquisition economist, *the hooks canon - How to Double Your Marketing in Under 5 Seconds* (a portfolio holding company, 2025)
> **Opening quote (the Madison-Avenue copy archetype, quoted by the acquisition economist):** "After you've written your headline, you've spent eighty cents of your advertising dollar."

## The Definition

A hook is whatever gets attention first. The first few seconds of a video. The first line of an email. The first image the eye lands on. It is the single piece of the ad that decides whether anything else in the ad gets seen.

the acquisition economist's weight on this: "If you have ten hours to advertise, focus your first eight hours on the hooks."

## The Two-Part Structure

Every hook has two components:

1. **Call-out** - gets the prospect to say "this is for me." Uses the "cocktail party effect": in a noisy room, your name cuts through. The call-out is the equivalent of the prospect hearing their own name.
2. **Condition for value** - implicit or explicit promise of payoff if they keep consuming. "If you [target] [do this], you will get [value]."

Bad hooks miss one or both. Good hooks do both in one sentence.

## The a direct-response pitchman / a late-night interview archetype Lesson

the acquisition economist opens the playbook with this story. an educational-direct-response operator spent heavily recreating a a late-night interview archetype-style set to run infomercials - and it worked, breaking records. Years later, he got a late-night interview archetype himself to actually do the interview. The ad bombed. Diagnosis: the educational-direct-response operator's ego-serving opener ("Tonight on a late-night interview archetype, I'm here with my guest a direct-response pitchman, he's a multi-New-York-Times bestseller...") centered an educational-direct-response operator. They don't care.

The re-recorded hook centered the viewer: "Have you ever in your adult life looked at yourself in the mirror and thought 'I should be further ahead by now'? If you have, you're not alone." Only the first sentences changed. The infomercial went on to sell millions of books.

Operator takeaway: the hook is about the reader, not the seller. Always.

## Verbal Hook Formulas (with the acquisition economist's own winners)

The playbook catalogs 121 top-performing the acquisition economist hooks. Patterns:

### Labels (name the avatar)
- "Local business owners, I have a gift for you."
- "Business owners: Do you ever wonder if you're working on the wrong stuff?"

### Questions

**Yes-questions** (prime a yes):
- "Would you pay $1,000 to have the business of your dreams in 30 days?"

**Open questions** (force a choice):
- "Which would you rather be? The guy pushing the boulder up the hill? Or the one with the boulder at the top who can just flick it and let it roll?"

### Conditionals (if/then)
- "If you're working all the time and your business isn't growing, you're working on the wrong sh*t."
- "Read this if you're tired of being broke."

### Commands (tell them what to do)
- "Read this if you want to win."
- "Throw out your morning routine and switch to a money routine."

### Statements (assert a claim that interests the reader)
- "The smartest thing you can do today..."
- "How to get ahead of 99% of people."
- "The best offer I've ever made."

### Lists or Steps (specific count = concreteness)
- "In this video I'm going to talk to you about the 28 ways to stay poor."
- "Thirteen lessons I learned after graduating college..."
- "I started 4 businesses that cracked ten million in a row."

### Narratives (open a loop)
- "One day I was in the back and this old lady comes in and she was piss angry."
- "I'm at her parents' house in an extra bedroom. I'm the guy she met from the internet that she quit her job for, who's just lost everything. I had $1000 left."
- "The sixth time I was arrested, they took me back to my parents' house."

### Exclamations / Provocative
- "Ahhhhh... This is the blueprint to becoming a millionaire."
- "On November 30th, 2022, the world changed forever."
- "Entrepreneurship f*cking sucks most of the time."

## Nonverbal Hooks

Hooks don't have to be words. A visual, a sound, a physical action can do the same work. the acquisition economist's recurring example: catching a banana at the start of an ad. "The amount of value I'm going to give you in the next 30 seconds is bananas..." The physical interruption pattern buys the first 2 seconds; the verbal hook takes it the rest of the way.

Operator move: when the platform supports it (video, image), pair a verbal hook with a visual interruption. They compound.

## The 70-20-10 Innovation Rule

the acquisition economist attributes this to Google (Page/Brin). Allocation of hook-creation effort:

- **70% Core Business** - use hooks already proven in your own content or others'. "More of what works." Stabilizes baseline performance. Needed because employees want to be paid every month.
- **20% Emerging Business** - "Winner Adjacent." Model concepts that worked in another niche. Permutations of your top 5 hooks.
- **10% Big New Ideas** - totally new things. Risky. High potential upside. If it works, it graduates into the 70%. If it fails, document so you don't repeat.

This is the ratio that governs every batch of creative the a portfolio holding company team makes.

## Testing Protocol

From the playbook:

- **Ads:** Record 10 hooks for every 1 piece of ad content. 10:1 ratio.
- **Short content:** Every winning tweet on another platform becomes a short; expand the hook into the short.
- **Long content:** Record 3-4 hooks; let post-production team pick the winner.
- **Email:** Active split-tests on every campaign. Document winners in a separate file.

## The Hook Spreadsheet

the acquisition economist's standard operating doc - a spreadsheet with tabs per platform. Columns: name, hook, views, link. Before every recording session, review the best hooks of all time. Winning X posts become short-video hooks. Winning ad hooks become content hooks. The spreadsheet is the compounding asset.

## The Author Note: Let The Data Do The Teaching

"If you're starting out, it's even easier. You just make a bunch with no idea what's gonna happen. Then you see what happened. Then you follow 70-20-10 going forward. My promise is, no matter how bad your content is, some of it will be in the top 10% of that content. Look at the hooks for that content. Make more like that. Look at the bottom 10%. Make less like that. Repeat until the end of time."

This is the explicit instruction for operators with no existing data: ship volume, extract the top 10%, compound.

## Variety Generator (chiropractor example from the Checklist)

The playbook provides a variety template. Same offer (chiropractor getting more patients), seven hook forms:

- **Condition:** "If you're a chiropractor, this video will get you more leads."
- **Label:** "Chiropractors I have a gift for you."
- **Question:** "Would you rather struggle to get patients for the rest of your life or fix it in 6 weeks?"
- **Command:** "Watch this if you want to get more patients."
- **Statement:** "The top 1% of chiropractor clinics follow these rules..."
- **List:** "11 ways Chiropractors get more patients without paid ads."
- **Story:** "All of a sudden, my phone starts ringing off the hook..."

Operator move: take your best-performing offer and run it through all seven formats. Ship all seven.

## Checklist (from the Playbook)

**First time through:**
- Look at top-performing ads/content of other people.
- Isolate the hooks.
- Write down your favorite 50.
- Use all of them in ads/content.

**Every time after:**
- Look at top 10% performers (top 5).
- Make 70% of next batch using those top 5 hooks.
- Make 20% permutations (visuals + words).
- Make 10% completely new hooks.

**Good hooks contain:**
- A call-out.
- An implication of value if they consume.

## Anti-patterns

- **Seller-centered opener** (the an educational-direct-response operator/a late-night interview archetype mistake).
- **One hook per piece of content.** The ratio is 10:1, not 1:1.
- **Skipping the spreadsheet.** Without the compounding archive, every session restarts at zero.
- **Refusing to use proven hooks.** Operators who insist on "originality" burn the 70% that should be stabilizing their feed.
- **Hook without condition for value.** "I have a confession..." without the implied payoff is just noise.
