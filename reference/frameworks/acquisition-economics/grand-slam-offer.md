# Grand Slam Offer - the acquisition economist Implementation Guide Distillation

> **Source:** the acquisition economist, *$100M Offers: Complete Implementation Guide* (21-day, 15-step, 6-phase build)
> **Companion:** `reference/frameworks/offer-architecture/hormozi-grand-slam-offer.md` (the offer architect vs. the acquisition economist comparison)

This file captures what the Implementation Guide actually prescribes, phase by phase. Use it as a build checklist, not a concept explainer.

## Definition

A Grand Slam Offer is an offer that cannot be comparison-shopped. It combines an attractive promise, a Value Equation that produces a 150+ score, stacked bonuses that each kill one objection, a guarantee that reverses risk, scarcity and urgency that are both true, and a name that signals who it's for and what they get.

## The 6-Phase 15-Step Build

### Phase 1 - Foundation (Days 1-3)

**Step 1: Market Assessment.** Score your market 1-10 on four criteria; total must be 28+ or pivot.
- Massive Pain
- Purchasing Power
- Easy to Target
- Growing Market (≥10% annually)

**Step 2: Avatar Niching.** Write the avatar as: "[Specific descriptor] [demographic] who [specific situation/goal]." Commit to one niche for 6+ months. Examples from the Guide:
- "Busy executives over 40 who want to lose 20+ pounds without giving up business dinners"
- "Local service businesses doing $500K-$2M who struggle to hire quality employees"

### Phase 2 - Value Creation (Days 4-8)

**Step 3: Dream Outcome.** Interview 5-10 target-market people. Ask: "What would be the perfect outcome if this problem was completely solved?" Synthesize into: "Help [avatar] achieve [specific outcome] so they can [deeper benefit/status gain]."

**Step 4: Problems List.** Map the journey step by step. For each step, list problems in four columns:
- Time Problems (takes too long, inconvenient)
- Effort Problems (too hard, confusing, skill gap)
- Achievement Problems (won't work for their situation, external factors)
- Financial Problems (too expensive, not worth it)

Target: 20-40 specific problems identified.

**Step 5: Solutions Development.** For each problem, write: "How to [solve the problem] so that [benefit]." Then assign a Delivery Method: One-on-One, Small Group, or One-to-Many.

### Phase 3 - Offer Construction (Days 9-12)

**Step 6: Solution Delivery Design.** Score each solution on the Delivery Cube:
- Attention Level: 1-on-1 / Small Group / 1-to-Many
- Effort Level: Done-For-You / Done-With-You / Do-It-Yourself
- Delivery Medium: In-person / Phone / Video / Audio / Written

Then Cost-Value filter:
- High Value, Low Cost → Include (Priority 1)
- High Value, High Cost → Include if profitable (Priority 2)
- Low Value, Low Cost → Consider (Priority 3)
- Low Value, High Cost → Exclude

**Step 7: Trim and Stack.** Bundle solutions into 3-7 named components. Each component needs:
- Core Deliverable
- Implementation Support
- Accountability Element
- Success Acceleration

Assign monetary value to each bundle based on (a) what they'd pay someone else, (b) time saved × their hourly rate, (c) cost of failure without it, (d) value of the outcome achieved.

### Phase 4 - Offer Enhancement (Days 13-16)

**Step 8: Guarantee Creation.** Five types, ascending strength:

| Type | Form |
|---|---|
| Unconditional | Money-back, no questions |
| Conditional | Results-based with specific requirements |
| Service | Keep working until they get results |
| Anti-Guarantee | All sales final (with strong reason why) |
| Performance | Only pay when they get results |

Written format: "If you don't [specific result] in [timeframe], we will [specific remedy]."

**Step 9: Scarcity + Urgency.** Scarcity = limited supply. Urgency = limited time. Both must be true.

Scarcity options from the Guide:
- Total Business Cap (only X clients total)
- Growth Rate Cap (only X per month)
- Cohort Cap (X per class)
- Limited Bonuses (special items for first X)

Urgency options:
- Rolling Cohorts (must join by X to start with next group)
- Seasonal Deadlines (promotion ends on a date)
- Pricing Deadlines (price increases after X)
- Bonus Deadlines (bonuses expire after X)

**Step 10: Bonus Creation.** Create 3-7 bonuses using this structure:
- Compelling Name: Benefit + method + timeframe
- Problem Solved: Which specific objection this removes
- Value Justification: Why it's worth $X
- Social Proof: How it has helped others

Bonus types (ranked by cost-to-deliver):
- Checklists/Templates - cheap, high value
- Tools/Calculators - one-time create, unlimited use
- Training Recordings - repurpose existing content
- Partner Discounts - negotiate group rates
- VIP Access - higher service level for a limited time

Rule from the companion file: total bonus value should be 3-5× the offer price.

**Step 11: Offer Naming - M-A-G-I-C formula.**
- **M**agnet: Reason why (Free, 50% Off, New, etc.)
- **A**vatar: Who it's for (busy moms, local dentists, etc.)
- **G**oal: What they'll achieve (debt-free, 6-pack, etc.)
- **I**nterval: Timeframe (21-day, 6-week, etc.)
- **C**ontainer: What it is (challenge, blueprint, system, etc.)

Guide examples:
- "Free 6-Week Busy Mom Debt Freedom Challenge"
- "New Patient $10K Local Dentist Revenue Blueprint"
- "21-Day Executive Six-Pack System"

### Phase 5 - Pricing & Assembly (Days 17-18)

**Step 12: Price Setting.** Guide pricing rules:
- Charge 5-10× cost to deliver
- Price should be 10-20% of total value stack
- Round to psychologically appealing numbers ($1,997 vs $2,000)
- Can you say it out loud without cracking a smile? If not, adjust.

**Step 13: Final Offer Assembly.** Guide template:

1. Problem/Pain Agitation (2-3 sentences)
2. Dream Outcome Promise (1 sentence)
3. Value Stack Presentation (Core + each Bonus with individual $ values)
4. Total Value: $XX,XXX
5. Scarcity/Urgency Statement
6. Price Presentation ("Normally $XX,XXX, today just $X,XXX")
7. Guarantee
8. Call to Action

### Phase 6 - Validation & Launch (Days 19-21)

**Step 14: Offer Validation.** Present to 5-10 target prospects. Success metrics from the Guide:
- 70%+ say they would buy at stated price
- Fewer than 2 major objections per person
- Clear excitement about specific components
- Willingness to buy immediately if available

**Step 15: Launch Preparation.** Required assets:
- Sales Script/Presentation
- FAQ Document
- Order Form/Sales Page
- Fulfillment Plan
- Client Onboarding sequence

Recommended: Lead Magnet, Email Sequences, Social Proof stack.

## Ongoing Optimization (from the Guide)

**Monthly review:** offers presented vs. sales made, most common objections, which components prospects value most, opportunities to add value without increasing cost.

**Quarterly evolution:** test new bonus additions, adjust pricing based on market response, create seasonal variations, develop higher-tier versions.

## Troubleshooting (Guide's own table)

| Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|
| Prospects aren't excited | Revisit avatar (too broad), increase outcome specificity, add bonuses, strengthen guarantee |
| Price objections common | Increase perceived value via presentation, add bonuses, offer payment plans, strengthen guarantee |
| Not enough leads | Improve naming, add urgency/scarcity, create a compelling lead magnet, test different channels |
| Customers not getting results | Simplify delivery, add implementation support, create better accountability, provide more quick wins |

## The Guide's Closing Reminder

"Your first offer won't be perfect. The goal is to get it 'good enough' to start making sales, then improve based on real market feedback. Every 'no' teaches you how to make the offer better."

## Anti-patterns

- **Running Phase 1 in under a day.** Market scoring cannot be faked. Operators who pick a niche that scores <28 will struggle regardless of offer quality.
- **Skipping the problems-list column work.** Operators who map problems as one blob instead of the four-column split always under-index on Time and Effort problems - the denominator.
- **More than 7 core components.** Dilution. The Guide caps the bundle at 7.
- **Bonuses that don't target a named objection.** Bonus stacking only works when each bonus has a specific "problem solved" slot.
- **Self-graded validation.** The Phase 6 metric is 70%+ of 5-10 real prospects. Not 70% of yourself.
