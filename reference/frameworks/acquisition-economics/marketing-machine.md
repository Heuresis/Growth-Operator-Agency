# Marketing Machine - Turning Customers into Ad Creative

> **Source:** the acquisition economist, *the marketing-machine canon - How to Turn Your Business Into an Ads Machine* (a portfolio holding company, 2025)
> **Frame:** "How to get endless ads by doing what you're already doing."

## Origin - The 2019 PE Rejection

the acquisition economist tried to sell a fitness-operator rollup in 2019. Bankers rejected: "The business depends on your face. If anyone would buy it, you'd have to stay in the business. If selling means becoming an employee of the very business I used to own, what's the point?" Unsellable.

Walking to clear his head, a friend texted: "What would you do to make another $5M per year without doing more work?" He reframed it: "If I got hit by a bus today - what would keep the business growing tomorrow?"

The breakthrough came at an awards dinner. Gym owners were telling stories about their journey - their sales call, their first breakthrough, their milestone. the acquisition economist realized: "The bottom of the list feeds the top of the list." Cut up calls into customer journeys. Record testimonials at events. Prize-incentivize ad-quality videos from clients.

A few weeks later: "The ads from the new campaign are CRUSHING... The member-generated ones are outperforming even your best direct-to-camera ones."

12 months later, he re-met the bankers. Pulled up the ad account. Sorted by sales generated. He hadn't been in any of the top-performing ads for 12 months. The business closed for $46.2M.

The key data point from the PDF: reviewing thousands of a fitness-operator rollup ads retroactively, **40 of the top 50 performers had no the acquisition economist in them.** 80% of top ads were customer-generated.

## The Machine - 9 Components

The Marketing Machine is a checklist of customer-as-ad-source channels. Every checked item is "a reliable stream of videos, images, and words you can use to advertise your business."

1. **Lifecycle ads** - cut-ups from recorded sales, support, onboarding, upsell calls.
2. **Social Media Scrape** - customer-tagged content across all platforms.
3. **In-Person Events** - stage shots, pro testimonials, man-in-the-street.
4. **Communication + Communities Scrape** - support chats, community posts, reviews.
5. **Bonuses + Award Ads** - content unlocked via testimonial submission.
6. **Competitions** - ad-performance contests among customers.
7. **Delivery / "Ribbon Cutting"** - physical delivery reaction shots.
8. **Chat / Messaging Scrape** - support-rep screenshots.
9. **Reviews Scrape** - Google / Yelp / Glassdoor / Trustpilot screenshots.

The PDF provides the exact checklist for each. Summary below.

## Component 1 - Lifecycle Ads

Record every call. Pull:
- **Before** moment from sales calls.
- **During** moment from 1-2 support calls.
- **After** moment from an upsell, milestone, or testimonial call.

Shows rather than tells the journey. Implementation checklist:
- Legal: compliance + permission language in all customer contracts.
- IT: record all calls (sales, onboarding, support, ascension, milestone). Buy storage. Turn off auto-delete.
- All departments: calendar events include client name.
- Sales: flag happy-customer calls to marketing weekly.
- Marketing: cross-reference calendar events to match names + dates. Clip before/during/after. Named folder system. Run as ads.

Local-business variant - **"Upon Delivery" Ads**. Ribbon-cutting moments: kitchen reveal, scale after X days, veneers, "you got the job!" reactions. The raw reaction becomes the hook (first 3-5 seconds). Highlight reels of only reactions crush.

## Component 2 - Social Media Scrape

- Assign a hashtag for customers. a fitness-operator rollup used `#brandedhashtag`. a supplement brand used `#`. Personal: `#personalbrandedhashtags`.
- Set regular reminders to capture tagged media before stories disappear (24h on IG/FB/Snap).
- Separate studs from duds - good organic performance correlates with good ad performance.
- Use best-performing organic images/videos as ad creative with already-performing ad copy.

## Component 3 - In-Person Events

Three types of ad capture at events:

**Stage ads.** Picture while on stage with people celebrating behind you. Selfies and video. Do this at end of day 1 or before lunch on day 2.

**Professional testimonials.** Reserve a separate room for recording during the event. Hire a dedicated camera crew (better than the one filming the main event - testimonials matter more). Pre-pick customers you want testimonials from. The **6-point script**:

1. *Internal struggle* - What was rock bottom? What did you hate having to do?
2. *External struggle* - What were your objective metrics?
3. *Skepticism* - What were your main concerns about buying?
4. *Overcome* - What made you do it anyway?
5. *External victory* - What are your objective metrics now?
6. *Internal victory* - What's been the best moment since? What can you do now you couldn't before?

Rules:
- The hook is the most important part. Have them record 2-3 hook variants.
- They say their name at the beginning (helps matching later).
- Give incentive for testimonials. Give the handler $50 per additional off-list capture.
- Print a hit list. Use a clipboard. Check off as you go.

**Man in the street.** Break-time soundbites. Prepare 10 core questions. Get 10+ answers to each. Turn into fast-hitting highlight reels of everyone saying a specific outcome.

Sample questions:
- How many new customers have you gained since you started?
- What were you making before vs. now?
- How much has your credit score improved?
- What was your old credit score and what is it now?

## Component 4 - Communication + Communities Scrape

**Support / Account Rep Chats.** Screenshot the juicy messages.

- List of key terms indicating a win: "PIF, Sales, $, Profit, Revenue, Monthly, close, closed, leads, show, scheduled, win."
- Folder accessible to all reps.
- Reminder to search all key terms in messages.
- Sort by date, screenshot new.
- Manager rewards reps for submissions. Optional: $100 gift card if their screenshot becomes a winning ad.

**Community Wins/Posts.**
- "Wins" post category in the community.
- Every Friday: encourage customers to post wins of the week.
- Monday: screenshot weekend wins.
- Bonus: screen-record scrolling through the comments/wins for the week. Yet another ad.

**Reviews/Comments.** Same process:
- List review sites (Google, Yelp, Glassdoor, Facebook, Trustpilot, Amazon).
- Calendar reminder, sort by newest, screenshot the good ones.
- Reward the person who does it.

## Component 5 - Bonuses + Award Ads

Two sub-mechanisms.

**Bonus Unlocks.** Create a giveaway you charge separately for (a training, one-time access, a status tier - sales scripts, ad training, whatever is "big and sexy"). Then: customers can pay - OR - make a testimonial video to unlock for free.

Instructions:
- 5-minute video hitting 6 points, emailed to `proof@yourco.com`.
- Posted in community with stats (gets you a *second* testimonial in written form).
- Community manager tagged.
- Keywords included for later scraping.

**Award Testimonials.** Create an aspirational goal (a fitness-operator community = $1M/year gym; others could be bikini photo, remodeled kitchen photoshoot). Make a trophy. Spend money on it.

For each award, get **four proofs**:
1. Post in community showing sales hit + testimonial template.
2. Email video to `proof@yourco.com`.
3. Unboxing video of the trophy.
4. Stage presentation footage (personalized/engraved trophy mailed; generic trophy presented on stage).

a fitness-operator rollup award: sword and shield for a fitness-operator community community. Swords already had prestige association in fitness.

## Component 6 - Competitions

Run 1-2× per year max (people tire of them).

Mechanism: customers submit raw iPhone-style testimonials. Winner determined by ad performance - the one with the most conversions over 30 days wins.

Requirements:
- Announcement date + submission deadline.
- Big award (example: 6 months of free service/product).
- Instructions: results template + 6-point script + last winner's video.
- Personal invitations + follow-up with top ~50 target customers.
- Submissions cut off; format as ads; run for 30 days; announce winner publicly.

Tip: ask them to record it in the moment ("Right now. Takes 5 min. Take out your phone"). Raw is better.

## Component 7 - Delivery (physical-business variant)

Covered under Lifecycle ("Upon Delivery" ads). See Component 1.

## Implementation Order (from the PDF)

the acquisition economist's recommended start-order for new operators:
1. Competition (biggest single influx).
2. Chat/messaging scrape.
3. Community scrape.
4. Reviews scrape.
5. Social media scrape.
6. Bonus unlocks.
7. Award unlocks.
8. Events.
9. Lifecycle (most edit-intensive - save for last).

First four are "stuff you already have - takes minimal editing." Bonuses and awards need something to give away. Events are expensive but pay for themselves in proof. Lifecycle is high-quality but highest editing cost.

## Weekly / Every-6-Months Rhythm

**Every week:**
- List of customers with great results.
- Scraped social (you, tags, customers - images + video).
- Chat/messaging screenshots.
- Community posts/comments testimonials (screenshots, videos, scroll-recordings).
- New 5-star reviews (screenshots + scroll recordings).
- Bonus unlock submissions (post screenshots + video inbox).
- Award deliveries (screenshots + raw emailed testimonials).
- Customer reaction videos (unboxing, first-time, long-time, demonstrations).

**Every 6 months:**
- Big influx from events + competitions. Run one competition and two events per year = a fresh pipeline every ~12 weeks.

## Content Ethics

"All our clients consent to using recordings of them for compliance, training, and advertising purposes. Even though they sign a document, we still ask if they feel OK with using their story. If they say no, we honor their wishes."

## Why This Works

Three structural reasons from the PDF:

1. A company that relies on the founder's face is always less valuable than one that doesn't.
2. Not having to dance on camera full-time makes the business feel like a business.
3. Customer-generated ads outperform founder-generated ads because the prospect is the star of the story - "they are the star of the story. So put them in the spotlight, not you."

## Anti-patterns

- **One-time sweep.** This is a weekly system, not a project. Compounding lives in the cadence.
- **Storing without using.** "Adding stuff to folders isn't gonna get you more customers. You need to use all that stuff in your advertisements."
- **Cheap trophies.** The award has to be crave-worthy. "Don't do a boring thing."
- **Forcing all 9 from day 1.** The PDF is explicit: "Don't get overwhelmed. One or two of these processes can completely change your life."
- **Skipping the 6-point script.** Unstructured testimonials don't convert; the 6 beats are load-bearing.
