# Core Four - The Only Four Lead-Gen Mechanisms

> **Source:** the acquisition economist, *$100M Leads* (book) + Marketing Machine PDF framing
> **Canonical line:** "There are only four ways to get attention. Stop inventing a fifth."

## The Matrix

Attention is classified on two axes: audience temperature (warm vs. cold) × delivery mode (1-to-1 vs. 1-to-many).

|  | Warm audience | Cold audience |
|---|---|---|
| **1-to-1** | **Warm Outreach** | **Cold Outreach** |
| **1-to-many** | **Posting Content** | **Paid Ads** |

Every legitimate lead-gen tactic collapses into one of these cells. "LinkedIn DMs" = Cold Outreach. "Newsletter" = Posting Content (warm if list is engaged, cold if it isn't). "YouTube shorts" = Posting Content. "Facebook ads" = Paid Ads. "Texting your old clients" = Warm Outreach. If an operator claims to have found a fifth channel, they've almost always relabeled one of the four.

## The Progression Logic

the acquisition economist's ordering rule for founders with no existing audience and no ad budget:

**1. Warm Outreach first.** Fastest time-to-first-dollar. Zero cost. Text, call, or DM everyone you already know who might need your thing - or who knows someone who might. The Marketing Machine PDF's postscript says it explicitly: "If you have a small amount of customers, refer to the Warm Outreach chapter in *$100M Leads* - work for free. Get those testimonials."

**2. Posting Content second.** Compounds trust while warm outreach gets you fed. Content is how strangers become warm. You can't start here because there's no signal for what to post until you've sold something.

**3. Cold Outreach third.** Scales with effort but buys no goodwill. Layer it in after content warms a portion of your ICP - that way your cold outreach lands in inboxes where the receiver might have already seen your name.

**4. Paid Ads fourth.** Only when unit economics are proven. The Closing Playbook is blunt: "Yes, advertising scales businesses. But you can only run more ads to more people if you make more money to pay for it. That money comes from closing." If close rates are broken, paid ads multiply the bleed.

## When To Use Each (decision rules)

### Warm Outreach

Best when: starting from zero; already have a network; offer is unvalidated; no testimonials yet.

Signal it's working: friends or ex-clients reply within 24 hours and 1-2 actually transact.

Move on from it when: warm network is exhausted (~200-500 people depending on the operator).

### Posting Content

Best when: you have 1+ case study worth telling; niche is searchable; you can commit to 60+ days of output before measuring.

Signal it's working: inbound DMs from people who quote specific pieces you made.

Kill it when: 90 days of consistent posting with no inbound from target ICP. Either the content targets the wrong avatar or the hook is weak (go to Hooks Playbook).

### Cold Outreach

Best when: ICP is findable in public datasets (LinkedIn, Apollo, industry directories); you have capacity for 50-200 touches/day; your warm outreach has been exhausted.

Signal it's working: positive reply rate ≥1-2% on a tight list; qualified-meeting rate per 1,000 touches is economically viable.

Kill it when: deliverability is broken (go fix domain/inbox infrastructure first), or ICP isn't reachable via cold (retreat to Content).

### Paid Ads

Best when: a winning organic hook exists (Hooks Playbook 70-20-10); close rate is known and stable; you can survive a 30-60 day learn-and-burn period.

Signal it's working: CAC < LTV with a 3+ multiple at minimum; CAC payback inside 60-90 days if you have working capital.

Kill it when: you've spent 2× your monthly closed-won revenue and CAC > LTV. Don't "scale through it."

## The Lead Getter Layer (exponential)

The Core Four scale linearly with operator effort. Above them are the **Lead Getters** - ways to get *other people* to advertise for you. These scale past founder capacity.

1. **Customers** - referrals, reviews, user-generated content (this is what the Marketing Machine PDF operationalizes).
2. **Employees** - sales team, CSMs who bring in warm intros.
3. **Agencies** - done-for-you acquisition services.
4. **Affiliates** - revenue-share partners.

the acquisition economist's line: "Personal effort yields linear leads. Lead Getters yield exponential leads." The order matters: Customers first (cheapest, highest-trust), then Employees (you control quality), then Agencies (fastest capacity), then Affiliates (highest scale but lowest control).

## Anti-patterns

- **Starting with paid ads because it "scales."** Unit economics prove out on Warm Outreach first. Ads at negative unit economics just accelerate the loss.
- **"I post content and do ads."** Two channels without Warm Outreach skips the feedback loop that makes both channels better.
- **Spray-and-pray cold outreach without ICP qualification.** 100% of cold sequences that target an unsegmented list underperform 10% of sequences that target a tight segment.
- **Counting a lead magnet as a fifth channel.** Lead magnets are offers, not channels - they run *inside* one of the four.
- **Counting SEO as a fifth channel.** SEO is Posting Content with a search-engine delivery layer. Same cell.

## Invariants

1. Four channels. Period.
2. Warm → Content → Cold → Ads in that order.
3. Don't scale ads if close rate is broken.
4. Lead Getters are where exponential growth lives - but don't skip to them.
