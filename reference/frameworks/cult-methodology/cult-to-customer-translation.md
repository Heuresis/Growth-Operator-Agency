# Cult-to-Customer Translation

> **Why this matters:** Cult gravity is the source; funnel revenue is the downstream. Most operators build one or the other and lose the compounding. The translation is mechanical — each of the 4 cult markers has a specific funnel artifact that captures it. This file is the conversion map.

## Translation 1 — Sacred Vocabulary → Copy Phrases

The sacred phrases are not decorative; they are **load-bearing copy**. Every high-impact surface of the funnel carries one.

### Where the phrases land

| Surface | Role of the phrase |
|---------|---------------------|
| Ad hook | Pattern-interrupt for warm audience; signal of "this is one of us" |
| VSL opening (first 15 seconds) | Frame-setting — earns the next 30 minutes |
| VSL mechanism reveal | The phrase IS the mechanism (see `primitives/unique-mechanism.md`) |
| Sales page section headers | Reinforce vocabulary; aid skim-reading |
| Checkout button | "Join the [tribe name]" / "Start my [vocabulary action]" |
| Welcome email subject line | Re-invokes vocabulary at peak trust moment |
| Onboarding module names | Vocabulary becomes curriculum structure |
| Community channel names | Daily exposure to vocabulary inside membership |

### Rule of 5

Pick the 5 phrases that must appear. They cannot be negotiable.
Examples:

- **Brunson** funnel copy uses "funnel hack," "hook-story-offer," "perfect webinar," "two-comma club," "Funnel Hacker" — the same 5 terms appear in every surface.
- **the acquisition economist** offer copy uses "grand slam offer," "value equation," "dream outcome," "perceived likelihood," "time delay / effort."

### Audit test

Pull the live funnel. Count unique appearances of the 5 chosen phrases. Baseline: each phrase appears 3+ times per funnel surface. Strong: 5+ per surface. The phrase density IS the tribal-recognition signal.

### Anti-pattern

Rewriting every sales piece with fresh copy. Sacred vocabulary compounds through repetition. Fresh copy per launch resets the compounding clock. Write the phrases once, reuse, refine.

## Translation 2 — Binary In-vs-Out → Positioning + Vehicle Switch

The in/out dynamic translates to two specific copy structures: the **"who this is for / who this is not for"** block, and the **Vehicle Switch** (7-phase offer methodology).

### The "for / not-for" block

Standard placement: sales page (above fold), VSL close, webinar registration confirmation.

Standard structure:

```
This is for:
- [ICP descriptor 1]
- [ICP descriptor 2]
- [ICP descriptor 3]

This is NOT for:
- [Out-group descriptor 1]
- [Out-group descriptor 2]
- [Out-group descriptor 3]
```

The "NOT for" list is non-negotiable. It's the tribal filter made copy. It also functions as a Jim Camp "permission to say no" — which raises close rate among the fit ICPs because it lowers perceived pressure.

### The Vehicle Switch

the offer architect's mechanism for naming the out-group category of approach and pivoting the prospect onto the creator's vehicle.

Three-beat structure, dropped inline in the VSL/webinar:

1. **Old vehicle** (what the out-group uses) — named specifically, its failure mode explained
2. **Why it fails structurally** — not "they're bad people," "the approach has this structural limit"
3. **New vehicle** (the creator's methodology) — named with sacred vocabulary; pivot explained

Example:

> *Old vehicle:* Most coaches run an ad-to-VSL-to-book-a-call funnel with a generic "book a strategy call" CTA. It converts at 0.5-1% and burns good creators who can't sustain the traffic spend.
>
> *Why it fails:* The CTA doesn't filter. You get 80% tire-kickers on calls, you burn your Opening rate, you start hating your business.
>
> *New vehicle:* A Signal-First Funnel — where the ad IS the qualification, the VSL IS the application form, and the call IS the close. 3-5% to cash. Called people show up pre-framed.

The Vehicle Switch converts the tribal in/out into a specific mechanical choice the buyer now identifies with.

### Ethical constraint

Per `ethical-guardrails.md` Red Flag 3: the Old Vehicle is a *category of approach*, never a list of named humans. "Most coaches doing X" is fine. "Specifically, John Smith's method is a scam" is not.

## Translation 3 — Shared Ritual → Email + Content Cadence

Ritual in tribe language = cadence in funnel language. The cadence is the LTV engine.

### The 3 cadences to design

**1. Pre-purchase cadence — the warm-up**

- Lead enters list (from lead magnet, content, ad)
- First 5-7 days: daily email (high cadence, acclimatizes the subscriber to creator's voice)
- Day 8+: settle into long-term cadence (daily, 3x/week, or weekly depending on creator)

Target: by Day 7, subscriber has received 5+ touches of sacred vocabulary. This is why the authority-provocation archetype's daily-broadcast pattern compounds — the audience's neural groove for the phrases is cut in the first two weeks.

**2. Launch/promo cadence — the ritualized sell**

- Launches happen on a nameable rhythm (quarterly, seasonal, named event)
- Audience learns to expect and save for them
- Standard structure: pre-launch (7-14 days warming) → open cart (3-7 days) → close cart (24-48 hours) → post-launch debrief (1 email, public, showing numbers)

Launch-as-ritual is what lets a creator run 4 high-revenue weeks a year and take the other 48 off. See Brunson's Funnel Hacking Live → quarterly launch cadence.

**3. Post-purchase cadence — the membership rhythm**

- Weekly live (named hour, named format)
- Monthly content drop
- Quarterly in-person or half-day intensive
- Annual event

The post-purchase cadence is the retention core. It's what converts a one-time buyer into an LTV asset.

### The "sacred hour" move

Pick one recurring content moment. Name it. Defend it. Examples:

- **Ben Settle's** daily email lands at the same time every day for a decade-plus.
- **The Daily Stoic** lands a meditation at 6am ET daily.
- **the acquisition economist's** weekly long-form posts on Monday.

When the audience self-schedules around the cadence, ritual is operational.

### Audit test

Can the audience, without checking, state the cadence? (Yes/No per touchpoint.) 3+ "yes" = strong ritual. 0-1 = no ritual, only output.

## Translation 4 — Mission vs. Transaction → Brand Voice + Bonus Narrative

The mission lives in three places in the funnel:

### Place 1 — The About / Manifesto page

Not a bio. A manifesto. Structure:

- What I saw in the market that was wrong
- Why it matters beyond my income
- What I'm building instead
- Who this is for and who it's not

One page, 800-1500 words, linked from every sales surface. The manifesto converts skeptical prospects at a materially higher rate than a conventional About page — measurable.

### Place 2 — The VSL/webinar mission block

Standard placement: minute 18-22 of a 45-minute VSL, after the mechanism reveal, before the offer stack.

Structure:

- "Here's what this is really about..."
- One story of someone the mission served
- The bigger stakes
- Return to the offer with mission-charged language

This block materially raises close rate among mid-sophistication buyers. (Low-sophistication buyers don't need it; high-sophistication buyers detect the absence of it.)

### Place 3 — The bonus stack narrative

The bonuses are not a pile of assets. They are **mission-delivery tools**.

Standard framing: each bonus answers "because the mission is X, you also need Y."

Example (mission: help solo operators keep revenue while keeping their life):

- Core offer: the main methodology
- Bonus 1: the time-boxing system — "because the mission isn't revenue, it's revenue without the 80-hour week"
- Bonus 2: the hiring template — "because the mission requires you to not become the bottleneck"
- Bonus 3: the spouse-conversation script — "because the mission fails if the people at home don't understand what you're building"

Mission-charged bonuses convert at 2-3x the rate of generic "here's extra stuff" bonuses.

### The guarantee as mission signal

A guarantee phrased in mission language outperforms a guarantee phrased in financial language.

- Financial: "60-day money-back guarantee"
- Mission: "I will not keep your money if you haven't [mission-outcome] in 60 days — the mission is you succeeding, not me keeping the fee."

Same mechanical refund terms, different frame, measurably higher close rate.

## The Translation Audit

Run across any funnel:

1. **Vocabulary density audit** — Count the 5 chosen phrases across landing page, VSL script, emails. Target: each phrase appears 5+ times across the funnel.
2. **Binary block audit** — Is there a clean "for / not-for" block on the sales page? Is there a Vehicle Switch in the VSL?
3. **Cadence audit** — Can the audience name the pre/launch/post cadence? If no — cadence is not yet ritual.
4. **Mission audit** — Is there a manifesto page? A mission block in the VSL? Mission-framed bonuses?

Score 4/4 = funnel is fully translating the tribe. 0-1 = the tribe is real but the funnel is generic and leaving 30-60% revenue on the table.

## Conversion Impact (order-of-magnitude, not guaranteed)

| Translation applied | Typical uplift over generic equivalent |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Vocabulary density (5+ placements) | +10-25% close rate on warm traffic |
| Binary for/not-for block | +5-15% close rate, -20-40% refund rate |
| Vehicle Switch in VSL | +15-30% close rate mid-sophistication audiences |
| Mission block in VSL | +10-20% close rate, +30-50% LTV via lower refund |
| Mission-framed guarantee | +5-10% close rate |
| Bonus stack as mission-tools | +15-25% perceived-value lift |

These numbers compound multiplicatively, not additively. A fully translated funnel is not 2x a generic funnel — it is typically 3-5x on net revenue per lead.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- **Cold-traffic low-ticket funnels ($7-$47 tripwires).** The full translation is over-engineered; use vocabulary + binary only.
- **Enterprise procurement sales.** The funnel is a sales team + RFP, not a VSL — the mission still matters; the ritual doesn't.
- **Affiliate-driven businesses where the creator is not the face.** The translation requires a public identity to anchor; without it, the artifacts have nothing to compound on.
