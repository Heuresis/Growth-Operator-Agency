## When To Use LinkedIn In The the content OS director Model

1. **ICP is B2B-adjacent or professional-services buyer.** Consumer info-product ICPs rarely justify LinkedIn capacity; B2B / enterprise-coaching / professional-services ICPs do.
2. **Capacity exists after YouTube + short-form cadence is sustained.** LinkedIn is added *after* the primary Flywheel is running at cadence, not as a parallel start-up effort.
3. **Creator's voice translates to professional tone.** A lifestyle-heavy voice that works on YouTube may not fit LinkedIn; the cross-post protocol has to adjust tone per platform.

## The Cross-Post Protocol (synthesized from the content OS director invariants)

### 1. LinkedIn As Derivative, Not Native

Every LinkedIn post should derive from an existing YouTube long-form video. The post summarizes the video's key insight, frames it in LinkedIn-appropriate tone (professional framing, fewer slang markers, explicit "here's the lesson" structure), and links back to the YouTube video as the primary CTA.

The post is not a standalone thought-piece. It is a distribution surface for the Flywheel's existing content. This matches the content OS director's "short-form as derivative of long-form" invariant — LinkedIn is to long-form video what Reels are to long-form video, just with a different audience and different format.

### 2. Post Frequency

- **3-5 posts per week** for creators with active LinkedIn capacity.
- **Each post derives from a published YouTube video** (this week's, prior weeks', or the evergreen top-performing library).
- **No native-only LinkedIn content** — if the creator wants to publish a thought-piece native to LinkedIn, it becomes a YouTube short first, then a LinkedIn post derived from the short. This sustains the Flywheel's unidirectional flow.

### 3. Post Structure

A LinkedIn post derived from a YouTube video typically carries:

1. **Hook line** — the single strongest one-sentence insight from the video, positioned as post-first line so the "see more" truncation lands after it.
2. **Context / stake-setting** — 2-3 short lines explaining what the insight applies to and why the LinkedIn reader should care.
3. **The insight, unpacked** — 3-5 short paragraphs with the actual teaching. Every paragraph ≤3 lines for mobile readability. Line breaks are generous (LinkedIn rewards whitespace).
4. **CTA to the YouTube video** — "Full breakdown on YouTube — link in comments." LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes external links in the post body; put the link in the first comment.
5. **Light tagging** — 3-5 relevant hashtags at the bottom. Not a wall of 30.

### 4. No External-CTA Policy Still Applies

The CTA policy from the Flywheel extends to LinkedIn: only Conversion-phase content carries an external CTA. If the LinkedIn post derives from a Value-phase YouTube video, the LinkedIn CTA is back to the YouTube video (internal, platform-crossing but Flywheel-internal). If the LinkedIn post derives from the VSSL (rare), the CTA is to the application.

### 5. Engagement Discipline

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards the first 60-minute engagement window. Cross-posts published during the creator's audience's LinkedIn-active window (typically US business hours 8-11am or 1-3pm local) and surfaced with authentic peer engagement perform substantially better than posts published at arbitrary times. The operator schedules posts to this window and the creator/operator personally engages with the first 5-10 commenters inside the first hour.

No engagement pods. No bot-farmed reactions. These are detectable and damage the creator's LinkedIn credibility with the actual ICP audience.

### 6. Pinned LinkedIn Post

Analogous to the pinned YouTube video, the LinkedIn profile should carry one pinned post that serves as Indoctrination on LinkedIn — the creator's worldview, the named mechanism, the "start here" for a LinkedIn-first discoverer. This pinned post is refreshed quarterly.

### 7. Profile Architecture

The creator's LinkedIn profile carries:
- **Headline:** the creator's positioning in 1-2 lines. No agency-speak.
- **About section:** 3-5 short paragraphs introducing the mechanism and the outcome. Ends with a CTA to the YouTube channel.
- **Featured section:** the pinned post, the YouTube channel, the VSSL link (treated as "book a call" language, not raw URL).

## Non-applicable scenarios

- Consumer info-product ICPs where the audience lives on YouTube + IG — LinkedIn adds cost without return.
- Creators whose voice is heavily lifestyle-coded — LinkedIn tone shift may damage brand consistency.
- Early-stage creators without a YouTube library to derive from — LinkedIn posts without long-form backing collapse to "another SMMA guy posting on LinkedIn."
