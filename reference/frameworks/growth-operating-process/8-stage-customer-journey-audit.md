# 8-Stage Customer Journey Audit

> **Why this matters:** Most high-ticket coaching businesses lose money in the gaps between stages, not inside any single stage. This audit exposes drop-off at each stage so the operator fixes the weakest link first, rather than optimising stages that are already performing.

## Source / Lineage

the operations director — operator-first growth practitioner for high-ticket coaches and consultants. the operations director's body of work strips the "marketing funnel" down to a mechanical eight-stage journey with measurable transitions, rejecting both agency-speak and esoteric positioning theatre. This audit is the quarterly diagnostic that underpins his operating system.

## The Eight Stages

| # | Stage | Definition (entry criterion) | Exit criterion (promotes to next stage) |
|---|-------|------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| 1 | Awareness | Person has seen the brand at least once (impression, view, follow). | Engages with content (comment, save, DM, click, reply). |
| 2 | Engagement | Actively consuming or interacting, not yet identified. | Gives name + email OR opens a direct conversation. |
| 3 | Lead | Identified contact in CRM. No scored intent yet. | Meets basic fit criteria (ICP match + self-declared problem). |
| 4 | MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead) | Self-identified problem + ICP match + a trigger event (opt-in, webinar reg, content deep-dive). | Books or requests a sales conversation. |
| 5 | SQL (Sales Qualified Lead) | Call booked, shows up, confirmed pain + budget + timing. | Accepts proposal / enrols. |
| 6 | Customer | Paying. Onboarded. | Hits first visible win (the "aha" result). |
| 7 | Advocate | Has shared a result publicly OR given a testimonial. | Refers someone who becomes a lead. |
| 8 | Referral source | Has produced at least one customer via introduction. | N/A (terminal — goal is volume and consistency). |

## Per-Stage Audit

### Stage 1 — Awareness

- **Core metrics:** monthly reach, follower growth rate, branded search volume, direct traffic.
- **Target bands (solo coach / small team):** 50k–250k monthly reach, 3–8% follower growth month-over-month, branded search trending up quarter-over-quarter.
- **Typical drop-off pattern:** reach grows but engagement does not. The content is discovered and ignored.
- **Diagnostic questions:** Is the hook specific to one avatar? Is the first 3 seconds / first line earning the next? Is the posted content matched to the feed where it's being consumed (reel vs. carousel vs. YouTube long-form)?
- **Common fixes:** narrow the avatar (one pain, one proof), rework hooks against a hook bank, drop posting frequency in favour of one higher-quality daily hero asset.

### Stage 2 — Engagement

- **Core metrics:** comments per post, saves per post, DM volume, profile-visit-to-follow rate.
- **Target bands:** 1.5–3% engagement rate on reels for coaches, 10+ inbound DMs per day once content is working, 25–40% profile-visit-to-follow rate.
- **Typical drop-off:** profile visits happen, follows don't. The bio + pinned content fails to close.
- **Diagnostic questions:** Does the bio state who it's for, what they get, and the next action in under 150 characters? Do the top three pinned posts each cover a different problem? Is there one clear CTA?
- **Common fixes:** bio rewrite using the "for X who want Y" pattern, pinned grid refresh, single CTA in bio (not five).

### Stage 3 — Lead

- **Core metrics:** opt-in conversion rate, cost per lead, lead-to-MQL rate, list size, list-decay rate.
- **Target bands:** 25–40% landing-page opt-in, 10–20% DM-to-opt-in, <15% monthly list decay.
- **Typical drop-off:** leads enter but never progress. The lead magnet solves a surface problem, not the paid problem.
- **Diagnostic questions:** Is the lead magnet one-step-before the paid offer, or adjacent/unrelated? Does the welcome sequence frame the core problem? Is there a lead-to-call path visible within 14 days?
- **Common fixes:** replace "ultimate guide" PDFs with a 10-minute diagnostic or scorecard, rewrite welcome sequence as problem-frame / evidence / invitation, add a soft-CTA to book a call in email 3.

### Stage 4 — MQL

- **Core metrics:** MQL volume per week, MQL-to-SQL rate, MQL response time, content-asset-to-MQL attribution.
- **Target bands:** 15–30% lead-to-MQL rate, <2 hours to first human response on inbound MQL, 40–60% MQL-to-SQL.
- **Typical drop-off:** MQLs arrive but don't book calls. Qualifier is too light, or the setter/automation is too slow.
- **Diagnostic questions:** What qualifies an MQL in writing (criteria list), and is it enforced? Does the form ask a qualifying question (budget range, current revenue, timing) before the calendar appears?
- **Common fixes:** two-step application (qualifier → calendar), SLA on setter response time, nurture track for MQLs who don't book within 7 days.

### Stage 5 — SQL

- **Core metrics:** show rate, close rate, proposal-to-close rate, time from call-booked to call-held, cash-collected vs. contracted.
- **Target bands:** 65–80% show rate, 25–45% close rate on SQL, <72 hours call-booked-to-held, 80%+ cash-collected-within-7-days of contract.
- **Typical drop-off:** calls book but no-show. Or calls happen, no close.
- **Diagnostic questions:** Is there a pre-call video that re-sells the outcome? Is there an SMS + email confirmation at 24h and 1h? Is the closer running a documented framework or winging it? Is objection handling recorded and reviewed?
- **Common fixes:** pre-call asset (3-minute Loom from founder), 3-touch confirmation sequence, closer scorecard reviewed weekly, post-call follow-up template with decision deadline.

### Stage 6 — Customer

- **Core metrics:** onboarding completion rate, time-to-first-win, refund rate, NPS at day 30, at-risk flag rate.
- **Target bands:** 90%+ onboarding completion within 7 days, first visible win within 21 days for most offers, <5% refund rate.
- **Typical drop-off:** customer pays, disappears. Buyer's remorse kicks in week 2. Never posts a win.
- **Diagnostic questions:** Is there a day-0 onboarding ritual (welcome call, kit, public welcome)? Is there a scheduled check-in at day 7, 14, 30? Is the "first win" defined and tracked per customer?
- **Common fixes:** welcome kit + day-1 call, named "first win" per customer, at-risk flag triggered by no-login / no-post / missed call, fortnightly 1:1 for first 90 days.

### Stage 7 — Advocate

- **Core metrics:** testimonials captured per month, case studies published per month, organic mentions, UGC volume.
- **Target bands:** 30–50% of customers produce at least one shareable asset within 90 days.
- **Typical drop-off:** customers get results, never share. Or share once, never again.
- **Diagnostic questions:** Is there a result-capture ritual baked into delivery? Is there a deadline on the testimonial ask? Is there a ladder from quote → written → video → case study?
- **Common fixes:** result-capture form at every coaching call, 14-day testimonial ask with a script, private "wins" channel in the community that auto-prompts to go public.

### Stage 8 — Referral Source

- **Core metrics:** referrals received per month, referral-to-lead rate, referral-to-customer rate, % of revenue from referrals.
- **Target bands:** 20–40% of new customers referral-sourced once the advocate base crosses ~50 people.
- **Typical drop-off:** advocates don't know how to refer. No asset to forward. No incentive + no ask.
- **Diagnostic questions:** Is there a referral asset (one-pager, short video, landing page) any advocate can forward? Is there a quarterly referral ask built into the delivery cycle? Is there a tracking mechanism (link, code, form)?
- **Common fixes:** dedicated referral landing page, quarterly "who do you know" ask, named reward (credit, bonus session, charity donation), simple tracking via UTM or form field.

## Running the Audit

1. **Extract last-90-day data** for each stage's core metric. One sheet, one tab per stage.
2. **Score each stage red / amber / green** against the target bands. Use the operator's historical best, not an industry benchmark, as a secondary reference.
3. **Identify the weakest transition**, not the weakest stage. The stage where promotion-rate is furthest below target is the constraint.
4. **Pick one fix per quarter.** Two is the maximum; most teams over-change and lose attribution.
5. **Re-audit in 90 days** with identical definitions. Moving the definitions is the number-one way operators fool themselves.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- Pure e-commerce / physical product brands (the MQL / SQL split is not meaningful — use a standard e-commerce funnel).
- Very low-ticket digital products (<$200) where the call stage is absent.
- Enterprise B2B with 9+ month sales cycles — the stage definitions hold but the target bands do not.
