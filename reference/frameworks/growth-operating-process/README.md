# Growth Operating Process — Straight Process for Ops

> **Reference:** the operations director (Deep Dive, 10 videos, $25M+ generated for info clients)
> **Purpose:** The straight operational process — no esoteric flourishes — for running a high-ticket info business day-to-day.
> **When to use:** Scale (SOPs, hiring, KPI tracking), all departments (process discipline), customer-journey operations.

## Core Thesis — "Death by a Million Papercuts"

> *"Scaling an info product isn't difficult by any means, but it DOES require lots of attention to detail. Little 'leaks' throughout your customer journey = death by a million papercuts."*
>
> — the operations director, Deep Dive

The operational thesis: **a well-designed offer loses 60-80% of potential revenue to tiny operational leaks.** Each leak alone is invisible. Combined, they kill the business.

Growth operating is the **continuous audit + fix loop** that seals the leaks.

## The Customer Journey Audit (8-stage checklist)

Every high-ticket business has this journey. Audit each stage weekly:

1. **Traffic** — Meta Ads (or organic) → cost/lead, CPM, CTR, frequency cap
2. **Landing Page** — opt-in conversion, page-speed, device split, copy clarity
3. **Application** — start rate, completion rate, qualification rate, DQ rate
4. **TY Page** (confirmation) — show-up preparation, video engagement, expectation-set
5. **Setter** — response time (< 5 min target), show-up rate, qualification accuracy
6. **Pre-Call** — bullet-point prep delivered to closer, reminder cadence (24h/6h/1h)
7. **On-Call** — discovery quality, pitch execution, close rate, objection handling
8. **Post-Call** — payment processing, post-purchase experience, onboarding speed, first-win delivery

**Every stage has 3-7 operational levers.** Death by papercuts happens when you optimize macro (ad spend, offer) and ignore micro (setter response time went from 3 min to 12 min).

## The 7-Step Launch Process

Not a creative launch — the operational sequence for launching/scaling an offer:

1. **Initial offering** — MVP offer, small cohort (5-10 customers), no paid traffic
2. **Beta launch** — 30-50 customers, case study capture, objection mining
3. **Reposition + Infra** — tighten positioning based on real buyer signal + build tech stack
4. **Hard launch** — full paid traffic, email list + partners, measure at volume
5. **Second wind / Live event** — mid-cycle re-acceleration (re-invigorate dead leads, live event for urgency)
6. **Optimization** — week-by-week leak plugging on the 8-stage journey
7. **Amplify + New conversion mechanisms** — once one funnel is dialed, add a second (VSL + webinar, or webinar + challenge, etc.)

**Rule:** don't add conversion mechanism #2 until #1 has proven leak-free at $500K+/mo volume.

## The 60/30/10 Revenue Mix (ideal at $130K+/mo)

From the operations director's $130K/mo Growth Operating breakdown:
- **60% call funnel revenue** (highest intent, highest trust, largest AOV)
- **30% webinar funnel revenue** (scaleable, evergreen or live)
- **10% email broadcast revenue** (low-cost, high-margin re-activation)

This mix balances:
- Acquisition cost (call funnel = highest, email = lowest)
- Scale ceiling (webinar = highest, call funnel = limited by closer capacity)
- Revenue quality (call funnel = premium, email = commodity)

**Rule:** if any single channel is > 80% of revenue, the business is fragile. Multi-mechanism is anti-fragile.

## The 3 Operational Rules

### Rule 1 — "Always start HIGH TICKET"
Low-ticket is an ascension mechanism, not a standalone business. Prove the high-ticket first. Low-ticket entry can come after. Reverse order kills margin.

### Rule 2 — "Setter-to-closer model (staff depending)"
Below $100K/mo: founder-closes + admin setter.
$100K-$500K/mo: 1 closer + 2 setters.
$500K-$1M/mo: 2 closers + 4 setters + 1 SDR.
$1M+/mo: full sales team with SDR/setter/closer/AE split.

Don't over-hire. Each role has a revenue threshold. Don't under-hire at scale.

### Rule 3 — "Don't try 3 sports at once"
At any given cycle, master ONE:
- Webinar scaling
- VSL scaling
- DM-to-call scaling

Running all three simultaneously = fractional attention = half-assed everywhere. Pick one. Master it. Add the next only after the first is running on rails.

## KPI-Driven Diagnostics — The Metrics Discipline

> *"Complaining about performance but not understanding why you're underperforming is the same as complaining about being out of shape but never tracking a calorie or a workout in your entire life."*

The core metrics dashboard for a high-ticket info business:

**Acquisition KPIs:**
- Cost per lead (CPL)
- Lead-to-application rate
- Application-to-show-up rate
- Show-up-to-close rate
- Cost per acquisition (CAC)

**Economics KPIs:**
- Average order value (AOV)
- Lifetime value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC ratio (MUST be ≥ 3:1 per INV-4)
- Gross margin %
- Payback period (months to recoup CAC)

**Ops KPIs:**
- Setter response time (< 5 min target)
- Closer utilization (% of available time on calls)
- Refund rate (< 5% target)
- Show-rate (> 60% target for applications, > 40% for webinar)

**Delivery KPIs:**
- First-win time (how fast do they win?)
- NPS or equivalent satisfaction score
- Retention / churn per cohort
- Upsell rate

**Team KPIs:**
- Revenue per employee
- Cost per seat
- Team utilization

## Implement → Iterate → Amplify (the Continuous Loop)

> *"The entire game is: Implement, Iterate, Amplify. Rinse and repeat."*

- **Implement** — deploy the change
- **Iterate** — measure, audit, fix leaks
- **Amplify** — once stable, increase volume

Most businesses get stuck in Implement (change for change's sake, no measurement) or Amplify too early (scale a broken unit economic). The Iterate phase is where the money lives.
