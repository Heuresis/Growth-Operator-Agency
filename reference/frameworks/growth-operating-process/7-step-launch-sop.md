# 7-Step Launch SOP (High-Ticket)

> **Why this matters:** High-ticket launches ($2k–$50k, book-a-call model) fail for different reasons than IPO-style product launches. They fail from thin priming, rushed proof, and no scarcity mechanic — not from poor cart-page design. This SOP fixes that.

## Source / Lineage

the operations director — operating-process practitioner for coach and consultant businesses in the $500k–$5M/year range. This SOP is the condensed version of the 7-week rollout he runs with clients quarterly. It assumes an application + call sales motion, not a direct cart.

## Scope

- Applies to: high-ticket offers, $2k–$50k, book-a-call or application-to-call sales motion.
- Does not apply to: product launches (Jeff Walker PLF-style), physical-product drops, app launches, or low-ticket tripwires.
- Assumed channels: email list (1k+), social profile (IG + one other), existing warm audience. Cold-only launches need a different SOP.

## The Seven Weeks

### Week 1 — Prime

**Objective:** re-establish relevance with the audience and surface the problem the offer solves, without naming the offer.

- **Content cadence:** 5 posts / week. 3 problem-naming posts, 2 authority posts (origin, proof, contrarian take).
- **Email cadence:** 2 sends. (a) Re-intro + what I've been thinking about, (b) a diagnostic framework for the problem.
- **Calls:** none booked from launch. Back-channel conversations only.
- **Metrics:** re-engagement rate on email (open + click trend vs. trailing 30 days), reply volume, DM volume on "is this you?" posts.
- **Exit gate:** at least 3% of list replies or engages across the week. If not, extend prime by 7 days.

### Week 2 — Tease

**Objective:** signal that something is coming, name the outcome (not the offer), start collecting interest list.

- **Content cadence:** 5 posts / week. 2 "here's what I'm building" posts, 2 customer-result posts (stage 7 advocates from the last cohort), 1 behind-the-scenes.
- **Email cadence:** 2 sends. (a) Interest-list open with a single CTA ("reply YES if you want early access"), (b) one detailed customer story.
- **Calls:** none. Interest-list replies are logged but not called.
- **Metrics:** interest-list size, reply-to-send rate on the interest email, save-rate on result posts.
- **Exit gate:** interest list ≥ 5% of active list OR ≥ 2× last launch. If not, rework the outcome framing before moving on.

### Week 3 — Open

**Objective:** open applications, run the first batch of calls, confirm the offer is resonating.

- **Content cadence:** 6 posts / week. Daily post with a direct application CTA. Mix: problem, proof, process, objection, offer framing, invitation.
- **Email cadence:** 3 sends. (a) Doors open to interest list, (b) public announcement, (c) first-week results / momentum.
- **Calls:** 10–20 calls booked in the week. Calendar capped — scarcity starts here.
- **Metrics:** application volume, application-to-call-booked rate (target 40–60%), call-held rate (target 70%+), close rate (target 25–40%).
- **Exit gate:** at least 5 customers closed OR application volume ≥ 2× calls available. If neither, diagnose the offer before Week 4.

### Week 4 — Content

**Objective:** deepen conviction for the fence-sitters with long-form content that does the heavy lifting sales calls cannot.

- **Content cadence:** 4 posts / week + 1 long-form (30–60 min video, webinar replay, or deep essay).
- **Email cadence:** 3 sends, all sequenced around the long-form asset. (a) "Here's the full breakdown," (b) the framework extracted, (c) Q&A from the long-form.
- **Calls:** continue 10–20 / week. Track whether calls booked this week consumed the long-form.
- **Metrics:** long-form completion rate (target 25%+), long-form-to-application rate, new applications from week-2 interest list (laggards converting).
- **Exit gate:** long-form view count ≥ 20% of active audience. If not, repurpose into a second long-form.

### Week 5 — Proof

**Objective:** saturate with in-launch proof. Week-3 customers are now 14 days in; their early results become the flywheel.

- **Content cadence:** 5 posts / week. 3 customer-result / in-launch proof, 1 framework post, 1 objection-handling post.
- **Email cadence:** 3 sends, each anchored to a customer story. Format: name + before state + what they did + after state + invitation.
- **Calls:** 15–25 / week. Volume peaks here because proof is driving conviction.
- **Metrics:** proof-post engagement (target 2× baseline), applications-from-proof-attribution, close rate this week (target equal or better than week 3).
- **Exit gate:** at least 2 new customer stories captured and approved for public use.

### Week 6 — Scarcity

**Objective:** introduce a real scarcity mechanic. Not fake urgency — an actual capacity or deadline constraint.

- **Valid scarcity mechanics:** (a) cohort close date, (b) number of seats remaining, (c) price rising on X date, (d) bonus expiring, (e) founder's time cap for onboarding.
- **Content cadence:** 6 posts / week. Countdown-driven. Mix: seats-left update, last-objection post, "what happens if you wait" post, founder's voice note, cohort preview, client voice.
- **Email cadence:** 4 sends. (a) 7 days left + what's changing, (b) 5 days left + objection handled, (c) 3 days left + last call invitation, (d) 24 hours + final.
- **Calls:** 20–30 / week. This is peak volume.
- **Metrics:** show rate (must stay >70% — scarcity without show-up is meaningless), close rate in the final 72 hours (often 1.5–2× steady-state), cash-collected at the close.
- **Exit gate:** none — week ends with the scarcity event.

### Week 7 — Close

**Objective:** close enrolments, close the launch mentally, capture everything. A launch that ends without a debrief is a launch that can't be repeated.

- **Content cadence:** 3 posts / week. (a) Cart-closed / doors-closed announcement, (b) thank-you-to-customers post, (c) "what's next" signal for the audience who didn't buy.
- **Email cadence:** 3 sends. (a) Doors closed / waitlist opens, (b) what you missed (without being rude), (c) nurture-track reset.
- **Calls:** final stragglers only (booked before the deadline). No new bookings in this week.
- **Metrics:** total revenue (contracted + cash-collected), customer count, cost-per-customer (ad spend + setter + tooling), time-to-first-win tracker setup for each new customer.
- **Exit gate:** debrief document completed within 7 days of close. See debrief template below.

## Debrief Template (mandatory)

1. Total revenue, cash-collected, customer count, average deal.
2. Application volume, application-to-call, call-held, close rate, week-by-week.
3. Which content assets drove the most attributable revenue.
4. Which emails drove the most attributable revenue.
5. Top 3 objections heard, with response notes.
6. One thing that worked better than expected.
7. One thing that broke and will be fixed before the next launch.
8. Re-run date + pre-mortem assumptions for the next cycle.

## Guardrails

- Never compress the SOP below 5 weeks. Prime + Tease cannot be collapsed without the list fatiguing.
- Never run two launches back-to-back without 60+ days gap — the audience needs nurture weeks to reset.
- Scarcity must be real. Fake scarcity degrades the list permanently; the operations director treats this as a non-negotiable.
- Setter / closer capacity is the binding constraint. Model the calendar at 60% utilisation, not 100%.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- List under 1,000 engaged contacts (launch won't clear break-even; use a cohort-evergreen model instead).
- First-ever offer with no prior customers (no Week 5 proof; substitute with pilot testimonials or a beta-only model).
- Offers priced under $2k (the application-to-call motion over-engineers the sale).
- Enterprise sales (the 7-week cadence does not map to procurement cycles).
