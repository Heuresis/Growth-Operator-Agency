# the psychological copywriter 11-Step Pull-Push-Persuade VSL — Variant B

> **Source:** the psychological copywriter — *The 5,280-Word VSL Conversion Framework* · 50+ funnels, $1M+ pipeline each
> **Target runtime:** 8-12 min
> **Best for:** No-camera / faceless / paid-traffic cold audience

## The 3 Phases × 11 Steps

### PULL (Steps 1-4) — Earn attention

| # | Step | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Promise (Hook)** | Specific promise to specific person about specific outcome |
| 2 | **Profile** | Self-selection — describe their situation back to them |
| 3 | **Proof** | Results / specificity / pattern proof (3 types blended) |
| 4 | **Presence** | 2-3 sentence introduction — who you are |

### PUSH (Steps 5-8) — Build pressure

| # | Step | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Pain Points** | Compounding cost of inaction — specific + emotional |
| 6 | **Case Studies** | Before/after mirroring viewer — isomorphic |
| 7 | **Process** | Name your framework + 3-5 sub-steps |
| 8 | **Future Pacing** | Concrete future self — vivid, specific |

### PERSUADE (Steps 9-11) — Close

| # | Step | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | **Objection Handling** | 4 objections (price, fit, decision-avoidance, trust) — pre-emptively handled |
| 10 | **The Close** | 5-layer: Core Deliverable → Value Stack → Price Anchor → Guarantee → CTA |
| 11 | **Postscript** | Quiet close, final reminder |

## The 8 Invariants (Pull-Push-Persuade methodology)

1. One viewer, one problem, one solution
2. Write how people talk, not how marketers write
3. Specificity beats creativity ("$25,000 in 90 minutes")
4. Script does the selling, visuals support
5. **Never sell before Step 9** (same invariant as the VSL director)
6. Every sentence earns the next (1,800 words in 12-min VSL — every word pays rent)
7. Stack case studies by relatability, not impressiveness
8. Close like you mean it

## Decision Logic

### Why PULL before PUSH
> *"Skip the Pull phase, and the Push feels manipulative."*

Earn the right to create pressure by first earning attention honestly.

### Why PUSH before PERSUADE
> *"Skip the Push phase, and the Persuade falls flat because there's no emotional momentum."*

Decisions require information + emotional stakes. Pure logic doesn't close.

### The 4 Objections (Step 9 — handle in order)
1. **Price** — "Too expensive" (reframe as investment + ROI math)
2. **Fit** — "Won't work for me specifically" (isomorphic case study closest to viewer)
3. **Decision-avoidance** — "I need to think about it" (Crossroads-style pressure on cost of delay)
4. **Trust** — "Why should I believe you?" (proof + guarantee reinforced)

### The 5-Layer Close (Step 10)
Same as `sales/value-stack-architecture.md`:
- Layer 1: Core Deliverable
- Layer 2: Value Stack (itemized)
- Layer 3: Price Anchor
- Layer 4: Guarantee
- Layer 5: CTA

## Critical Invariants

- **Never sell before Step 9**
- Specificity-heavy language throughout
- Silent-viewing optimized (text + clear visuals for muted watchers)
- Pull phase 25% of runtime / Push 50% / Persuade 25%
