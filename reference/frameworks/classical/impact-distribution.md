# Impact Distribution Principle (40/40/20) — Sultanic

> **Source:** Alen Sultanic (Nothing Held Back) via Eugene Schwartz lineage
> **Status:** CORE INVARIANT of Growth OS (INV-1). Referenced by every division.

## The Principle

```
  40% Audience × 40% Offer × 20% Copy = Results
```

**Audience quality drives 40%.** Offer strength drives 40%. Copy + messaging drives 20%.

**Rule:** always fix upstream before downstream.

## The Audit Order

When anything underperforms, audit in this exact order:

1. **Audience (40%)** — Is the ICP right? Is the market right?
2. **Offer (40%)** — Is the offer economics viable? Does the Value Equation score ≥ 150?
3. **Copy (20%)** — Is the copy specific + voice-matched + mechanism-threaded?

**Rule:** never optimize copy before audience + offer are proven. A strong offer to the right audience converts with mediocre copy. Great copy to the wrong audience or with a weak offer always loses.

## Why the Split

### The 40% for Audience
Wrong audience = no amount of offer sophistication or copy craft saves the business. "Great offer to wrong audience" is a common $250K/mo plateau trigger.

Symptoms of wrong-audience:
- Low conversion despite polished copy
- Objections that don't match ICP assumptions
- Refund rate > 5%
- Low LTV:CAC despite good CAC
- "Everyone loves my content but nobody buys"

### The 40% for Offer
Weak offer = broken economics. Even the right audience can't buy an offer that doesn't deliver (or isn't priced right, or doesn't resolve their limiting belief, or misses bonuses).

Symptoms of weak offer:
- Low close rate despite qualified leads
- "I love it but I can't justify the price"
- Refund rate > 5% (offer didn't deliver what copy promised)
- Churn in first 30 days (no first-win engineered)
- Low Value Equation Score (< 150)

### The 20% for Copy
Copy matters — but LAST. The copy optimization ROI only compounds when audience + offer are already aligned.

Symptoms of copy-only issues (after audience + offer proven):
- Strong VSL open but weak pitch
- Good cold ad CTR but weak downstream conversion
- High engagement but low click-through
- Generic phrases leaking through (see `primitives/specificity.md`)

## The 80/20 Inside the Equation

Most creators spend 80% of their time on copy and 20% on audience + offer. **Invert this.** 80% on audience + offer, 20% on copy. Copy is the easiest to iterate once audience + offer are locked.

## Application Across Skills

### In `/research`
Output explicitly ranks audience/offer/copy gaps:
- Audience compartment completeness
- Offer compartment completeness
- Copy compartment completeness

Recommendation always prioritizes upstream.

### In `/build-icp`
ICP Doc is the Audience (40%) foundation. Completeness ≥ 80 required before any downstream work.

### In `/design-offer`
Offer Doc Value Equation Score must be ≥ 150 (Grand Slam threshold) AND LTV:CAC ≥ 3.

### In `/build-vsl`, `/ad-creative`, `/write-*`
Copy skills CANNOT ship when Audience or Offer compartments are below threshold. Gate-blocking per `spec/CONTEXT-THRESHOLDS.md`.

### In diagnostic calls (performance analysis)
When creator asks "why is conversion dropping?":
1. Check Audience — has the ICP shifted? Market matured?
2. Check Offer — has the Value Equation weakened? Pricing off?
3. Only then check Copy — has messaging drifted?

## The Market Hierarchy (companion principle)

Sultanic's related hierarchy:
```
  Market (90% of outcome) → Audience → Offer → Copy
```

A rising market lifts mediocre offers. A declining market sinks great offers. Before audience/offer/copy, check the MARKET.

Growth OS `/research` Section 2 (Market State) is this check. If market is declining, repositioning is urgent — not copy tweaks.

## Invariants

- Audit order: Audience → Offer → Copy (never skip)
- 80% of creator time should be on Audience + Offer (the 80% that matters)
- Copy skills gate-blocked when upstream compartments below threshold
- Market hierarchy check before audience (rare but decisive — declining markets need pivot, not optimization)

## Tacit Application

Creators who hit $250K-$500K/mo plateaus almost always do so because they optimized copy 80% of the time at $50K/mo and audience/offer stayed mediocre. The plateau is audience/offer debt catching up.

Break the plateau: go back upstream. `/research` fresh. `/build-icp` fresh. `/design-offer` fresh. Copy refresh is a side-effect, not the work.
