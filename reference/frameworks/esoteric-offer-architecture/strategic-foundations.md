# Strategic Foundations

> The business-model and economic architecture layer. Competing on Economics, the AIDVVA update to AIDA, Rational vs Irrational Math, the Three Economics Dimensions, Intentional Business Models, Dynamic vs Static Markets, Creative vs Mechanical Markets, Need vs Want Utility, Format Innovation.
>
> Source framing: *these are the highest-level thinking frameworks that govern all tactical decisions.* Everything downstream (copy, funnels, creative) is an application of these foundations to a specific context.

## Competing on Economics

**Core definition from source:** competing on trade-offs and understanding their psychological impact on human decision-making.

### The three universal constraints

All economic decisions reduce to trade-offs between Time, Money, and Energy.

- **Time**, if unlimited, any problem is solvable. Mortality makes time precious.
- **Money**, if unlimited, you can buy time and energy from others.
- **Energy**, if unlimited, you can compress time through sustained effort.

**Optimization principle:** optimal offers minimize required time, money, AND energy. Companies that remove all three constraints better than competitors dominate markets.

### The AIDVVA update to AIDA

- **Old AIDA**, Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
- **New AIDVVA**, Attention → Interest → Desire → Verification → Validation → Action

**Why it changed:**
- Verification, *who else is using this?* Social-proof requirement.
- Validation, *will this work for me? how does the math back out?* Personal-fit requirement.

Modern buyers need BOTH social proof (verification) AND personal relevance (validation) before taking action. The two new steps are not optional, they are diagnostic failure points where modern campaigns quietly leak prospects without generating measurable objection signals.

### Abundance / lack positioning

Markets are defined by what they have (abundance) and what they lack.

- Primary resources: Time, Money, Energy
- Secondary resources: Knowledge, Skills, Network, Tools

**Positioning principle:** lead messaging with solution to primary lack, close with utilization of primary abundance.

**Counter-intuitive insight from source:** *this is opposite of conventional marketing (which leads with benefits). Leading with lack removal is more powerful.*

### Perceived vs actual constraints

*"I can't"* is economic language revealing perceived limitations.

Translation:
- Conscious: "I can't" = I don't know how (knowledge gap)
- Subconscious: "I can't" = I don't want to (motivation gap) OR I don't have will-power (resource gap)

**Strategic application:** offers must remove BOTH perceived and actual constraints. All resistance is economic, lack of resources (time / money / energy / knowledge / will-power).

### Change-work philosophy

**Fundamental definition from source:** all marketing and copy is change work, exchanging current belief state for different belief state.

**Economic principle:** change requires energy. People resist change because it costs cognitive resources.

**Strategic insight:** *best change work happens when the person believes THEY changed themselves (not that you changed them).* When change happens without detection of external influence, no resistance is triggered.

### Rational vs irrational math

**Rational math:** Product → if/then → Outcome. Direct cause-effect. Fewer objections, clear value path, easier to justify. Example: *if I buy this trading system, then I make money.*

**Irrational math:** Product → if/then → if/then → if/then → Outcome. Multiple logical-chain links. Example: *if I become a copywriter, then I'll get clients, and if I get clients they'll pay me, and if they pay me I'll have money, and if I have money I'll have status / security.*

**Requirement for irrational math:** asymmetrical outcome. The bigger the irrational-math chain, the bigger the promised outcome must be.

**Objection principle:** objections arise from if/then statements with uncertainty variables.

**Testing:** ask *"what else could it mean?"* Multiple answers = irrational math. One answer = rational math.

**Strategic control:** control the rationality of the math, you control value, you control trade-offs.

### Value-control mechanism

**Core principle:** making what you offer FAR MORE VALUABLE THAN IT REALLY IS.

Control of the sales process:
- When you control: they buy on your timeline, your terms, your structure
- When they control: they buy on their timeline, their terms, comparison-shop forever

How to control: structure creates rules, rules create boundaries, boundaries enforce rules.

**Saying-no principle:** structure, boundaries, and saying NO increases perceived value and control.

### The three economics dimensions

**Attention economics.** Every time someone gives you attention, they can't pay attention to something else. Attention fatigue + allocation. Strategic application: *the more attention you can hold in isolation, the more you can sell.*

**Financial economics.** Every purchase = can't spend money on something else. Financial fatigue + resource allocation. Understand their access to money, credit cards as financial-capacity indicators.

**Decision economics.** Every choice = can't choose something else. Decision fatigue + cognitive load. Strategic application: minimize decision steps while maximizing value extraction. *The more decisions they can make in fewer steps, the more money you make.*

### Pricing ratio frameworks

From source:
- Recurring pricing = ~1% of their monthly income
- High-ticket = ~10% of their average income
- Team-building = ~10% of your revenue
- Daily ad spend = total funnel value (if 1 person buys everything = break even)

### Exclusion vs inclusion marketing

**Funnel-centric exclusion (traditional):** 3% conversion = excluding 97% of people from giving you money. Always leaving money on the table, always need MORE traffic.

**Inclusion marketing:** design offers that include more people at different price points. Collaborative pricing vs competitive pricing. Structure:
- YES right now, front-end offer for immediate buyers
- YES but not right now, payment plans, wait lists
- MAYBE, higher ticket, more consideration
- NO not right now, nurture sequence
- NO, exclude permanently

Strategic advantage: capture more of the market at different readiness levels.

## Philosophy of Offers

**Definition from source:** an offer is *a value vehicle that gets someone an outcome in exchange for currency.*

### Two types of truth

- **Absolute truth**, objective truth. Repeatable outcome based on variable inputs.
- **Relative truth**, subjective truth. Personal experience or belief.

**Offer requirement:** must be built on absolute / objective truth, repeatable, mathematical, works for others. An offer built on relative truth is pre-broken; it will fail downstream in ways that look like marketing failures but are actually truth failures.

### Philosophy power

- **Defensive capability:** when you have a philosophy, you can defend it against anyone.
- **Conviction source:** the truth and the way they get the outcome, not about being right, about having truth.
- **Self-conviction first:** you can't have conviction without first convincing yourself. If you spend 3 months finding "the big idea" or "the hook," you're trying to convince yourself it's valuable.
- **Inside-out vs outside-in:** philosophy lets you go inside-out (conviction-based) instead of outside-in (searching for hooks).

### Marketing vs product focus

- **First-acquisition trap:** when you focus only on first acquisition, you can't scale, they don't come back.
- **Repeat-business model:** focus on 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th acquisition and you CAN scale.

**Core principle from source:** *your marketing should convince them to buy ONCE. Your product should convince them to buy AGAIN and AGAIN and AGAIN.*

### Three business stages

- Creative stage: $0-$250K, economics, intentional business models, philosophy of offers
- Formulaic stage: $250K-$1M, systems and frameworks
- Systems stage: $1M+, automated systems and scale

## Intentional Business Models

### Core principles

- **Customers as renewable resource:** your customers are a renewable financial resource. Your job is to get money out of them over and over again.
- **Sales and relationship cycles:** first acquisition vs 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th acquisitions.
- **Ratio-based thinking:** it's all about RATIOS, marketing geometry.

**Money-access indicators from source:** Amex holders have more money than Mastercard holders → offer higher-priced upsells. Discover Card → offer payment plans. Zip code targeting → architect pricing funnels based on zip code redirects.

### Dynamic vs static markets

**Dynamic market.** High rate of information change and usability. Measured by number of self-replicating players. Example metric from source: *for every 500 books sold in Automatic Clients, created one competitor.* Psychological driver: they want what you got because they're always doing the math. Offer lifecycle: ~2 years before major reinvention required. Examples: biz-op, finance, fashion, day trading, crypto, influencer, YouTuber, TikToker.

**Static market.** Low rate of information change, information useful 10+ years later. Test: *if I use this information today, will it work 10 years from now? YES = static.* Offer lifecycle: much longer, more stable. Examples: dog training, relationships, music, English teaching.

**Strategic transformation:** if you shift a market from dynamic to static, you OWN it. You become a utility.

### Creative vs mechanical markets

**Creative market.** Customer must use creativity to get results. Harder to get results; very small % succeed. Easier to ascend due to variability of inputs. Creativity does not scale. Example: copywriting, must know how to write AND sell AND get clients. Responsibility on them.

**Mechanical market.** Customer follows step-by-step instructions to get results. Easier short-term results; harder to ascend due to limited variability. Process-driven, scalable, systematizable.

**Strategic transformation:** if you shift market from creative to mechanical, you OWN it. Examples: Jasper.AI (copywriting), Canva.com (design).

### Need vs want utility

- **Want**, people think about if they SHOULD NOT buy it. Evoked set (do I want this, that, or that?). High comparison shopping.
- **Need**, people think about if they SHOULD buy it. Embedded in function, can't function without it. Low comparison shopping.

**Wealth principle from source:** richest people focus on NEEDS. DuPont family sold paint and tape, things people NEED.

**Strategic goal:** build business around NEED at the core, then expand from there.

### Commodity vs utility

- **Commodity**, competing on price with many alternatives. Killing each other for scraps. Low barrier to entry. Low and compressing margins.
- **Utility**, useful without alternatives. They MUST use you. Real money, pricing power, market control. High barrier to entry. Examples: Facebook is a utility, ClickFunnels is a utility.

**Prediction from source:** within 5-10 years, MOST online businesses will be commodity or utility. Barrier to entry, attraction of margins, and access to same information drive this.

**Strategic meta-goal:** become a utility, collapse the market by actually solving the problem.

### What business you're really in

**Principle:** you're NEVER in the business you think you're in. The real business is the one that causes fulfillment of the product.

Examples from source:
- Coaching business → really customer service + experience business
- Copy Accelerator → really customer service + employment agency
- Dating business → really communication business
- Agency business → really people management + recruitment

**Strategic insight:** if you're in revenue business (copywriter delivers revenue), there are 500 other ways to generate revenue, you can sell them other stuff.

**Growth principle:** the more businesses you're in, the more money you make. They come for the front thing, stay for what you're really there for.

## Market-Based Funnel Architecture

### Core principles

- Every market is unique, every market has a unique timeline of problems
- Funnels as timelines of problems and solutions
- A funnel is nothing more than a series of decisions
- **Decision compression:** the more decisions they can make in fewer steps, the more money you'll make

### Upsell elasticity principle

**Ratio from source:** 100x, for every $1 you get on front-end, you can charge $100 in upsell path and it will convert.

**Price-doubling effect:** if you double the price, conversion drops from 25% to 18% (28% reduction), but 100% price increase = net positive.

**AOV contribution thinking:** always look at AOV contribution, not just conversion rate.

### Downsell architecture

- With downsells between: 3 upsells with 3 downsells = 6 decisions (actually 6 upsells) = dramatically higher AOV
- Without downsells: 3 upsells only = 3 decisions = lower AOV
- After all upsells: downsells AFTER all upsells = same 3 decisions but salvages failed upsells

Strategic insight: positioning matters, downsells between upsells creates more decisions and higher AOV than either alternative.

### Outcome-based thinking

**Fundamental principle:** trade-offs do NOT take place on features and benefits. They take place on OUTCOMES.

*Think about holistically healing your customers, what's better than OTO1? what's better than OTO2?*

General progression: Bigger, Faster, Stronger, Done-For-You.

**Maximize / minimize / simplify:**
- Maximize output, the outcome they get
- Minimize input, effort, time, cost required
- Simplify process, make it easier to execute

### Timeline thinking by market (verbatim from source)

**Biz-op:** FE = DIY (Learning) → OTO1 = DWY (Templates, enablement for speed) → OTO2 = DFY (Done completely for them).

**Dating female:** FE = Get the guy / attract → OTO1 = Keep the guy → OTO2 = Get the guy to commit.

**Survival:** FE = What's going on today → OTO1 = What's going to happen next 30-90 days → OTO2 = What's going to happen in next 5 years.

**Weight loss:** FE = Lose weight → OTO1 = Keep the weight off → OTO2 = Fix problems caused by weight loss (diet face, etc.).

**Crypto / finance:** FE = Trading system → OTO1 = Done-for-you trades / research reports → OTO2 = Staking / investing (passive income).

**Pain relief:** FE = Get rid of the pain → OTO1 = Fix the damage the pain has done → OTO2 = Start doing things you couldn't do + ensure pain never comes back.

### Format-to-price elasticity

**Principle:** the more attention you can hold, the more you can sell.

Format → price-point mapping:
- Book: $37
- Course: $97
- Software: $497

**Strategic application:** change the product FORMAT to change the PRICE. Same information, different container = different price elasticity.

### Optimization / scaling directions

- Optimization: BACK TO FRONT (start with backend, work forward)
- Scaling: FRONT TO BACK (perfect front, then add backend)
- Who you get on the front is who you get on the back
- How you measure success IS the way they measure outcomes

## Format Innovation Frameworks

**Core principle:** all format innovation is based on hiding the sales process.

### Awareness / resistance cycle

**Formula:** Awareness of Sales Process = Resistance / Rejection of Sales Process.

**Human behavior:** people LOVE to buy but HATE being sold to.

**Mechanism:** as soon as they detect sales process, brain switches from *associative state* (consuming content) to *dissociative state* (rejecting sales message).

**Control shift:** you go from controlling sales process to THEM controlling it.

### Format lifecycle

- **Innovation stage**, market unaware it's a sales process. Longer form, indirect. High conversion, low resistance.
- **Saturation stage**, market becoming aware, starting to resist. Must shorten to extend lifespan. Declining conversion. Format compression required.
- **Compression stage**, highly aware, high resistance. Short form, faster. Lower conversion but volume compensates. Further compression required.
- **Direct stage**, completely aware, format is dead. Direct CPA / eCom / hybrid. New format required. Innovation or exit.

### Format innovation formula

**Equation:** Attention span + Network + Market maturity / sophistication = Format.

**Components:**
- Information consumption behavior sets buying behavior, sales process mirrors content consumption
- Network attention patterns, pay attention to where attention is and what format it's in
- Market sophistication, how aware are they of sales processes?

**Strategic application:** develop formats they're NOT used to seeing. Competition is comparison, if they can compare you to something, there's buying resistance.

### Current format landscape (verbatim from source)

**Podcast VSL.** Awareness: completely unaware it's a sales process. Emotional state: generates high-state customers. Consumption pattern: conditioned to watch up to 3 hours. Attention isolation: high, holds attention long enough for checkout. Price point: $497+. Conversion: 1% at $497 = 5% conversion at $97. Secret: the podcast HOST is the secret.

**10-minute VSL.** Awareness: aware it's a VSL but numbers work due to volume. Market type: aware markets. Duration: 6-18 minutes. Price point: $37-$97. Upgrade paths: $997 or $297. Conversion: 3-4%.

**Hybrid CPA / eCom long-form.** Awareness: half unaware / half aware. Emotional state: high. Consumption: conditioned to scan. Price point: $5-$57. Conversion: 15-25% pre-algo updates, 8-15% after.

### Attention-isolation principle

**Core insight:** Attention in Isolation = Price Elasticity.

**Formula:** the more you can isolate attention and hold it, the more price elasticity you have.

**Sales-format → price hierarchy:**
- Text sales letters: $5-$97
- VSLs: $37-$97
- Webinars: $997+
- Phone sales: $5,000+
- Events: $25,000+ (back-of-room sales)

## Strategic Integration

The full integration loop from source: Economics ↔ Format ↔ Psychology ↔ Offer ↔ Copy ↔ Funnel, all interconnected.

Optimization order: Strategy (Audience + Offer + Economics) → Psychology → Tactics (Copy).

Scaling principle: fix economics and offer BEFORE optimizing copy. Copy can't save bad audience-offer-economics match.

Long-term thinking: build for repeat business, not just first acquisition. Build for utility, not just quick wins.
