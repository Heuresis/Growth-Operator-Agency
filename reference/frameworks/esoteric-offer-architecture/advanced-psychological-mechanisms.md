## The Three Limiting Beliefs Architecture

**Fundamental principle from source:** *all profit is made in the perceptive gap of feeling. All purchases are made to change a feeling state. To feel better.*

**Core insight:** limiting beliefs are *resourceless* beliefs. The source's origin story: *when we are about up to 10 years old we have experiences that we do not have resources to deal with. So we assume beliefs and act in a way that fill those gaps that make us FEEL safe.*

### The three types in depth

**Worthless.** Core question: *who am I compared to others?* Resource lack: no status items. Age group: young. Emotional driver: greed. Buying pattern: all gain, they buy status. Problem / solution sales-process split: 50% problem (why) / 50% solution (how). Language: *"because of ___,"* *"you made me feel that way."* Emotional state: low, reactive, unresourceful, shame-defensive, shiny-object-syndrome, hop-hop. Market behaviors: crypto guys chasing worthiness, group-challenge participation, need-to-belong to create hierarchy, 0-to-1 thinking, justify out of greed, push marketing works, external-belief mechanism (*if I have that, then I get whatever*), paint a compelling future, need confidence given to them, live in the future. Marketing approach: beliefs hide in the FUTURE, give them a mechanism that delivers status, screenshots of money attract them, respond to outcome, indecisive (want you to make decisions for them), low dopamine. **Empowering shift:** *I am worthy. Your product leads to self-worth.*

**Helpless.** Core question: *what can I do compared to others?* Resource lack: no data / knowledge. Age group: middle-age. Emotional driver: need. Buying pattern: they buy solutions. Problem / solution split: 10% problem / 90% solution. Language: *"if you knew,"* *"if you had this."* Emotional state: rational, in-motion, solution-driven, resourceful, self-critic. Market behaviors: respond to process / journey, pull marketing works, confidence already present, buy from few people, trust-basis, demonstrable value. Marketing approach: beliefs hide in the PRESENT, things they are in control of, process-oriented messaging. **Empowering shift:** *There is help. Your product gives help.*

**Hopeless.** Core question: *there is no hope for me.* Resource lack: no energy, no money. Age group: old / older. Emotional driver: fear. Buying pattern: all loss, they buy hope. Problem / solution split: 90% problem / 10% solution. Language: *"they're going to do this,"* *"this is going to happen."* Emotional state: anger / anxiety, new-savior syndrome, group-forming (safe space = groups), heavy external judgment. Market behaviors: invisible enemy they can't touch / see / feel but can know at a distance, push marketing works, painting no future, lost confidence, living in the past, low-to-medium ticket stuff from many people. Marketing approach: beliefs hide in the PAST, anger / fear / lack emotions, things they are NOT in control of, safety seeking, operate on pessimism, low serotonin. **Empowering shift:** *There is hope. Your product gives hope.*

### Belief progression

**Source's documented order of operations:** *fix worthless → helpless fixes itself → hopeless fixes itself.* Worthless is upstream because status-anxiety underlies much of the capability-doubt and hopelessness-spiral downstream. An intervention at the worthless layer resolves a surprising amount of what appears on the surface to be helplessness or hopelessness.

**Market aging principle from source:** *Agora's customers AGED out of HELPLESS to HOPELESS, and became MASSIVELY RISK AVERSE.* The same audience, as it ages, migrates down the limiting-belief stack, which changes the messaging, offer architecture, and risk-reversal requirements.

### Buyer mathematics

- **Irrational buyers** do irrational math: *if / then → if / then → if / then → money.*
- **Rational buyers** do rational math: *if / then → money.*
- **Driver formula:** fear / need / greed.

### Desire + Justification formula

**Equation:** Desire + Justification = ACTION.

**Duality note from source:** the justification part has duality, the *inner-facing hidden motivation* the buyer is not aware of, and the *outer-facing motivation* they are aware of. The inner one is master of the external because inner becomes external.

**Gender differences:** for women, focus on justification (desire is built in because they are more socially aware). For men, focus on desire (they are not socially aware). For married men, create a mechanism of justification for the wife, sell the wife through the guy.

## Building Beliefs: The Three Core Belief-Builders

**Formula from source:** Authority + Evidence/Reference + Repetition = Belief.

**Agreement principle:** *any sales process is a series of agreements.* Progression: agree to problem → agree to solution → agree to product. Optimization question: *what are they disagreeing with?*

### 1. External authority

A generally-accepted expert who excels at their craft. You don't have to explain what it means. If you say *this doctor said so and so,* the chances of argument are very low because the receiver is arguing against the whole system of education.

Examples from source: Dr. Berg, Dr. Jason Fung, Dr. Mercola, Dr. Gundry, Dr. Phil, Dr. Oz, Dr. Joe Dispenza, Dr. Atkins. Strategic note from source: one of the biggest supplement companies on Amazon is "Drs. Best," not "Dr's Best", pluralizing the authority signal.

Religious authority is a special case: *what is God if that's not external authority? We're following the human safety structure of existence.*

Effect: as authority goes up, belief goes up, resistance goes down.

### 2. Compelling reference and evidence

*The mind needs it. If you look at all successful offers, they have compelling reference and evidence. Then the mind cannot argue with it.*

Types: reference (citing the claim) and evidence (visual proof, Thomas Delauer's ripped body example from source). Hierarchy (from source): Authority (social) → Evidence (visual) → Reference → Repetition.

**Compensation principle:** if you don't have enough authority, compensate with compelling evidence and reference.

### 3. Repetition

**Formula:** 3-5x and it sticks. The mind has to hear it from various angles.

**Convincer models:** automatic (hear it once), need to hear it multiple times, over time, from others.

**Strategic application:** repetitions should use the audience's criteria. If the audience is *away-from* (low emotional state), repetition emphasizes away. If *toward* (high emotional state), repetition emphasizes toward.

### Court-system analogy

- Expert witness = Authority
- Trial evidence = what the jury needs to believe you
- Repetition = saying it over and over so it sinks in
- Lawyer's question = *can reasonable minds differ?*

### Hierarchy of winning

Level 1: all things being equal, the authority with more compelling evidence and reference wins. Level 2: if authority + evidence are equal, the one with more repetition wins.

## The Three Levels of Beliefs

**Market context:** skepticism is increasing. Higher skepticism = higher resistance = lower conversion. Production of content is increasing while consumption is decreasing. The cost of production is falling, the cost of distribution is zero, so content floods the ecosystem, which destroys trust.

**Skepticism detection:** *you Google famous people in the niche and type in "scam", that will lead you to the level of skepticism.*

**Matching principle:** *you have to match their level of thinking to get THROUGH their level of thinking.* If the market is skeptical, you have to talk about the things they are skeptical about, you cannot ignore it.

### Level 1, Irrational beliefs

**State:** largely dream-state. 

**Definition:** irrational faith, belief in something someone told you that you don't understand the process or rationality behind.

**Examples from source:** don't walk under a ladder (bad luck), black cat crosses the street (bad luck), Tulip Mania (1600s), Beanie Babies, NFTs, crypto currencies, certain biz-ops.

**Generation mechanism:** irrational faith arises when enough people are telling them the same thing. Sociology at play. *Desire distorts reality, the more desire there is, the more irrational beliefs become.*

**Marketing approach:** talk about how other people are doing it, how everyone else is succeeding, *"everyone is getting rich but you."* Advertorials and flogs generate irrational beliefs.

**Money-making insight:** *the easiest money you'll ever make is when the market is irrational.*

### Level 2, Rational beliefs

**State:** awakening state.

**Definition:** something you cannot know directly on your own, but it makes sense. Can be explained with evidence you have direct experience with.

**Example from source:** you don't know that atoms exist or that electrons exist, yet you use electricity and the concept makes sense.

**Requirement:** to affect rational beliefs, be aware of what direct experiences the audience has.

**Probability principle from source:** *we believe only in things which seem probable to us, though we do not know them. This means that as most thinking and beliefs are in the RATIONAL LEVEL, the rational level and the irrational is all about the PROBABILITY OF TRUTH, NOT THE TRUTH.*

### Level 3, Direct beliefs

**State:** awakened / state of knowing.

**Definition:** things you can actually see, experience, and perceive directly.

**Examples from source:** all infomercials are direct beliefs, *"LOOK AT THIS THING,"* never *"LET ME TELL YOU ABOUT THIS THING."*

**Strategic principle:** irrational beliefs are never good, generate rational beliefs and do our best to turn rational beliefs into direct perception.

**Safety mechanism:** belief systems are safety systems. If you KNOW, the safety system is at 100% functionality.

**Market maturity:** when markets mature, they go full direct beliefs.

### Funnel belief-matching rule

**Critical rule from source:** *what belief channel they come in on the front is what belief channel they go to the back. The great mistake people make is mismatching these in the funnel.*

Directional flow: front-to-back works, back-to-front never works.

Example mistake (verbatim from source): *people will do direct beliefs on the front end and irrational on book-a-call. And then they'll be like "I don't know why people are not showing up to my sales calls."*

## Timeline Language

**Fundamental principle:** marketing is change work. Time is the medium of change. *If you want to become a great marketer / copywriter, then you have to become someone who can change things.*

**Time processing:** the mind processes everything through time. Creating effective structure in copy means using timeline language.

**Timeline language words (source list):** once, when, then, while, since, now, not now, until, before, after, soon, coming, later, never, always, imagine, just before, right after, future, previous, remember, as soon as you, suddenly, by the time, you will.

**Emotions mapped to time:**
- Fear, future-based (can't fear the past)
- Regret, past
- Gain, future
- Anxiety, future
- Sadness, past, present, or future
- Hope, future
- Joy, any tense

**Transition power:** timeline language is mostly used in transitions. Creates good structure in the brain. *In essence we're not trying to create good structure in copy, but rather in the brain.*

### Potentiality scale

Six levels from weakest to strongest commitment:

1. **You COULD do that**, lowest level, most trade-offs. Start with the negative of what else they COULD do.
2. **You MIGHT do that**, conditional on something better existing.
3. **You WOULD do that**, willingness level.
4. **You SHOULD do that**, push marketing. *Should* is a rule they tell themselves. Most people live in the "I should" reality.
5. **You WILL do that**, pull marketing. Will-power shows up here. Decision-making moment.
6. **You ARE doing that**, identity level. Commitment. The future is so compelling they have already moved.

### Excuse language

- *Try* = I did and failed
- *I can't* = I don't want to OR I don't know how
- *Should* = something lacking in me OR a rule I'm violating
- *Wish* = wishy-washy, no solidity
- *Would / Could* = future-paced

## Social Optics and Symbolism

**Fundamental principle:** symbols are generalizations of generalizations of generalizations. They control public opinion. The public can't nuance the details, so they accept the meta-messages.

### Four levels

- **Label**, "you're this or that." Negative symbols. Absorb energy (push us away). Examples: communists, terrorists, extremists. Your name is a label.
- **Logo**, represents a specific brand.
- **Mantra**, verbal symbol. Examples from source: "one funnel away," "just do it," "think different," "because you're worth it," "we try harder," "move fast break things," "I'm loving it," "have it your way," "nothing held back," "eat fresh," "top G," "a computer on every desk."
- **Symbol**, represents an idea beyond words. *A symbol is worth all of the words and it's beyond words.* The 2-Comma Club is a symbol of success in the industry.

**Symbol power from source:** *the one that gives out the award or a symbol, controls the one who receives it.*

### Ritual framework

*Rituals are rite-of-passage to something new, that is the significance of change.* Truth levels: believe it's true (logical) → know it's true (emotional) → experience it's true (spiritual). Rituals are experience-level. *Truth cannot be told with words. It has to be experienced.*

Ritual vs ceremony: ritual is internal, ceremony is external (being given something).

Transformation mechanism: *rituals are designed to change someone THROUGH experience. Rituals change people, when they change, they become converts, and they get conversion.*

### Sales experience vs sales process

Reframe from source: sales should be called a sales experience and a buying experience. Process is *the way it happens*. Experience is *how it happens*. When someone has a bad experience, they avoid repeating it.

## The Three Core Mechanisms

### Mechanism of Motivation, the feedback loop

**Fundamental principle:** *the mechanism of motivation is a feedback loop. If there is NOTHING to validate it, it cannot be true, it just cannot not be true.*

Validation is internal (patterns, beliefs, structures in the mind) plus external (behavior, evidence, reference, repetition in the real world). The external validation is what converts belief into motivation.

**Diet-failure example (verbatim):** most diets fail because feedback loops are poor, the scale does not move fast enough. Keto works because the first week produces a ten-to-fifteen-pound water drop that validates the decision immediately.

**Industry correlation:** every industry with good feedback loops is a big-money industry. Energy drinks, Facebook (post-and-response), Athletic Greens (social proof feedback to others), luxury products (you're not buying the product, you're buying the feedback loop from society).

**Feedback loop types:**
- **Primary**, them doing something: *do this, then this happens.* Example: Double Your Dating's "kiss test."
- **Understanding**, explain something so they can look under the surface and see the feedback.
- **Prediction**, tell them what's coming next in the market.
- **Tertiary**, pull other people as evidence: *look, it worked for this person.*

**Future of marketing principle:** moving from secondary experience (see results, believe, then get results) to primary experience (do it, get results, believe). Try-before-you-buy shift.

### Mechanism of Comprehension

**Fundamental principle:** *for them to consume, the mind has to comprehend, and to comprehend it has to digest. If it cannot comprehend it cannot absorb, and no change will take place.*

**Consumption → Choice → Conversion** is the path. Increase consumption, increase choice, increase conversion.

**Reaction vs response.** Reaction is pure emotion (quick snap-back). Response is thought (time gap). Look for response rates, not reaction rates. This is why VSLs sometimes need to slow down, scrolling is reaction, pausing is response.

**Absorption rate:** the mind can only absorb data at a certain rate. Going too fast means SEEING but not CONSUMING.

**OTO delay strategy from source:** OTO 1 VSLs, delay 8 minutes → conversions up. No delay → conversions down. Scrollability → conversions down.

**Energy balance:** has to be more gain than loss for the mind to consume. Hierarchy of trade: time → attention → emotions → energy → money.

**Choice mechanism:** *they are always trying to get more choice. X, Y, Z options, if it chooses X, it loses Y and Z. So choosing X has to gain GREATER than Y or Z lost.* Close structure: lose one choice, gain 10-20 others. *This is what causes a close to work, the more choices they have, the better they feel.*

**Comparison principle:** *comparison is the seed of comprehension.* Formula: Separate → Compare → Comprehend → Consume → Change → Convert.

**Language patterns:** the offer architect uses *"remember when"* or *"remember how"* to trigger memory retrieval. Metaphors, analogies, and all comparisons are the same mechanism.

### Mechanism of Love

**Fundamental truth:** *the world is pushed and pulled by fear, need, and greed. But what the world wants is love.*

Love vs fear table:
- Love, attracts, creates, gains
- Fear, repels, destroys, loses

**Conditional vs unconditional love:**
- Unconditional, no conditions, no trade-offs. Only true love. God is unconditional love because God created everything and needs nothing.
- Conditional, "I love you IF ___." All human love is conditional. *The more conditions in doing business with us, the less we love our customers.*

**Copy application:** count the conditions in your offer's outcome. More conditions = worse feeling = less love. Conditional structure (bad): *"first you have to, then you have to."* Unconditional structure (good): *"once you ___, then you will ___."*

**Diet example:** "if you want to lose weight, you have to _____ and then _____ and then _____." No one loves to diet because you HAVE TO do a bunch of stuff. Contrast: "you don't have to count calories, just eat delicious meat."

**Opposition strategy:** put conditions on your opposition, remove conditions from your stuff. This shifts love from them to you.
