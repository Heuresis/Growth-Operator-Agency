# Lead Nurture System - The Four Pillars

> **Source:** Alex Hormozi, *$100M Playbook: Lead Nurture - How to get more leads to respond, schedule, and show* (Bumble IP / Acquisition.com, 2025)
> **Frame:** "If they do not show, they cannot buy."

## Origin - ALAN + Vlad (2020)

Hormozi built a software called ALAN (Artificial Lead Automation & Nurture) for brick-and-mortar gyms. COVID closed brick-and-mortar; he pivoted to agencies with a few hundred sub-customers overnight. The product was managing 4,000+ appointments per day - and breaking many of them. He hired Vlad (Russian ML-olympics winner), gave him ~1 million rows of appointment data, and asked what drove show rate.

Vlad came back end-of-week with four correlates. These four became the playbook's Four Pillars.

## The Three Metrics (definitions)

- **Schedule rate** = % of engaged leads who schedule an appointment.
- **Show rate** = % of scheduled leads that show up.
- **Throughput** = % of engaged leads that show up (the product).

Example: 100 leads × 50% schedule rate = 50 appointments × 50% show rate = 25 shows. Throughput = 25%.

A 20-40% increase in throughput typically 2-3×'s annual profit at 20% margins. That's the economic weight of this playbook.

## Pillar I - Availability

> "If leads cannot schedule, they do not show."

Vlad's finding: availability was the **biggest single predictor** of throughput - bigger than speed. "The number of days, hours per day, and time slots per hour, in aggregate, is the biggest predictor."

### Tactics

1. **Take appointments more days per week.** Most operators run 5 days. Going to 7 = 40% more availability. "You pay rent 7 days a week."
2. **Take appointments more hours per day.** For US nationwide: 6am-6pm PST covers the highest share of sales.
3. **Give more flexible appointment slots.** Four per hour (12:00, 12:15, 12:30, 12:45) instead of one or two - awkward gaps get repurposed (see Pillar II).
4. **Have inbound, outbound, and self-scheduling options** - all three simultaneously.

### Online scheduler rules

- Header confirms right place; headline states next action; subheadline says why.
- Available dates and times visible on page load. Mobile and desktop.
- Eliminate steps. If they already gave you info, don't re-ask.

### When to ADD friction

If too many bad appointments: add a video to watch before the scheduler, a sales letter, a price on the page, a delay before the scheduler appears. These decrease volume and increase quality.

## Pillar II - Speed

> "Money loves speed. Wealth loves time. Poverty loves indecision."

### Published statistics (from the playbook)

- 391% increase in sales conversions when leads are contacted within 60 seconds (Velocify).
- 7× more likely to qualify a lead when contacted within 1 hour vs. 1 hour later; 60× vs. 24 hours (Harvard Business Review).
- 78% of customers buy from whoever responds first (Lead Connect).

### Three types of speed

**1. Speed to first contact.** Call within 5 minutes of opt-in. Slower contact requires more contact attempts before conversion. Litmus test: "If you aren't getting 'man that was fast!' at least once a day, you're not calling your leads fast enough."

**2. Speed to first appointment.** Limit scheduling to 3 days out max. Same-day shows higher than not-same-day; immediate (talking now) is 100%.

Five possible outcomes on the confirmation call:
- Doesn't respond → remove from calendar (or double-book if in-person).
- Contacted + unqualified → remove, optional downsell or free value.
- Contacted + qualified + free right now → this is the best outcome. Hot-handoff to closer or close yourself.
- Contacted + qualified + pulled forward → new sooner appointment.
- Contacted + qualified + confirmed as scheduled → baseline.

**3. Speed of response.** Between the schedule and the call. This is where most deals fall through. Every message system has timestamps - use them. Drop in show rates → look here first, especially for a specific rep.

### The Confirmation / Pull-Forward Script

```
Hey, this is Jim calling from XYZ on a recorded line. I saw you just booked a
time for our X on Monday. Just calling to confirm some details before your
appointment. Is now a terrible time?
(WHO) So I see you are a [qualification 1], is that right?
(WHAT THEY WANT) And you're looking to [qualification 2]?
(WHAT THEY'RE STRUGGLING WITH) What have you tried so far?
So this is a priority for you or [bad thing] happens?
Okay great. Two things. First, good news - I think we can help you out.
Second, better news - I have an opening that canceled later today at 4pm for
us to get you all squared away. Unless now is a good time?
```

If they can't pull forward: "We always like to offer more times to make it convenient. We want to cost you as little time as possible. So we're all good for two days from now at 4pm. Is there any reason you think you won't be able to make that appointment?"

### Hot Handoff Script

```
You're in luck. You're actually going to speak with one of our top experts on
X. He's been in this game for a while and helped Y achieve Z. Let me check his
calendar. Yep, I'm gonna slide you in so you can get this taken care of ASAP.
The moment we get off, I'm going to introduce you via text. Does the XYZ
number work? Great. Enjoy your time with CLOSER.
```

## Pillar III - Personalization

> "Personalize, don't pressure."

Origin: Hormozi's beta tester had wildly higher show rates for no visible reason. Dug in. Found the owner was texting leads from a separate iPhone: "Hey, I've got a shirt here waiting for you. Do you prefer black or pink?" As soon as the prospect picked a color, they became a near-guaranteed show.

### Six tactics

**1. Use the lead's preferred communication method.** Initiate on all channels (call, text, DM, email). Continue where they respond. Think about it like reaching your parents in an emergency - you'd use every channel.

**2. Qualify the leads.** Cancel sales appointments with obvious bad fits. A weight-loss operator Hormozi profiled actively canceled bad sales appointments and ran way better margins than competitors.

**3. Send best leads to best closers.** 5-step implementation:
- Look at your best customers. Actions + demographics.
- Ask those questions in opt-ins/applications.
- Score leads 1-5 or red/yellow/green.
- Route best leads to best closers.
- Repeat.

Origin story: Hormozi met the #1 sales rep for the #1 timeshare company in the US, finished 1st in 5 of 6 years, convinced the CEO to give him the best leads. Local office 5×'d production, rolled out nationwide, company went from $200M to $1B over 5 years.

**4. Segment messaging.** HubSpot's 7× email ROI improvement cited as benchmark. Two modes:
- *Easy:* 5 minutes of research per lead before contact.
- *Hard:* pre-built email/text sequences per segment.

**5. Incentives - push vs. pull.**
- *Push* (gift card, gifted before appointment to create reciprocity): "Since you opted in, I'm mailing you a $5 coffee gift card. Check your email." They get it whether they show or not.
- *Pull* (A/B offer assumes they'll show): "Do you want one bottle or two?" "Morning or afternoon?" "Chocolate or vanilla?" The choice itself commits them. A/B incentives depend on three things: (a) they want it, (b) it has obvious connection to the offering, (c) you incurred real cost preparing it.

**6. Proof.** Weave into messaging. Match demographics to demographics. Older white male lead → show results of older white male clients. Integration points:
- After schedule, before show.
- Between first and second call.
- If they ghost: every 90 days show people who signed up about when they ghosted. Makes them feel left behind but preserves the option.

### Pro tips from the playbook

- **iPhones for texting.** Blue-bubble messages get higher response rates than green.
- **Local area code call wrapping.** Dynamic area-code switching to match the prospect's region - "significantly higher pickup rates." [UNVERIFIED - no specific figure cited.]

## Pillar IV - Volume

> "Volume negates luck."

Nearly half of all salespeople give up after the first attempt. This is the volume opportunity.

### Three volume types

**1. Scheduling appointments.** The 8-step cadence from the playbook:

```
1. Call within 5 min of opt-in.
2. Double-dial (first call often blocked; second gets through).
3. If nothing → voicemail.
4. Text immediately after voicemail.
5. Two more double-dial-plus-text attempts that day (hours apart).
6. Call 2× per day for next 2 days (morning + later). Text after second.
7. Call + text once a day for next 4 days.
8. After week 1 → long-term nurture (free value, soft CTA).
```

The more days that pass, the less likely they schedule. Front-load the attempts.

**2. Reminding them to show.** Automated reminder sequence:

```
Immediately after schedule - automatic confirmation (time, date, number, rep).
24 hours before.
12 hours before.
3 hours before.
```

Plus manual reminders (from a real cell phone, not automated): night before, morning of, hour before. Add the area code you'll be calling from (2× pickup rate). Use the lead's local time zone.

**3. BAMFAM - Book A Meeting From A Meeting.** Credit: Sharran Srivatsaa.

> "NEVER get off the call with 'We'll circle back later' or 'I'll follow up and propose some times.' You're both there. Right now. Pull up your calendar. Pick a time."

Internally at Acquisition.com: "BAMFAM as a way of life." Someone being dodgy about their next call is an objection - handle it.

## The Culture Layer

The playbook's closing chapter is about execution as culture. Hormozi's rep Jacob (hired at 15) became top salesman out of 26 by taking every "garbage" lead the other reps rejected. Coined "Yellows are the new gold" and "Volume negates luck." These -isms permeate the team.

Cultural reinforcements:
- **Money:** small commission bump for show-up rates (5-30% of sale commission).
- **Non-money:** attention, affection, approval. Public kudos for highest show rates. The recipient always says "I just followed the process" - which gets more approval.

## The Checklist (condensed)

- Open 7 days/week, widest hours your market allows.
- 4 time slots per hour.
- Inbound + outbound + self-scheduling all active.
- Contact within 5 minutes.
- Schedule within 72 hours.
- Initiate across all channels; continue on the first one that replies.
- Qualify via application.
- Route best → best.
- Personalize per segment.
- Send a gift (push) or force an A/B (pull).
- Weave proof into messaging.
- 7-8 reach-out attempts minimum.
- Automated + manual reminders.
- BAMFAM every call.

## Anti-patterns

- **Giving up after 1-2 attempts.** 44% of salespeople do this (InsideSales/RAIN Group, cited in the playbook). This is where leads evaporate.
- **Open-ended scheduling ("whenever works").** Schedule > 3 days out = ghosting risk spikes.
- **One-channel initiation.** Only texting (or only calling) caps your response rate.
- **Treating availability cost as fixed.** "I can't open more hours" usually means "I don't want to." The ROI math says otherwise.
- **Circling back later.** This is where BAMFAM violations cost money.
