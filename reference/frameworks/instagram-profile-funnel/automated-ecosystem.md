# Automated IG Ecosystem

> **Why this matters:** An Instagram profile with no automation layer bleeds intent. Someone comments, DMs, or watches a story at a moment of high interest — and if there's no capture mechanism within minutes, the intent cools. The automated ecosystem is the plumbing that converts that intent into a qualified lead or booked call while it's still warm.

## Source / Lineage

the paid media director — practitioner who built his body of work around turning an IG profile into a high-ticket lead machine for coaches selling their own offer. The ecosystem pattern below reflects his five-playbook system, adapted for a single operator promoting their own flagship offer — not an agency servicing external brands.

## Scope Guard (INV-8)

**This framework applies when the operator is promoting their OWN coaching / consulting offer via their personal IG profile.** It does NOT apply to:

- Agencies providing IG growth services for external clients (separate workspace).
- E-commerce brand accounts.
- Multi-author content brands where no single human is the face.

The ecosystem assumes: one human profile, one flagship offer, coach/consultant avatar, book-a-call or application motion.

## The Five Automation Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Comment-to-DM Triggers

**What it is:** a trigger word or emoji in a comment on a post or reel opens a DM from the account to the commenter, delivered via an automation tool.

**Tool choices:**
- ManyChat (most common for IG, native Meta integration).
- ManyChat alternatives: Respond.io, Chatfuel. All three work; ManyChat has the largest template library.

**Standard flow:**
1. Post or reel CTA: "Comment WORKBOOK and I'll send it to you."
2. Commenter comments "WORKBOOK."
3. Automation sends a DM within 60 seconds: "Hey [first name], here's the workbook you asked for: [link]. One quick question — are you currently running [X] or [Y]? Just curious which one to send you next."
4. The question is a qualifier. Response routes into a nurture tag or a "hot lead" tag.

**Metrics / targets:**
- Comment-to-DM-sent rate: 85%+ (the other 15% are users with DM-closed privacy settings or bots that get filtered).
- DM-opened rate: 70–85%.
- Qualifier-response rate: 20–35%.
- Qualifier-to-call-booked rate: 8–15% within 7 days.

**Guardrails:** do not over-automate the first reply. A single generic "Thanks!" kills the sequence. The first automated message must carry real value (the promised asset) plus one natural question.

### Pillar 2 — Story Highlight Stack

**What it is:** a curated set of 6–10 highlight covers on the profile that serve as an evergreen navigation layer. Each highlight answers one question or stage of buyer awareness.

**Standard stack (left to right on the profile):**
1. **Start here** — 3–5 stories introducing the operator and the avatar.
2. **The problem** — 5–8 stories naming the pain in customer language.
3. **The method** — 5–8 stories walking through the framework.
4. **Proof** — 10+ stories of customer wins, rotated quarterly.
5. **FAQ** — 5–8 stories answering the top objections.
6. **Behind the scenes** — 5–10 stories humanising the operator.
7. **Book a call** — 1–3 stories pointing to the application link with a swipe-up or link sticker.

**Metrics / targets:**
- Highlight tap-through rate from profile visit: 15–25%.
- Proof highlight completion rate: 40%+ (it's the highest-intent highlight).
- Link clicks from "Book a call" highlight: 1–3% of profile visits.

**Maintenance cadence:** refresh proof highlight monthly, rebuild the whole stack quarterly. Stale highlights (6+ months old) signal to the viewer that the account isn't active.

### Pillar 3 — Reel-to-DM Flow

**What it is:** reels are the top-of-funnel volume play. The reel-to-DM flow converts viewers into a DM conversation without requiring a landing page or opt-in.

**Standard flow:**
1. Reel with a clear value hook (problem named + partial solution shown).
2. On-screen text and caption CTA: "DM me CALLS for the full breakdown."
3. Viewer DMs the keyword.
4. ManyChat (or equivalent) responds in 60 seconds with the promised asset + a qualifying question.
5. Human picks up the conversation within 2–4 hours for qualified responses.

**Metrics / targets:**
- Reel-view-to-DM-sent rate: 0.1–0.5% (sounds small, but on a 100k-view reel, that's 100–500 DMs).
- DM-to-qualified rate: 20–30%.
- Qualified-to-booked-call rate: 10–20%.

**Design note:** the keyword should be a single word, easy to type, not confusable with normal conversation ("CALLS," "GUIDE," "AUDIT" all work; "INFO," "MORE," "HEY" do not — too much normal-conversation collision).

### Pillar 4 — Link-in-Bio Funnel

**What it is:** the single link on the profile routes to a custom page (not a link tree of 12 options). The page has one primary CTA and 2–3 supporting secondary CTAs.

**Tool choices:**
- Custom landing page (hosted on the operator's domain) — preferred. Use Framer, Webflow, Carrd.
- Beacons / Stan Store / Koji — acceptable if the operator has no site yet.
- Linktree — serviceable but caps customisation; most operators outgrow it by $500k revenue.

**Standard page structure:**
1. Headline + sub-headline matching the IG bio.
2. Primary CTA: "Book a discovery call" (or the equivalent application entry).
3. Secondary CTAs: lead magnet download, case study, testimonial reel.
4. Social proof: logos of outlets + 3 short customer quotes.
5. Footer: secondary contact, secondary socials.

**Metrics / targets:**
- Profile-visit-to-link-click rate: 3–7%.
- Link-click-to-primary-CTA-click rate: 8–15%.
- Primary-CTA-click-to-application-submit rate: 40–60%.
- End-to-end profile-visit-to-application rate: 0.2–0.6%.

### Pillar 5 — DM Automation Sequences

**What it is:** a longer-form DM sequence that nurtures leads who expressed interest but didn't book. Runs in the DM channel because open rates beat email 3–5x.

**Standard 5-touch sequence (over 14 days):**

| Day | Message type | Purpose |
|-----|--------------|---------|
| 0 | Asset delivery + qualifier | Immediate, automated. |
| 2 | Customer story | Social proof, keeps the conversation open. |
| 5 | Framework snippet | Value-first teaching, no pitch. |
| 9 | Direct invitation | "If you want to go deeper, here's the call link." |
| 14 | Soft close | "Not ready? No worries — here's a free resource. I'll stop the sequence now." |

**Metrics / targets:**
- Sequence-completion rate: 60–75% (higher than email because DMs are one-on-one).
- Sequence-to-call-booked rate: 5–12% across the full 14 days.
- Opt-out rate: <5% (if higher, messages are too pitchy).

**Compliance:** Meta has active limits on automated DM volume. Keep sequences under 6 messages and under 100 messages/day per account to stay under policy.

## Tool Stack (Representative)

| Function | Tool | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| DM automation | ManyChat | Most common; native IG integration. |
| Landing page | Framer / Webflow / Carrd | Custom domain preferred. |
| Scheduling | Calendly / SavvyCal / Cal.com | Cal.com for team handoffs. |
| CRM | HubSpot / Go High Level / Close | GHL common in coaching; HubSpot for $2M+ teams. |
| Analytics | Native IG + landing-page analytics + CRM source fields | Tag every channel; otherwise attribution breaks. |

Do not over-stack. A coach at $500k–$1M usually runs: ManyChat + Framer + Calendly + one CRM. Everything else is premature.

## Implementation Order

1. Week 1: set up ManyChat, build 3 keyword flows (one per pillar content type).
2. Week 2: rebuild the highlight stack.
3. Week 3: ship the custom bio-link landing page.
4. Week 4: build the 5-touch DM sequence.
5. Week 5: layer in attribution tagging (which pillar is producing which lead).
6. Week 6+: iterate on the weakest pillar quarterly.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- Operators without a defined offer yet — build the offer first; automation amplifies clarity but cannot create it.
- Accounts with <5k followers — volume is too low for automation ROI; go manual until 5k+.
- Niches where IG is not the primary channel (B2B SaaS procurement, enterprise sales) — LinkedIn or email is a better host channel.
