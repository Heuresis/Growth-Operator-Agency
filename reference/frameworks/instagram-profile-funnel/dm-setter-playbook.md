# DM Setter Playbook

> **Why this matters:** Automation gets the DM started. A human setter is what actually books calls. The setter role is the highest-ROI first hire in a coaching business — they convert warm DMs into held sales calls at 3–5× the rate of founder-only handling, because the founder isn't checking DMs at 9pm on a Friday.

## Source / Lineage

the paid media director — IG profile funnel architect. The setter playbook below is the standard pattern across coaching businesses at $30k–$200k MRR where IG is the primary acquisition channel. It assumes a book-a-call sales motion and a closer (human, often the founder) who handles the actual enrolment call.

## Scope Guard (INV-8)

**For operators promoting their OWN offer through their OWN IG profile.** The setter is an internal role (part-time contractor or full-time staff) working for the operator — not an external agency service sold to clients.

## Role Definition

### Who the setter is NOT

- Not the closer. The setter does not pitch price, handle objections about the offer, or run the sales call.
- Not a content creator. They do not post on the account.
- Not a customer-success manager. They do not handle post-sale onboarding.

### Who the setter IS

- A trained inbox operator whose only job is: triage inbound DMs → qualify → book call → confirm show-up.
- First human touch after automation. Represents the brand voice in writing.
- Responsible for a booking pipeline, measured weekly.

## Daily Cadence

| Time block | Activity | Duration |
|------------|----------|----------|
| Morning (1 hr) | Triage overnight DMs. Reply to hot leads first, warm second, cold last. | 60 min |
| Midday (45 min) | Outbound follow-ups. 15–25 messages to leads from the last 14 days who haven't booked. | 45 min |
| Afternoon (1 hr) | New DM qualification. Handle replies from morning messages. Book calls. | 60 min |
| Late afternoon (30 min) | Call confirmations. SMS + DM reminders for tomorrow's calls. | 30 min |
| End of day (15 min) | Log activity. Update CRM. Note hot leads for the closer. | 15 min |

**Total: ~3.5 hours/day.** A part-time setter handles one operator at this cadence. A full-time setter can handle two operators or one operator with >100k followers.

## Volume Targets (Weekly)

| Metric | Target band |
|--------|-------------|
| Inbound DMs handled | 75–150 |
| Outbound follow-ups sent | 75–125 |
| Qualifiers passed | 20–40 |
| Calls booked | 8–20 |
| Calls held (after confirmation) | 6–15 |
| Calls no-show | <30% of booked |
| Calls that closed | depends on closer — setter is measured up to held-call |

## The Five-Message Qualifying Framework

Not every DM deserves a call invitation. The setter's job is to qualify before booking. Calendar space is finite; unqualified calls burn the closer's capacity.

### Message 1 — Opener (if the lead came from automation, skip this)

**Context:** lead replied to a reel CTA or sent a cold DM.

**Template:**
> "Hey [first name] — thanks for reaching out. Before I send anything, quick question — what made you DM? Are you running into [pain A] or more of [pain B]?"

**Purpose:** surface the actual pain in the lead's own words, so the qualification is grounded.

### Message 2 — Qualify current state

**Template:**
> "Got it. And right now, what does [relevant metric] look like for you? Like, ballpark — are you at [tier 1], [tier 2], or [tier 3]?"

**Purpose:** get a rough tier of fit. Coaches at $10k MRR and coaches at $100k MRR need different offers; knowing which tier the lead is in routes the conversation.

### Message 3 — Qualify timing and budget (indirect)

**Template:**
> "Makes sense. Last question before I point you in the right direction — are you looking to fix this in the next 90 days, or is it more of a 'next year' thing? No wrong answer, just helps me know what to send."

**Purpose:** timing filter. The "no wrong answer" framing keeps the lead honest without feeling cornered. Budget qualification happens implicitly — people who say "next 90 days" are operationally ready to spend.

### Message 4 — Offer the call

**Template (for qualified lead):**
> "Cool — based on what you've said, the fastest path for you is probably a quick call with [founder first name]. 20 minutes, no pitch, just a look at where you're at and whether it makes sense to work together. Here's his calendar: [link]. Want to grab a slot?"

**Template (for unqualified lead):**
> "Based on where you're at, a call wouldn't be the best use of your time yet. Here's a free resource that'll help you get to the next stage: [link]. When you're at [milestone], DM me again and we'll revisit."

**Purpose:** qualified leads get routed to the calendar; unqualified leads get routed to nurture (not ghosted — ghosting degrades brand long-term).

### Message 5 — Confirm the call

**Template (sent within 2 hours of booking):**
> "Locked in for [day] at [time] [timezone]. Before the call, [founder] asked if you could watch this 3-minute video — it'll make the call way more useful: [link]. See you [day]."

**Purpose:** pre-call asset + confirmation. Show rates jump 15–25 points with this step.

## Call Confirmation Sequence

Beyond the booking, the setter runs confirmations to protect show rate.

| Timing | Channel | Content |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Booking confirmation | DM + email (via CRM) | Booking confirmed + calendar invite + pre-call video. |
| 24 hours before | SMS (if opted in) + DM | "Looking forward to tomorrow at [time]. Anything you want to make sure we cover?" |
| 1 hour before | SMS + DM | "Hey — we're on in an hour. Zoom link: [link]. See you soon." |
| 5 min before | SMS | "Jumping on now — here's the link one more time: [link]." |

Show rate without this sequence: 50–65%. With it: 75–85%.

## Handoff to Closer

A clean handoff is the second-biggest lever on close rate after the call itself.

### Pre-call handoff note (setter → closer, written before the call)

```
Lead: [first name, last name]
Source: [reel / post / cold DM / referral]
Profile: [IG handle or link]
Current state (in their words): "[direct quote]"
What they want: "[direct quote]"
Timing: [90 days / next year / unclear]
Budget signal: [implicit tier based on current state]
Qualifier: [what the setter qualified them on]
Objections pre-surfaced: [any hesitation, price mentions, comparisons]
Pre-call video watched: [yes / no / unknown]
Setter note: [one-line read on energy, readiness]
```

A closer walking into a call with this note runs 1.3–1.8× the close rate of one walking in blind.

## Post-Call Follow-Up

The setter owns post-call follow-up when the closer hasn't closed in the room.

| Timing | Message purpose |
|--------|-----------------|
| T+2 hours | "Thanks for the call — here's the proposal [founder] mentioned: [link]." |
| T+24 hours | "Any questions come up on that after the call?" |
| T+72 hours | "Just checking in — were you leaning in or leaning out?" |
| T+7 days | "Wanted to close the loop — if now's not the right time, no worries. Here's the next step we talked about: [link]." |
| T+14 days (if still open) | Soft close: "Marking this one as 'not right now' — DM me anytime if things change." |

## Compliance and Brand Voice

- **Voice match:** the setter writes in the founder's voice, using the founder's cadence, abbreviations, and emoji usage (or lack of). A style guide is maintained and updated quarterly.
- **No hard-sell:** setters do not close. Pitching a price in DMs is out of scope and usually damages conversion.
- **Meta policy:** do not use forbidden words (engagement-bait triggers), do not send more than 100 DMs/day per account, do not use URL shorteners that flag as spam.
- **Disclosure:** if the setter is not the founder, no active misrepresentation. Most operators run the setter in third person ("[Founder] will be on the call with you") to avoid ambiguity.

## Weekly Setter Review (30 min, with founder or ops lead)

1. Volume: DMs handled, outbound, qualifiers, bookings, holds.
2. Quality: objections heard this week, patterns, stuck leads.
3. Conversion ratios vs. target bands.
4. Top 3 lessons for the week.
5. Script or template updates.
6. Blockers (tooling, CRM, approval).

## Hiring Profile

Good setters share five traits:
1. Written communication (can match brand voice in 2–3 weeks).
2. Empathy without mirroring — they hold a frame.
3. Process tolerance — they follow the script and improve it, rather than winging it.
4. Availability in the founder's peak DM window (typically late afternoon + evening in founder's timezone).
5. 5–15 hrs/week to start. Scale to full-time by ~$80k MRR if volume justifies.

Compensation: $1k–$3k/month base + $25–75 per booked-and-held call. Avoid commission on closes — misaligns setter with closer.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- Pre-$30k MRR — founder should still be handling DMs to learn the objections firsthand.
- Text-light channels (YouTube, podcast) where the inbound motion is different.
- Enterprise sales with procurement cycles — no DM setter motion applies.
