# Content Studio Model

> **Why this matters:** Coaches burn out on content because they produce daily. The studio model flips that — produce in concentrated bursts, distribute across formats, repeat. One studio day can output two weeks of content. Without this model, content becomes a second job; with it, content becomes infrastructure.

Fix the cadence, the content follows.

## Scope Guard (INV-8)

**Applies when the operator is producing content about their OWN offer, for their OWN IG profile.** Not a framework for content agencies producing for clients. The assumption is the operator is the on-camera talent and the subject-matter expert — both roles in one human.

## Core Principles

1. **Batch produce, drip distribute.** One day of concentrated recording → two weeks of published content.
2. **One pillar idea, many formats.** A single long-form thought repurposes down the cascade rather than being re-invented per format.
3. **Cadence beats novelty.** The audience needs rhythm more than it needs surprise. Same time, same format, same studio day, week after week.
4. **Edit for format, don't re-script.** Repurposing is an editing job, not a writing job. If it requires re-scripting, the long-form wasn't structured well enough.

## The Repurposing Cascade

One long-form asset produces, on average:

| Cascade level | Output | Time to produce (from the source) | Where it lives |
|---------------|--------|-----------------------------------|----------------|
| Source | 1 long-form video (30–60 min) or deep essay (2–4k words) | Recorded in studio day. | YouTube, podcast, newsletter, blog. |
| Level 1 | 3 reels / short-form videos (30–90 sec each) | 1–2 hrs / reel in editing. | IG reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts. |
| Level 2 | 5 story sequences (3–8 story slides each) | 30 min / sequence. | IG stories, FB stories. |
| Level 3 | 1 carousel post (7–10 slides) | 2–3 hrs. | IG carousel, LinkedIn carousel. |
| Level 4 | 1 feed post (image + caption, 300–600 words) | 1 hr. | IG feed, LinkedIn, X thread. |
| Level 5 | 1 email (400–800 words) | 1 hr. | Email list. |

**Total from one source: 12 distinct pieces of distribution.** Two source pieces per week → ~24 distribution units per week, which is enough to run every IG surface plus email, YouTube, and one secondary platform.

## Studio Day Playbook

### Pre-studio (1–2 days before)

1. **Topic list finalised.** 4–6 topics for the day, each with a one-sentence hook and a three-bullet outline.
2. **Hook bank open.** Pull 3 hooks per topic from the hook bank (maintained over months from trending reels, customer DMs, high-performing historical posts).
3. **Wardrobe + set prepped.** Two outfits minimum to signal different days in the edit. Clean background, tested lighting, tested audio.
4. **Equipment checked.** Mic, camera, prompter, backup recorder. 20 min the night before saves a day.

### Studio day (one full day, 6–8 hours)

1. **Block 1 (morning, 2 hrs):** long-form recording. One 30–60 min video or two 20–30 min videos. This is the source-asset block. Do it first, when energy is highest.
2. **Block 2 (mid-morning, 1.5 hrs):** short-form recording. 8–12 reels. Half are cut-downs of the long-form, half are standalone hooks. Outfit change for variety.
3. **Block 3 (afternoon, 1.5 hrs):** story-piece recording. 5–8 story sequences, shot vertically. Lower-production, more conversational.
4. **Block 4 (late afternoon, 1.5 hrs):** written and carousel production. Operator writes email draft, post captions, carousel slide copy while the energy from recording is still fresh.
5. **Wrap (30 min):** back up raw footage, hand off to editor with a brief per asset.

### Post-studio (days 1–5 after)

1. **Edit.** Editor produces rough cuts for all short-form within 48 hours.
2. **Review pass.** Operator reviews, approves or requests one round of edits. No second round — edit discipline starts on the brief.
3. **Schedule.** All content scheduled into the next 14 days across platforms.
4. **Debrief.** 15-minute note-to-self on what worked in the studio day, adjusted for next time.

## Per-Format Cadence

The studio model outputs this distribution schedule:

| Format | Cadence | Per week | Purpose |
|--------|---------|----------|---------|
| Reels / short-form | Daily | 7 | Top-of-funnel reach. |
| Stories | 3–5 per day | 25+ | Retention, depth, humanisation. |
| Carousel | 2× per week | 2 | Saves, education, authority. |
| Feed post (single image) | 2× per week | 2 | Anchor, community, conversation. |
| Long-form video (YouTube) | 1× per week | 1 | Deep trust, search discovery. |
| Email | 2× per week | 2 | Direct, owned channel. |
| Podcast | 1× per week (optional) | 0–1 | Repurpose of long-form. |

Two studio days per month is the minimum. One every two weeks produces the cadence above without operator burnout.

## Hook Bank Maintenance

A hook is the first 3 seconds of a reel or the first line of a post. Hook quality is the single biggest lever on reel performance.

- Maintain a running hook bank (spreadsheet or Notion doc) with 100+ hooks classified by: (a) pattern (question, bold claim, contrarian, curiosity gap, numbered), (b) performance tier (A / B / C), (c) source (own post, competitor, inspiration).
- Add 5–10 hooks per week. Review and re-tier monthly.
- Before every studio day, pull 3 hooks per topic from the bank. Do not improvise hooks on camera — they're the one thing worth scripting.

## Metrics That Matter (Content-Layer)

| Metric | Target band | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Reel avg. view count | 3–5× follower count when working | Format reach health. |
| Reel 3-sec retention | 60–75% | Hook quality. |
| Reel full-video retention | 35–55% | Body + payoff quality. |
| Carousel save rate | 3–6% of reach | Education value. |
| Story completion rate | 65–80% | Sequencing + first-slide hook. |
| Email open rate | 35–50% | List health + subject line. |
| Email click rate | 4–8% | Content-to-offer alignment. |

If any metric drops below band for 3 weeks, the studio output for that format gets a structural review — not just a topic change.

## Editor Brief Template

Handed to the editor after studio day, one per short-form asset:

```
Asset ID: [Date-Topic-01]
Source: [long-form video timestamp range OR standalone]
Target format: Reel 30-45 sec
Hook (first 3 sec): [exact line]
Body (sec 4-25): [key point + supporting clip]
Payoff (sec 26-end): [CTA or punchline]
CTA: [Comment KEYWORD / DM KEYWORD / Save this / Follow]
Caption: [first-line hook + 2-3 sentences + CTA]
B-roll: [list of inserts, or "none"]
Music: [mood + tempo, or "none"]
Deadline: [48 hrs from studio day]
```

Editor discipline is the hidden variable. A mediocre editor with a great brief ships better content than a great editor with a fuzzy one.

## Studio Variants

- **Solo studio:** operator + tripod + one editor. Minimum viable. $500–$1,500/month editor cost.
- **Partnered studio:** operator + videographer + editor. Higher production quality, 3–5× output. $3k–$8k/month.
- **In-house studio:** dedicated room + full team. Makes sense at $1.5M+ ARR, rarely before.

Start solo. Move to partnered when consistency has held for 6 months and the business can absorb the cost without straining the 60/30/10 mix.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- Operators producing less than 2 reels / week — the studio overhead isn't justified; go lean-weekly instead.
- Operators with no existing audience signal — produce smaller batches while testing what resonates.
- Faceless accounts or multi-author brands — studio model assumes one on-camera human.
