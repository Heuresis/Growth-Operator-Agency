# YouTube Planner (IG-to-YouTube Bridge)

> **Why this matters:** IG produces attention; YouTube produces trust. The two platforms compound differently, and operators who build them in parallel (not sequentially) compress their trust curve by 6–12 months. The planner below is the bridge — how to use IG for awareness and YouTube for deep conviction, without doubling the content workload.

## Source / Lineage

the paid media director — IG profile funnel architect. The YouTube bridge is the outer layer of the profile funnel: IG handles the top-of-funnel capture, YouTube handles mid-funnel trust and search discovery. the paid media director'ss take is that coaches who use YouTube as "just another content platform" miss the point — it's a different funnel with different economics.

## Scope Guard (INV-8)

**For the coach promoting their OWN offer on their OWN YouTube channel, using IG as the adjacent platform.** Not a YouTube-agency playbook, not a YouTube shorts-farm strategy, not a faceless-channel monetisation play.

## IG vs. YouTube — Different Economics

| Dimension | Instagram | YouTube |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Content half-life | 24–72 hours for most posts; 1–2 weeks for top reels. | 6–24 months for main videos; some evergreen for years. |
| Primary discovery | Feed algorithm + explore. | Search + suggested + browse. |
| Viewer intent | Passive scroll. | Active search (often problem-specific). |
| Watch time per user | 30–90 sec per asset. | 8–20 min per video. |
| Trust-build rate | Slow (many touches, shallow depth). | Fast (fewer touches, high depth). |
| Best for | Awareness, DM conversation, top-of-funnel capture. | Mid-funnel trust, case-study depth, search discovery. |
| Subscriber monetisation | Follower value ~$0.50–$3 / follower for coaches. | Subscriber value ~$3–$15 / subscriber for coaches. |

**Key insight:** a YouTube subscriber is worth 3–10× an IG follower for high-ticket coaches, because they self-selected into long-form deep content. They've demonstrated the attention span and intent that correlates with high-ticket buying.

## Role of YouTube in the Profile Funnel

YouTube is not a standalone funnel. It's the depth layer within the IG-primary system.

```
IG content (awareness, volume)
        │
        ├─→ "For the full breakdown, my YouTube video covers it" ──→ YouTube
        │
IG DM conversation (mid-funnel)
        │
        ├─→ "I did a 30-min video on exactly this, here's the link" ──→ YouTube
        │
YouTube deep trust
        │
        └─→ "Book a call" in video + description ──→ Application
```

## Cadence and Production

### Frequency

- **Starting:** 1 video / week. Consistent for 6 months minimum. Breaking cadence is the #1 reason channels stall.
- **Stable:** 1 video / week + occasional 2nd shorter video.
- **Scaled:** 1–2 videos / week + Shorts cross-posted from IG reels.

### Length

- **Main videos:** 12–25 minutes. This is the sweet spot for coaching content in 2025–2026 — long enough for depth, short enough for completion.
- **Short videos (optional 2nd slot):** 6–10 minutes for tactical topics.
- **Shorts:** 30–60 sec, cross-posted from IG reels.

### Production flow

- Main YouTube video is recorded during the monthly studio day (see `content-studio.md`) as the source asset.
- The studio-day cascade produces: 1 YouTube video → 3 reels → 1 carousel → 1 email. The YouTube video is the parent; everything else is repurposed child.
- Thumbnails are produced by a specialist. CTR on the thumbnail is the single biggest lever on YouTube growth.

## Topic Strategy

Two topic buckets, roughly 60 / 40 split:

### Bucket A (60%) — Search-driven

Topics a prospect would search when they have the exact problem the offer solves. Evergreen, keyword-anchored, case-heavy.

**Examples (for a $5k sales-coaching offer):**
- "How to book more sales calls on Instagram in 2026"
- "5 objections I hear on every high-ticket sales call"
- "Why coaching funnels stall at $50k/month (and how to fix it)"

These videos do the compound work — they accumulate views over months and bring search-ranked leads in cold.

### Bucket B (40%) — Brand-driven

Topics that build the operator's voice and point of view. Opinion pieces, frameworks, behind-the-scenes, contrarian takes.

**Examples:**
- "Why I don't run ads (and what I do instead)"
- "The real reason most coaches don't scale past $1M"
- "What changed in my business after [pivotal moment]"

These videos deepen trust with existing followers and convert lurkers into callers.

## Per-Video Structure

1. **Hook (0–30 sec):** the problem named + the promise of the video.
2. **Payoff preview (30–60 sec):** what they'll walk away with.
3. **Context / credentials (60–90 sec):** why this operator can teach this.
4. **Body (90 sec – 18 min):** the actual teaching, broken into 3–5 beats. Each beat has: framework, example, action.
5. **CTA integrated (19–22 min):** soft mention of the offer, book-a-call, or next resource — not a hard sell.
6. **Close (22–25 min):** summary, reflection prompt, subscribe ask.

Place the book-a-call link in: pinned comment, description (first line), and the end-screen card. Three placements, one link.

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target band | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Click-through rate (CTR) on thumbnail | 5–10% (cold audience); 10–15% (subscribed) | Thumbnail + title quality. |
| Avg. view duration | 45–60% of video length | Hook + pacing quality. |
| Avg. views per video (first 30 days) | 5–20% of subscriber count when healthy | Algo delivery health. |
| Subscriber growth rate | 5–15% month-over-month in year 1 | Channel momentum. |
| Traffic from search | 25–40% of views by month 6 | Evergreen health (Bucket A working). |
| YouTube → application rate | 0.1–0.3% of video views | End-to-end funnel health. |

If CTR drops below 4% on cold audience for 3 videos in a row, the thumbnail / title pattern is broken — fix before shipping the next one.

## Subscribers Monetise Differently

IG follower behaviour: follow on impulse, consume for months or years before acting (or never).

YouTube subscriber behaviour: subscribe with intent after 10+ minutes of watching. They already trust, already engaged deeply, already self-selected.

**Implications for the operator:**
1. YouTube descriptions and pinned comments should always have the application link.
2. YouTube subscribers should be invited to a separate, higher-value email sequence than IG followers (they're hotter).
3. When running a launch, YouTube subscribers often convert at 2–5× the rate of IG followers on the same email.
4. YouTube is where the operator ships their signature framework (single long-form, 45–60 min) once a year. That one asset compounds.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Treating YouTube like IG.** Short clips, high volume, low depth. Doesn't work on YouTube; the algorithm punishes shallow.
- **Inconsistent cadence.** Posting 3 videos in one week, then nothing for a month. Channel stalls; algorithm deprioritises.
- **Weak thumbnails.** Operator produces thumbnails themselves because it feels like a $30 job. It is the single biggest growth lever. Invest in a specialist.
- **No calls-to-action.** Operator records 20-min videos with no application link anywhere. Trust builds, but doesn't convert.
- **YouTube as an afterthought.** Repurposing IG captions as YouTube scripts produces shallow videos. YouTube deserves its own outline.

## Relationship with IG Content Calendar

YouTube and IG aren't competing — they compound. The weekly cadence looks like this:

| Day | Platform | Asset type |
|-----|----------|------------|
| Mon | IG | Reel + stories |
| Tue | YouTube | Main video drop |
| Tue | IG | Reel teasing the YouTube video |
| Wed | IG | Carousel (from YouTube transcript) |
| Wed | Email | Newsletter linking YouTube video |
| Thu | IG | Reel + stories |
| Fri | IG | Feed post + stories |
| Sat | IG | Reel (repurposed from older YouTube) |
| Sun | IG | Rest / behind-the-scenes story |

The YouTube drop anchors the week. IG orbits around it.

## When to Start YouTube

- **Start when:** IG audience has crossed 10k engaged followers AND there's a defined offer that's converting AND the operator can commit to 6 months of weekly uploads.
- **Do not start when:** IG is still in the "figuring out the offer" phase. YouTube doesn't save a weak offer; it amplifies whatever exists.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- Pre-offer coaches still testing positioning — too early for YouTube investment.
- Faceless or anonymous operators — YouTube rewards on-camera presence.
- Markets with no search demand (hyper-niche, local service, or brand-new categories) — rely on IG / referral until search demand exists.
