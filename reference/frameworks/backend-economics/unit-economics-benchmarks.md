# Unit Economics Scorecard — Info-Product YouTube Funnels

> **Why this matters:** At 8-figure scale, conversion rate stops being a useful primary metric. Two funnels with identical conversion rates can have 5x different profitability depending on revenue-per-lead, cohort LTV, and per-source LTV:CAC. the backend economist's thesis (EP83 + ClickFunnels Radio #777) is that operators who lead with the scorecard outperform operators who lead with landing-page conversion.

## The scorecard (the four metrics)

**1. Revenue per lead (RPL)** — total revenue ÷ total leads in.
- Replaces "conversion rate" as the primary funnel health metric.
- A funnel with lower conversion and higher RPL wins the economics contest.
- Measured per lead cohort (weekly / monthly).

**2. LTV by cohort** — 30 / 60 / 90 / 365-day revenue per buyer.
- Captures continuity, ascension, and re-sell revenue, not just first-purchase.
- Cohort-based, not blended — the cohort that bought in January cannot be averaged with the cohort that bought in June.

**3. LTV:CAC by source** — per traffic source, not blended.
- Facebook / Meta ads have different economics from YouTube organic from email.
- Blended LTV:CAC hides loss-making channels inside winning channels.
- Per-source breakdown is the diagnostic.

**4. Contribution margin per call** — net of ad spend, closer cost, merchant fees.
- True unit profit per booked call.
- Includes all variable costs attributable to that call.
- A high close-rate with low contribution margin per call = the call operation is subsidizing everything else; fix before scaling.

## How the scorecard replaces conversion rate

**Scenario A:**
- Funnel converts at 8% (opt-in to call).
- RPL: $180.
- Front-end revenue per lead is paying for traffic cost.
- Cohort LTV at 90 days: unknown, not tracked.

**Scenario B:**
- Funnel converts at 4% (opt-in to call).
- RPL: $420.
- Cohort LTV at 90 days: $1,200.
- LTV:CAC by source (YouTube organic): 6:1. By source (Meta ads): 1.8:1.

Scenario B wins the economics contest despite a lower conversion rate. The scorecard makes this visible; the conversion rate alone hides it.

**Operating implication:** when a funnel is underperforming, do not first ask "how do I raise conversion." First ask "which of the four metrics is broken." If RPL is high and conversion is low, the offer is right but the funnel leaks qualified traffic. If conversion is high and LTV is low, the front-end is over-selling a backend that cannot deliver.

## Front-end vs. backend economics (the $75M insight)

**the backend economist's EP83 thesis:** at 8-figure scale, the front end pays for itself and the backend pays the company.

- **Front-end economics target:** break-even or slight profit. Not a profit center.
- **Backend economics target:** the majority of lifetime revenue comes from continuity, ascension, and re-sell. Email is the primary compounding layer (per CFR #777).
- **Implication:** a front-end funnel with 1.0x LTV:CAC (break-even on first purchase) plus a backend with 5x first-purchase LTV at 90 days = a healthy 8-figure business.

Operators below $1M/yr often misread this. They try to build backend before the front end is break-even, and they starve the top of funnel. Sequencing: front-end to break-even FIRST, then build backend. Skipping either step collapses the model.

## When to invoke this scorecard

- **Above $1M/yr in coaching / info.** Conversion rate stops being the useful primary metric. Swap to RPL + LTV:CAC by source + contribution margin per call.
- **Diagnosing a "scaling" decision.** Is the operator scaling the funnel or escaping it? Scorecard tells you — if RPL and contribution margin are strong, scale the funnel. If they're weak, fix before scaling.
- **Choosing between two traffic sources.** LTV:CAC by source, not blended. The source-specific ratio determines which channel gets more ad spend.
- **Evaluating a second funnel.** Per EP83: before shipping a second funnel, prove the first cannot be made 2x better on RPL. Scorecard is the proof document.

## When NOT to invoke this scorecard

- **Below $100K/yr.** Operators in pre-product-market-fit phase need to first find conversion at all. Unit economics scorecard comes after you have a funnel that converts.
- **Low-ticket / e-commerce.** Scorecard structure still applies conceptually (RPL, LTV:CAC by source), but the specific call-funnel architecture assumed here does not map — e-commerce operates on different math.
- **Agency / services (non-info).** Call-funnel economics still apply, but cohort LTV behaves differently (retention / churn-driven, not continuity-driven).
