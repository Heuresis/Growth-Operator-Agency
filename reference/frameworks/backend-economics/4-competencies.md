## Competency #1 — Meta Mastery

**Definition:** Deep expertise in organic video content strategy, human psychology, and platform mechanics.

**This is NOT about:** trending audios, hashtags, YouTube SEO tricks, latest editing fads.

**This IS about:** the fundamentals of human attention, pattern recognition across thousands of videos, systems that continuously improve, viral value that breaks through viewer circles.

### Core skill: the Viral Value Framework

**Concentric circles model:** `[Your Subscribers] → [Their Network] → [Broader Interest Group] → [Mass Audience]`

Most videos start strong with subscribers, get initial engagement, fizzle at circle 2 or 3, never reach mass audience. They fizzle because they don't feel valuable enough to wider audiences who don't know who you are, don't understand your brand, don't see why they should care.

**Solution — the General + Specific Framework:** every concept needs two elements.
- **General element** — broad audience appeal (accessible to anyone): universal human experiences, popular culture, common problems, widely known phenomena.
- **Specific element** — unique angle (makes it unmissable): your unique expertise, surprising statistics, counterintuitive angles, personal credibility, unusual details.

**Worked example:**
- Boring (niche-only): "4 Ways to Say Thank You in Chinese" — only Chinese learners click, estimated reach 5K views.
- Viral (general + specific): "In China, There's a Way to Say 'Thank You' Used Once Every 10,000 Years" — same exact information, anyone curious about culture clicks, actual result: millions of views.

### Core skill: solving the two constraints

**Time constraint** — platforms change constantly, trends evolve too fast to follow. Solution: focus on fundamentals. Storytelling hasn't changed in thousands of years. Conflict and narrative drive all great content. Human psychology remains constant. Get good at these and your content works on all platforms.

**Platform constraint** — "make YouTube content for YouTube, TikTok content for TikTok." Who has time to create 5 versions? Solution: one masterful piece everywhere. Videos designed for human curiosity work everywhere. The 5-10% platform optimization is not worth 500% more effort.

## Competency #2 — Persuasive Writing & Scripting

**Core truth:** Writing needs purpose. Every word serves the goal.

### Three pillars of persuasive scripts

**1. Fulfillment.** Users clicked = they showed intent. Title + thumbnail = contract with viewer. Script must fulfill the contract 100%.
- First 5 seconds: restate exact title keywords.
- First 10 seconds: establish the Essential Question.
- First 30 seconds: prove unique value delivery.
- Throughout: continuously reinforce the promise.

**2. Momentum.** Sequence of ideas. Lead with strongest material. Bad sequence: "4 Ways to Get Rich — #1 Dropshipping (boring), #2 Real Estate (predictable), #3 Stocks (yawn), #4 [Incredible never-heard-before method]." By #4, 80% of viewers already left. Good sequence: lead with #4, then compound.

Logic ladders: connect every idea explicitly. Don't assume viewers "get it." Bridge each concept to the next.

**3. Story.** The "But / Therefore" rule (David Perell). Every section connects with BUT or THEREFORE, never AND THEN. Story = character + conflict + transformation.

### Core skill: the 3-act YouTube structure (modified, no denouement)

- **Act 1:** Hook + Essential Question
- **Act 2:** Rising tension + value delivery
- **Act 3:** Climax (EQ answered) → END VIDEO

Critical difference from traditional 3-act: cut everything after the climax. No conclusion, no summary. Answer the question = video ends. Viewers leave after climax anyway.

**Answer structure (repeat 3x):** Answer #1 (abstract → specific → problem with it → necessitates Answer #2) → Answer #2 (abstract → specific → problem → necessitates Answer #3) → Answer #3 (abstract → specific → EQ fully answered, video ends).

### Core skill: the Essential Question

The central tension that keeps viewers watching until resolution. Characteristics: clear, compelling, singular, solvable.

Tests:
- **Binary test:** would someone watch the entire video to get this answer?
- **Friend test:** would you excitedly tell a friend "I just learned [answer]"?
- **Cliffhanger test:** if video stopped right before answering, would viewers be frustrated?
- **Specificity test:** is it specific enough that the answer will be valuable?

### Writing for voice vs. writing for reading

One idea per sentence. Contractions. Repetition for emphasis. Fragments. Conversational transitions (not "furthermore" or "additionally"). Address viewer directly ("you"). Rhetorical questions. Mandatory read-aloud test before finalizing.

## Competency #3 — Digital-Native Editing & Design

**Core principle:** Every visual and auditory element serves retention.

### The editing hierarchy (priority order)

1. Retention-driven cuts
2. Visual engagement (B-roll, graphics)
3. Audio design (music, SFX)
4. Color grading
5. Final polish

### B-roll commandments

1. Thou shalt not simply show text for whatever you're saying.
2. Thou shalt not use corny stock footage (instant credibility killer).
3. Thou shalt maintain visual consistency (color palette, graphic style).
4. Thou shalt mix up on-screen and full-screen.
5. Thou shalt engage all senses.
6. Thou shalt not let 2 sentences pass without B-roll or SFX.

**B-roll content types:**
- To make unknown → known: historical footage, diagrams, tables, animated explanations, visual metaphors.
- For talking-head videos: no face-only for 10+ minutes will hit 50% retention. Even handsome subjects need B-roll.

### Retention-optimized editing

First 30 seconds critical. Must retain 70%+ of viewers. Introduce multiple visual elements rapidly. Show face → introduce element → transition. Don't abruptly cut away from host.

Stimulus throughout: keyframing (zoom ins/outs), layered visual elements, sound design variations, maintain high pace.

Common mistakes:
- Logo in thumbnail (wasted real estate)
- Old-school graphics and title stacks
- Posting horizontal in vertical format
- Using Zoom quality for content videos

## Competency #4 — Data Analytics & Pattern Recognition

**Core truth:** "Insanity is doing the same thing over and over expecting different results." If you want continuously improving results, you need a strategy that improves every week/month.

### The scientific method for YouTube (5 steps)

1. **Observation** — what's in the retention graph (spikes, continuous segments, drop-offs).
2. **Pattern recognition** — why this pattern? What do winning sections have in common?
3. **Hypothesis formation** — testable predictions ("sound effects during statistics increase retention").
4. **Experimentation** — one hypothesis per video. Test one new technique OR double down on what's working.
5. **Implementation** — weekly review, document results, form new hypotheses.

See `scientific-method-content.md` for the full 5-step protocol with the weekly review ritual and the insight documentation database structure.

### Retention graph reading

- **Spikes** (good but requires understanding): viewers skipping ahead to specific part; either curiosity or dissatisfaction.
- **Continuous segments (IDEAL)**: flat line in middle, everyone engaged. The flatter the better.
- **Drop-offs** (critical to analyze): beginning drop = normal (5-30%), end drop = normal, MIDDLE DROP = problem.

**Death by a thousand cuts:** 10 drop-offs of 2% each = 20% total audience lost. Compounds throughout video.

## How the 4 competencies compound

- Meta Mastery (#1) identifies viral concepts and the human-psychology reasons they work.
- Persuasive Writing (#2) turns the concept into a script that fulfills, maintains momentum, and tells a story.
- Digital-Native Editing (#3) executes on the script with retention-first visual/audio decisions.
- Data Analytics (#4) observes what happened, names the pattern, forms hypotheses, feeds back into Meta Mastery for the next video.

Skipping the ranking inverts the leverage. An operator who starts at #3 (editing) without #1 (meta) produces well-edited videos of bad concepts. An operator who starts at #1 and loops through #4 compounds every week.

## Binding into the Growth Optimal System

- Talent hiring sequence: hire for #1 (strategist) before #3 (editor). Most creators hire editors first; wrong order.
- Skill-development plan: operator works on one competency at a time (E56 logic from the backend economist's channel — serialize, don't diversify).
- Review cadence: weekly analytics review (competency #4) drives monthly concept-bank updates (competency #1).
