# 4 Funnel Archetypes — Pampa

> **Source:** Eli Pampa — *B1 Funnel Architecture Selection* + *A-Z Marketing Plan*
> **Thesis:** Every creator-business funnel maps to one of four archetypes. Choosing the wrong archetype for your offer type and price point is the single most common Blockage-3 (Offer) mistake.

## The 4D Funnel View

Before archetype selection, Pampa frames every funnel as a 4D object: Platform Journey (spatial) + Emotional Journey (depth) + Belief Journey (breadth) + Time Journey (progression). The archetype is the shape this 4D journey takes.

## Archetype 1: Authority Lead Magnet

**Flow**: Free high-value resource (PDF, video series, checklist) → 7+ email nurture sequence installing beliefs → micro-commitment $47-$97 mini-offer (qualifies buyers) → high-ticket VSL or sales call → backend (upsells, continuity, mastermind).

**Best for**: coaches, consultants, info products — where trust-building over time is the primary conversion mechanic.

**Why it works**: the lead magnet self-selects people who are in-market. The nurture sequence installs the 8 required beliefs one at a time over 14-30 days. The micro-commitment filters real buyers from freebie-seekers. High-ticket comes last, after trust is earned.

**Critical components**:
- Lead magnet must deliver real value (not teaser) — builds Trust-Expertise (Belief #8).
- Email nurture sequence: Week 1 beliefs 1+2, Week 2 beliefs 3+4, Week 3 beliefs 5+6, Week 4 beliefs 7+8.
- Micro-commitment is a filter, not a profit center — pricing it wrong kills the ascension.

**When to avoid**: if you can't maintain 14-30 day email discipline, this funnel rots. Also inappropriate for impulse-buy low-ticket products where the nurture timeline is overkill.

## Archetype 2: Direct Application

**Flow**: Strategic content (YouTube / Instagram / podcast) drives to application → application form (filters non-buyers) → sales call → high-ticket close → fulfillment → ascension to done-for-you services, masterminds.

**Best for**: agencies, high-ticket coaching ($3K-$30K+), done-for-you services — where human closer + custom fit matters.

**Why it works**: content does the belief-installation work (beliefs 1-6 installed before they ever apply). The application form is the qualifier. The sales call closes the trust-based beliefs (7 and 8) while customizing the offer.

**Critical components**:
- Content engine must install beliefs 1-6 before the application — no shortcut. This is where Pampa's "50+ long-form videos before short-form" rule binds.
- Application must be friction-positive: asking enough to qualify, not so much it kills volume.
- Sales call uses 8-stage structure: Rapport → Discovery → Amplification → Future-Pacing → Offer Presentation → Objection Handling → Close → Post-Call.
- Every sales call is recorded.

**When to avoid**: if you don't have an audience + content engine running, this funnel has no input. Also avoid if the offer can scale without a human closer — Application Funnel caps at closer capacity.

## Archetype 3: Low-Ticket Tripwire

**Flow**: Irresistible $7-$47 front-end offer → immediate upsell $97-$297 (OTO 1) → second upsell or downsell (OTO 2) → email nurture to high-ticket → community/continuity for recurring revenue.

**Best for**: info products, courses, community offers — where volume economics work and the tripwire is genuinely valuable.

**Why it works**: the $7-$47 price point converts cold traffic at rates high-ticket cannot. OTOs monetize the in-the-moment psychology ("while I'm already buying"). Email nurture post-purchase ascends buyers to high-ticket. Community creates recurring revenue that compounds.

**Critical components**:
- Tripwire must be valuable enough to be a standalone product — not a teaser of the upsell.
- OTO ladder: the first OTO should be a natural complement to the tripwire (accelerator, toolkit, done-for-you version). Second OTO: bigger commitment or downsell fallback.
- Backend nurture is 80% of total LTV on this archetype — weak email = weak funnel.

**When to avoid**: if margins on the tripwire + ad spend + refunds don't mathematically work at low-ticket scale. Also inappropriate for high-consideration purchases where low-ticket front-end erodes positioning.

## Archetype 4: Webinar / VSL

**Flow**: Registration page → 30-90 minute webinar or VSL pitch → limited-time close → follow-up email sequence for no-shows and non-buyers → personal outreach, DMs, calls for ascension.

**Best for**: courses, masterminds, certification programs — where one persuasion event + structured close works better than drip nurture.

**Why it works**: concentrated 30-90 min of structured persuasion installs all 8 beliefs in a single session. Limited-time close creates decision forcing. Follow-up catches the 70-80% who didn't buy on the webinar (typical webinar buy rate is 2-8%).

**Critical components**:
- The VSL or webinar structure must follow Pampa's 15-step sequence (see `15-step-vsl-deep.md`).
- Registration page: headline + subheadline + video + CTA — eye-direction principle.
- Post-event email sequence: 5-7 emails over 5-7 days, each reinforcing one or more of the 8 beliefs + mitigating specific objections.
- No-show rate is 50%+ on webinars — either use a replay strategy or switch to VSL (on-demand = 100% show rate).

**When to avoid**: if you don't have the structure / discipline to produce a 30-90 minute pitch asset. Also avoid if your offer can convert with shorter-form content (Tripwire or Application).

## Funnel Stage Breakdown (cross-archetype)

All four archetypes move prospects through 4 stages, each with its own platform and KPI:

**Stage 1: Attention Phase**
- Platform: TikTok / Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts
- Content: pattern interrupts, curiosity gaps, contrarian statements
- KPI: views, profile visits, basic engagement
- Objective: maximum reach to cold audiences

**Stage 2: Authority Phase**
- Platform: YouTube long-form
- Content: value delivery, proof, methodologies
- KPI: watch time, subscribers, link clicks
- Objective: convert attention into credibility

**Stage 3: Action Phase**
- Platform: Instagram / DMs / email
- Content: results, lifestyle, direct offers
- KPI: DM conversations, application submissions, calls booked
- Objective: convert credibility into conversations

**Stage 4: Ascension Phase**
- Platform: email / Telegram / personal contact
- Content: deep value, community, exclusivity
- KPI: purchases, upsells, referrals
- Objective: monetize and expand customer value

## Decision Rules — Which Archetype For Which Offer

| Signal | Archetype |
|---|---|
| High-ticket ($3K-$30K) coach/consultant, no big audience yet | Archetype 2 (Direct Application) — content does the work, closer handles custom fit |
| Mid-ticket course ($297-$2,997), want scalable funnel | Archetype 4 (VSL) — one asset, infinite scale |
| Low-ticket ($7-$47) info product with upsell path | Archetype 3 (Tripwire) — volume economics |
| Coach/consultant building an audience, 6-figure ceiling | Archetype 1 (Authority Lead Magnet) — nurture-first trust build |
| Community / continuity offer as primary revenue | Archetype 3 with community backend |
| Certification / mastermind at $5K-$30K | Archetype 4 (Webinar variant) with human closer post-webinar |
| Done-for-you services $10K-$100K+ | Archetype 2 (Application) — human closer mandatory |
| Personal brand, ultra-high-ticket ($50K+) | Archetype 2 with no guarantee, custom fit required |

## Archetype Switching Signals

- Application Funnel stuck at low volume → add Tripwire in front (filters to Application).
- Tripwire not converting to high-ticket → add Authority Lead Magnet nurture layer between Tripwire and high-ticket.
- Webinar show rate is destroying economics → convert webinar to evergreen VSL.
- Authority Lead Magnet ascension stuck → add mid-tier Application step between nurture and high-ticket.

## Common Mistakes

(a) Running Tripwire economics on a low-volume audience — math doesn't work below a threshold of daily traffic.
(b) Running Application Funnel without a content engine — no input, no applications.
(c) Running VSL without the 15-step discipline — most generic "webinar funnels" fail because they install maybe 3 of the 8 required beliefs.
(d) Running Lead Magnet without nurture discipline — the magnet is bait without the follow-through.
(e) Picking archetype by what's trendy instead of what fits the offer + audience + operator's actual execution capacity.
