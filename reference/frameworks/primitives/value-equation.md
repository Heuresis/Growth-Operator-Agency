# Value Equation — The Primitive

> **Source:** the acquisition economist, a high-value offer canon (Chapter 4)
> **Status:** First-class primitive. Referenced by every offer/pricing/VSL skill.

## The Equation

```
             Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay  ×  Effort & Sacrifice
```

**Top of the fraction creates value. Bottom of the fraction destroys value.**

## The Four Variables

### 1. Dream Outcome (numerator — multiply)
*The ultimate transformation the prospect wants.*

Questions to maximize:
- What does their post-offer life look like in vivid, specific detail?
- What problem disappears permanently?
- What identity shift happens?
- What status shift happens (the offer architect's "Status transformation" for Worthless belief)?

Amplification tactics:
- Paint the future-self with sensory specificity
- Contrast current-self vs future-self
- Show social proof at the aspirational identity level (not the current identity level)

### 2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (numerator — multiply)
*How much the prospect believes THIS offer will deliver the outcome FOR THEM.*

Questions to maximize:
- What's the unique mechanism that makes this different?
- What proof exists for people like them (not generic)?
- What's the risk reversal / guarantee?
- What are the objections, and are they pre-handled?

Amplification tactics:
- Name and demonstrate the unique mechanism
- Stack isomorphic-story case studies (same starting situation → same outcome via same process)
- Make guarantee ROI-positive (they can't lose)
- Pre-handle the 10 main objections in the offer structure

### 3. Time Delay (denominator — divide)
*How long until they experience the result.*

Questions to minimize:
- How fast can they get the first win?
- What's the phase-1 "quick win" they experience in 7 days?
- What does "day-1 installation" look like?

Minimization tactics:
- Engineer the first-win (Nurture compartment 9 `activation_strategy`)
- Promise specific milestones (Day 7, Day 30, Day 90)
- Provide pre-built assets so they skip setup

### 4. Effort & Sacrifice (denominator — divide)
*What they have to do + give up to get the outcome.*

Questions to minimize:
- What's the total time commitment per week?
- What skills do they need (or can we remove that need)?
- What do they have to stop doing?
- What emotional/social cost (learning curve, ego blow, public failure risk)?

Minimization tactics:
- Done-for-you components (reduce "do")
- Template library (reduce "figure-out")
- Plug-and-play adapters (reduce setup effort)
- Private community (reduce social risk)
- Predictable timeline (reduce emotional cost)

## The Geometric Principle

Because the equation is multiplicative, **the smallest denominator variable dominates**. If Time Delay is 0 (instant), value approaches infinity. If Effort is infinite, value approaches zero.

**Leverage:** most offers fail at the denominator. Creators obsess over making Dream Outcome bigger (numerator), but the real unlock is usually in reducing Time Delay or Effort.

## The Opposite Rule (offer repositioning)

If an offer is underperforming, check the equation's four variables:

| If this variable is weak... | The offer needs... |
|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | Better outcome-painting, aspirational-identity framing |
| Perceived Likelihood | Unique mechanism + better proof stack + stronger guarantee |
| Time Delay | Faster first-win, phase-1 quick win, done-for-you components |
| Effort & Sacrifice | Templates, DFY components, community support |

## Measurement

Score each variable 1-10. Multiply. Compare to benchmark:
- `< 50` = weak offer, revise or shelve
- `50-150` = competitive offer, can scale
- `150-500` = grand-slam offer, scale hard
- `> 500` = category-defining (rare)

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Making Dream Outcome unrealistic ("make $100K/month in your first week") — drops Perceived Likelihood to zero, kills value
- ❌ Amplifying numerator while ignoring denominator — offer sounds great but prospects can't commit
- ❌ Reducing Effort by removing substance — offer becomes worthless (low Perceived Likelihood)
- ❌ Guarantee without risk reversal — doesn't move Perceived Likelihood
