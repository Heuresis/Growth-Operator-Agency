# Educate-Before-Pitch — The Primitive

> **Source lineage:** Todd Brown (*Educational VSL Method* — explicit principle), Jon Benson (3X VSL — three tips before pitch), Jeremy Haynes (Hidden VSSL — teaches mechanism before offer), Alen Sultanic (VSL opening + middle teaches, close sells), Lucas Hogendoorn (Pull-Push-Persuade — never sell before Step 9).
> **Status:** First-class primitive. The trust-building substrate.

## The Principle

> **Buyers buy after they're convinced, not after they're asked.**

Teach the mechanism first. Deliver real value. Earn the right to pitch. THEN pitch.

Naive AI copy pitches immediately. Effective human sales teaches first. This primitive forces the discipline.

## The Ratio

Across sales assets, the teach-to-pitch ratio:

| Asset | Teach | Pitch |
|---|---|---|
| VSL (Pampa 15-step) | 60-70% | 30-40% (pitch starts ~step 9) |
| VSL (Hogendoorn 11-step) | 73% | 27% (pitch = steps 9-11) |
| Webinar (7-Figure) | 70-75% | 25-30% (pitch after transition at ~min 75) |
| YouTube VSSL (O'Halloran 5-chapter) | 80% | 20% (only mechanism, then CTA) |
| Long-form post / email | 85% | 15% (teach with soft CTA) |
| Ad (paid) | 50% | 50% (faster, but still teach-first) |

**Rule (Hogendoorn invariant #5):** *Never sell before Step 9.* Applies across variants — adapt the exact step number to the framework but honor the principle.

## Why It Works

Three mechanisms:

### 1. Trust Transfer
Teaching demonstrates expertise. Prospect extrapolates: "if they can teach THIS much for free, the paid thing must be even better."

### 2. Belief Installation (Pampa 8 Required Beliefs)
Beliefs 3-6 (can-fulfill, urgency, this-offer, better-faster) can only be installed via teaching. You can't assert them; you have to demonstrate them.

### 3. Reciprocity (Cialdini)
When you give real value first, prospects feel obligation to reciprocate. Offer becomes the natural next-step.

## The Counter-Intuitive Rule

**Teach WHAT, not HOW.**

- **WHAT** = the concept + why it works + the sub-steps + the framework. Creates understanding.
- **HOW** = the implementation details. If you teach HOW, prospect thinks "I can do this alone."

Brown: teach the Big Idea, Unique Mechanism, and sub-steps. Don't teach the 147-step implementation course.

Sultanic: the Core Offer is the HOW. The teaching is the WHAT.

## The 3-Tip Pattern (Benson)

Jon Benson's 3X VSL teaches exactly **three tips** of real value before the offer:
1. Tip 1: a useful insight from the mechanism
2. Tip 2: a deeper insight
3. Tip 3: the most useful (they can apply today)

Three is the magic number:
- 1 tip = shallow
- 2 tips = incomplete
- 3 tips = satisfying
- 4+ tips = information overload (prospect thinks they have enough, skips pitch)

## Application Across Skills

### In VSLs
- Opening: hook + authority (teaching earning the right to teach)
- Middle: 60-70% teaching the mechanism
- Close: last 30-40% = pitch + bonuses + guarantee + CTA

### In Webinars (7-Figure structure)
- Intro 15-30 min: authority + open loops + mechanism tease
- Content 45-60 min: teach 3-5 main points (WHAT, not HOW)
- Transition: signal we're switching to pitch + permission
- Pitch 15-20 min: offer + bonuses + guarantee + CTA
- Q&A 30+ min: more teaching + objection-handling + CTAs

### In YouTube VSSLs (O'Halloran 5-chapter)
- Chapter 1-4 = teach (80% of runtime)
- Chapter 5 = two options framing (soft pitch)

### In Email Sequences (Nurture type)
- Emails 1-5: pure teaching
- Emails 6-8: teaching + soft offer mention
- Email 9+: direct offer asks

### In LinkedIn / X / Reels
- Short-form teaches one insight
- CTA is soft ("follow for more" / "comment X for the playbook")
- Rarely direct-offer-ask in first touch

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Pitching in the hook ("Buy my course!")
- ❌ Teaching HOW instead of WHAT (erodes offer value)
- ❌ <3 real tips before pitch (insufficient value delivered)
- ❌ Teaching mechanism but never naming it (see `primitives/unique-mechanism.md`)
- ❌ Teaching without offer at all (brand-building, not business-building)
- ❌ Generic teaching (if the teaching applies to anyone, ICP doesn't hear themselves)

## Validation

Educate-before-pitch is applied when:
- [ ] Teach ratio hits target per asset type (VSL 60-70%, webinar 70-75%, YouTube VSSL 80%)
- [ ] No sales ask before the designated pitch point (Step 9 for VSL, min 75 for webinar, Chapter 5 for VSSL)
- [ ] Teaching is WHAT, not HOW
- [ ] 3+ real tips delivered before offer
- [ ] Prospect leaves feeling they received value even if they don't buy
