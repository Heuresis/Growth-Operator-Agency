# Unique Mechanism — The Primitive

> **Source lineage:** Todd Brown (E5 Method + Big Idea + Unique Mechanism), Stefan Georgi (Two-Part Mechanism — Problem + Solution), Alen Sultanic (mechanism as positioning anchor), Alex Hormozi (unique mechanism inside Grand Slam Offer), Eli Pampa (mechanism in 15-step VSL).
> **Status:** First-class primitive. Referenced by 8+ skills.

## What it is

A **unique mechanism** is a named, specific explanation for WHY the offer works when alternatives fail. It is the buyer's answer to:

> "Why will THIS work for me when the other things I've tried didn't?"

Without a mechanism, the offer competes on the same axis as every competitor — commodity territory.

## The Two-Part Structure (Georgi + Brown)

Every complete mechanism has **two parts**:

### Part 1 — The Mechanism Behind the Problem
*Why does the prospect's problem actually persist? What's the HIDDEN cause they haven't addressed?*

Example (weight loss): "It's not willpower. It's the insulin-response loop your hormones hijack after 7pm."

### Part 2 — The Mechanism Behind the Solution
*Why does YOUR specific method actually resolve Part 1?*

Example: "Our protocol shuts down the post-7pm insulin-response by timing fat-burn markers — not calorie restriction."

**Rule (Georgi):** Both parts needed. Most creators name only Part 2. Without Part 1, Part 2 sounds arbitrary.

## The Three Criteria (Brown)

A mechanism is valid only if:

1. **Specific** — named with a proprietary 2-5 word term, not generic ("my framework" fails)
2. **Credible** — backed by either science, repeated results, or inevitable logic
3. **Different** — identifiably distinct from what competitors claim

## Naming Conventions

Good mechanism names have:
- **2-5 words** (longer = forgettable)
- **Noun-form** ("System" / "Method" / "Protocol" / "Framework" / "Engine" / "Architecture")
- **Creator-ownable** (can't be used generically by competitors)
- **Descriptive-suggestive** (name hints at the mechanism without explaining it)

Examples:
- "Grand Slam Offer" (Hormozi)
- "Pull-Push-Persuade" (Hogendoorn)
- "RMBC Method" (Stefan Georgi — Research-Mechanism-Brief-Copy)
- "Educational VSL Method" (Todd Brown)
- "3X VSL Formula" (Jon Benson)
- "Hidden VSSL" (Jeremy Haynes)
- "Growth OS" (Heuresis itself)
- "FIOVA Growth OS" (this workspace)

Bad mechanism names:
- "My System"
- "The Framework"
- "Our Method"
- "The Process" (all too generic)

## The Sub-Step Layer

A mechanism needs **3-5 sub-steps** for credibility (not exhaustive implementation). Each sub-step:
- Named (brief name)
- Has a purpose (what it accomplishes)
- Logically leads to the next

Example (Pull-Push-Persuade):
- PULL: Earn attention (Promise → Profile → Proof → Presence)
- PUSH: Create emotional pressure (Pain → Cases → Process → Future Pace)
- PERSUADE: Remove barriers (Objection Handling → Close → Postscript)

Three-letter acronym or 3-phase structure is the most memorable pattern.

## The Category-Failure Framing (Brown)

Your mechanism should **name the category failure** that competitors commit + explicitly avoid it.

```
"Everyone else does X [category failure] → which causes Y [broken outcome].
 Our mechanism does Z [different approach] → which produces W [desired outcome]."
```

This frames you as category-aware and solution-specific simultaneously.

## The Educate-Before-Pitch Application (Brown)

Once you have a mechanism, your marketing teaches it BEFORE revealing the offer. The 5-chapter YouTube VSSL body (O'Halloran) and the Pampa 15-step VSL both teach the mechanism in the middle third, then reveal the offer last.

See `primitives/educate-before-pitch.md`.

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Generic name ("my coaching method")
- ❌ Skipping Part 1 (only naming solution-side)
- ❌ Too many sub-steps (7+ = forgettable)
- ❌ Competitor-unlinked (mechanism doesn't clearly position against alternatives)
- ❌ Unfalsifiable (can't demonstrate or prove)

## Validation

A mechanism is ship-ready when:
- [ ] Named in 2-5 words
- [ ] Has Part 1 (problem mechanism) AND Part 2 (solution mechanism)
- [ ] 3-5 named sub-steps
- [ ] Passes 30-second explanation test (creator can explain to stranger in 30s)
- [ ] Demonstrably different from 3 direct competitors
- [ ] Threads through Hook + Problem + Solution + Close of VSL
- [ ] Installs Pampa Belief #5 (This-offer is right one) AND #6 (Better/faster than alternatives)
