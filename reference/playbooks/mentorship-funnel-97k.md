# $97k/mo High-Ticket Mentorship Funnel Playbook

> **Why this matters:** Operators with a defensible $5k–$30k mentorship or coaching offer can reach ~$97k/mo cash collected on LinkedIn alone without ads, a team, or paid traffic — provided the funnel is built around authority content, a tight application, and a full-stack 40–50 minute sales call. This playbook documents the exact architecture that works as of Dec 2025 (post-algo-shift) and is the default path Growth OS recommends for founders selling expertise, transformation, or productized coaching.

## Source / Lineage

- Synthesized from LinkedIn operator patterns observed across 2024–2025 (the offer architect, the content OS director, the VSL director school and adjacent operators)
- Calibrated to the Dec 2025 LinkedIn algorithm change (reach favors personal-narrative + mechanism posts, penalizes pure-sales CTAs)
- Economics verified against 6-figure/mo operator P&Ls in the $5k–$30k offer tier
- Downstream of the Growth OS offer-architecture + signal-theory frameworks in `reference/frameworks/`

## Funnel Architecture

Linear 6-step funnel. Every step has one job and one KPI.

```
[1] CONTENT POST     → impressions, profile visits
[2] PROFILE          → profile → DM or profile → link-in-bio click
[3] DM or LINK       → reply rate or application start
[4] APPLICATION      → application completion
[5] CALL BOOKING     → show rate
[6] CALL + CLOSE     → close rate → cash collected
```

### [1] Content post
Daily or near-daily (see Cadence). Purpose: signal expertise + prime a specific pain/desire state that the offer resolves. Never pitch in the post; the post's job is to earn a profile click.

### [2] Profile
Profile banner + headline + About section = the actual sales letter. Visitor lands here within 20s of reading a post. Must contain: (a) one-line positioning, (b) proof (numbers, logos, case names), (c) the mechanism name, (d) a clear next action (DM trigger word, link-in-bio, or featured post).

### [3] DM or link
Two viable routes:
- **DM route:** "Comment [word] and I'll DM the [resource]" → manual DM → qualify → invite application. Higher-touch, higher conversion, does not scale past ~$150k/mo solo.
- **Link-in-bio route:** Featured section links to a gated long-form (VSL or written sales page) → application form. Lower-touch, scales further, ~30–40% lower conversion than DM route.

Growth OS default: DM route until operator crosses $50k/mo, then introduce link-in-bio as secondary.

### [4] Application
8–10 question form. See Application section below.

### [5] Call booking
Auto-scheduler (Calendly / SavvyCal / GHL). Show rate target: 65–75%. Send 24h + 1h + 15min reminders. One no-show reschedule allowed, then disqualify.

### [6] Call + close
Single 40–50 minute full-stack call. Close on the call or not at all. No "second call to think about it." See Call section below.

## Content Pillars

Run 5 pillars on rotation. Each post maps to exactly one.

| Pillar | Purpose | Post-type examples | % of cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Authority** | Establish you know the domain cold | Contrarian take, teardown of a bad practice, data/benchmark post | 25% |
| **Transformation** | Show the before/after state the offer delivers | Client-state narrative (no logo), personal-founder-story, before/after framed abstractly | 25% |
| **Mechanism** | Name + explain the unique method | "The 3-stage X framework", diagram post, step-by-step breakdown | 20% |
| **Case study** | Proof the mechanism works | Named client (with permission), anonymized numbers-first, "what we did for [segment] client" | 15% |
| **Offer / CTA** | Explicit invitation | "I'm opening 3 spots for [program]", "DM me [word]", application-open post | 15% |

Rule: Never stack two Offer posts back-to-back. The algorithm (post Dec 2025) will suppress reach on the second one within 48h. Authority and Transformation absorb penalty better.

## Cadence (Dec 2025 algo-calibrated)

- **3–4 posts/week** is the current sweet spot. Daily posting triggers reach decay after week 2. Twice/week leaves money on the table.
- **1 long-form per week (1,200–2,000 chars)** — this is where mechanism + transformation live.
- **2–3 short-form per week (400–800 chars)** — authority, case study, offer.
- **Comment activity > post activity.** 15–25 thoughtful comments/day on ICP-adjacent posts drives 30–50% of total profile visits for operators under 50k followers.
- **No reposts of own content within 30 days.** Algorithm flags it.
- **Post time:** 7:30–9:30am operator-local weekdays. Skip weekends except case-study posts.

## Application (8–10 questions)

Application is a **filter**, not a form. Target: disqualify 60–70% before the call. Questions must do three jobs: qualify fit, qualify budget, qualify readiness.

**Template (use exactly these or close variants):**

1. Full name, email, LinkedIn URL, phone
2. What is the specific outcome you want in the next 90 days? *(fit)*
3. What have you tried so far, and why hasn't it worked? *(readiness)*
4. What is your current monthly revenue / role / context relevant to the offer? *(fit + budget signal)*
5. What is the cost to you of NOT solving this in the next 6 months? *(pain scale + budget permission)*
6. Our program investment is $[X]. Do you have the ability to invest at that level if we determine it's a fit? (Yes / Need to discuss with partner / Not right now) *(budget qualifier — explicit)*
7. On a 1–10, how committed are you to solving this in the next 90 days? *(readiness)*
8. Why now? *(readiness + pain)*
9. What would have to be true on our call for you to say yes to working together? *(close-ready signal)*
10. Anything else we should know? *(wildcard)*

**Explicit price question (Q6) is non-negotiable.** Operators who hide price get 70%+ show rates with 5–10% close. Operators who expose price get 45–55% show rates with 30–40% close. Second is strictly better.

## The Call (40–50 min, full-stack)

One call. Close on the call. Structure:

| Block | Minutes | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Frame + rapport | 0–5 | Set agenda, "tell me about you, and if we're a fit I'll walk you through how we'd work together, if not I'll point you elsewhere" |
| 2. Diagnosis | 5–20 | Dig into current state, past attempts, root cause. 80% their talking, 20% yours. |
| 3. Gap + cost-of-inaction | 20–30 | Reflect back the gap between current and desired. Quantify cost of staying stuck. |
| 4. Mechanism + pitch | 30–40 | How you'd solve it specifically for them. Name the mechanism. Explicit price. |
| 5. Objection + close | 40–50 | Handle 1–3 objections max. Close with "does this make sense as a yes today, or no, not a fit?" |

**Rules:**
- No slide deck. Voice only (or camera-on, no screenshare).
- Explicit price stated once, clearly, then silence.
- Offer exactly one path. No "starter / premium" tiers on the call.
- Payment link sent live during the call if yes.
- If "I need to think about it" — that is a soft no. Book a 7-day follow-up only, do not chase.

## Economics

Benchmarks for a validated operator (offer-market-fit, 5k–25k followers, consistent cadence):

| Metric | Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per lead (application) | $10–$30 | Opportunity cost of operator's content time; ~0 hard cash |
| Application → call booking | 35–55% | Below 35% → application too long or too tight. Above 55% → not filtering enough. |
| Call → show rate | 65–75% | Below 65% → qualification gap, fix Q5+Q6 |
| Call → close rate | 30–40% | Below 30% → diagnosis weak or price-state not set. Above 40% → probably underpriced. |
| Average cash collected per call | $1,500–$12,000 | Depends on offer price + pay-in-full rate |
| Monthly target path to $97k | 25–35 calls/mo at $3k AOV × 30% close, or 15–20 calls/mo at $8k AOV × 35% close | Pick the lane that matches offer |

**Implication:** To hit $97k/mo on a $5k offer needs ~20 closes/mo = ~60 calls/mo = ~120 applications/mo = ~300 DMs/mo. Achievable solo. $15k offer: ~7 closes → ~20 calls → 40 apps → 100 DMs. Much easier.

## The 90-Day Build Sequence

Most operators try to run this funnel at week 1 and fail. The funnel compounds. Real activation follows a predictable curve:

| Phase | Weeks | Primary activity | Expected output |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 — Offer + profile hardening** | 1–2 | Rewrite profile (headline, banner, About, Featured). Finalize offer price + mechanism name + application. Draft 10 reserve posts across 5 pillars. | Profile passes 20s-visitor test. 10 posts in backlog. Application live. |
| **Phase 2 — Content activation** | 3–6 | Post 3–4x/week. Comment 15–25x/day on ICP posts. Track profile-visit rate weekly. First DMs come in. | 3–8 applications/week. 0–2 booked calls/week. Iterating hooks. |
| **Phase 3 — Funnel tuning** | 7–10 | First calls happen. Record every call. Debrief after each. Tighten diagnosis block. Revise application based on call-pattern. | 5–12 applications/week. 2–5 booked calls/week. 1–3 closes. |
| **Phase 4 — Compound** | 11–13 | Content backlog compounds. Profile visits 2x vs week 6. Old posts still generating DMs. | 10–20 applications/week. 6–10 booked calls/week. 3–5 closes. First $30k+ month. |

Rule: do not judge funnel health before week 8. Too many operators kill a working funnel at week 4.

## Failure Modes + Fixes

| Failure mode | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Low profile-visit rate | Posts get likes but no profile clicks | Rewrite headline + banner. Add explicit positioning + one proof number. |
| Low application completion | People start apps, don't finish | Cut to 8 questions. Remove any Q that doesn't filter. |
| Low show rate | Apps book but no-show | Add Q6 price qualifier. Send 3-touch reminder sequence. Require phone number. |
| Low close rate | Calls go well but no yes | Diagnosis block is too short. Extend to 15min min. Quantify cost-of-inaction in dollars. |
| "I need to think about it" on every call | Soft nos dominate | Offer is not specific enough, or price is not stated early enough. Fix pitch block. |
| Good calls, refunds at day 14 | Closes work but clients bail | Onboarding is not delivering early win. Add a day-3 and day-7 checkpoint. |
| Reach collapse after 3 weeks | Posts suddenly get 10% of usual reach | Too much Offer/CTA content. Rebalance to 25/25/20/15/15. Comment activity may have dropped. |

## Objection Library (top 5, with responses)

Every call will hit at least one. Memorize responses.

1. **"I need to think about it."**
   *Response:* "Totally fair. When you say 'think about it,' is there a specific piece you're unsure about, or is it more that the price feels like a lot right now? I'd rather address the real thing than have you think in circles for a week."
   *Principle:* Name the real objection before it hides behind "think about it."

2. **"I need to talk to my [partner / business partner / boss]."**
   *Response:* "Makes sense. What's their main question going to be when you bring this up? Because if we can pre-answer that now, you'll have a much easier conversation than coming back to me for answers afterward."
   *Principle:* Sell to the proxy stakeholder live on the call; do not let the thread cool off.

3. **"Can I get a discount?"**
   *Response:* "The price is the price — it reflects the outcome, not the inputs. What I can flex on is payment terms if cashflow is the constraint. Is that the real issue, or is it more that you're not sure it's worth the full amount?"
   *Principle:* Never discount. Offer payment terms instead. Surface real objection.

4. **"I've tried this kind of thing before and it didn't work."**
   *Response:* "What specifically didn't work? Because usually it's one of three things — [list]. If it's any of those, this is built differently. If it's a fourth thing, tell me and I'll tell you honestly if we solve it."
   *Principle:* Diagnose the prior failure. Most operators' last-thing-tried wasn't close to this offer's mechanism.

5. **"Can you send me some info and I'll review it?"**
   *Response:* "I don't send decks — every program is scoped to the person on this call, so there's nothing generic to send that would be useful. If there's a specific concern we haven't addressed, let's handle it now; otherwise you'd be reviewing something that doesn't apply to you."
   *Principle:* "Send info" = polite soft-no. Never send info. Force the decision now or book a firm follow-up within 7 days.

## When to run this playbook

**Good fit:**
- Offer price $3k–$30k (sweet spot $5k–$15k)
- Offer resolves a specific, named pain with a named outcome
- Operator has at least 12 months of domain credibility (case studies, track record, or personal transformation)
- Operator is willing to be on camera / in content consistently
- Buyer is on LinkedIn (B2B operator, founder, executive, consultant, agency owner, coach)

**Not a fit — do not run:**
- Offer price under $1k (economics don't work; run a low-ticket funnel instead)
- Offer price over $50k (needs outbound + multi-touch; see Outreach OS playbook)
- Buyer is consumer / not on LinkedIn
- Operator unwilling to post consistently for 90+ days (funnel takes 60–90 days to compound)
- No case studies and no founder-story to lean on (build credibility first)
- Product is a SaaS / PLG / usage-based offer (wrong funnel shape)

## INV-8 Scope Note

**This playbook is scoped to the operator posting about THEIR OWN offer on THEIR OWN LinkedIn profile.** It is not a LinkedIn agency service, a done-for-you ghostwriting engagement, or a social-media-management retainer. Growth OS template assumes the operator is the subject-matter authority and is the one appearing in content. If a founder wants to sell LinkedIn services TO others, that is a separate workspace (`linkedin-agency-os`, not yet built) with different economics, team structure, and delivery model.

## Non-applicable scenarios

- **Ads-driven funnels.** This playbook is organic-only. Paid LinkedIn ads require a different creative + landing page + retargeting stack.
- **Low-ticket info products.** $47–$497 products need a tripwire/webinar funnel, not an application-call funnel.
- **Consumer (B2C) offers.** LinkedIn buyer intent is B2B / prosumer.
- **Enterprise 6-figure deals.** These need ABM + multi-stakeholder cycles, not one-call-close.
- **Founder refuses to be on camera/in content.** Growth OS cannot generate synthetic authority; founder presence is load-bearing.
