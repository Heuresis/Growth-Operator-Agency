# Partnerships — Shared Knowledge

## Purpose
Shared vocabulary, partner-fit tests, revenue-split economics, and compliance rules inherited by every skill in the Partnerships division. Partnerships multiply reach without multiplying ad spend — but only when the partner fit is real, the economics are legible, and compliance is clean.

## Division Skills (3)
- `/jv-webinar-proposal` — propose a joint venture webinar with a complementary list-holder
- `/affiliate-program` — build + recruit for an affiliate/partner program
- `/referral-program` — build a customer-led referral program

## Core Vocabulary
- **JV (Joint Venture)** — One-time or recurring co-promotion with a partner who owns a list overlapping your ICP. Typically centered on a webinar or limited launch.
- **Affiliate** — Ongoing promotional partner compensated per conversion (typically 20–50% rev share for first purchase, sometimes recurring).
- **Referral** — Existing customer who introduces another buyer. Reward structure differs from affiliate — usually smaller, often credits / access / recognition.
- **Partner Fit** — Quantified overlap between your ICP and partner's audience (target ≥ 40% overlap) + non-competitive offer + brand-value alignment.
- **EPC (Earnings Per Click)** — $X earned per click sent. Partners expect ≥ $1 EPC to repromote. Calculate before asking.
- **Rev-Split** — % of gross revenue paid to partner. Info products: 30–50%. Services: 10–25%. SaaS: 20–30% first year.
- **Attribution Window** — Days after click a sale is credited to partner. Standard: 30–60 days for affiliates, 90 days for JV, lifetime (with frequency cap) for referrals.
- **Cookie + Server-Side** — Attribution redundancy. Client-side alone loses 20–30% of credit to browser privacy features.

## Partner-Fit Test (Pass All 4)
1. **Audience Overlap** — Partner list is ≥ 40% your ICP (validated via survey, past joint promos, or list-matching tools).
2. **Offer Non-Competition** — Partner's offer is adjacent, not substitute. Complementary: sells time-management course + you sell mindset course. Competitive: both sell mindset courses at same price.
3. **Brand-Value Alignment** — Audience values intersect. Partner's tone, promises, customer-treatment standards do not violate yours.
4. **Reciprocity Potential** — You can promote to them back. One-sided JVs burn relationships.

If any test fails, decline the partnership — even with short-term revenue upside.

## Rev-Split Economics
| Offer Type | Partner Type | Typical Split | Payment Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Info product / course | Affiliate | 40–50% first purchase | Day 30 post-purchase (after refund window) |
| Info product / course | JV partner | 50% first purchase, 25% upsells | Day 45 post-purchase |
| High-ticket coaching | Affiliate | 20–30% first purchase | Day 30 + delivery-started |
| High-ticket coaching | JV partner | 30–50% first purchase | Day 45 + contract signed |
| Services (done-with-you) | Affiliate | 10–20% first engagement | Net-30 after client-signed invoice |
| SaaS | Affiliate | 20–30% recurring for 12 months | Monthly |
| Any | Referral (customer) | $100–$500 flat or 10% credit | 30 days post-purchase |

## Compliance Rules
- **FTC disclosure** (US) — Partners must disclose "I earn a commission" clearly and conspicuously near every link + in every email + on every post. Not buried in footer.
- **GDPR / CAN-SPAM / CASL** — Partners must have opt-in consent for the list they promote to. You get written confirmation before launch.
- **Trademark & IP** — Partners use only approved creatives. No brand hijacking bids on your branded keywords without written permission (or banned entirely).
- **Incentives** — Do not pay for reviews. Do not pay customers to post as "organic". Referral incentives are allowed if disclosed.
- **Tax** — W-9 (US) or W-8BEN (non-US) collected before first payout. 1099-NEC issued at year-end for $600+ paid.
- **Contract** — Every partnership documented: rev-split, attribution, window, payment terms, exclusivity, termination clauses, disclosure obligations.

## Core KPIs
| KPI | Target | Measured By |
|---|---|---|
| Partner EPC | ≥ $1 (affiliate), ≥ $3 (JV) | attribution platform |
| JV Webinar Show-Rate | ≥ 40% | webinar platform |
| JV Webinar CVR | ≥ 5% (registrants → buyer) | funnel |
| Affiliate Activation | ≥ 30% of approved promote in first 30 days | affiliate dashboard |
| Referral Rate | ≥ 15% of happy customers refer | customer survey + referral logs |
| Partner Retention | ≥ 70% of partners promote again | 12-mo cohort |
| Compliance Incidents | 0 FTC / platform violations | audit |

## Handoff Patterns
1. Partner lead identified → `/jv-webinar-proposal` drafted using partner-fit test. **Gate:** all 4 tests pass before outreach.
2. Proposal sent → partner agrees → brief + creatives + tracking handed over 14 days before promotion window.
3. `/affiliate-program` launched once core offer has 3+ verified case studies + ≥ $1 proven EPC internally. **Gate:** do not recruit affiliates to unproven funnels.
4. Top 20% customers (by NPS + result) → `/referral-program` invites them into the referral track. **Gate:** reward visible in portal, tracking verified.
5. Quarterly review — top and bottom decile partners. Top: double down. Bottom: coach or deactivate.

## Anti-patterns (block these)
- JV with partner whose audience has < 20% ICP overlap (waste of both lists)
- Affiliate program before internal EPC is proven (partners churn fast after a dud promotion)
- Recurring affiliate rev-share with no cap + no clawback (structural margin erosion)
- No compliance disclosure on promoted content (FTC exposure, platform bans)
- Paying affiliates on lead, not on sale (fraud magnet)
- No attribution redundancy (losing 20–30% partner credit) — partners notice and leave
- Referral rewards too small to matter ($10 for a $2K referral)
