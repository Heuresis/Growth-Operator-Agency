# Vertical — Sales Training

> **Who this is for:** creators who train salespeople — individual reps, teams, or managers. Covers inside sales, field sales, SaaS sales, high-ticket closer training, sales-leadership coaching. Different from general business coaching because the buyer is a sales operator or team owner, not a generalist founder.

---

## 1. Audience Primer

Sales-training buyers come in three shapes: (1) individual reps paying out-of-pocket to level up, (2) managers paying for team training, and (3) founders / sales leaders paying to build sales orgs. Each has different pain, different pricing tolerance, and different content angles.

Sales is a results-visible profession — buyers judge training by whether reps hit quota, close rates rise, and deal sizes grow. The market is saturated with generic "sales tips" content. Differentiation comes from a specific methodology (that works in the buyer's context), specific proof (trained rep results), and specific delivery format.

### Sub-niches

1. **SaaS AE / SMB sales training** — most common target
2. **Enterprise sales / complex deals** — fewer buyers, higher ticket
3. **High-ticket closer training** — for creators' closers (hybrid with info-creator vertical)
4. **SDR / prospecting training** — function-specific
5. **Sales leadership / VP Sales training** — smaller audience, high ticket
6. **Real estate / loan officer sales training** — vertical-specific
7. **Consulting / professional services sales** — relationship-sales
8. **Retail / B2C sales training** — different regulatory context

### Search + research behavior

- LinkedIn (primary for sales operators)
- YouTube (tactical how-to)
- Podcasts (authority)
- Sales-specific communities (RevGenius, Pavilion, Bravado, etc.)
- Books (sales is book-heavy)
- Conference attendance (Sales Hacker, Outreach etc.)

### What they already spend on

- Sales software (CRM, prospecting tools, call recording)
- Individual training courses
- Team training ($10K-$200K engagements)
- Conferences ($500-$3K per ticket)
- Memberships / communities

## 2. Sample ICP (SaaS AE Training Sub-Niche)

**Avatar name:** "The Stalled AE"

### 2.1 Demographic

- Age: 25-40
- Gender: 60% male
- Geography: US primarily
- Income: $80K base + variable → $150K-$300K OTE
- Role: Account Executive or senior AE
- Experience: 2-7 years in sales

### 2.2 4-Layer Pain

| Layer | Typical pain |
|---|---|
| L1 Functional | Below quota, low close rate, can't close Q4, pipeline issues |
| L2 Behavioral | Rushing deals, discounting, chasing non-buyers, burning out |
| L3 Private | "I might not be as good as I thought. What if this is as good as I get?" |
| L4 Identity | "Am I going to make it to VP, or am I going to get stuck at AE forever?" |

### 2.3 Limiting belief pattern

- **Most common:** Helpless ("I don't have the skill / methodology yet") → Capability transformation
- **Secondary:** Worthless ("the top reps have something I don't") → Status transformation

### 2.4 Awareness level

- Solution-aware to Product-aware dominant
- Familiar with category language (SPIN, MEDDIC, Challenger, etc.)

### 2.5 Sophistication stage

- **Skeptical** — many generic sales trainers
- Respond to specific methodology + rep-outcome proof

## 3. Offer Archetype

### 3.1 Typical shape

- **Individual:** 3-6 month cohort with weekly calls + role-plays + call reviews
- **Team:** 8-12 week engagement with manager + reps + call-review + methodology rollout
- **Leadership:** Higher-ticket 1:1 or small-group coaching over 6-12 months

### 3.2 Value stack components that resonate

- Specific methodology (named, proven)
- Weekly live coaching / role-plays
- Call review (recorded call + personalized feedback)
- Script / template library
- Role-play practice with peers
- Community / cohort
- Manager integration (for team programs)
- Access to creator's playbook of proven patterns

### 3.3 Bonuses that work

- Script library (discovery, demo, closing, objection handling)
- Email / LinkedIn outreach templates
- Deal-desk decision frameworks
- Forecasting templates
- Discovery question library
- Call-recording access with creator's annotations
- Manager coaching on how to coach the reps through the program

### 3.4 Guarantee patterns

- Outcome-tied: "hit [specific metric] by end of program or continued coaching until you do"
- Milestone-based: "complete X calls, attend Y role-plays, etc. or refund"
- Satisfaction-based: 30-day window

Avoid: income guarantees (commission tied to too many variables).

## 6. Content Angles

### 6.1 Content pillars

- **Pillar 1 — Tactical how-to** (specific scripts, specific moves, specific patterns)
- **Pillar 2 — Deal breakdowns** (anonymized real deals, what worked / didn't)
- **Pillar 3 — Rep transformation stories** (AE went from quota-miss to president's club)
- **Pillar 4 — Methodology + frameworks** (the creator's proprietary approach)

### 6.2 Hook patterns

- "The exact script I use to [specific sales moment]"
- "Why most [AE / SDR / closer] lose this deal — and how to keep it"
- "I watched a rep blow a $[N] deal. Here's the specific mistake."
- "The #1 [metric] killer — and the fix"
- "If your [specific metric] is below [benchmark], read this"

### 6.3 Platforms

1. **LinkedIn** — dominant for sales operators
2. **YouTube** — tactical long-form
3. **Podcast (own or guest)** — authority-building
4. **Newsletter** — high-warmth
5. **TikTok** — growing for younger sales reps
6. **Discord / Slack communities** — sales-community-native

### 6.4 What to avoid

- Generic "motivation" content (oversaturated)
- Hype / bro-culture messaging (alienates half the audience)
- Tactics without context (buyers want methodology, not tips)
- Outdated frameworks without acknowledgement

## 7. Proof Patterns

### 7.1 What works

- Named rep archetype with before/after metrics (quota attainment, close rate, AOV)
- Call recordings (with consent) showing specific moves
- Client-company logos (for team programs, with release)
- President's club / ranking shifts
- Long-term retention stories (rep promoted, team scaled)
- Manager / VP Sales endorsements

### 7.2 What to avoid

- Unverifiable "10x'd my commissions" claims
- Lifestyle marketing (car / watch / house)
- Single-rep outliers marketed as typical
- Anonymized cases that could be fabricated

## 10. Common Pitfalls + Fixes

| Pitfall | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Generic "sales tips" positioning | Market default | Specific methodology + specific sub-niche (SaaS AE, enterprise, etc.) |
| Commission-based outcome claims | Hype-friendly | Outcome claims tied to controllable behaviors (calls, close rate) not commissions |
| No team integration for team buyers | Individual-rep focus | Manager component + team rollout + manager coaching |
| One-off vs recurring | Transaction mentality | Continuity tier for alumni + quarterly refresher programs |
| Methodology without application | Teaching without practice | Role-play + call review + peer practice built into cohort |
| Case-study gap | No systematic capture | Monthly rep-outcome surveys; quarterly case-study capture |
| Oversaturated content | Everyone posts "sales tips" | Specific, opinionated, mechanism-led content |

## 12. Regulatory + Disclaimer Notes

- **Income claims** — commissions are variable; disclosures required
- **Specific metric claims** — "we 3x'd close rate" requires specific starting point + timeframe + methodology
- **Testimonials from employed reps** — require clarity that opinions are their own, not employer's
- **Team programs with large employers** — contract + IP + confidentiality + data handling
- **Recorded calls as training content** — consent + anonymization + compliance with recording laws (varies by state / country)
