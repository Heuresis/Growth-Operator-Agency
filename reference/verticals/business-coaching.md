# Vertical — Business Coaching (Founders + CEOs)

> **Who this is for:** creators coaching founders, CEOs, operators, and business owners — typically on revenue growth, leadership, operations, or scaling. Excludes functional-role coaching (sales training, marketing consulting) which have their own packs.

---

## 1. Audience Primer

Business coaching buyers are time-poor, cash-available, and pattern-resistant. They've built enough to have real money and real scars. They don't need motivation — they need leverage. The default failure mode of business coaches is selling them "mindset" when they need operating systems, or selling them tactics when they need strategic clarity.

The $3M-$30M revenue founder is the sweet spot — past survival, before the point where their board picks the coach. They feel stuck between "the system that got me here" and "the system I need to scale." They pay well when a coach names that gap precisely.

### Search + research behavior

- Podcasts during commutes (founder interviews, scaling stories)
- Twitter/X threads on operational insights
- LinkedIn for peer benchmarking
- Books recommended by trusted founders
- Small mastermind conversations (they value signal)

### What they already spend on

- Executive coaches ($500-$2000/hr typical peers)
- Industry-specific consultants ($10K-$100K projects)
- EO / YPO / Vistage-style peer groups
- Retreats, masterminds ($5K-$50K)
- Books, courses (low relative spend for this tier)

## 2. Sample ICP (Business Coaching)

**Avatar name:** "The Stalled Scaler"

### 2.1 Demographic

- Age: 35-55
- Gender: 70% male, 30% female (varies)
- Geography: US / UK / AUS / CAN urban hubs
- Income: $250K-$3M annual take-home
- Education: typically college-educated; credentialing varies by industry

### 2.2 Firmographic

- Role: Founder / CEO / Managing Partner
- Company stage: $1M-$30M ARR typical
- Revenue stage: past product-market fit, pre-institutional-scale
- Industry: mixed — SaaS, services, ecom, professional services most common
- Team size: 5-75

### 2.3 4-Layer Pain

| Layer | Typical pain |
|---|---|
| L1 Functional | Growth has plateaued, team isn't executing at the level they need, personal calendar is full |
| L2 Behavioral | Working weekends, answering Slack during family time, micromanaging, delaying key hires |
| L3 Private | "I'm the bottleneck, but I don't know how to step back without things breaking" |
| L4 Identity | "Am I actually a CEO, or just a senior operator with a bigger P&L?" |

### 2.4 Limiting belief pattern

- **Most common:** Helpless ("I don't have the capability yet") → Capability transformation
- **Secondary:** Worthless ("I'm not the kind of person who runs a $50M company") → Status transformation

### 2.5 Awareness level

- Solution-aware to Product-aware dominant
- They've tried coaches before, sometimes consultants
- They can recognize generic frameworks and will shut down when they hear them

### 2.6 Sophistication stage

- **Skeptical** is the default — they've seen the coach carousel
- Exhausted in segments where coach-saturation is high (SaaS, agency-owner)

## 3. Offer Archetype

### 3.1 Typical shape

- High-ticket 1:1 coaching or tight-cohort group (<20 members)
- 6-12 month engagement minimum
- Voxer / async chat access
- Monthly in-person or quarterly retreat (high-end)
- Operating frameworks delivered alongside coaching

### 3.2 Value stack components that resonate

- Operating system templates (OKR frameworks, leadership team cadence, financial-model templates)
- Founder 1:1 calls (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Async access (Voxer / Slack channel)
- Peer community (the cohort)
- Guest expert sessions (legal, finance, talent)
- Annual retreat or offsite

### 3.3 Bonuses that work

- Operating template library (founders love templates)
- Direct intros to high-signal peer network
- Coaching-of-coaches for their leadership team
- Review of their next 5 key hires
- Legal / financial expert referrals

### 3.4 Guarantee patterns

- Revenue-tied: "add $X ARR in 12 months or I work for free"
- Outcome-tied: "place your first $250K operator in 90 days or refund"
- Time-tied: "if in month 3 you don't see systematic change, full refund"

Avoid unconditional guarantees at this price tier — they flag as low-trust.

## 5. Typical Funnel

**Archetype:** Application + book-a-call

**Why:** price tier ($15K+) demands qualification, buyers expect a conversation before commitment, close rates on qualified apps run 20-40%.

**Structure:**

1. Cold traffic (podcast guest spots > paid > LinkedIn thought leadership)
2. Landing page or VSL
3. Application form (qualifies revenue, role, intent)
4. Screening (coach or team reviews)
5. Discovery call (45-60 min)
6. Close on call or 24-48 hour decision window
7. Onboarding / kickoff

## 6. Content Angles

### 6.1 Content pillars that work

- **Pillar 1 — Founder-honesty** (what's actually hard about scaling, contrarian takes)
- **Pillar 2 — Operating frameworks** (templates, systems, concrete tools)
- **Pillar 3 — Case stories** (how Archetype A went from X to Y by doing Z)
- **Pillar 4 — Personal** (lifestyle, reading, family integration)

### 6.2 Hook patterns that convert

- "The thing no one tells you about scaling past $X..."
- "I watched a [archetype] founder do [thing]. Here's what it cost."
- "If you're at $X ARR and stuck, here's the one question to ask yourself."
- "Three frameworks I'd pay $100K for if I were starting again."
- "The [role] I hired last year made me $X. Here's what I looked for."

### 6.3 Platforms that work (in order)

1. **LinkedIn** — highest signal, peer-to-peer, long-form posts
2. **Podcast (guest spots)** — highest-trust, warm buyers
3. **YouTube** — long-form authority, case studies
4. **X / Twitter** — fast iteration, contrarian takes
5. **Newsletter** — warmest list, highest buyer density

### 6.4 Platforms that generally don't work

- TikTok (wrong demographic density for high-ticket business buyers)
- Instagram reels (works for lifestyle-adjacent coaches; not pure operations)

## 7. Proof Patterns

Business-coaching buyers need specific, numeric, archetype-matched proof. Generic "growth coach" testimonials don't move them.

### 7.1 What proof looks like

- Before/after ARR numbers with timeframe ("took Archetype A from $4M to $9M in 14 months")
- Specific operating changes that drove the outcome
- Named frameworks / systems implemented
- Third-party verifiable markers (company announcements, hires, public events)
- Video testimonials from recognizable peer archetypes

### 7.2 What proof to avoid

- Feel-good lifestyle claims ("I feel so much less stressed")
- Vague peer-praise ("everyone in my mastermind learned from this coach")
- Unverifiable one-off outcomes

## 8. Common Objections (top 10 in this vertical)

| # | Objection | Primary reframe |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "I've worked with coaches before, didn't move the needle" | "The coaches you worked with probably sold insight. I sell operating systems. Here's the difference." |
| 2 | "I don't have time to add another commitment" | "You don't need time. You need leverage. The whole point is to give you time back." |
| 3 | "My business is unique, generic frameworks won't apply" | "Agreed — that's why the frameworks are starting points, not rules. Here's how we adapt them." |
| 4 | "$X is a lot" (price) | "Compared to what? The opportunity cost of staying stuck for another year is Y. Here's the math." |
| 5 | "I want to wait until Q3 / after this raise / etc." | "Understood. When's the right time? And what changes between now and then that makes it right?" |
| 6 | "I should probably just hire a COO / VP of X" | "Maybe. But you need to know what you're hiring for. That's what this coaching clarifies first." |
| 7 | "My spouse / partner is skeptical" | "Reasonable. Would it help if you connected us so I can explain how this works for families?" |
| 8 | "What makes you different from [common archetype]?" | "I don't teach motivation or mindset. I teach the operating system of the business you're building. Here's what that means." |
| 9 | "I'm worried about the ROI" | "Here's the math on ROI. If we add $X ARR, the engagement pays for itself in Y months. And if it doesn't, you have my guarantee." |
| 10 | "Can I talk to a past client first?" | "Yes. Here are three clients I'll connect you with. Do your diligence." |

## 10. Common Pitfalls + Fixes

| Pitfall | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Selling motivation instead of systems | Generic coaching-industry default | Every offer + content asset specifies operating frameworks delivered |
| Overpromising on revenue outcomes | Buyer pressure to justify price | Guarantee tied to INPUTS (hires placed, systems implemented) not OUTPUTS (revenue) where possible |
| Price anchored at "coach" tier instead of "strategic asset" tier | Under-pricing for the buyer's identity | Reposition as peer-to-peer strategic access, not service purchase |
| Generic case studies | Buyer shuts down at "7-figure founder" vagueness | Archetype-match cases tightly: specific ARR, specific industry, specific year |
| Founder-chasing (creator running every call) | Creator-ego overriding system | Group cohort or support-pod structure lets creator scale |
| Launch without ICP specificity | "Any founder above $1M" is too broad | Pick one specific archetype (e.g., "B2B SaaS founders at $3M-$10M ARR with a VP of Sales they inherited") |

## 12. Regulatory + Disclaimer Notes

- Income claims: require disclaimers. Most jurisdictions require "results not typical" language.
- Testimonials with dollar figures: signed release required.
- Cross-border coaching: consider tax implications (VAT, sales tax, registration).
- Do not give legal, tax, or investment advice unless licensed. Reference out to licensed professionals.
