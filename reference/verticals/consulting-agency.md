# Vertical — Consulting Agency (Productized)

> **Who this is for:** creators running productized consulting businesses — a defined scope of work, a clear outcome, a repeatable delivery. Examples: sales operations consulting, RevOps, people/HR, product ops, finance/FP&A, data, process improvement, M&A prep, fractional leadership. Not pure coaching, not full-stack agency.

---

## 1. Audience Primer

Productized consulting buyers are functional leaders or founder-operators at B2B companies who have a specific, diagnosable problem. They don't want "consulting" — they want a package that solves a named problem in a defined timeframe. They pay well for specificity and punishment for scope creep.

The $5M-$100M revenue B2B company is the sweet spot. Smaller can't afford; larger has in-house or Big-4 alternatives. In between is where productized consulting wins on cost + speed + senior-operator caliber.

The "consultant" category has reputation issues. Productized consulting differentiates by being outcome-scoped, time-boxed, and fee-fixed.

### Search + research behavior

- LinkedIn (primary — especially for B2B functional roles)
- Industry-specific conferences + events
- Peer referrals (highest-signal)
- Niche podcasts
- Published whitepapers, industry reports

### What they already spend on

- Software (CRM, ERP, HRIS, BI tools — $50K-$1M+ annual)
- Big-4 or tier-1 consulting ($250K-$5M projects — for larger companies)
- Individual consultants / freelance executives
- Fractional leaders
- In-house team (building or scaling)

## 2. Sample ICP (Consulting Agency — Functional Leader at SMB)

**Avatar name:** "The Functional Leader Without Leverage"

### 2.1 Demographic / Firmographic

- Role: VP / Director / Head of [function] OR Founder serving as de-facto function owner
- Company stage: 25-500 employees
- Revenue stage: $5M-$100M ARR
- Industry: B2B software, services, light industrial, professional services
- Geography: US primarily; UK/EU/AUS secondary
- Age: 30-50

### 2.2 4-Layer Pain

| Layer | Typical pain |
|---|---|
| L1 Functional | [Function] is broken — misaligned, under-resourced, producing wrong outputs |
| L2 Behavioral | Firefighting, reactive, can't get to strategic work, team underperforming |
| L3 Private | "I can diagnose the problem but I don't have the bandwidth to fix it." |
| L4 Identity | "Am I the leader this company needs, or will I get replaced by someone senior?" |

### 2.3 Limiting belief pattern

- **Most common:** Helpless ("I know what's wrong but can't execute the fix alone") → Capability transformation
- **Secondary:** Worthless ("I should be able to handle this without outside help") → Status transformation

### 2.4 Awareness level

- Solution-aware to Product-aware dominant
- They know consulting exists; they've seen generic pitches

### 2.5 Sophistication stage

- **Skeptical** default
- **Exhausted** in common problem spaces (RevOps, sales enablement)

## 3. Offer Archetype

### 3.1 Typical shape

- 6-12 week engagement (sometimes 90 days)
- Fixed scope + fixed fee (or milestone-based)
- Senior-operator delivery (not junior staff augmentation)
- Specific deliverables (not hours)
- Often combined with executive access during the engagement

### 3.2 Value stack components that resonate

- Diagnostic / audit phase (weeks 1-2)
- Strategic plan / recommendation deck
- Implementation plan / playbook
- Team training / knowledge transfer
- Templates / frameworks the team keeps
- Weekly executive sync during engagement
- Post-engagement 30/60/90 day check-ins

### 3.3 "Bonuses" that work

- Benchmark data from similar engagements
- Direct access to consultant for 30 days post-engagement
- Team workshop or training session included
- Documentation deliverable (process docs, playbooks)
- Introductions to vetted vendors / tools
- Follow-on retainer option at fixed rate

### 3.4 Guarantee patterns (consulting-specific)

- Deliverable-based: "if deliverables X, Y, Z aren't complete by week 8, week 9 is free / we continue without fee"
- Satisfaction-based (limited): "30-day satisfaction check — if strategic plan isn't actionable, full re-scope at no cost"
- Milestone-based: "weekly milestones with defined acceptance criteria"

Avoid: outcome guarantees tied to business metrics (consultant can't control execution), blanket refund guarantees (unsophisticated signal).

## 5. Typical Funnel

**Archetype:** Application + direct booking (or referral → direct call)

**Why:** high price per engagement, specific fit required, relationship-based sale. Most closes are off referrals; cold inbound is a minority but growing.

### Structure

1. Cold traffic (LinkedIn organic primary; content + podcast + occasional paid)
2. Audit offer or case study content
3. Landing page with specific problem-frame
4. Application form (qualifies company size + problem-fit)
5. Diagnostic call / scoping call (60-90 min)
6. Proposal / SOW
7. Close + contract + kickoff

## 6. Content Angles

### 6.1 Content pillars

- **Pillar 1 — Diagnostic insights** (how to spot broken [function])
- **Pillar 2 — Case evidence** (archetype-matched engagements with specific outcomes)
- **Pillar 3 — Frameworks / playbooks** (concrete intellectual assets)
- **Pillar 4 — Industry commentary** (function-specific trend analysis)

### 6.2 Hook patterns

- "I just audited a $[N]M company's [function]. Here's what was broken."
- "If your [function] has these 3 symptoms, you have this specific problem"
- "Why most [function] consulting fails — and what actually works"
- "The [specific metric] framework I use with every $10M+ engagement"
- "This is what [function] looks like when it's working"

### 6.3 Platforms

1. **LinkedIn** — primary for B2B functional buyers
2. **Newsletter** — high-warmth authority
3. **Podcast (own or guest)** — for authority building
4. **YouTube (occasional)** — for functional deep-dives
5. **Conferences / speaking** — still high-signal in B2B

### 6.4 Cadence

- LinkedIn: 3-5x/week minimum
- Newsletter: weekly or bi-weekly
- Long-form content (podcast / article): monthly
- Case study: quarterly at minimum

## 7. Proof Patterns

### 7.1 What works

- Named company archetype with specific function + starting state + outcome
- Quotes from functional leader (named with permission)
- Before/after metrics specific to the function (e.g., pipeline coverage, win rate, cycle time)
- Client logo wall (with release)
- Industry-recognized endorsements or co-authorships
- Publications, conference talks, books

### 7.2 What to avoid

- Generic "helped companies grow" claims
- Broad industry average numbers without engagement-specificity
- Anonymous cases when most buyers won't buy without verifiable references

## 8. Common Objections

| # | Objection | Primary reframe |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "We've worked with consultants — didn't stick" | "Most consulting doesn't stick because it's strategy without implementation. Here's what we do differently." |
| 2 | "We're thinking about hiring a [fractional / FTE] instead" | "Legitimate. Here's the decision matrix for when consulting makes more sense." |
| 3 | "What makes you different from [tier-1 firm]?" | "Three things: [specific]. We're faster, we're senior-delivered, we're fixed-fee." |
| 4 | "$X is a lot" | "Compared to what? Here's the breakeven math against [specific pain — lost revenue / bad hire / tool waste]." |
| 5 | "We want to handle this internally first" | "Fair. Here's what success looks like internal-only vs with us. Sometimes internal works." |
| 6 | "We can't commit to 12 weeks right now" | "I understand. Here's a compressed 6-week scope." |
| 7 | "We don't have the budget this quarter" | "Next quarter's budget or this quarter's revenue — which matters more? Here's the urgency math." |
| 8 | "Can you talk to our board / CEO first?" | "Happy to. That's part of scope anyway." |
| 9 | "Will you work with our existing vendors?" | "Yes. We assess vendor fit as part of the diagnostic." |
| 10 | "We need references in our industry" | "Here are [N] references in [industry]. Let me make intros." |

## 10. Common Pitfalls + Fixes

| Pitfall | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Scope creep | Soft boundaries in early engagement | Written SOW with acceptance criteria + change-order protocol |
| Underpricing | Operator identity; "I'm not worth $X" | Benchmark against Big-4 alternative + value of problem solved |
| Junior delivery | Founder sold engagement, junior delivers | Founder-delivered senior engagement as a rule; no bait-and-switch |
| No follow-through | "Here's the deck, good luck" | Implementation phase built into scope |
| Generic positioning | "Consulting for growing companies" | Specific function + specific stage + specific outcome |
| Case-study gap | No systematic post-engagement capture | 30-day post-engagement case-study interview as SOP |
| Over-reliance on referrals | Single-channel growth risk | LinkedIn + content + podcast as parallel channels |
| No retention path | One-and-done engagement | Retainer or fractional offer built into close |

## 12. Regulatory + Disclaimer Notes

- **Service contracts** — MSA + SOW structure; IP ownership clauses clear; confidentiality standard
- **Independent contractor classification** — rules vary; ensure compliance (US: IRS SS-8; UK: IR35; etc.)
- **Client work as case study** — release required
- **Regulated industries** — if serving finance, healthcare, legal, know the regulatory overlay (SOX, HIPAA, GDPR)
- **Professional liability insurance** — recommended at this price tier ($1M-$5M E&O typical)
