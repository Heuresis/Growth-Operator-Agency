# Vertical — Info / Course Creator (Evergreen)

> **Who this is for:** creators selling evergreen, self-paced information products — courses, digital products, templates, ebooks. Typically a lower-touch business model than coaching. Revenue scales through traffic + funnel conversion + upsell ladder.

---

## 1. Audience Primer

Info-product buyers are diverse. They sit across age groups, income levels, and intentions. What unites them: they prefer self-paced learning over live coaching, they want access to knowledge on their schedule, and they're often price-sensitive at entry but can ascend if the first purchase delivers.

The business model is different from coaching. The creator ships a product once, then runs traffic to the funnel indefinitely. Unit economics are driven by LTV (ascension + recurring) and CAC. Many creators start here because it's scalable; the discipline is in funnel engineering + product quality + ascension architecture.

### Sub-niches (course creators sell in any vertical)

Info-course creation spans verticals. This pack focuses on the operational and go-to-market patterns specific to the *business model*, independent of subject matter. A creator teaching photography, online business, meditation, cooking, or any other skill can use this pack.

Sub-categories by product type:

1. **Single course** — one flagship offering ($97-$2K)
2. **Course + community** — course with ongoing membership element
3. **Multi-course library** — subscription access to a catalog
4. **Templates / digital product** — non-course digital assets
5. **Course + cohort hybrid** — self-paced core + periodic live cohort layer
6. **Course + 1:1 unlock** — self-paced core with 1:1 upgrade option
7. **Free course + upsell** — freemium model
8. **Evergreen webinar + course** — hands-off webinar-driven funnel

### Search + research behavior

- YouTube (discovery + authority)
- Social platforms (Instagram, TikTok, X, LinkedIn — varies by niche)
- Search engines (for specific topics)
- Paid ads (most aggressive in this vertical)
- Email newsletter subscribers (warmest buyers)
- Reviews / Reddit / forums

### What they already spend on

- Other courses ($27-$997 typical range)
- Subscriptions (Netflix, Audible, MasterClass, Skillshare)
- Tools for their niche
- Books

## 2. Sample ICP (Generic Info-Course Buyer — Solution-Aware Adult)

**Avatar name:** "The Self-Directed Learner"

### 2.1 Demographic

- Age: 22-55 (varies by niche)
- Gender: mixed by niche
- Geography: US primary, with strong international buyers
- Income: $40K-$200K
- Education: varied
- Lifestyle: has free time to learn (nights, weekends, commute)

### 2.2 4-Layer Pain

| Layer | Typical pain |
|---|---|
| L1 Functional | Wants to learn [skill]; free content is scattered; no clear path |
| L2 Behavioral | Binges YouTube, joins free communities, gets overwhelmed, stalls |
| L3 Private | "I start things and don't finish them. Will I follow through on this?" |
| L4 Identity | "I want to be the kind of person who can [skill/outcome]." |

### 2.3 Limiting belief pattern

- **Most common:** Helpless ("I don't have the structured knowledge") → Capability transformation
- **Secondary:** Hopeless ("I've tried courses before and didn't finish") → Safety transformation

### 2.4 Awareness level

- Problem-aware to Solution-aware dominant
- Familiar with "online courses" as a category

### 2.5 Sophistication stage

- **Aware to Skeptical** — course-industry reputation issues
- Respond to specific outcomes, clear curriculum, honest time estimates

## 3. Offer Archetype

### 3.1 Typical shape

- Self-paced course with modules (5-10 modules typical)
- Lifetime access (industry norm)
- Private community (increasingly standard)
- Sometimes: live Q&A session or cohort layer
- Certificate or completion marker

### 3.2 Value stack components that resonate

- Structured curriculum with clear outcome
- Workbook or action-oriented exercises
- Templates / scripts / resources bundled in
- Private community (Skool, Circle, Discord, FB Group)
- Live Q&A or office hours (if included)
- Lifetime access to updates
- Completion certificate (for relevant niches)
- Bonus modules for specific sub-use-cases

### 3.3 Bonuses that work

- Template library / swipe file
- Cheatsheet PDFs for quick reference
- Interview / expert session unlocks
- Advanced module for alumni
- Accountability pod or buddy system
- Early-access to new courses or content
- Community events or meetups

### 3.4 Guarantee patterns

- Completion-based: "complete modules 1-3 and do the exercises; if not useful, refund"
- 30-day satisfaction
- Action-based: "take the specific action we teach in lesson 1; if no result, refund"

Avoid: unconditional refunds (abuse), outcome guarantees tied to external market conditions.

## 5. Typical Funnel

**Archetype A:** VSL + direct checkout
**Archetype B:** Evergreen webinar + course pitch
**Archetype C:** Tripwire + upsell sequence + ascension

### Dominant pattern (evergreen webinar funnel)

1. Cold traffic (paid heavily + organic)
2. Lead magnet or webinar registration
3. Evergreen webinar or long-form VSL
4. Core course offer with stack + bonuses + price + deadline
5. Checkout with order bump
6. Upsell immediately post-checkout
7. Downsell if upsell declined
8. Email ascension sequence over 30-90 days
9. Backend offers (additional courses, community, 1:1)

Cross-reference `reference/templates/funnel-blueprint-template.md` Sections 3.1 (VSL), 3.2 (Webinar), and 3.5 (Tripwire).

### Unit economics focus

- CAC must be well below front-end LTV (not total LTV) for stable paid scaling
- Order bump attach rate: 30-50% target
- Upsell attach: 20-40% target
- Blended AOV: target 2-4x the core course price
- LTV over 12 months including backend: 3-5x first purchase

## 6. Content Angles

### 6.1 Content pillars

- **Pillar 1 — Free teaching** (high-value tactical content in the creator's niche)
- **Pillar 2 — Student outcomes** (archetype-matched case studies)
- **Pillar 3 — Behind-the-scenes** (creator's personal process, lifestyle-adjacent)
- **Pillar 4 — Category / industry commentary** (for authority)

### 6.2 Hook patterns

- "How to [specific outcome] in [timeframe]"
- "The [N] mistakes most [archetype] make when [goal]"
- "I've taught [N] students. Here's what I've learned."
- "Stop doing [common mistake]. Do [specific alternative] instead."
- "The difference between [amateur] and [expert] in [niche]"

### 6.3 Platforms (by product niche)

- **YouTube** — highest long-term ROI for most course creators; teach-sell
- **Instagram** — visual niches (design, fitness, photography)
- **TikTok** — reach for younger audiences, low-ticket bias
- **LinkedIn** — B2B-adjacent niches
- **Podcast** — authority-building
- **Pinterest** — specific niches (crafts, home, education)
- **Email** — warmest buyers; highest conversion

### 6.4 Cadence

- Heavy publishing required — content is the top-of-funnel
- YouTube: 1-2x/week minimum
- Social: 3-7x/week
- Email: 1-3x/week
- Paid: ongoing

## 7. Proof Patterns

### 7.1 What works

- Archetype-matched student outcomes with specifics
- Student-generated content (they share their wins)
- Before/after results (skill progression, not just testimonials)
- Community-generated momentum (screenshots of community wins)
- Enrollment counts ("Join 5,000+ students")
- Review aggregation (platforms, testimonials, star ratings)
- Creator's own demonstrated expertise in content

### 7.2 What to avoid

- Fake student counts
- Unverifiable outcome claims
- Photoshopped / generated testimonials
- Exaggerated specificity without proof

## 8. Common Objections

| # | Objection | Primary reframe |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "I already have [N] unfinished courses" | "The difference with this one is [specific mechanism]. Here's how we ensure completion." |
| 2 | "I can find this for free on YouTube" | "Agreed — the information is free. The structure + sequence + community + accountability are what the course delivers." |
| 3 | "$X is a lot" | "Compare to [alternative — in-person course, other platform, time savings]. Here's the ROI math." |
| 4 | "I don't have time right now" | "Here's what 30-60 min/week looks like. Also, lifetime access means you can come back." |
| 5 | "I'm worried I won't finish" | "Specifically, here's what we've built in to drive completion: [milestones, community, reminders]." |
| 6 | "Does this cover [specific sub-topic]?" | "Yes — module [N] addresses that. OR no — but here's what we cover adjacent to it." |
| 7 | "Will this still be relevant in [N] months?" | "Course includes lifetime access + updates. Here's the update cadence." |
| 8 | "Can I see inside first?" | "Here's a sample lesson / free module. Watch this first." |
| 9 | "Can I pay monthly?" | "Yes — here's the 3-pay or 6-pay option." |
| 10 | "What if I'm not satisfied?" | "30-day refund guarantee. Complete modules 1-3 and if it's not delivering, full refund." |

## 10. Common Pitfalls + Fixes

| Pitfall | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Low completion rate | Content without structure or accountability | Milestone-driven curriculum + community + email nudges |
| No backend | One-and-done sale | Build upsell + membership + advanced tier from day one |
| CAC > front-end LTV | Scaling paid without economics | Model LTV including backend before scaling spend |
| Generic positioning | Competitive course market | Specific outcome + specific mechanism + specific audience |
| Creator burnout on content | Solo production | Batch production + team + repurposing |
| Refund spikes | Over-promised or weak first module | Strengthen first 3 modules; honest marketing |
| No community | Pure course delivery | Add community from day one — biggest retention lever |
| Stale course | No updates after launch | Quarterly refresh schedule; alumni access to updates |
| Category ignorance | Not tracking competitor positioning | Quarterly competitor teardown via `/competitor-intel` |
| No affiliate program | Missing partner channel | Launch affiliate program post-first-cohort (see `reference/legal-templates/affiliate-agreement.md`) |

## 12. Regulatory + Disclaimer Notes

- **Income claims** — if course teaches income-generating skill, earnings disclosures required (FTC)
- **Health / finance / legal courses** — specific regulatory overlays
- **International sales** — VAT/GST handling (EU, UK, AUS, etc.); use appropriate platform or processor
- **Refund laws** — consumer protection varies by country (EU cooling-off period, AUS consumer law)
- **Accessibility** — closed captions on video; screen-reader compatibility; WCAG considerations
- **Copyright** — music licensing, stock asset licensing, third-party content permissions
- **Platform terms** — if using Teachable / Kajabi / Thinkific / Skool, comply with platform rules
