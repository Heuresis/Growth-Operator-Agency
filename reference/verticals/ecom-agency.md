# Vertical — E-Commerce Agency

> **Who this is for:** creators running agencies that serve DTC (direct-to-consumer) e-commerce brands — paid media, email/SMS, CRO, creative production, full-stack growth. This is a service business, not a coaching business. The creator's revenue comes from monthly retainers, project fees, or performance-based engagements.

---

## 1. Audience Primer

Ecom agency buyers are DTC brand owners and operators who need more revenue, cleaner attribution, better creative, or a second pair of hands. They've typically tried one or more of: in-house team, freelancer, prior agency, and usually have a mixed opinion of "agencies" as a category.

The $1M-$20M ARR DTC brand is the sweet spot. Below that, they can't afford good agency work. Above $30M, they bring it in-house or go to top-shelf firms. Between is where new agencies win by being specific, aligned, and operational.

The agency category is crowded. Differentiation comes from vertical specialization (supplements, beauty, apparel, food, etc.) or function specialization (creative-first, retention-first, full-stack).

### Search + research behavior

- Twitter / X (ecom-operator community is heavily active)
- Agency directories (not always useful; reputation-driven)
- Referrals from other brand owners (highest-signal)
- Podcast (ecom-specific shows)
- LinkedIn (less active than other operator segments)
- DTC-focused newsletters

### What they already spend on

- Ad spend ($10K-$500K/mo typical range)
- Software stack (Shopify, Klaviyo, attribution tools, CRO tools, etc.)
- In-house team (marketing manager, creative, ops)
- Other agencies or freelancers
- Production (photography, video, UGC creators)

## 2. Sample ICP (Ecom Agency — DTC Brand Owner)

**Avatar name:** "The Stalled DTC Brand"

### 2.1 Demographic / Firmographic

- Role: Founder / CEO / Head of Growth
- Company stage: 1-30 person team
- Revenue stage: $1M-$20M ARR
- Category: DTC consumer brand (pick sub-niche)
- Geography: US / UK / AUS
- Age of founder: 28-48

### 2.2 4-Layer Pain

| Layer | Typical pain |
|---|---|
| L1 Functional | ROAS dropping, CAC rising, growth plateaued, attribution broken |
| L2 Behavioral | Switching agencies, in-housing, firefighting creative, managing too many freelancers |
| L3 Private | "I don't actually know what's working. I'm spending a lot and the numbers don't match." |
| L4 Identity | "Am I a real operator or just a founder who got lucky early?" |

### 2.3 Limiting belief pattern

- **Most common:** Helpless ("I don't have the expertise in [specific channel]") → Capability transformation
- **Secondary:** Hopeless ("the market got harder, my product won't work like it used to") → Safety transformation

### 2.4 Awareness level

- Solution-aware to Product-aware dominant
- Can quickly identify generic agency pitch ("we run Meta ads!") and will ghost

### 2.5 Sophistication stage

- **Skeptical** is the default
- Many are Exhausted in specific channels (META-only, email-only)

## 3. Offer Archetype

### 3.1 Typical shape

- Monthly retainer: $3K-$15K/mo typical; $15K-$50K+ for larger brands
- Performance-based alternative: base + % of incremental revenue
- 6-12 month engagement minimum (shorter engagements produce worse results and poor case studies)
- Often hybrid: retainer for ongoing + project fee for specific builds

### 3.2 Service stack components that resonate

- Paid media management (META, Google, TikTok, YouTube)
- Creative production (UGC, static, video ads)
- Email/SMS retention program (Klaviyo, Postscript)
- Landing page / CRO
- Attribution setup + reporting
- Strategy / weekly operator call
- Ad-library intelligence + competitor teardowns
- Brand / creative strategy

### 3.3 "Bonuses" that work for service sales

Agencies don't sell bonuses the same way courses do, but equivalents:

- Free audit / teardown during the sales process
- Quarterly strategic review / executive session
- Direct Slack access to senior team (not just account manager)
- Proprietary benchmark data from other accounts
- Access to vetted creative / production resources
- Performance report transparency (daily dashboard, raw data access)

### 3.4 Guarantee patterns (agency-specific)

- ROAS floor: "if we don't hit [baseline] in [timeframe], month is free / discounted"
- Performance tier: "base fee + % of revenue above [baseline]"
- Milestone-tied: "set up phase deliverables on time or [month free / discount]"
- Mutual opt-out: "30-day mutual out clause — either party can leave for any reason"

Avoid blanket refund guarantees on services — they're structurally hard to honor and signal amateur operation to sophisticated buyers.

## 5. Typical Funnel

**Archetype:** Case-study-led + book-a-call

**Why:** DTC operators buy on proof. They'll skim a LinkedIn post, check case studies, and book a call. High-ticket monthly engagement needs a conversation before close.

### Structure

1. Cold traffic (LinkedIn / Twitter organic, occasional paid, podcast guest spots)
2. Case study or content hook (specific result with numbers)
3. Landing page or Notion-style case library
4. Audit form or direct booking
5. Audit delivery (30-60 min, produces value first)
6. Proposal / pitch call
7. Close with contract + starting date

## 6. Content Angles

### 6.1 Content pillars

- **Pillar 1 — Case studies** (specific brand archetypes + numbers + mechanism)
- **Pillar 2 — Teardowns** (public competitor analysis, ad library breakdowns)
- **Pillar 3 — Frameworks / operational insights** (how to diagnose CAC bloat, etc.)
- **Pillar 4 — Industry commentary** (current ecom dynamics, platform changes)

### 6.2 Hook patterns

- "Audited a $[N]M DTC brand yesterday. Here's what was broken."
- "This brand went from $X to $Y ROAS in Z weeks. Here's exactly what we changed."
- "Why your CAC is rising (and the specific fix)"
- "Most DTC brands waste [%] of ad spend. Here's where."
- "The [specific mechanism] that added $[N] to our client's quarter"

### 6.3 Platforms

1. **Twitter/X** — primary ecom-operator social graph
2. **LinkedIn** — secondary; more polished tone, longer form
3. **Podcast guest spots** — high-trust warm leads
4. **YouTube** — niche but growing for ecom teardowns
5. **Email newsletter** — warmest buyers
6. **Slack / Discord communities** — founder peer networks

### 6.4 What to avoid

- Generic "we help brands grow" pitches — filtered out immediately
- Lifestyle / luxury marketing — doesn't land with operator buyers
- Overly aggressive DMs — burns LinkedIn / Twitter reputation fast

## 7. Proof Patterns

### 7.1 What works

- Specific brand-archetype results (supplement brand $2M → $5M in 8 months)
- Month-over-month channel-level metrics with attribution notes
- Before/after screenshots (ad account, Shopify, Klaviyo dashboards) — redact sensitive
- Video testimonials from recognizable operators
- Long-form written case studies with the mechanism walk-through
- Creative samples (anonymized or with brand consent)

### 7.2 What to avoid

- Unverifiable revenue claims
- "We 10x'd this brand" without specific starting point + attribution caveats
- Generic "we're the best agency for DTC" claims
- Case studies where the brand didn't give release

## 10. Common Pitfalls + Fixes

| Pitfall | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Selling "META ads" generically | Category default | Niche by brand vertical or function (creative-first, retention-first, etc.) |
| Over-promising ROAS targets | Sales pressure | Set conservative floors, use ranges, tie guarantees to inputs |
| Client churn at month 3-4 | Attribution unclear, expectations mismatched | First 30 days: attribution setup + baseline + public goals |
| Creative production bottleneck | Underestimated effort | Dedicated creative pod inside agency, not shared-resource model |
| Client poaching talent | Senior people visible on calls | Structure engagement with team-pod not individual-dependent |
| Scaling chaos (no SOPs) | Growth outpaced operating system | `/build-sop` before hiring past 5-person team |
| Case-study gap | No systematic capture post-win | Use `testimonial-capture-kit.md` every 90 days with active clients |

## 12. Regulatory + Disclaimer Notes

- **Service contracts** — MSA + SOW structure standard; IP assignment clauses; confidentiality; term + termination
- **Performance-based pricing** — ensure payment structure survives attribution disputes
- **Client work as case study** — always require explicit written release
- **Employee vs contractor** (talent working with agency) — classification matters, especially US
- **Ad-platform compliance** — creator is responsible for platform policy compliance in client work
- **Data handling** — GDPR, CCPA implications if handling EU/CA customer data
