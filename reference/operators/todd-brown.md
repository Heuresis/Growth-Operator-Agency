## Canonical frameworks

### 1. The E5 Method — 5-stage campaign engineering
Framed as *"a five-stage method designed to engineer customer acquisition campaigns from scratch."*

Central tenets:
- One message → thousands of sales, deliverable as webinar / VSL / DM piece / email.
- Built around a **Big Marketing Idea**, not a product feature list.
- Anchored on a **Unique Mechanism** — the thing that makes the product categorically different and superior.
- The **argument portion** = education portion: *"prove that the unique mechanism is not only different, but superior to every other method, mechanism, framework out there for the prospect to experience the result."*

The five stages are named in Todd's book (E5) and the *E5 CAMP* framing extends this (*CAMP* = Campaign Acquisition Marketing Program).

### 2. The Educational VSL Method
A delivery vehicle for the E5 message. Structural rules:
- **Educate before persuade.** Training prospects to consume full VSLs without filtering.
- **Argument over hype.** *"Instead of using hype, it uses education; instead of screaming a louder promise, it presents a compelling marketing idea; instead of hyped-up claims, it demonstrates unique positioning and provides proof."*
- **Body teaches, offer sells.** Do not speak about the product or offer inside the body of the marketing message; only during the offer presentation.
- **Argument length matters.** Example given: a 1hr17min VSL where the offer is not presented until *after the 60-minute mark* — 60+ minutes of claims-and-proof before the offer.

### 3. Big Marketing Idea + Unique Mechanism (the twin primitive)
This is the load-bearing pair across all Todd's work:
- **Big Marketing Idea** — the one compelling idea that organizes the whole message.
- **Unique Mechanism** — the named reason *this* solution works when others don't.

Together they create demand for *your* specific product or service and make the prospect *"view you as an authority."*

## Invariants
- Engineer campaigns. Don't craft ads.
- Big Idea + Unique Mechanism is the primitive. Everything else is assembly.
- Educate first, pitch late. 60+ minutes of argument is not too long if the argument is airtight.
- The message is the asset. The channel is interchangeable (webinar, VSL, DM, email).
- Unique Mechanism is not a USP — it's a *named* reason the category has been failing prospects.
