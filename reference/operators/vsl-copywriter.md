## Canonical framework — three-phase VSL formula

Structural building blocks (from *The three-phase VSL close formula* document + an early marketing-podcast interview):

### The 1-2-3 Formula (body spine)
1. **Nightmare Story** — dramatize the prospect's current state; evoke the cost of inaction.
2. **Dream Story** — paint the transformed future state specifically.
3. **Three Tips** (two parts) — deliver three genuinely useful insights that prove expertise *before* the pitch.
4. **Four Comparisons** — frame your solution against four alternatives (usually: do-nothing, DIY, competitor A, competitor B).

### Rapport Opening — the Reluctant Hero
> "Hey, look at me; I'm not that different from you; I was no one at one point — in debt, close to bankruptcy, ashamed, confused..."

The operator opens *not* as expert but as fellow-sufferer. This converts skepticism into identification in the first 60–90 seconds.

### Story Engine — "Dramatic Visual Story"
The copy tells *"a dramatic visual story that feeds from everyone's love for tragedy."* Tragedy → struggle → discovery → redemption is the baseline arc.

### Close Architecture
The 3X Close is the final movement: reinforce the mechanism, stack proof, present the offer, handle objections, urgency/scarcity, call-to-action. Jon's claim: the close makes prospects *"URGE you to want to buy it"* rather than feeling sold.

### 5-Step VSL Process (the VSL copywriter's underlying system)
Jon's own framing: "My 5-Step VSL Process is found within the world's most profitable video sales letters." (The 3X Formula is the execution of this 5-step process.)

## Invariants
- Tragedy before triumph. Nightmare before dream.
- Reluctant hero opens; confident mechanism closes.
- Deliver real value (three tips) *before* the pitch, not after.
- Frame against four alternatives, not one competitor.
- The offer arrives after the argument is airtight, not before.
