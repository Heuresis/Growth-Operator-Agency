## Canonical framework — The research-mechanism-brief-copy process

### R — Research
Gain a deep understanding of prospect demographics, beliefs, pain points, prior solutions tried.
Questions the operator must answer:
- Who is the customer (demographics, beliefs, values, behaviors)?
- What are their pain points?
- What outcome do they want?
- What's already in the market?
- What do they like / dislike about existing solutions?
- Horror stories about competitors?

Primary sources: **forum discussions** (authentic language) and **Amazon reviews** (5-star + 1-star especially).

Operator principle: *"Research reveals surprising insights about your target demographic that you didn't expect,"* letting you speak their language and position as *"one of their own."*

### M — Mechanism (two-part)
The core innovation of the method.
1. **Unique Mechanism Behind the Problem** — why prospects have struggled with past solutions. Names a hidden cause.
2. **Unique Mechanism Behind the Solution** — why this product succeeds where others fail.

Together they identify *"ONE CRUCIAL PIECE OF INFORMATION"* the prospect was missing — the knowledge gap that held them back.

Canonical example (weight loss):
- **Problem mechanism:** PGRMC2 protein deficiency → impaired fat-burning.
- **Solution mechanism:** product activates PGRMC2 → restores metabolism.

### B — Brief
A structured pre-copy document. Write answers *as if they are going directly into the sales letter*. Brief prompts include:
- Who's the audience?
- Pain points / biggest fears?
- Big promise (1–3 sentences)?
- Why aren't existing solutions working?
- Unique mechanism (problem + solution)?
- Product spec?
- Background story / story of discovery?

This produces pre-written copy segments so the drafting phase is assembly, not invention.

### C — Copy (7-section outline)
1. **Lead** — build rapport, capture curiosity, make the big promise.
2. **Background Story** — establish credibility; the discovery arc.
3. **Unique Mechanism Behind the Problem.**
4. **Unique Mechanism Behind the Solution.**
5. **Product Build-Up and Reveal.**
6. **Close** — features, benefits, emotional vision, price, CTA.
7. **FAQ** — claims a 10–15% conversion lift.

Stefan's full outline is 53 bullets underneath these seven headers.

## Invariants
- Research before writing. Always.
- Mechanism is a *pair*, not a single claim.
- Brief is the work. Copy is the transcription.
- Customer language > clever language. Pull phrasing from reviews and forums.
- Modular structure enables replicable output at volume (12+ letters/mo).
- FAQ is not an afterthought — it's a conversion lever.
