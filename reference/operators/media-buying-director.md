### Methodology — "How to test Facebook ads like the top 1%" — `https://www.linkedin.com/in/evan-seech-307a59151/` (LinkedIn post/template) + podcast reinforcement

**Runtime:** [UNVERIFIED — podcast treatment estimated 30–60 min; LinkedIn post is a written template]
**Core thesis:** The bottom 99% of Facebook ad operators test at the ad level and scale by raising budgets; the top 1% test at the creative-family level and scale by duplication. Specificity is the single lever that separates ads that scale from ads that flop.
**Signature mechanism:** Creative-Family Testing Protocol — hold audience and placement constant, vary hook / angle / offer positioning across a disciplined family of 5–10 creatives per test cycle, promote winners into scale campaigns via duplication at stable budgets.

**Key beats:**
1. Creative-family is the correct test unit; single-ad tests produce statistical noise, not signal.
2. Audience quality compounds over the ad account's life; creative quality decays weekly. Rotate creative, hold audience.
3. [UNVERIFIED] CPR (Cost Per Result) thresholds are vertical-specific — coaching and agency offers operate on different CPR ceilings than law-firm or e-com.
4. Scale by duplication: when a creative wins, duplicate the ad set into a scale campaign with a fresh budget rather than editing the winning ad set's budget.
5. Specificity is the five-dimension audit — audience, problem, mechanism, outcome, creative — and a creative missing specificity on any one dimension will underperform regardless of the other four.

**Notable quotes / phrases:**
- "After 7 years running ads across B2B and coaching... the main difference between ads that scale and ads that flop is specificity." — the media buying director, LinkedIn (2025) [verified public statement]
- "Over $50,000,000 in client revenue through ads." — LinkedIn positioning line [verified public statement]

**Takeaways for Growth Optimal System operators:**
- Run ad reviews at the creative-family level. If a review is looking at a single ad's CTR in isolation, the review is mis-scoped.
- Before shipping any creative, run the five-dimension specificity audit (audience, problem, mechanism, outcome, visual).
- When a winner is found, scale via ad-set duplication into a separate campaign — never via in-place budget pumping on the original ad set.

**What does NOT apply to most Growth Optimal System operators:**
- Operators at <$5K/mo ad spend do not have the creative throughput to run 5–10 creative families per test cycle — the protocol requires a minimum production rate.
- Brand/awareness campaigns on longer attribution windows will not show clean CPR signals inside the test cycle; the protocol is direct-response-native.

**Signature mechanism:** Agency-Acquisition Parity — the agency's own Meta ad account and funnel are subject to the same creative-family testing, same specificity audit, same scale-by-duplication rules the agency sells to clients.

**Key beats:**

2. Specificity applies hardest to the agency's own avatar — "we help businesses grow" is the single most common killer positioning.
3. Booked-call volume is the agency KPI that matters; show-rate and close-rate sit downstream.
4. [SPECULATIVE] Vertical-specific agencies (law, coaching) outperform horizontal agencies on CPR because their own creative can match their ICP's specificity ceiling.
5. Agencies that hit a ceiling are usually hitting a positioning ceiling, not a media-buying ceiling.

### Podcast Episode — High-ticket client acquisition — podcast *Sell More Online w/ the media buying director*

**Runtime:** [UNVERIFIED]
**Core thesis:** High-ticket client acquisition via Meta requires the ad account to hand off to a disciplined call funnel; the ad account and the call funnel are one system, not two.

3. [UNVERIFIED] Vertical-specific close rates vary widely; the media buying director's law-firm playbook and coaching playbook quote different bands.
4. Creative specificity must match the call-funnel avatar — a generic creative filling a specific calendar collapses show-rate.
5. Budget expansion without closer capacity expansion is the most common paid-media waste pattern at $500K–$2M/yr.

### Cross-episode synthesis

Across the testing methodology and the three podcast episodes, the media buying director's unified frame is: *specificity before tactics, creative-family before single ads, scale-by-duplication before budget-pumping, and the call funnel as part of the ad system.* Growth Optimal System operators importing the media buying director should import the specificity audit first — it is the upstream dependency that makes every downstream tactic work.
