# Team Leadership

## Purpose
Leadership framework for Growth Operators running 2-15 person teams, tuned to revenue stage. The Operator's job changes shape at each revenue threshold — this doc tells you what your job is *right now* and what it needs to become at the next step.

## Leadership by Revenue Threshold

The Operator wears different hats at each stage. Most failure modes come from running a $1M playbook at $3M, or a $5M playbook at $500k. Match the hat to the number.

| Stage | Revenue Band | Team Size | Operator's Primary Role | Time Split |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Build | $0 – $500k | 1-2 | Do the work | 90% doing / 10% leading |
| Systemize | $500k – $1M | 3-5 | Write the SOPs | 60% doing / 40% leading |
| Delegate | $1M – $3M | 5-10 | Hand ownership to managers | 30% doing / 70% leading |
| Scale | $3M – $5M | 10-20 | Build the leadership layer | 10% doing / 90% leading |
| Refactor | $5M – $10M+ | 15-40 | Work on the business, not in it | 0% doing / 100% leading + strategy |

At each transition, the Operator must *stop* doing something they were good at. The most dangerous version of this is the $1M Operator who keeps running sales calls personally. It caps the business at their personal capacity.

### Stage Transition Triggers
- Move from Build → Systemize when you've done a task 3 times and know the reps
- Move from Systemize → Delegate when you have a Closer and a Content Manager both hitting their numbers 2 months in a row
- Move from Delegate → Scale when any single direct report has >7 direct reports themselves
- Move from Scale → Refactor when EBITDA margin drops below 15% or net revenue retention drops below 100%

## Direct Report Cadences

Every direct report gets exactly this cadence. Non-negotiable.

| Cadence | Frequency | Duration | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:1 | Weekly | 30 min | Direct report drives agenda |
| Skip-level | Quarterly | 45 min | Operator meets report's direct reports |
| Performance review | Quarterly | 60 min | Operator |
| Annual review + comp | Yearly | 90 min | Operator + CFO input |
| Career conversation | Every 6 months | 45 min | Direct report |

Break this cadence and you'll learn about problems 6-12 weeks after they started. That's the expensive way.

## Weekly 1:1 Template (30 min)

Fill in advance. Shared doc in Notion, one page per report.

```
Date: _______
Attendees: Operator + [Report Name]
Report's agenda (they write, before meeting):
  1. Wins this week: _______
  2. Blocks / asks: _______
  3. One decision I need from you: _______
  4. Number(s) I own this week: [actual vs target]

Operator's agenda:
  1. Feedback (specific, behavior-based): _______
  2. Priority for next week: _______
  3. One thing I'll unblock for you: _______

Actions (captured live):
  - [ ] [Owner] [Action] [Due]
Notes for next 1:1: _______
```

Rules:
- Never cancel. Reschedule only. Canceling a 1:1 signals the report is not a priority.
- No status updates. Status goes in async weekly update (see `team-collaboration.md`). 1:1 is for feedback + decisions + career.
- Operator talks <40% of the time.

## Performance Reviews — Quarterly

Every 90 days. 60 minutes. Written form shared 48 hours in advance.

**Scorecard (1-5 scale, 3 = meets expectations):**
1. Outcomes — did they hit their numbers this quarter
2. Ownership — did they drive work or wait to be driven
3. Craft — is their actual work quality improving
4. Collaboration — are they making the team better
5. Values — did they embody the team's values

**Written output:**
- 2 things to keep doing
- 2 things to stop doing
- 1 behavior change for next quarter
- Goals for next quarter (max 3, measurable)

A 1 or 2 in any category triggers a 30-day improvement plan. Two consecutive quarters at 2 triggers the firing protocol.

## Annual Review + Comp

Cycles in Q1. Takes into account:
- 4 quarterly scorecards
- Market comp benchmark (Pave, Levels.fyi, or industry survey)
- Revenue contribution (attributable to role where possible)
- Team growth / retention impact

Comp bands refresh annually against market data. See `finance-tracker-schema.md` → team cost section.

## Firing Protocol — 3-Strike System

Fire fast on values. Fire slower on outcomes (give them the plan to improve). Never fire in anger, never fire without documentation.

**Strike 1 — Verbal warning**
- Private conversation, specific behavior, specific correction
- Written recap sent same day: "Here's what we discussed, here's what changes, here's when we'll review"
- Duration: 2 weeks to demonstrate change

**Strike 2 — Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**
- 30-day written plan with 3-5 measurable goals
- Weekly check-ins during PIP (replaces 1:1)
- HR/legal review if applicable (US employees)
- Clear: "If these goals are not met, your role ends on [date]"

**Strike 3 — Termination**
- Prepared script, prepared severance offer, prepared announcement
- Conduct termination Monday morning (not Friday — gives them weekday to start job search, protects team morale)
- Operator + one witness
- Severance: minimum 2 weeks, 4 for >1 year tenure (US standard, adjust for region)
- Same-day: collect access, send team announcement, brief remaining team

**Values violation = skip to strike 3.** Harassment, theft, fraud, lying to customers — immediate termination.

### Firing Script (Termination Meeting, 10 min max)
```
"Thanks for coming in. I have hard news. Effective today, we're ending your role at [Company].
The reasons are: [2-3 specific, documented items].
This is a final decision.
Here's what happens next: [severance detail], [health benefits continuation], [access removal today].
[HR person] will walk you through the paperwork.
I wish you well. We'll announce this to the team this afternoon."
```
Do not negotiate. Do not explain more than the prepared reasons. Do not apologize for the decision.

## Leadership Failure Modes

| Failure | Symptom | Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| Founder still closing | Revenue caps at Operator's call capacity | Hire Closer, train 60 days, transfer pipeline |
| No written SOPs | Same question answered 5x/week | Block 4 hours/week for SOP writing, target 1 SOP/week |
| Cancelling 1:1s | Team surprises at performance review | Re-lock calendar, communicate that 1:1s are non-negotiable |
| Avoiding hard feedback | Same mistakes repeat 3+ times | Book feedback-specific session, script it, deliver it |
| Hiring too fast | Cash burn exceeds revenue growth, culture dilutes | Freeze hiring, run 60-day team performance review, cut bottom 10% |
| Hiring too slow | Operator doing IC work >50% time at >$1M | Write 3 job specs this week, start interviews within 14 days |
| Promotion without role clarity | Manager unclear what they own | Write role charter: outcomes owned, decisions owned, metrics reported |
| No skip-levels | Manager-level problems hidden | Add quarterly skip-levels immediately |

## Metrics / Success Criteria
- Regrettable attrition <10% annually
- 1:1 completion rate >90% (tracked in calendar)
- Direct reports' quarterly scorecards average >3.0
- Operator IC time aligned to stage (see table above), ±10%
- Time-to-fill open roles <45 days
- New hire 90-day retention >90%

## Failure Modes (Recovery)
- **Team surprise at performance review:** reset 1:1 cadence; feedback must land weekly, not quarterly
- **Regrettable attrition spike:** exit interviews + skip-levels to find root cause (usually a manager)
- **Operator burnout:** stage mismatch (doing $500k work at $3M revenue). Force the time split rebalance.
- **Star performer demands promotion:** write promotion criteria before the conversation, not during
