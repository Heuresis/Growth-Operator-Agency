# Reel Variant — Money Reveal

> **Signature move.** The reel opens with a specific dollar number (or a specific result expressed in money) that is so concrete the first two seconds cannot be skipped. The tension is "how is that real." The body resolves the tension by backing the number with a timestamp, a mechanism, and a proof asset on screen. The close is either a loop back to the opener or a single CTA.

## When to Use

- The creator has a recent, specific, verifiable result expressed in money (revenue day, close, refund-offer reversal, price jump, collection, contract size).
- Pillar is Case Study or Revenue Proof.
- Conversion stage is Decision or late-Consideration — the viewer is close to trusting, needs one more concrete reason.
- An on-screen asset exists: a Stripe row, a contract number, a calendar screenshot, a bank alert, an invoice, a DM, a dashboard tile.

## When NOT to Use

- No verifiable asset exists. Money Reveal without on-screen proof is a fabrication risk (INV-6) and a Truth Gate violation.
- Offer is health, relationships, or any regulated niche where income language is liability.
- The creator is in a "voice-of-customer" stage where the audience is naive and a revenue reveal reads as "sales bro."
- Audience is Unaware — a dollar figure with no context produces disbelief, not curiosity.

## Structure — 60-Second Grid

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:02 | Number Hook | Dollar figure on screen and spoken; no context yet |
| 0:02-0:05 | Disbelief Reaction | Creator's face reaction or a single line of surprise — installs "is this real" |
| 0:05-0:15 | Backstory Setup | One sentence that names the situation (who, what product, what context) |
| 0:15-0:35 | Mechanism Reveal | The 1-3 specific things the creator did that produced the number |
| 0:35-0:50 | Proof On Screen | Stripe / invoice / bank / DM / screenshot visible for at least 5 continuous seconds |
| 0:50-0:58 | Takeaway | One sentence that turns the number into a rule the viewer can use |
| 0:58-1:00 | CTA or Loop | "Follow for more" or re-state the opener for loop |

## Hook Patterns (5 specific openers)

1. **Dollar-first with timestamp.** "$47,300 in the last 48 hours from one email." The number is first. The timestamp is second. The mechanism is teased in the noun ("one email").
2. **Dollar-first with WTF contrast.** "I made $21,800 last week from a Google Doc I wrote in 30 minutes." Contrast: the size of the number vs. the size of the effort.
3. **Negative-dollar reveal.** "I lost $14,000 on this mistake last year. Here's the rule I use now." Loss Aversion (2.5x weight) is the hook.
4. **Price-jump reveal.** "I raised my price from $3,000 to $10,000 on Tuesday. Closes went up." The number is the price. The tension is "how did closes go up."
5. **Dollar-per-something reveal.** "$1,100 per subscriber per year. Here's what they get." Turns a revenue fact into an economics insight.

## Body Patterns

### A. The Three-Lever Body (default for business / offer reveals)

After the hook and backstory, name 3 specific things that produced the result. Each lever 8-10 seconds.

- Lever 1 — the mechanism ("I stopped pitching the course and pitched the outcome instead.")
- Lever 2 — the asset ("I used the same 4-email sequence I'd run the year before, but changed the subject line to name the result.")
- Lever 3 — the timing ("I sent it the morning after the last interest spike, not the afternoon like I usually do.")

### B. The Single-Lever-Deep Body (when 1 thing dominates)

If one decision drove 80% of the outcome, spend the entire body on that decision. Structure:

- The decision as one sentence.
- What most people do instead.
- Why what most people do breaks.
- Why this decision works.
- One concrete example of the decision in action.

### C. The Before-Mechanism-After Body (when mechanism is subtle)

If the mechanism is a mindset or framing change and can't be pointed at, use Before / Mechanism / After.

- Before — what the creator used to do and what the result was (lower dollar).
- Mechanism — the one shift.
- After — the new behavior and the new result (higher dollar).

## CTA and Loop Patterns

Reel CTAs are one action max. Choose one.

- "Follow for more case studies like this."
- "Comment the word [specific word] and I'll send you the email template."
- "Link in bio for the full breakdown." (Only if bio link genuinely leads to an asset that delivers the same value.)

Loop pattern — preferred when possible. Last line re-states the opener with a mild twist, so viewers who replay don't notice the cut. Example:

- Opener: "$47,300 in 48 hours from one email."
- Closer: "So yes — $47,300 in 48 hours from one email. Let me show you what was in it." (Re-hook triggers replay intent; the algorithm reads replay as watch-time weight.)

## Voice-Match Requirements

- Three-plus `phrases_to_use` from Compartment 1 in the 60-second script.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- No "turning point," no "transform," no "unlock," no "that's a great question" self-narration.
- Read the script aloud. If a line sounds like a corporate VO, rewrite.

## Specificity Floor

Hook Specificity ≥ 8 required. The floor:

- Dollar figure with cents or specific figure ("$47,300" beats "about $47K").
- Timestamp ("in the last 48 hours" or "on Tuesday" beats "recently").
- Named noun ("one email" beats "an email sequence").

## Visual Brief (scene-by-scene shot list)

- 0:00-0:02 — Creator on-camera, medium shot. Text overlay: the dollar number, large, top-third placement.
- 0:02-0:05 — Creator reaction shot (mild surprise or laugh) — keep authentic, not performed.
- 0:05-0:15 — Cut to b-roll of the context (desk, laptop, dashboard logo-blurred if necessary).
- 0:15-0:35 — Cut between creator on-camera and b-roll of the three levers. Text overlay names each lever on screen.
- 0:35-0:50 — Full-screen proof asset. Keep asset visible 5+ continuous seconds. Redact anything private (emails, names, full bank-account numbers) — show the revenue columns only.
- 0:50-0:58 — Creator on-camera, close medium shot, delivers takeaway line.
- 0:58-1:00 — Text-overlay close — final CTA or opening line restated.

## Platform Adaptations

- Instagram Reels: Vertical 9:16. Caption opens with the dollar figure. Hashtags 3-5, case-study-specific. Save-optimized — the takeaway at 0:50 is phrased as a rule.
- TikTok: Pattern interrupt at 0:00-0:01 — dollar figure flashes on screen with a sharp sound cue. TikTok tolerates a faster hook.
- YouTube Shorts: Title is literal. "$47,300 in 48 hours from one email" as the Shorts title. The opener of the video must pay off the title within the first second.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- No income guarantee implied ("you can make $47,300" is a guarantee; "I made $47,300" is a claim about self).
- Proof asset genuinely visible and belongs to the creator.
- FTC-style disclosure present if the story is about a client ("this is a client of mine").
- No health claims. No medical claims. No regulatory-adjacent claims.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Dollar figure too round.** "$50K" reads fabricated. "$47,300" reads real. Specificity wins.
- **Proof asset not visible long enough.** If the Stripe row flashes for one second, viewers don't register it. Five continuous seconds minimum.
- **Mechanism not specific.** "I worked harder" is not a mechanism. "I stopped the cold outreach sequence and pivoted to the DM-to-call motion" is a mechanism.
- **Loop that doesn't actually loop.** If the last frame doesn't visually bridge to the first frame, the replay is jarring, not seamless.
- **Treating a one-off as a rule.** "I made $100K in a day once" does not generalize. If the creator can't honestly say "this is repeatable because [specific reason]," the reel is a brag, not a case study. Viewers spot the difference in 3 seconds.

## Worked Example 1 — High-Ticket Coaching Offer

```
0:00-0:02 | Creator on-camera, laptop open beside him.
TEXT: "$23,000 in 90 minutes"
VO: "I closed $23,000 in 90 minutes on Tuesday."

0:02-0:05 | Reaction cut — creator shakes head slightly, small smile.
VO: "Still feels fake when I type it."

0:05-0:15 | Cut to desk / calendar b-roll.
VO: "It was one discovery call. The prospect had been in my email list for six months and never booked. Here's what changed."

0:15-0:25 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "Lever 1: Named the outcome, not the program"
VO: "I stopped pitching the program. I pitched the outcome — the number in his pipeline after ninety days."

0:25-0:35 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "Lever 2: Showed the isomorphic case study"
VO: "I opened the call with a case study of someone exactly his stage — not the biggest I've ever had."

0:35-0:45 | Full-screen Stripe dashboard showing the $23,000 row, dated Tuesday. Name redacted.
VO: "Closed at the end of the call. Here's the receipt."

0:45-0:55 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule: prospects don't buy the program. They buy the outcome, presented with a story that looks like them."

0:55-1:00 | Text-overlay.
TEXT: "Follow for more case-study breakdowns"
VO: "Follow if you want more of these."
```

## Worked Example 2 — Info Product Revenue Day

```
0:00-0:02 | Creator at desk, dashboard visible in background.
TEXT: "$14,800 yesterday"
VO: "Fourteen thousand eight hundred dollars yesterday. From one email."

0:02-0:05 | Reaction cut — small laugh.
VO: "I rewrote the subject line three minutes before I sent it."

0:05-0:15 | Cut to b-roll of laptop showing an email draft.
VO: "The list is four thousand people. The product is $297. The email was the same email I always send at the end of the month."

0:15-0:35 | Single-Lever-Deep body.
TEXT: "The only thing that changed:"
VO: "I changed the subject from 'new training available' to the specific outcome a buyer would get by Friday. Not a pitch. Just the result, dated."

0:35-0:50 | Full-screen email analytics dashboard — open rate, click rate, purchase rate, revenue.
VO: "Thirty-eight percent open. Seven percent click. Four percent of openers bought."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "People don't open subject lines about you. They open subject lines about what their Friday looks like."

0:58-1:00 | Loop back.
VO: "Fourteen thousand eight hundred dollars yesterday. From one email."
```

## Worked Example 3 — Price-Jump Reveal

```
0:00-0:02 | Creator on-camera, tight close.
TEXT: "I raised my price 3x last Tuesday"
VO: "I raised my price three times last Tuesday. Closes went up."

0:02-0:05 | Reaction shot — mild shrug.
VO: "Not how I thought it would go either."

0:05-0:15 | Cut to calendar and proposal template b-roll.
VO: "The offer used to be $3,000. I moved it to $10,000 on Tuesday morning. By Friday I'd closed three."

0:15-0:35 | Three-Lever body.
TEXT: "Three things I changed with the price:"
VO: "One — I rewrote the deliverable list to match the higher price, not shrink it. Two — I removed payment plans under $5,000 and kept one above. Three — I stopped apologizing for the number in the pitch."

0:35-0:50 | Full-screen Stripe rows showing three $10,000 transactions that week.
VO: "Here are the three. Same week. Same ad. Same funnel."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule: the price change isn't the work. The work is the congruence around the price."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more pricing teardowns"
```

## Worked Example 4 — Negative-Dollar Reveal

```
0:00-0:02 | Creator at desk.
TEXT: "I lost $14,000 on this mistake"
VO: "I lost fourteen grand on this mistake last year."

0:02-0:05 | Pause, then tight cut.
VO: "Still costs me to think about it."

0:05-0:15 | B-roll of email campaign platform.
VO: "I ran a launch without segmenting my list. Same email to everyone. Most of the list hadn't opened me in six months."

0:15-0:35 | Before-Mechanism-After body.
TEXT: "Before vs. after:"
VO: "Before, I treated the list as one audience. After, I segmented into three — hot, warm, cold — and sent different offers to each. The hot list got the premium tier. The cold list got a free training that cost them nothing."

0:35-0:50 | Split-screen dashboard — the bad launch revenue vs. the segmented launch revenue.
VO: "Fourteen thousand loss before. Thirty-one thousand gain after — same list, same offer, different segmentation."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule: the list isn't the asset. The segmentation is the asset."

0:58-1:00 | Loop-style close.
VO: "Fourteen grand was the cost of learning that."
```

## Worked Example 5 — Dollar-Per-Something

```
0:00-0:02 | Creator on-camera, soft lighting.
TEXT: "$1,100 per member per year"
VO: "Eleven hundred dollars per member per year. Here's what they get."

0:02-0:05 | Reaction shot — matter-of-fact.
VO: "That number is why I run the business this way."

0:05-0:15 | Cut to community-software b-roll (blurred).
VO: "The community has 412 members. Revenue split: $197/month membership plus a yearly retreat add-on that 30% attend."

0:15-0:35 | Three-Lever body (the three things the member gets).
TEXT: "What keeps them renewing:"
VO: "One — weekly office-hours that get their specific question answered, not generic teaching. Two — a member directory so they meet each other without me. Three — a twice-yearly in-person retreat at cost, not margin."

0:35-0:50 | Full-screen dashboard showing retention rate (92%).
VO: "Ninety-two percent renew after year one. That's the whole business."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule: the unit economics of a community is not what they pay in. It's what keeps them in."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more community economics"
```

## Re-Engage Pattern

- T+24h reply comment: pin a comment that adds one more specific detail not in the reel ("fun fact — the proposal was three paragraphs, not a deck").
- T+48h re-post: clip the 15-second mechanism section as a standalone Reel with a new hook.
- T+7d follow-up: full-length teardown on YouTube or long-form — reel drives to long-form.
