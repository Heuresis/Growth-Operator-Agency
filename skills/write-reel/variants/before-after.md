# Reel Variant — Before/After

> **Signature move.** Two states of the same subject — a dashboard, a body, a workspace, a deck, an email, a relationship with work — shown side-by-side with a specific time-compression between them. Tension is "how did that happen." Resolution is the named mechanism that bridges the two states. The reel sells transformation, with the visible gap as the proof.

## When to Use

- A specific, documentable transformation exists — with visible artifacts on both sides.
- Pillar is Transformation, Case Study, or Proof.
- Conversion stage is Decision — Before/After is the closer variant for prospects who need to see the gap they can cross.
- The "before" state is the audience's current state. Isomorphism is the mechanism.

## When NOT to Use

- Either the "before" or the "after" is not verifiable — the transformation reads as aspirational rather than documented.
- The transformation is so dramatic it becomes unbelievable (lottery-style "$0 to $1M in 30 days" framing).
- The subject is a client whose before/after would embarrass them publicly (even with permission, some transformations shouldn't be broadcast).
- The niche is regulated and before/after claims trigger compliance risk (health, finance).

## Structure — 60-Second Grid

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:03 | Before/After Hook | "X days ago this looked like this. Today it looks like this." |
| 0:03-0:10 | Before State Shown | Hold the "before" on screen for a beat |
| 0:10-0:20 | After State Shown | Hold the "after" on screen for a beat |
| 0:20-0:35 | The Bridge | What happened between the two states |
| 0:35-0:50 | Three-Step Mechanism | 3 specific things that bridged the gap |
| 0:50-0:58 | Takeaway Rule | The generalizable principle |
| 0:58-1:00 | CTA or Loop | Follow or re-state opener |

## Hook Patterns (5 specific openers)

1. **Time-compression hook.** "Ninety days ago this dashboard showed eighteen thousand. Today it shows forty-one. Here's the bridge."
2. **Identity-shift hook.** "A year ago I was selling time. Today I'm selling outcomes. Three decisions made the shift."
3. **Pre / post hook.** "Before I changed this one thing, my close rate was twenty-two percent. After, forty-eight. Watch."
4. **Side-by-side artifact hook.** "The email on the left converted at two percent. The email on the right — eleven percent. One change."
5. **Identity-rejection hook.** "This was my landing page eight months ago. This is my landing page today. I have no idea how I slept at night."

## Body Patterns

### A. Hold / Hold / Bridge (default)

- Hold the "before" state on screen for 5-7 seconds. Creator voice-over names what it was.
- Hold the "after" state for 5-7 seconds. Creator voice-over names what it is.
- Cross-fade or hard cut to the "bridge" — creator on-camera explaining the 3 things that made the transition.

### B. The Visual Side-by-Side (for artifact-heavy transformations)

- Split-screen for the entire 60 seconds. "Before" on left, "After" on right.
- Creator voice-over walks through the differences dimension by dimension.
- The split-screen itself is the structure.

Works when the transformation is visual — landing pages, dashboards, workspaces, before/after design.

### C. The Chronological Reveal (for time-based transformations)

- Month by month, show the transformation unfold.
- Each month gets 8-10 seconds on screen.
- The mechanism is revealed at the end as the through-line.

Good for 90-day / 180-day transformations where the progression is as interesting as the endpoints.

## CTA and Loop Patterns

- "Follow for more transformation breakdowns." — Decision-stage, proof-forward.
- "Comment [word] and I'll send the bridge framework." — asset capture.
- Loop pattern — end on the "before" state briefly, then cut to the "after" state as the opening. Viewers who replay see the transformation inverted.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- Three-plus `phrases_to_use` from Compartment 1.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Before/After voice-over avoids corporate-speak. "Revolutionary transformation" = banned. "Specific difference this made" = preferred.

## Specificity Floor

Hook Specificity ≥ 8. The floor:

- Time compression is a specific number of days, weeks, or months.
- "Before" and "After" both include a specific measurable — number, size, state, dimension.
- The bridge is a specific mechanism, not a vague "growth."

## Visual Brief

- 0:00-0:03 — Split-screen or quick alternation between "before" and "after" states. Creator voice-over.
- 0:03-0:10 — Full "before" state on screen. Optional subtle color-grade cooler.
- 0:10-0:20 — Full "after" state on screen. Optional subtle color-grade warmer.
- 0:20-0:35 — Creator on-camera explaining the bridge — b-roll intercut.
- 0:35-0:50 — Three text cards — Mechanism 1, 2, 3 — with b-roll of each.
- 0:50-0:58 — Creator on-camera delivering the takeaway.
- 0:58-1:00 — Text-overlay CTA.

## Platform Adaptations

- Instagram Reels: split-screen native to 9:16. Caption repeats the before/after numbers for save-optimization.
- TikTok: fast pattern-interrupt hook — before flashes for 0.5 seconds, after flashes for 0.5 seconds, then cut to creator speaking.
- YouTube Shorts: title is the transformation literal. "$18K to $41K in 90 days — the bridge" — Shorts title is the claim.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- Both "before" and "after" are real, documented, verifiable.
- No income-guarantee implication ("I did this" is a claim; "you will get this" is a guarantee).
- Permission on file if using a client's before/after.
- No health claims, no medical before/after, no regulated-niche transformations.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Staged before.** Creator creates an artificially-bad "before" state to make the after look better. Viewers detect manipulation fast.
- **Non-isomorphic before.** The "before" is at a stage the audience has never been at. Viewers can't see themselves in it.
- **Mechanism-gap mismatch.** The three mechanisms don't plausibly explain the scale of the transformation. Viewers do the math and sense overclaim.
- **Visual before/after with no narrative.** The split-screen is pretty but the bridge isn't explained. Viewers see the gap but can't act on it.
- **Transformation-as-lottery framing.** "Zero to six figures" with no specific method. Reads as bait-content.

## Worked Example 1 — Dashboard Transformation

```
0:00-0:03 | Split-screen: dashboard at $18K MRR vs. same dashboard at $41K MRR.
TEXT: "90 days. $18K → $41K."
VO: "Ninety days ago this dashboard showed eighteen thousand. Today it shows forty-one. Here's the bridge."

0:03-0:10 | Full "before" dashboard on screen.
VO: "Eighteen thousand monthly. Flat for six months. Good product. No clear top-of-funnel."

0:10-0:20 | Full "after" dashboard on screen.
VO: "Forty-one thousand. Three specific decisions in ninety days."

0:20-0:35 | Creator on-camera with b-roll.
VO: "We didn't scale spend. We didn't add a new channel. We didn't raise prices more than fifteen percent. Here's the actual bridge."

0:35-0:50 | Three mechanism cards.
TEXT 1: "Added a qualifier before the call"
TEXT 2: "Swapped discovery calls for async Looms"
TEXT 3: "Killed the lowest tier"
VO: "One — qualifier before the call filtered forty percent of the traffic. Two — async Looms replaced half the discovery calls. Three — killed the lowest tier, which was eating founder time at low revenue per hour."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — doubling revenue is usually removing friction, not adding effort."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more transformation teardowns"
```

## Worked Example 2 — Time vs. Outcome Pricing

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "Year ago: selling time. Today: selling outcomes."
VO: "A year ago I was selling time. Today I'm selling outcomes. Three decisions made the shift."

0:03-0:10 | Split-screen: old proposal template (hourly rate) vs. new proposal template (outcome-based).
VO: "This was last year. Hourly rate. Scope creep every engagement."

0:10-0:20 | New proposal template full-screen.
VO: "Today. Outcome pricing. Fixed deliverable, fixed timeline, fixed number."

0:20-0:35 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "Same work. Same capacity. Revenue up two-point-seven times. The shift wasn't the number on the contract. It was the shape of the contract."

0:35-0:50 | Three mechanism cards.
TEXT 1: "Priced at customer's ROI, not my hours"
TEXT 2: "Named the outcome in the contract"
TEXT 3: "Refused any scope beyond the outcome"
VO: "One — priced at what the outcome was worth to the buyer. Two — wrote the outcome into the contract, not the hours. Three — refused any scope beyond the outcome, with a clean add-on for extras."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — when you sell time, you cap your own growth at the hours in the week. Outcomes don't cap."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Comment OUTCOME for the template"
```

## Worked Example 3 — Close Rate Shift

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "Close rate: 22% → 48%. One change."
VO: "Before I changed this one thing, my close rate was twenty-two percent. After, forty-eight. Watch."

0:03-0:10 | Split-screen: two call-workflow diagrams — old vs. new.
VO: "The old flow — discovery call, follow-up, pitch call, objection handling, close. Five touchpoints."

0:10-0:20 | New flow on screen.
VO: "The new flow — one call. Twenty-five minutes. Outcome, mechanism, price, guarantee, single next step."

0:20-0:35 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The old flow was losing deals in the gaps. Every 'let me think' between calls was a deal walking."

0:35-0:50 | Three mechanism cards.
TEXT 1: "Collapsed 5 calls into 1"
TEXT 2: "Put the price on minute 22"
TEXT 3: "Asked for the close, not the decision"
VO: "One — collapsed five calls into one. Two — the price comes on minute twenty-two, not minute fifty. Three — asked for the close directly. 'Are you in?' Not 'take some time to think.'"

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — close rates don't get better with more touchpoints. They get better with fewer gaps."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more"
```

## Worked Example 4 — Two Emails, Two Conversion Rates

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera, two-up email screenshots visible.
TEXT: "Email A: 2% → Email B: 11%. One change."
VO: "The email on the left converted at two percent. The email on the right — eleven. One change."

0:03-0:10 | Full-screen of email A — solid product-announce email.
VO: "Email A. Three paragraphs of context. Product announcement. Three CTAs at the bottom."

0:10-0:20 | Full-screen of email B.
VO: "Email B. Three sentences. One specific outcome by a specific date. One CTA."

0:20-0:35 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "Same list. Same product. Same week. One rewrote the subject and the first three sentences around the outcome the buyer would have by Friday."

0:35-0:50 | Three text cards breaking down the specific differences.
TEXT 1: "Subject line: outcome + date"
TEXT 2: "First sentence: specific number"
TEXT 3: "One CTA, not three"
VO: "Subject — the outcome plus the date. First sentence — the specific number the buyer would see. One CTA, not three. That was the entire difference."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — email CVR is not about length. It's about the first five percent of the email being about the reader's Friday, not your Monday."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Comment EMAIL for the template"
```

## Worked Example 5 — Landing Page Teardown

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "My landing page 8 months ago vs. today"
VO: "This was my landing page eight months ago. This is my landing page today. I have no idea how I slept at night."

0:03-0:10 | Full-screen of old landing page — long, busy, multiple offers.
VO: "Old page. Three offers. Two calls-to-action. Five testimonials scattered. Fourteen hundred words above the fold."

0:10-0:20 | Full-screen of new landing page — single offer, clean hero, three proof rows.
VO: "Today. One offer. One call-to-action. Three proof rows, each for a different starting stage."

0:20-0:35 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "Same product. Same audience. Same traffic. CVR went from one-point-one percent to three-point-four."

0:35-0:50 | Three mechanism cards.
TEXT 1: "One offer, not three"
TEXT 2: "Three isomorphic proofs, not five vanity ones"
TEXT 3: "Hero fits above fold, not 1,400 words"
VO: "One — one offer, not three. Two — three proofs, each one picked to match a specific starting stage my buyer comes from. Three — the hero fits above the fold. Everything in the fold communicates the single offer."

0:50-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — landing pages don't convert on length. They convert on clarity of the one thing being sold to the right one person."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more page teardowns"
```

## Re-Engage Pattern

- T+24h pinned comment: a specific number the reel didn't include ("the cost of those 3 changes was one weekend").
- T+48h follow-up reel: take one of the three mechanisms and make it its own reel.
- T+7d long-form: full teardown on YouTube.

## Hand-off to Editor

- Before/after framing is clean — color-grade of "before" can be slightly cooler, "after" warmer, but not so dramatic it reads as manipulation.
- Split-screens are proportional — neither side visually dominates.
- Dashboard / artifact numbers are legible at phone-screen size. Zoom if necessary.
- Transitions between before / after / bridge are hard cuts, not flourishes.
