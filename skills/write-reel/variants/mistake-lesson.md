# Reel Variant — Mistake / Lesson

> **Signature move.** The creator names a specific, expensive, painful mistake they made — with the dollar cost or time cost visible — and then extracts the generalizable rule. Tension is "how expensive was it." Resolution is the rule that cost the creator the money to learn. The reel sells earned authority, not a product.

## When to Use

- The creator has a specific, documentable mistake in their own history that is safe to talk about now.
- Pillar is Myth-Bust, Hard-Won Lesson, or Personal Narrative.
- Conversion stage is Awareness or Consideration — building creator credibility through "I paid to learn this so you don't have to."
- The mistake is safely in the past (6+ months minimum) — active-mistake content reads as crisis content, not lesson content.

## When NOT to Use

- The mistake involves other parties who would be identifiable and hurt by the reel.
- The mistake is still unresolved — the creator doesn't yet know what the lesson actually is.
- The mistake is so severe it signals lack of judgment rather than hard-won wisdom.
- The niche is regulated and the mistake involves compliance / legal / medical issues.

## Structure — 60-Second Grid

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:03 | Cost Hook | "I lost $X on this mistake" or "This mistake cost me N months" |
| 0:03-0:10 | The Setup | What the creator was doing that led to the mistake |
| 0:10-0:25 | The Mistake | What specifically went wrong |
| 0:25-0:40 | The Lesson Learned | The rule the creator now holds |
| 0:40-0:50 | How It Applies Now | A concrete current example of the rule in action |
| 0:50-0:58 | Takeaway / Invitation | One line the viewer can take away |
| 0:58-1:00 | CTA or Loop | Follow or re-state the cost |

## Hook Patterns (5 specific openers)

1. **Dollar-cost mistake.** "I lost forty thousand dollars on a hire I made in six minutes. Here's the rule I use now."
2. **Time-cost mistake.** "I spent fourteen months building the wrong thing. Here's what I should have built instead."
3. **Relationship-cost mistake.** "I almost lost my best customer over a boundary I didn't set. Here's the boundary I set today."
4. **Identity-cost mistake.** "I spent four years trying to be someone I'm not. Here's what cost me."
5. **Opportunity-cost mistake.** "I said no to a hundred-thousand-dollar contract in twenty-twenty-two. Worst decision of that year."

## Body Patterns

### A. Situation → Mistake → Lesson → Now (default)

- Situation: What the creator was doing. Neutral tone.
- Mistake: The specific decision that went wrong. Honest, specific, no self-pity.
- Lesson: The rule the creator now holds.
- Now: A current example of the rule being applied — proves the lesson was internalized.

### B. The "I Made This Three Times" Pattern

- The creator admits the mistake wasn't a one-time thing.
- Describes the first time briefly.
- Describes how it happened again.
- Describes how it happened a third time.
- Then — finally — the lesson.

Works well because it defuses the "you got lucky and learned once" objection. Most real lessons take multiple repetitions.

### C. The Externalized Lesson (client-based)

- Creator tells the story of watching someone else make the mistake.
- Creator had made the same mistake themselves years earlier.
- The specific pattern repeats.
- The rule that both creator and client eventually learned.

Use sparingly — too often and the creator is always "the wise one," which erodes trust.

## CTA and Loop Patterns

- "Follow for more hard-won lessons." — authority-building.
- "Comment the mistake that cost you the most." — engagement-forward.
- "DM me if you want the checklist I built after this one." — asset capture.
- Loop pattern — the closing line mirrors the opening cost claim. "Forty thousand dollars in six minutes. Never making that mistake again." Re-hook for replay weight.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- Three-plus `phrases_to_use` from Compartment 1.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Voice must sound reflective, not self-flagellating. "I was a fool" reads as fishing for sympathy. "I skipped a step I know better than to skip" reads as earned lesson.

## Specificity Floor

Hook Specificity ≥ 8. The floor:

- The cost is specific — dollar, time, or relationship — not vague "big mistake."
- The mistake is specific — one decision, one moment, one omission.
- The lesson is specific and actionable — not "think before you act" but "never hire in under six conversations."

## Visual Brief

- 0:00-0:03 — Creator on-camera, direct-to-lens. Text overlay with the cost.
- 0:03-0:10 — Cut to b-roll of the context (office, workspace, calendar, document).
- 0:10-0:25 — Creator on-camera for the mistake. Keep tone level, not dramatic.
- 0:25-0:40 — Text card with the rule. Creator voice-over.
- 0:40-0:50 — B-roll of the creator applying the rule now — a specific artifact.
- 0:50-0:58 — Creator direct-to-lens for the takeaway.
- 0:58-1:00 — Text-overlay CTA or cost-recap.

## Platform Adaptations

- Instagram Reels: save-forward — the rule at the end should be one bookmarkable sentence.
- TikTok: pattern interrupt at 0:00 — the cost flashes on screen as a number.
- YouTube Shorts: title is the cost + mistake literal. "$40K in 6 minutes — the hiring mistake" — title is the claim.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- No defamation — if other parties are implicated, they're anonymized and unidentifiable.
- No active disputes — mistake is safely resolved, not in litigation.
- If money figures are quoted, they're verifiable.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Too dramatic.** The reel reads as crisis content, not lesson content. Viewers feel manipulated.
- **Too generic lesson.** The lesson applies to literally anything. Specific mistakes produce specific lessons. Keep the specificity.
- **No "now" section.** The creator names the mistake but doesn't show the rule in action today. Viewers suspect the lesson wasn't really learned.
- **Self-flagellation.** Creator dwells on the mistake for too long. 15 seconds max on the mistake itself. The lesson and application are the payoff.
- **Fabricated mistake.** The creator invents a mistake for content. Detected fast — lacks the texture of a real wound. Kills trust.

## Worked Example 1 — $40K Hire in 6 Minutes

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "Lost $40K on a hire. 6-minute decision."
VO: "I lost forty thousand dollars on a hire I made in six minutes. Here's the rule I use now."

0:03-0:10 | Cut to b-roll of resume / calendar / contract.
VO: "A candidate came recommended. Impressive resume. Previous role at a name-brand. Conversation went smoothly. I was excited to move. I made the offer in six minutes on a Zoom call."

0:10-0:25 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "The mistake: no second conversation"
VO: "The mistake — no second conversation. No reference check in enough depth. No sample work. The resume did the sell. My excitement did the rest."

0:25-0:40 | Text card with the rule.
TEXT: "No hire under 6 conversations"
VO: "The rule now — no hire under six conversations. Not six calls. Six substantive conversations. At least two involve other people on my team. At least one involves a paid trial piece of work."

0:40-0:50 | B-roll of creator reviewing a paid trial from a recent candidate.
VO: "Last hire I made — six conversations. Paid trial over two weeks. Three references called in depth. Didn't need to let them go later."

0:50-0:58 | Creator direct-to-lens.
VO: "The rule cost me forty thousand dollars. Pass it along so it costs you zero."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more hard-won lessons"
```

## Worked Example 2 — 14 Months on the Wrong Product

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "14 months building the wrong thing"
VO: "I spent fourteen months building the wrong thing. Here's what I should have built instead."

0:03-0:10 | B-roll of old product interface / codebase / marketing site.
VO: "I was convinced the market needed a tool. I spent fourteen months building it. Beautiful tool. Nobody wanted it."

0:10-0:25 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "The mistake: I skipped the pre-sell"
VO: "The mistake — I skipped the pre-sell. I built before selling. I never tested whether a single person would pay for this version of the thing before I committed a year of build."

0:25-0:40 | Text card with the rule.
TEXT: "Pre-sell 10 customers before building"
VO: "The rule now — pre-sell ten customers before building. Get ten people to pay a deposit for the thing. If I can't get ten deposits, the thing isn't worth building yet."

0:40-0:50 | B-roll of current product — pre-sold to 47 people before build started.
VO: "Current product — forty-seven deposits before writing a line of code. Now the product exists because customers paid for it to exist."

0:50-0:58 | Creator direct-to-lens.
VO: "Fourteen months. Taught me the rule that makes every project faster and more honest."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more"
```

## Worked Example 3 — Lost Best Customer Over Boundary

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "Almost lost my best customer"
VO: "I almost lost my best customer over a boundary I didn't set. Here's the boundary I set today."

0:03-0:10 | B-roll of Slack / email screen.
VO: "Long-term customer. High-paying. Started messaging me at ten PM expecting same-day replies. I replied. Pattern got worse."

0:10-0:25 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "The mistake: I replied at 10 PM"
VO: "The mistake — I replied at ten PM the first time. Then eleven. Then midnight on a Saturday. Each reply trained the customer that my time had no shape."

0:25-0:40 | Text card with the rule.
TEXT: "One window. Every customer. Written into the contract."
VO: "The rule now — one response window. Every customer. Written into the contract. Business-hours reply, twenty-four-hour commitment. Nothing outside that window."

0:40-0:50 | B-roll of current onboarding email with the response-window line bolded.
VO: "Current onboarding email — response-window line bolded. Every customer sees it before they sign. Fewer objections than I expected. Most customers prefer the clarity."

0:50-0:58 | Creator direct-to-lens.
VO: "Nearly lost the customer. Kept the lesson. The lesson saves the next customer."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more boundary lessons"
```

```
VO: "Current content is slower. Longer. Less produced. And the audience — the audience that shows up now is the one that would have shown up four years ago if I'd just been honest."

```

## Worked Example 5 — Said No to $100K Contract

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "Said no to $100K in 2022. Worst call of the year."
VO: "I said no to a hundred-thousand-dollar contract in twenty-twenty-two. Worst decision of that year."

0:03-0:10 | B-roll of email screen / proposal template.
VO: "A partner offered a hundred-thousand-dollar project. I said no because I was 'focused.' I was actually just afraid of the scope."

0:10-0:25 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "The mistake: I called it focus. It was fear."
VO: "The mistake — I called it 'focus.' It was fear. Focus is saying no to the unrelated thing. Fear is saying no to the adjacent thing because you're not sure you can deliver."

0:25-0:40 | Text card with the rule.
TEXT: "Name the no. Focus or fear?"
VO: "The rule now — every no gets named. Is this no a focus no or a fear no. If it's a fear no, I go find out what I don't know, then decide."

0:40-0:50 | B-roll of a current contract that was a fear-no converted to yes.
VO: "This year — three contracts that would have been fear-nos two years ago. All delivered. All expanded the business in ways I wouldn't have seen otherwise."

0:50-0:58 | Creator direct-to-lens.
VO: "Hundred thousand dollars taught me to name the no. Now the no gets honest."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more"
```

## Re-Engage Pattern

- T+24h pinned comment: a second, smaller mistake from the same era.
- T+48h follow-up reel: the one artifact the mistake produced (a contract, a checklist, a new template) — "here's what came out of that mistake."
- T+7d long-form: YouTube video that expands the story into 10-15 minutes of context and detail.

## Hand-off to Editor

- Creator direct-to-lens is primary — 60% of the reel at minimum.
- B-roll is illustrative, not flashy. No stock footage. Real creator artifacts.
- Text cards for the rule are simple, unadorned, large font.
- Music is absent or extremely low — lesson content needs space to land.
- Pacing is slower than other variants — viewers need time to feel the weight.
