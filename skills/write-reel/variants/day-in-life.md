# Reel Variant — Day in the Life

> **Signature move.** A sequence of 4-7 vignettes from a real day, compressed to 60 seconds. Not aspirational. Not a morning routine. A real day — including the boring parts — narrated with voice-over that names what is actually happening and what it teaches. The reel sells identity association, not a product.

## When to Use

- Pillar is Lifestyle, Personality, or Cult-of-Creator.
- Conversion stage is Awareness — building identity association with the creator's way of working.
- The creator's daily reality is distinct enough to be interesting (founder running a specific type of business, parent juggling specific demands, athlete with specific schedule, creator with unusual rhythm).
- Days of the creator's week have repeatable shape — this reel should be re-playable as a weekly / monthly cadence.

## When NOT to Use

- The creator's day is mostly meetings the viewer can't access or relate to.
- Showing the day would expose proprietary information (client names, trade secrets).
- The creator is in a niche where "lifestyle content" cheapens the brand (B2B enterprise, regulated industries).
- The "day" is a one-off production day, not a real representative day.

## Structure — 60-Second Grid

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:03 | Day-Frame Hook | "A Tuesday running a [specific thing]" |
| 0:03-0:12 | Vignette 1 (morning) | 7-10s scene, what starts the day |
| 0:12-0:22 | Vignette 2 | Next key moment |
| 0:22-0:35 | Vignette 3 (core work) | The central work block of the day |
| 0:35-0:45 | Vignette 4 | End-of-day moment or decision |
| 0:45-0:55 | The Rule / Identity Line | What this shape of day makes the creator |
| 0:55-1:00 | CTA or Loop | Follow or re-state day-frame |

## Hook Patterns (5 specific openers)

1. **Day-frame specific.** "A Tuesday running a five-person content team." Names the specific identity.
2. **Role-reveal.** "A day in the life of a founder who doesn't take meetings before noon."
3. **Constraint-frame.** "A full work-day with a toddler at home. Founder edition."
4. **Outcome-frame.** "What a $100K-month Tuesday actually looks like. Hour by hour."
5. **Season-frame.** "Day 3 of a 7-day cart launch. Nothing on schedule until the cart-open."

## Body Patterns

### A. Four Vignettes, Each Named (default)

- Four real scenes, 10-12 seconds each.
- Each scene has a text overlay naming the moment ("8:14 — coffee + inbox review," "11:03 — first sales call," "14:30 — editing session," "19:00 — cart-close meeting").
- Creator voice-over for each scene says what's actually happening and one thing it teaches.

### B. The One-Block Deep Dive

- Instead of four vignettes, pick one 60-90 minute block and show that.
- "What my 9-10:30 AM work block actually looks like."
- Deeper, less breadth.

Works when the creator's edge is a specific block (deep work, sales calls, content production) and the audience wants inside that.

### C. The Chronological Compression

- Every 10 seconds is one hour of the day.
- Fast-paced. Text overlays mark the hour.
- Works for 10-14 hour day compression.

Good for founders whose identity is "working a full day in [specific field]."

## CTA and Loop Patterns

- "Follow for more days like this." — low-friction.
- "Comment Tuesday if you want me to post this weekly." — engagement test.
- "DM me if you want my schedule template." — asset capture.
- Loop pattern — end with a scene that matches the first scene (morning coffee at the end too), creating a day-complete feeling.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- Three-plus `phrases_to_use` from Compartment 1.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Voice-over tone is conversational, not narrated. "I woke up at 7" beats "The day began at 7."

## Specificity Floor

Hook Specificity ≥ 8. The floor:

- Day-frame names a specific identity ("founder running a five-person content team" not "busy entrepreneur").
- Each vignette has a specific timestamp.
- The rule at the end is a specific identity claim, not a generic "work hard."

## Visual Brief

- 0:00-0:03 — Wide establishing shot of the day's setting. Text overlay names the day-frame.
- 0:03-0:12 — Morning vignette. Handheld or mounted camera. Text: timestamp.
- 0:12-0:22 — Second vignette. Different location / angle than morning.
- 0:22-0:35 — Core-work vignette. Close-ups of the actual work.
- 0:35-0:45 — End-of-day vignette. Often a decision or conversation.
- 0:45-0:55 — Creator on-camera or voice-over with b-roll — the rule.
- 0:55-1:00 — Final scene — could be a "full-circle" shot or a CTA.

## Platform Adaptations

- Instagram Reels: save-forward if the day-frame resonates (people save identity-association content).
- TikTok: works on TikTok because FYP loves "day in life" content. First 0.5 seconds should be the specific identity claim.
- YouTube Shorts: title is the day-frame literal. "A Tuesday running a 5-person content team" — Shorts title is the identity claim.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- No client faces, names, or financial details shown without permission.
- If the day includes team members, they've consented to being filmed.
- No regulated-niche exposure (medical, legal, financial advice given on-camera).

## Common Failure Modes

- **Aspirational instead of real.** The day is beautifully lit, perfectly scheduled, obviously staged. Viewers tune out within 10 seconds.
- **No narrative through-line.** Four random scenes with no connecting thread. The reel feels like filler content.
- **Generic identity claim.** "A day in the life of an entrepreneur" — every reel on TikTok is this. Needs specificity.
- **Missing the boring parts.** The day is edited to only show highlights. Viewers sense the omission and the reel feels curated.
- **Over-narrated.** Voice-over fills every second explaining what's happening. Let the footage breathe. 70% narration, 30% ambient.

## Worked Example 1 — Tuesday Running a 5-Person Content Team

```
0:00-0:03 | Wide shot of creator at laptop, calendar visible over shoulder.
TEXT: "A Tuesday running a 5-person content team"
VO: "A Tuesday running a five-person content team."

0:03-0:12 | Morning vignette — 8:14 AM, coffee, inbox, Loom playing.
TEXT: "8:14 AM. Loom from the editor. Changes needed."
VO: "Eight-fourteen. Watching a Loom from my editor. She has changes to a video I thought was done. Changes are right."

0:12-0:22 | Second vignette — 10:00 AM, Zoom call with writer.
TEXT: "10:00 AM. Brief review with writer."
VO: "Ten. Thirty-minute brief review with a writer. One piece got redirected. One piece got killed. Neither got greenlit in the call."

0:22-0:35 | Core work vignette — 11:30 AM, creator writing.
TEXT: "11:30 AM. 90 minutes. Writing."
VO: "Eleven-thirty. Ninety minutes blocked. Writing. Phone face-down. Door closed. This is the block the week turns on."

0:35-0:45 | End-of-day vignette — 17:00, review meeting with team.
TEXT: "5:00 PM. Weekly team review."
VO: "Five PM. Weekly team review. Thirty minutes. What shipped. What didn't. What's shipping Friday."

0:45-0:55 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — running a content team isn't making content. It's protecting one ninety-minute block a day and using the rest to unblock five other people."

0:55-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more team-building teardowns"
```

## Worked Example 2 — Founder Who Doesn't Take Morning Meetings

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator at desk, dim light, coffee.
TEXT: "Founder who doesn't take morning meetings"
VO: "A day in the life of a founder who doesn't take meetings before noon."

0:03-0:14 | 7 AM - 11 AM vignettes compressed.
TEXT: "7 AM - 11 AM. Deep work window."
VO: "Seven to eleven. No calls. No Slack. Writing, product decisions, long emails, strategy documents. This is where the business gets made."

0:14-0:24 | Noon onward — scheduled calls.
TEXT: "12 PM. First call."
VO: "Noon. First call. By this point I've done four hours of work. The calls get the leftover brain, which is fine — the calls are coordination, not creation."

0:24-0:38 | Afternoon — three calls back-to-back.
TEXT: "2-4 PM. 3 calls back-to-back."
VO: "Two to four. Three calls. A hiring conversation, a client onboarding, a partner check-in. All coordination. All could be async but aren't yet."

0:38-0:48 | Evening — end of work.
TEXT: "5:30 PM. Work closed."
VO: "Five-thirty. Laptop closed. No evening work. Evening work is where the next day gets wrecked."

0:48-0:55 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — the morning is the asset. Spend it on the work only you can do. Everything else waits."

0:55-1:00 | Loop close.
TEXT: "Founder who doesn't take morning meetings"
```

## Re-Engage Pattern

- T+24h pinned comment: the one scene the creator cut from the reel because it was "too real."
- T+48h follow-up reel: a specific block from the day expanded into its own reel.
- T+7d long-form: a YouTube video that expands the day into a 20-minute walkthrough.

## Hand-off to Editor

- Footage is unpolished — handheld camera, natural light preferred.
- Color grade is subtle — avoid Instagram-filter aesthetic.
- Music is ambient, volume low. Voice-over primary.
- Text overlays are timestamp-forward ("8:14 AM" large) plus brief descriptor.
- Transitions are hard cuts between vignettes — no flourish transitions.
