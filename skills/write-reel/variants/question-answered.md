# Reel Variant — Question Answered

> **Signature move.** A specific question the audience actually asks is answered directly in 60 seconds, with the question itself shown on screen as the hook. Tension is "does the creator give a real answer or a non-answer." Resolution is the direct answer plus the reasoning. The reel sells trust and search-alignment, not a product.

## When to Use

- Pillar is How-To, FAQ, or Audience Service.
- Conversion stage is Awareness or Consideration — the viewer had a question the creator is now answering.
- The creator has a specific, defensible answer — not a generic "it depends."
- The question is one that the audience actually asks (from DMs, comments, email, or search queries), not one the creator invents.

## When NOT to Use

- The creator doesn't actually know the answer and is winging it.
- The answer is nuanced enough to require 5+ minutes — a reel compresses too much nuance.
- The question is niche enough that the reach will be low (use for long-form instead).
- The question is better answered with a tool or a doc than a video.

## Structure — 60-Second Grid

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:03 | Question Hook | The question on screen verbatim, creator reads it |
| 0:03-0:08 | Short Direct Answer | One sentence direct answer |
| 0:08-0:25 | The Reasoning | Why the answer is the answer |
| 0:25-0:40 | The Nuance | The "it depends" — handled specifically, not wavy |
| 0:40-0:50 | One Concrete Example | A case where this answer played out |
| 0:50-0:58 | Restate + Rule | Compressed answer + takeaway |
| 0:58-1:00 | CTA or Invite More | Follow or "ask next question in comments" |

## Hook Patterns (5 specific openers)

1. **Question-on-screen hook.** Viewer sees the question verbatim ("Should I raise my prices before I have proof?") and creator reads it aloud.
2. **"I get this DM" hook.** "I get this DM every week. Let me answer it once." Frames the reel as service.
3. **Reverse-question hook.** "Here's a question I think you should be asking instead." Reframes a common question into the better one.
4. **Category-question hook.** "Every founder at sixty-K monthly asks this specific question. Here's the answer." Names the stage.
5. **Comment-reference hook.** "A comment on yesterday's post asked this. Answering it here." Community-loop.

## Body Patterns

### A. Direct Answer → Reasoning → Nuance (default)

- Give the direct answer in one sentence. No throat-clearing.
- Explain why it is the answer. 2-3 specific reasons.
- Handle the nuance — the "it depends" factors — specifically. Name the exact condition that flips the answer.

### B. The "Most People Answer Wrong" Pattern

- Show the wrong answer most people give.
- Explain why it's wrong.
- Give the right answer.
- Justify it.

Works when the question has a strong conventional wisdom that the creator disagrees with.

### C. The "Two Situations" Framework

- Sometimes the answer is different depending on a single variable.
- Situation A, the answer is X.
- Situation B, the answer is Y.
- The variable is Z.

Good for questions that are legitimately conditional on one clean branch.

## CTA and Loop Patterns

- "Follow for more direct answers." — positioning claim.
- "Comment your next question." — content-loop.
- "DM the question you're stuck on, I'll do a reel on the best one." — asset-capture loop.
- Loop pattern — last line is the one-sentence direct answer repeated. Viewers who replay hear the answer twice.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- Three-plus `phrases_to_use` from Compartment 1.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Voice-over is direct, not consultative. No "great question" opener. No "it depends" as the answer. Give the answer first.

## Specificity Floor

Hook Specificity ≥ 8. The floor:

- The question is specific and quoted verbatim from a real source.
- The answer is specific — a number, a rule, a named decision.
- The reasoning is specific, not "general wisdom."

## Visual Brief

- 0:00-0:03 — Question text on screen, large, readable. Creator voice-over reads it.
- 0:03-0:08 — Creator on-camera, direct-to-lens, one-sentence answer. Text overlay: the answer compressed.
- 0:08-0:25 — Creator on-camera with b-roll of the reasoning points.
- 0:25-0:40 — Creator on-camera for the nuance. Text overlay names the conditional variable.
- 0:40-0:50 — B-roll of the example — a dashboard, a screenshot, a case.
- 0:50-0:58 — Creator direct-to-lens for the restate + rule.
- 0:58-1:00 — Text-overlay CTA.

## Platform Adaptations

- Instagram Reels: question-on-screen is IG-friendly because the caption can also repeat the question and the answer for save-ability.
- TikTok: question flashes large in first 0.5 seconds. TikTok users respond well to question reels because the FYP loves interrogative content.
- YouTube Shorts: title is the question literal. "Should I raise my prices before I have proof?" — Shorts title is the question.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- No income guarantees in the answer — "you'll make X if you do Y" is banned.
- If the answer references a specific law, regulation, or medical / legal advice, a disclaimer.
- Question sources (DM, comment, search) are not identifying — no usernames, no private details.

## Common Failure Modes

- **"It depends" as the answer.** Viewers feel cheated. If the answer is genuinely conditional, name the specific conditions clearly, then give both answers.
- **Throat-clearing before the answer.** The creator explains the context for 30 seconds before answering. By then, viewers churned. Give the answer in the first 8 seconds, then explain.
- **Question that only the creator finds interesting.** The viewer wasn't asking this. Reel gets low reach. Pull questions from real sources.
- **Answer that's wrong for the stage of the asker.** The creator gives an $80K-MRR-stage answer to a question from a $2K-MRR-stage viewer.
- **Performative humility.** "That's a great question, I'll try my best to answer." Wasted seconds. Ban the entire pattern.

## Worked Example 1 — Raise Prices Before Proof

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera, question text overlaid.
TEXT: "Should I raise prices before I have proof?"
VO: "Question from the DMs. Should I raise prices before I have proof?"

0:03-0:08 | Direct answer.
TEXT: "Yes. And here's how to know it's time."
VO: "Yes. And here's how to know it's time."

0:08-0:25 | Reasoning.
TEXT: "3 signals: demand, capacity, clarity"
VO: "Three signals say raise. One — demand outpaces your calendar. Two — you have capacity to deliver at the higher tier. Three — you can articulate why the higher price produces a different outcome, not just the same thing with a bigger number."

0:25-0:40 | Nuance.
TEXT: "Don't raise if: proof gap > 6 months"
VO: "The nuance — don't raise if your last closed client was more than six months ago. Raising prices on stale proof gets you a longer decision cycle on new prospects. Close a few more at the current price, then raise."

0:40-0:50 | B-roll of creator's own pricing change + Stripe row.
VO: "I raised mine from three to ten last year. All three signals present. Three closes in the first week. That's what the signals feel like when they're there."

0:50-0:58 | Creator direct-to-lens.
VO: "The rule — price follows demand, not permission. But wait for recent proof."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Comment your next question"
```

0:08-0:25 | Reasoning.

0:25-0:40 | Nuance.

VO: "Here's the template I used for my first ten. Opened with a named observation. Closed with a single next step. Three-minute max runtime."

0:50-0:58 | Creator direct-to-lens.
VO: "The rule — first clients come from ten specific Looms, not from a hundred generic posts."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.

```

0:03-0:08 | Direct answer.

0:08-0:25 | Reasoning.

VO: "Low-ticket, say five hundred to two thousand dollars — eight to twelve minutes. The buyer doesn't need a full belief-install, they need proof and a price. 0:25-0:40 | Nuance.

0:40-0:50 | B-roll of two VSL dashboards — one short, one long.
VO: "Three-thousand-dollar offer to a problem-aware audience — twenty-eight minutes. Ten-thousand-dollar offer to a most-aware audience — twenty minutes. The most-aware audience earned the shorter runtime through their own awareness."

0:50-0:58 | Creator direct-to-lens.
VO: "The rule — VSL runtime is a function of price and awareness. Optimize to both, not one."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Follow for more VSL teardowns"
```

## Re-Engage Pattern

- T+0 to T+90 min: creator replies to follow-up questions in comments. Loops into more content.
- T+24h pinned comment: the one answer that didn't fit in the reel ("actually, one more condition for when this doesn't apply").
- T+48h: save the best follow-up comment as the next reel's hook.
- T+7d long-form: a YouTube video answering the same question in 15-20 minutes.

## Hand-off to Editor

- Question-on-screen is large, readable, high contrast.
- Direct answer text card is bold and held for 5-7 seconds.
- B-roll is illustrative of the reasoning — avoid random stock.
- Music is minimal. Voice-over primary.
- Pacing is faster than Mistake/Lesson but slower than Speed Build.
