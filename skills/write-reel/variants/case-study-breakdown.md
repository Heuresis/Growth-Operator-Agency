# Reel Variant — Case Study Breakdown

> **Signature move.** A specific client outcome reverse-engineered into 3 visible mechanisms. The hook is the outcome (dollar figure, time compression, or identity shift). The body is the decomposition — three things that produced the outcome. The close is the rule that generalizes. The reel sells the mechanism, with the case study as the proof asset.

## When to Use

- The creator has a specific, documented client outcome with permission to reference.
- Pillar is Case Study or Proof.
- Conversion stage is Decision or late Consideration — the viewer needs social proof that is isomorphic to their own situation.
- The case study is recent enough to be credible (within 12 months preferred).

## When NOT to Use

- No permission from the client to reference the outcome (even anonymized, some clients don't want the shape of their result public).
- The outcome is not actually explained by the mechanism the creator is selling — viewers detect the mismatch.
- The case study is non-isomorphic to the audience (a $2M/mo client's case study doesn't convert $50K/mo prospects).
- The creator has already posted this case study repeatedly — audience fatigue kicks in.

## Structure — 60-Second Grid

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:03 | Outcome Hook | The specific result — dollar, time, or identity shift |
| 0:03-0:08 | Starting Situation | Who the client was, what stage |
| 0:08-0:18 | Mechanism 1 | First specific thing that drove the outcome |
| 0:18-0:30 | Mechanism 2 | Second specific thing |
| 0:30-0:42 | Mechanism 3 | Third specific thing |
| 0:42-0:52 | Proof On Screen | The actual artifact — revenue screen, testimonial clip, dashboard |
| 0:52-0:58 | Rule That Generalizes | The takeaway the viewer can apply |
| 0:58-1:00 | CTA or Loop | Single action |

## Hook Patterns (5 specific openers)

1. **Outcome + client role + time.** "A SaaS founder went from two calls a week to thirty applications in nine days. Here's what we did."
2. **Identity shift + proof.** "A freelancer became a $20K-retainer agency in six months. Three specific decisions."
3. **Before-after numbers.** "From $8K/mo to $47K/mo in one product cycle. Three levers pulled."
4. **Compression claim.** "We closed in seventy-two hours what usually takes two months. Here's the sequence."
5. **Reversal claim.** "A creator was about to shut down. Ninety days later, best month ever. Three things changed."

## Body Patterns

### A. Three Mechanisms in Parallel (default)

Each mechanism gets 10-12 seconds. Each has the same internal structure:

- Name the mechanism (e.g., "We rewrote the offer from monthly to per-outcome.")
- One detail (e.g., "Specifically, we moved pricing from $3,000/mo to $15,000 per outcome delivered in ninety days.")
- One visible proof (e.g., a pricing page screenshot or a new contract).

### B. Sequential Mechanism Narrative

Each mechanism is described in the order it happened. Works when the case study has a clear sequence where each step unlocked the next.

- Mechanism 1 was required before Mechanism 2 could happen.
- Mechanism 2 was required before Mechanism 3 could happen.
- The outcome is the arrival point.

Good for 60-90 day transformations where ordering matters.

### C. The Before / After / Because (compressed)

- Before state — one specific sentence.
- After state — one specific sentence.
- Because — three things, each 5-7 seconds.

Fastest variant, works for Awareness-stage audiences that don't have patience for sequential.

## CTA and Loop Patterns

- "Comment [word] and I'll send the full teardown." — data-capture move.
- "Follow for more case-study breakdowns." — pillar-building.
- Loop pattern — last line re-states the opener outcome. "From two calls to thirty applications. Nine days. That's the full breakdown." Works well for replay weight.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- Three-plus `phrases_to_use` from Compartment 1.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- If quoting the client, the quote is verbatim — not "they said something like." Exact wording or skip the quote.

## Specificity Floor

Hook Specificity ≥ 8. The floor:

- The outcome is a number or a named identity shift, never vague.
- The starting situation is named specifically — not "a client," but "a SaaS founder at $40K MRR."
- Each mechanism is specific enough that a viewer could attempt to replicate it.

## Visual Brief

- 0:00-0:03 — Creator on-camera. Text overlay states the outcome.
- 0:03-0:08 — B-roll of the client's category (SaaS dashboard, freelancer's desk, creator's content, etc.).
- 0:08-0:18 — Mechanism 1 on screen as a text card; creator on-camera explains; b-roll shows the artifact that represents the mechanism.
- 0:18-0:30 — Mechanism 2 — same treatment.
- 0:30-0:42 — Mechanism 3 — same treatment.
- 0:42-0:52 — Full-screen proof — Stripe row, dashboard chart, testimonial clip, contract.
- 0:52-0:58 — Creator on-camera for the rule.
- 0:58-1:00 — Text-overlay CTA.

## Platform Adaptations

- Instagram Reels: save-forward — the three mechanisms are listed as a mini-framework so viewers save. Caption is the full teardown in text.
- TikTok: faster pacing, first 0.5 seconds is the outcome number flashed.
- YouTube Shorts: title is the outcome + client type. "SaaS founder went from 2 calls to 30 applications in 9 days." The title is the claim literal.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- Client permission on file (written, dated, signed or email confirmation).
- No income guarantees — "a client" is a claim, "you can make this" is a guarantee.
- FTC disclosure if the case study is paid for the testimonial.
- Numbers verifiable — if the dollar figure is quoted, the underlying artifact exists and can be shown.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Non-isomorphic case study.** The client in the case study is at a different stage / niche / price tier than the viewer. Viewers don't see themselves, don't convert.
- **Vague mechanisms.** "We optimized their offer" is not a mechanism. Specific: "We moved pricing from per-month to per-outcome and rewrote the proposal template."
- **Outcome-mechanism mismatch.** The outcome happened mostly due to the client's own work; the creator's three mechanisms are real but didn't drive 80% of the outcome. Viewers sense over-claim.
- **No on-screen proof.** Case study without a visible artifact reads as anecdotal. Every Case Study Breakdown needs at least one visible proof asset.
- **Too many mechanisms.** Five or seven mechanisms flattens all of them. Three is the ceiling. If there are more, pick the three with the biggest contribution.

## Worked Example 1 — SaaS Founder from 2 Calls to 30 Applications

```
0:00-0:03 | Creator on-camera.
TEXT: "SaaS founder. 2 calls → 30 applications. 9 days."
VO: "A SaaS founder went from two calls a week to thirty applications in nine days. Here's what we did."

0:03-0:08 | B-roll of SaaS dashboard.
VO: "Forty thousand monthly recurring, stuck. Good product. No top-of-funnel mechanism."

0:08-0:18 | Creator on-camera. Text overlay: "Mechanism 1: Swapped the lead magnet for a qualifier"
VO: "First — we replaced the ebook with a diagnostic quiz. Seven questions. The output was a tier — 'ready,' 'close,' 'not yet.' 'Ready' got routed to a calendar."

0:18-0:30 | Creator on-camera. Text overlay: "Mechanism 2: Rewrote ads as outcome-first, not feature-first"
VO: "Second — we rewrote the ads. Out: product feature ads. In: outcome ads — 'thirty qualified demos a week without an SDR.' Same product. Different promise."

0:30-0:42 | Creator on-camera. Text overlay: "Mechanism 3: Turned the founder into the closer for the first 60 days"
VO: "Third — we made the founder the closer. No SDR relay. Every 'ready' from the quiz went straight to the founder's calendar. For sixty days. Founder saw every objection firsthand."

0:42-0:52 | Full-screen dashboard — the client's application counter, day-by-day from 2 to 30.
VO: "Day one — two. Day four — eleven. Day nine — thirty-one. Same product. Same founder. Three mechanisms."

0:52-0:58 | Creator on-camera.
VO: "The rule — you don't need more traffic. You need a qualifier, an outcome promise, and a founder who sees every objection."

0:58-1:00 | CTA.
TEXT: "Comment TEARDOWN for the full breakdown"
```

## Re-Engage Pattern

- T+0 to T+90 min: creator replies to the first 20 comments with specific detail.
- T+24h pinned comment: the fourth mechanism (what wasn't in the reel) — gives committed viewers bonus depth.
- T+48h follow-up reel: if a specific mechanism got the most comments, turn that one into its own reel.
- T+7d long-form: full YouTube teardown with the client on-camera if possible.

## Hand-off to Editor

- Mechanism cards are visually consistent — same font, same color treatment, same position on screen.
- Proof asset is redacted only for private information (names, exact bank numbers) — revenue figures and chart shapes stay visible.
- Music is minimal.
- B-roll matches the client's actual category (SaaS, freelance, creator, service business).
