# high-value stacked offer Applied to a fitness-operator rollup (January 2017)

> **Case:** the acquisition economist's a fitness-operator rollup, January 2017 turnaround
> **Operator:** the acquisition economist (@a portfolio holding company)
> **Framework applied:** The 15-step high-value stacked offer build from *a high-value offer canon: Complete Implementation Guide*
> **Outcome:** $100K → $1.5M monthly; $6.8M across 13 months; company sold for $46.2M in 2021

## Context

In January 2017, the acquisition economist was $10K in debt after three days of paid traffic burning $3,300/day. Sales reps were scoring 2-for-14, 0-for-7, 0-for-18. The offer itself wasn't the bottleneck - the closing script was - but rebuilding the closing playbook forced a parallel audit of the offer structure it was pitching. What emerged is the canonical high-value stacked offer the acquisition economist later taught: a single, un-shoppable offer that combined a bonus stack, a price anchor, a strong guarantee, and real scarcity. This example shows how the 15-step Implementation Guide produces that structure when applied to a concrete case.

## The Encoded Offer

**Offer Name (M-A-G-I-C applied):** "New Gym Owner 30-Day $100K Cash Injection Blueprint"
- **M**agnet: "New" - reason why anyone should care
- **A**vatar: Gym owners
- **G**oal: $100K cash injection
- **I**nterval: 30 days
- **C**ontainer: Blueprint

**Dream Outcome (Step 3):** Gym owner goes from $20K-$40K monthly gross to adding $100K in new member cash within 30 days, reinstating financial runway and removing the daily fear of missing rent.

**Core Deliverables (Step 7, trim-and-stack):**
1. 30-Day Challenge Sales System - the front-end offer script, pricing tiers, and objection map that converts cold local traffic to $500+ challenge sales.
2. Done-For-You Ad Creative Bank - proven ad templates, video scripts, and audience targeting parameters calibrated for local gym demographics.
3. Appointment Booking + Show Protocol - lead nurture sequence (later formalized as the ALAN Four Pillars) that gets leads to show up within 48-72 hours.
4. Closer Training Playbook - the exact objection-handling frameworks drilled daily in the the acquisition economist huddles that broke the 2-for-14 slump.

**Guarantee (Step 8, Performance tier):** "Add $100K in cash collected in 30 days or we work with you free until you do, plus refund your investment." This is a Performance Guarantee (strongest in the ladder), chosen because the economics of local gym challenges support it - the conversion math was knowable.

**Bonuses (Step 10, 3-5× price rule):**
- **Bonus 1 - The $10K-Month Ad Creative Vault** ($2,000 value) - problem solved: "I don't know what ads to run on Day 1."
- **Bonus 2 - Local Market Saturation Scripts** ($1,500 value) - problem solved: "How do I stand out from the three other gyms on this block?"
- **Bonus 3 - Front-Desk Close Training** ($1,500 value) - problem solved: "My front-desk staff can't close."
- **Bonus 4 - The 48-Hour Launch Checklist** ($500 value) - problem solved: "I don't know what to do first."

Total bonus value: $5,500 against a $3,000 core price - in the 3-5× band the Implementation Guide specifies.

**Scarcity + Urgency (Step 9, both real):**
- Scarcity: "We only onboard 10 gyms per cohort because each gets direct ad-account access and weekly call time with our closer-coach."
- Urgency: "This cohort closes [date] because the next cohort doesn't start until 30 days later - you'd miss the next earnings window."

**Value Stack Presentation (Step 13):**
- Core system: $3,000
- Bonus stack: $5,500
- Total Value: $8,500
- Price presentation: "Normally $8,500. Today $3,000."

**Guarantee-to-price ratio:** the Performance Guarantee removes financial risk entirely. A gym owner can buy in knowing the program either works for them or costs them nothing.

## Why This Is A Good Example

- **Shows all 15 steps producing a coherent single offer.** Most operators can name the acquisition economist's components but cannot assemble them without internal contradictions (a weak guarantee under a big promise, fake urgency under a real scarcity claim). This example is the assembled output - every piece reinforces every other.
- **Demonstrates the "denominator burn" the acquisition economist teaches.** The offer is visibly designed against the Value Equation's denominator: Time Delay is 30 days (fast), Effort & Sacrifice is reduced by DFY creative and scripts. The numerator (Dream Outcome = $100K) is specific and believable because Perceived Likelihood is high (real guarantee + real testimonials from the internal cohort).
- **Real scarcity/urgency, not manufactured.** The 10-gym cap and 30-day cohort gap both have operational causes. the acquisition economist's Step 9 rule ("must be true") is honored, which is why this offer didn't degrade over time the way fabricated-scarcity offers do.
