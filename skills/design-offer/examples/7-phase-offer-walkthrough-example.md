## Context

This example walks the 7-Phase Offer Architecture through a concrete build: a solo consultant offering B2B growth advisory, stuck below $50K/month, trying to productize a done-with-you engagement. The purpose of the example is to show the *decision logic* at each phase rather than the final offer artifact. the offer architect's rule is explicit: Phase 0 runs before any discovery work; most operators skip it and pay for it later. This walkthrough respects that sequencing.

## Phase 0: Pre-Discovery

**Economics-validation check (5 questions):**
1. Can customers be acquired for less than their lifetime value? - Yes; $800 CAC on LinkedIn outreach, target LTV $15K+.
2. Path to 3:1 LTV:CAC minimum? - Yes; 18:1 projected on DWY tier.
3. TAM size? - ~200K solo consultants doing $100K-$500K/year in the target niche.
4. Existing proof of demand? - Yes; adjacent programs (Sam Ovens, Consulting.com) at scale.
5. Unfair advantages? - Yes; operator has 12 years inside enterprise procurement - a rare POV.

**Strategic positioning check:** "What problem do you solve that no one else solves in exactly the same way?" → "I help solo consultants win enterprise retainers ($10K-$50K/mo) instead of project work, using the inside view I developed as the person on the *other side* of those contracts."

Articulable in one sentence. No red flags. Five green lights. Proceed.

## Phase 1: Deep Audience Discovery

**1.1 Psychographic:** Consultants 32-50, $150K-$400K income, 3am thoughts: "If I lose my two big clients this quarter, I'm back to project-pitching at conferences."

**1.2 Limiting-belief:** HELPLESS dominant (70%). "I know I should be selling retainers but I don't know how to price them or pitch them." Secondary WORTHLESS (30%): "8 years in and still can't break $30K/month."

**1.3 Awareness-level:** Solution-aware. Medium copy length. Approach: differentiation + mechanism.

**1.4 Desire:** "Stop hustling for proposals." Shadow desire: "Be the consultant other consultants look up to." Justification: economic (retainer revenue 3× more predictable).

**1.5 Economics audit:** Capability 8/10. Application 5/10. Time: low. Implication: DWY format, templated assets.

**Phase 1 checkpoint:** can we describe this consultant's 3am thoughts? Yes. Proceed.

## Phase 2: Offer Architecture

**2.1 Outcome definition:** From-state: project-pitching at $5K-$25K/project, no predictability. To-state: 3-5 enterprise retainer clients at $10K-$25K/month each. Timeframe: 180 days. Evidence: signed MSAs visible in their CRM. Feeling: "I'm no longer selling my services every 30 days."

**The the offer architect test:** "Go from the consultant who lives in Q4 pitch-mode to the consultant with 12-month revenue visibility." Does it make them feel something? Yes. Has offer-shape.

**2.2 Mechanism identification:** Named mechanism: "The Procurement-Inside Method" (proprietary process + unfair advantage: the operator spent 12 years inside enterprise procurement). Explainable in 30 seconds: "I'll show you how enterprise buyers actually evaluate consultants - from the side of the table I sat on - so you can pitch retainers that get past procurement instead of getting stuck in 'add this to the list of vendors we might consider.'"

**2.3 Capability removal:** "You don't need to learn enterprise sales methodology from scratch. You'll use the exact procurement-evaluation rubric I sat across the table with." Validates HELPLESS identity.

**2.4 Feedback loop:** Primary intrinsic. First deliverable is a Retainer Pitch Rewrite of one existing project proposal, returned in 72 hours. The consultant sends the rewrite to a warm lead that week. Response rate visibly improves. Immediate, unmistakable, on-path validation.

**2.5 Risk reversal (Excellent tier):** Money-back + keep the Rubric + Contract Template + 120-day service guarantee ("if no new retainer signed, another 120 days free") + first-win guaranteed in 30 days.

**2.6 Pricing (tiered default):**
- Tier 1 Foundation (DIY) $1,497. Ratio 1X.
- Tier 2 Accelerator (DWY) $4,997. Ratio ~3.3X. MOST POPULAR.
- Tier 3 Elite (DFY) $14,997. Ratio 10X. Operator attends first 2 pitch calls.

**2.7 Upsell ecosystem:** Immediate OTO Procurement Swipe File ($497). 7-day Quarterly Retainer Audit ($1,997). 30-day Account Expansion Playbook ($2,997). 90-day Mastermind ($15K). Continuity Monthly Procurement Brief ($97/mo).

**Phase 2 checkpoint:** can someone explain this offer's value in 60 seconds? Yes - the "Procurement-Inside Method" names the mechanism, the DWY tier anchors the commercial outcome, and the risk reversal closes the loop.

## Phase 3: Belief-Building Sequence

Authority: 12 years enterprise procurement experience. Evidence quality target: supreme tier - "Here's my exact 6-month journey helping [named client] go from 2 project clients to 4 retainer clients worth $780K/year, plus the before/after proposal documents." Repetition: 3-5× across email nurture, VSL, and sales page.

## Phase 4: Funnel Selection

Decision Point 1 (awareness-level, solution-aware): short VSL or sales page or application/book-a-call. Decision Point 2 (price-point $4,997 core): webinar or VSL-plus-application. Decision Point 3 (limiting belief HELPLESS): process clarity, step-by-step preview, capability-building messaging. Selected: **30-minute VSL → application → sales call.**

## Phase 5: Copy Creation

Copy opens with three stacked true statements: "Enterprise procurement departments evaluate consultants against rubrics you never see. Most consultants lose the deal before it reaches the decision-maker. This is the rubric - from someone who sat at that table for 12 years." Body: if/then chains from procurement-inside view → rubric adoption → retainer pitch → signed MSA.

## Phase 6: Traffic and Scaling

Aware market + paid + shorter timeline + higher front-end conversion. Phase 1 proof-of-concept: 10 manual closes via LinkedIn outreach. Phase 2 paid validation: $1,000-$5,000 LinkedIn Ads test. Phase 3 scaling: expand to YouTube + podcast appearances once unit economics hold.

## Why This Is A Good Example

- **Every phase has a decision, not just an artifact.** This is the offer architect's core distinction: the 7-phase architecture is a diagnostic process, not a fill-in-the-blank template. Phase 0 and Phase 1 consume the majority of the time; Phase 5 (copy) is 20% of the work.
- **The mechanism is named and defensible.** "Procurement-Inside Method" is Mechanism Type 2 (unfair advantage) - built on non-replicable operator experience. the offer architect's rule: if the mechanism can be named in 30 seconds with no fluff, it has offer-shape.
- **Capability removal before bonus stacking.** The offer does not add value through bonuses; it removes the barrier ("you don't need to learn enterprise sales methodology from scratch"). the offer architect: "the strongest offer move is not 'we include X more value' - it is 'you do not need to know how to do X.'"
