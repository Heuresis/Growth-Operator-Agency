# Live Cohort Launch — Archetype Playbook

> **When it wins:** Scheduled start date + real seat cap + community-based delivery. Belief installation via live teach + community peers. Common for $997–$15K cohort programs.

## When to pick this archetype

- Offer is delivered cohort-by-cohort (not individual enrollment anytime).
- You can support live teach + community engagement for the cohort's duration.
- Real seat cap exists (capacity OR community-size optimization).
- Launch cadence: 2–4 cohorts per year.
- Community dynamic is part of the offer value.

## Phase 1 — Pre-Launch (weeks -8 to -1)

### Week -8 to -4: Foundation
- Confirm cohort start date + end date
- Confirm seat cap + waitlist logic
- Finalize offer (price + payment plan + guarantee + bonuses)
- Finalize delivery calendar (live calls, modules, community cadence)
- Set revenue goal + breakeven math
- Team role assignments (host, community manager, support, marketing)

### Week -4 to -2: Assets
- Sales page / offer page published
- Webinar or cart-open-event script + slides
- Pre-launch email sequence (6–10 emails)
- Cart-open email sequence (5–10 emails)
- Cart-close email sequence (3–5 emails)
- Ad creative (if paid retargeting)
- Community platform configured + welcome assets
- Contract / onboarding documents ready

### Week -2 to -1: Warming
- Content cadence ramp (2–3 LinkedIn posts + X thread + podcast guest + YouTube video)
- List segmentation (warm vs cold)
- Partner / affiliate notifications sent
- Last-minute QA: checkout flow tested end-to-end
- Tech stack QA: email automation, triggers, delivery

## Phase 2 — Launch Day (cart opens)

### The morning of
- Cart opens email to list (8–10 AM typical)
- Social media posts across owned channels
- Personal DMs to 10–30 highest-probability close prospects
- Webinar or cart-open event (if that's the mechanism)
- Countdown / urgency elements live on sales page

### Throughout Day 1
- Monitor: opt-ins, cart adds, completed purchases, refund/chargeback signals
- Real-time support: live answer DMs / emails within 1 hour
- Stack testimonials / case studies in social as proof drops
- End-of-day summary email or post

### Metrics to track
- Day 1 revenue vs goal
- Conversion rate: traffic → cart open → purchase
- AOV on Day 1 vs benchmark
- Any tech friction (broken checkout, failed email, payment processor issue)

## Phase 3 — Momentum (days 2 to N-1 of cart window)

### Daily rhythm
- Morning email addressing one top objection
- Mid-day community post or testimonial share
- Evening email if list-based (or push in community)
- Continuous DM response + support

### Content plan per day
- **Day 2:** objection-reframe email (money or timing)
- **Day 3:** proof-story email (case study or testimonial)
- **Day 4:** "behind the scenes" email (what the cohort will actually do)
- **Day 5:** FAQ email (top 10 questions)
- **Day 6:** urgency beat ("48 hours left")

### Scarcity tracking
- Actual seats remaining (transparent updates if ethical)
- Bonus expiration countdowns
- Price-increase countdowns (if applicable)

### Common Day 2-N-1 dip
- Close rate drops middle of cart window; re-energize with new proof, bonus reveal, or community momentum post.

## Phase 4 — Follow-Up (last 24–48 hours)

### 24 hours before close
- Morning email: "24 hours left"
- Mid-day email: bonus expiration reminder
- Evening email: "tomorrow this closes, here's what changes"

### Final 12 hours
- 12-hour email: final chance + quick bonus recap
- 6-hour email: last-minute proof + FAQ
- 2-hour email: simple "closes tonight" with CTA
- Community / DM push for high-engagement prospects

### The close
- Cart closes at stated time — no extensions. Stated hard cutoffs protect the whole launch economics + brand.
- Auto-follow-up to non-closers: thank-you email + waitlist opt-in + content-orbit continuation.

## Phase 5 — Analyze (weeks +1 to +2)

### Launch report (`/launch-report`)
- Revenue vs goal
- Conversion rates at every stage
- Top-performing channels / angles / ads
- Objection patterns
- Refund / chargeback early indicators (30 days out)
- Community engagement post-cart
- Team retrospective

### Cohort onboarding (day 0 to day 14)
- Welcome email + orientation
- First live call / kickoff
- Community activation
- First-win engineering (every cohort member should hit one concrete win in 14 days)

### Next-launch improvements
- 3 things to keep
- 3 things to change
- 1 experiment to try next cohort

## Metrics dashboard

| Metric | Target | Red flag |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 revenue | 25–40% of goal | <15% |
| Day-of-close revenue | 30–50% of goal | <20% |
| Cart CVR blended | 2–8% | <1% |
| Refund rate (30-day post) | <5% | >10% |
| Cohort NPS (60 days) | >50 | <30 |

## Common failure modes

1. **Asset rush week-of-launch.** Quality suffers, errors leak into emails. Fix: finalize assets by week -2; week -1 is polish only.
2. **Flat middle of cart.** No new proof, no new urgency. Fix: daily plan with new beats — bonus reveal, testimonial, objection reframe.
3. **Extending the cart.** Destroys trust in future launches. Fix: hold the line. Announce waitlist for next cohort instead.
4. **Over-promising in pre-launch.** Cohort reality doesn't match hype. Fix: under-promise specifics; over-deliver on quality.
5. **Weak community kickoff.** Paid members join to silence. Fix: day-1 community activation sprint.
6. **No post-cart debrief.** Same mistakes repeat next cohort. Fix: `/launch-report` within 2 weeks.
