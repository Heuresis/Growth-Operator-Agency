# PDF Guide Playbook

## Role In the Funnel

A PDF guide is the workhorse lead magnet. It solves the problem of a reader who wants to be taught something they can take away and refer back to. Unlike a checklist or cheat sheet, the guide teaches a mental model end-to-end, not a single reference card. Unlike a mini-course, it does not require days of engagement to complete.

The PDF guide has three jobs:

1. Deliver a specific, useful outcome in one sitting. By the end of the reading session, the reader should be able to do something they could not do before.
2. Establish the creator as the person who teaches this mechanism. The guide is a teaching artifact that plants the name of the mechanism and the shape of the creator's approach.
3. Bridge to the core offer. The last 10 percent of the guide names the deeper problem the guide does not solve and points to the next asset that does.

A PDF guide is not a whitepaper and is not an ebook. Whitepapers are long, data-heavy, and distribute once. Ebooks are standalone products. A PDF guide is 15 to 30 minutes of reading, tightly scoped to one outcome, with a bridge.

## Gate-in Conditions

Ship a PDF guide only if:

- Compartment 2 (Audience) at 60 percent or higher, so the guide's scope matches a real ICP segment
- Compartment 3 (Offer) at 50 percent or higher, so the bridge-to-offer is real
- Compartment 5 (Copy and Messaging) has 10 or more proven hooks and angles to pull from for the title, section headers, and CTA
- Creator's unique mechanism from Positioning Doc is named and the three to five sub-steps are defined
- Designer or DIY design tool (Canva, Figma, Notion export) is lined up for the design pass

## Structural Anatomy

A 12-page PDF guide maps to this section layout:

| Section | Pages | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Cover | 1 | Title, subtitle, author credit, cohort or edition |
| Introduction | 1 | Who this guide is for, the specific outcome, how long to read |
| The Problem | 1 to 2 | The problem this guide solves, framed in ICP voice |
| The Mechanism | 1 | Name the mechanism, state the sub-steps at a high level |
| Sub-step 1 deep-dive | 1 to 2 | Full teaching with example |
| Sub-step 2 deep-dive | 1 to 2 | Full teaching with example |
| Sub-step 3 deep-dive | 1 to 2 | Full teaching with example |
| Putting it Together | 1 | Mini case study that uses all three sub-steps |
| Common Traps | 1 | 3 to 5 failure modes and their fixes |
| Next Step Bridge | 1 | What the guide does not solve; link to the core offer or the next asset |
| Resources | 1 | Links to related content, community, or calendar |

Longer guides (15 pages or more) extend the sub-step deep-dives with additional examples or add a fourth or fifth sub-step. Shorter guides (8 to 10 pages) compress the Common Traps and Resources sections.

## Writing Rules Per Section

### Cover Page
- Title hits specificity 8 or higher. Examples: "The 7-Day Lead Generation Map for Coaching Practices Stuck at $5K/mo" is specific. "The Ultimate Guide to Marketing" fails.
- Subtitle names the outcome and the timeframe.
- Author credit with the creator's actual name, not a brand alias.
- Edition or version number in small type, dated.

### Introduction (one page)
- Three paragraphs.
- Paragraph one: who this guide is for, described by ICP stage, role, and current pain.
- Paragraph two: what they will be able to do by the end.
- Paragraph three: how long to read, what they will need in front of them (pen, notebook, current metric), and what the guide does not cover.

### The Problem (one to two pages)
- Quote the problem back to the reader in their own words. Pull from Compartment 2 voice-of-customer if available.
- Name the three failure modes most readers are stuck in before reading this guide.
- End with a one-sentence thesis statement naming the mechanism that resolves it.

### The Mechanism (one page)
- Name the mechanism in full.
- State the three to five sub-steps as a numbered list, each with a one-sentence summary.
- State the order dependency: why sub-step one must happen before two, and so on.
- End with "The rest of this guide walks through each sub-step with a working example."

### Sub-step Deep-Dive (one to two pages each, three to five total)
Each deep-dive follows the same four-part structure:
- What it is: a one-paragraph definition in plain language.
- Why it matters: the specific failure that happens when this sub-step is skipped.
- How to do it: three to five concrete actions, each with a specific output to produce.
- Example: a real, isomorphic example showing the sub-step in action with real numbers or specifics.

### Putting It Together (one page)
- A mini case study that runs all three to five sub-steps on one ICP-isomorphic example.
- Before state, sub-step-by-sub-step walkthrough, after state.
- Specific numbers where available. Name and role of case subject only with permission.

### Common Traps (one page)
- Three to five traps, each with a one-sentence description and a one-sentence fix.
- These are the objections the reader will hit when they try to apply the guide alone.
- The traps foreshadow the reasons the next-step asset (core offer) solves more than the guide.

### Next Step Bridge (one page)
- One sentence: "Here is what this guide does not solve."
- Two to three sentences naming the deeper problem (implementation at scale, integration with adjacent systems, specific edge cases).
- One sentence introducing the next-step asset: the VSL, the application, the webinar, or the core offer.
- One CTA: button-style link in design, plain URL fallback.

### Resources (one page, optional)
- Three to five related content pieces from the creator's content hub.
- Link to community or social handle.
- Link to the calendar if calls are part of the bridge.

## Opt-in Page Copy Structure

The landing page that delivers this PDF follows a shared structure:

- Hook headline: the title of the guide, or a sharper variant.
- Subhead: the outcome stated in one sentence.
- Three-to-five-bullet "what is inside" list, each bullet a specific sub-outcome.
- Social proof block: if available, three to five names or logos of readers who have used the guide.
- Single CTA button: "Get the guide" or "Send me the PDF."
- Fine print: email sign-up commitment, unsubscribe promise, privacy note.

## Delivery Automation

- Opt-in form fires on submit.
- ESP adds tag `lm-pdf-[slug]` plus `welcome-active`.
- Delivery email fires within 2 minutes, containing the PDF as attachment or a link to a hosted copy.
- Delivery email subject: "Your [guide title] is inside."
- Welcome sequence begins immediately after delivery email.

## Design Specs

- Page size: US Letter or A4. Pick one and hold across all guides.
- Font: one heading face and one body face. Body at 11 to 12pt, line height 1.5.
- Color palette: two to three brand colors plus a neutral. No gradients.
- Section breaks visible, not subtle. Reader should know when a new section starts.
- Page numbers in the footer.
- Exported as PDF with embedded fonts. File size under 10 MB for email delivery.

## Benchmarks and What They Tell You

| Metric | Healthy Range | If Below | If Above |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opt-in rate on warm traffic | 30 to 50 percent | Title too vague or opt-in page too heavy | Fine |
| Opt-in rate on cold traffic | 15 to 25 percent | Offer-ICP mismatch or creative weak | Fine |
| Delivery email open rate | 65 to 85 percent | Subject or sender issue | Fine |
| PDF read completion (if tracked via hosted reader) | 30 to 50 percent | Guide too long, or hook weak | Fine |
| Bridge CTA click rate | 5 to 15 percent | Bridge too soft or offer mismatch | Fine |
| Delivery time to inbox | under 120 seconds | Automation delay, fix before reshipping | Fine |

## Common Failure Modes

1. Title is vague. Opt-in rate below 20 percent on warm traffic. Fix: rewrite title to specificity 8 or higher.
2. Guide teaches the full how-to of the core offer. Reader feels done, does not need the offer. Fix: teach the what and one working example; the full how-to lives in the offer.
3. Bridge-to-offer missing or buried. Reader consumes but does not move forward. Fix: explicit Next Step Bridge section in the last page.
4. Delivery email is delayed. Reader cools. Welcome sequence performs poorly. Fix: test opt-in to delivery under 120 seconds.
5. Guide is 25 or more pages. Completion rate drops to 20 percent. Fix: cut to 12 to 15 pages. Longer means less read, not more value.
6. Design is ugly enough that the reader distrusts the content. Fix: commit 30 percent of design time to typography and layout.
7. No permissions on case study names or quotes. Legal flags, content unusable. Fix: permissions cleared before publish.

## Voice-Match and Compliance

- 3 or more `phrases_to_use` distributed across the guide, not clustered.
- Banned-vocabulary sweep clean.
- Specificity score of 8 or higher on title and section headers.
- No income guarantees. Testimonial numbers carry "results vary" disclosure.
- No health claims without clinical backing.
- All case studies and quotes permission-cleared.

## Next Step Routing

After the PDF guide is delivered, the welcome sequence begins automatically. The bridge CTA in the last page points to one of these destinations depending on offer architecture:

- High-ticket offer: calendar booking page or application form
- Mid-ticket offer: VSL or landing page for core offer
- Low-ticket tripwire: direct cart page
- Webinar funnel: webinar registration page
- Community funnel: community join page
