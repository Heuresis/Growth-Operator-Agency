# Checklist Playbook

## Role In the Funnel

A checklist is the fastest-consumption lead magnet that still teaches something. The reader does not want a mental model. They want boxes to tick so they can go execute. The checklist meets them where they are.

The format has three jobs:

1. Give the reader a sequence of concrete actions they can execute today.
2. Demonstrate the creator's thinking by the completeness and ordering of the items.
3. Plant the mechanism: the checklist's structure is the mechanism's sub-step order, printed.

A checklist is not a PDF guide and is not a cheat sheet. Guides teach; checklists execute. Cheat sheets reference; checklists sequence. If the reader does not tick boxes left-to-right top-to-bottom, the asset is a cheat sheet, not a checklist.

## Gate-in Conditions

Ship a checklist only if:

- Compartment 2 (Audience) at 60 percent; the checklist hits a specific ICP task
- Compartment 3 (Offer) at 40 percent; the bridge-to-offer names a real next asset
- Creator's mechanism is named and its sub-steps order into a sequence of actions
- Designer or DIY design tool lined up for clean layout (Canva works; Notion-export-to-PDF does not)

## Structural Anatomy

A one-page checklist maps to this layout:

| Zone | Content |
|---|---|
| Header | Title + subtitle + author credit |
| Intro block | Two to three sentences: who this is for, what ticking all boxes produces |
| Main checklist | 10 to 25 items, grouped into 3 to 5 sections |
| Bridge block | "When you have ticked all boxes and still need [X], here is the next step" |
| Footer | URL to download more resources plus author mark |

Two-page checklists extend the main checklist section. The intro and bridge stay on the first page.

## Item-Writing Rules

Each checklist item follows the same grammar:

- Starts with a verb in imperative mood: "Draft the headline," "Confirm the deadline," "Test the opt-in."
- Specifies the exact output: what has to exist when the box is ticked.
- Bounded in time or scope: under 30 minutes, or until a specific condition is met.
- One action per box. If two actions, split into two boxes.

Bad item: "Work on your offer."
Better item: "Write your 7-phase offer document using the offer architect structure."
Best item: "Draft the value stack section of your offer document; it is done when you have 5 to 8 bonuses listed with a stated value per bonus."

## Section Grouping

Checklists of 15 or more items group into 3 to 5 sections. Each section has a section title that names a phase of the work:

- "Before you start" (prep items)
- "Core build" (primary actions)
- "Validation" (test and check items)
- "Ship" (publish or launch items)
- "After ship" (measurement or follow-up items)

Section titles make the checklist feel less like a wall and more like a sequence.

## Title Patterns — 10 Options

1. "The [N]-Item Checklist for [Specific Outcome]"
2. "Before You [Specific Action]: [N] Things to Check"
3. "The [ICP Role]'s [Task] Checklist"
4. "Ship a [Asset Type] This Week: The [N]-Item Checklist"
5. "[N] Boxes Between You and [Specific Outcome]"
6. "The [Industry] [Task] Checklist: [N] Items, Zero Guesses"
7. "Launch Your [Asset]: [N]-Item Pre-Launch Checklist"
8. "The [Mechanism Name] Activation Checklist"
9. "[N]-Point Audit: Is Your [Asset] Ready?"
10. "[Timeframe] to [Outcome]: The [N]-Step Checklist"

## Opt-in Page Copy Structure

- Hook headline: title with the specific ICP role plus outcome.
- Subhead: "Ship [outcome] with zero guesswork."
- Bullet list of the section titles from the checklist (not the items themselves).
- Single CTA button: "Send me the checklist."
- Optional social proof: count of downloads or a testimonial if available.

Keep the opt-in page under 300 words. Checklists are impulse opt-ins.

## Delivery Automation

- Opt-in form fires on submit.
- ESP tags: `lm-checklist-[slug]`, `welcome-active`.
- Delivery email fires in under 120 seconds with PDF attached or hosted link.
- Delivery email subject: "Your [title] is here. Print it."
- Welcome sequence triggered immediately.

## Design Specs

- Single page, US Letter or A4, landscape or portrait consistent with brand.
- Checkbox visible on the left of each item, item text on the right.
- Section titles in a larger weight than item text.
- Checkbox size: at least 14pt equivalent for printability.
- Color: one accent color for section titles plus black for item text.
- Export print-ready at 300dpi. A checklist that cannot be printed fails the format.

Design weight is 40 percent for this lead magnet type. A poorly designed checklist fails to deliver perceived value. A beautifully designed checklist with weak items still works.

## Benchmarks and What They Tell You

| Metric | Healthy Range | If Below | If Above |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opt-in rate on warm traffic | 35 to 55 percent | Title too vague or opt-in page too heavy | Fine |
| Opt-in rate on cold traffic | 18 to 28 percent | Topic too niche or creative weak | Fine |
| Delivery email open rate | 70 to 90 percent | Subject or sender issue | Fine |
| Print rate (if tracked via print-CTA) | 15 to 30 percent | Design not print-optimized | Fine |
| Bridge CTA click rate | 4 to 10 percent | Bridge too soft | Fine |

## Common Failure Modes

1. Items are vague verbs. "Improve X" "Work on Y." Readers cannot tick. Fix: every item starts with a concrete verb and names the output.
2. More than 25 items. Readers feel overwhelmed. Fix: cut to under 25, split into two checklists if needed.
3. No section grouping in a 20-item checklist. Feels like a wall. Fix: group into 3 to 5 sections.
4. Design is a basic word-processor export. Perceived value drops. Fix: use design tool with proper typography and checkbox alignment.
5. Bridge-to-offer missing. Reader ticks all boxes and leaves. Fix: explicit bridge block.
6. Items reference frameworks or concepts not defined anywhere. Reader cannot execute. Fix: self-contained items; if a framework is referenced, link to it.
7. Checklist is a cheat sheet in disguise (items are definitions, not actions). Fix: rewrite every item as a verb-first action.

## Voice-Match and Compliance

- Voice-match: 3 or more `phrases_to_use` distributed across the checklist.
- Banned-vocabulary sweep clean.
- No income guarantees.
- No fabricated stats or testimonials.
- Permissions cleared on any attributed content.

## Next Step Routing

After delivery, welcome sequence begins. Bridge CTA in the checklist points to:

- High-ticket offer: calendar or application
- Mid-ticket offer: VSL or landing page
- Low-ticket tripwire: direct cart
- Webinar funnel: registration page

- [ ] 10 to 25 items, imperative verb start, named output per item
- [ ] 3 to 5 section groupings if 15 or more items
- [ ] Single page or two pages, print-ready design at 300dpi

- [ ] Opt-in page under 300 words, single CTA
- [ ] Delivery automation under 120 seconds
- [ ] 3 or more `phrases_to_use`
