# Cheat Sheet Playbook

## Role In the Funnel

A cheat sheet is the reference lead magnet. The reader does not want to learn something new; they want a fast way to recall something they already half-know. The format rewards density, clarity, and scannability over narrative.

The three jobs:

1. Compress a framework, formula set, decision matrix, or terminology library onto one page.
2. Deliver persistent utility: the reader keeps referring back to it across weeks or months.
3. Plant the creator's mechanism naming. Every time the reader glances at the cheat sheet, the mechanism name is present.

A cheat sheet is not a checklist and is not a PDF guide. Checklists sequence actions. Guides teach mental models. Cheat sheets reference knowledge. The difference is the reader's use: they execute a checklist once, read a guide once, glance at a cheat sheet dozens of times.

## Gate-in Conditions

Ship a cheat sheet only if:

- Compartment 2 (Audience) at 60 percent
- Compartment 3 (Offer) at 40 percent
- A real framework, formula set, or decision matrix exists that can fit on one page
- Creator's mechanism is named and appears in the content
- Strong designer or designer-grade tool (Figma preferred, Canva workable) is available

## Structural Anatomy

A one-page cheat sheet has four zones:

| Zone | Content |
|---|---|
| Top strip | Title, subtitle, author credit |
| Main grid | The framework, formulas, or matrix as the central visual element |
| Side or callout panels | Definitions, quick references, or mini examples |
| Bottom strip | One-line bridge-to-offer with URL |

Visual density is the format's signature. A cheat sheet with three lines of text and a lot of whitespace fails the format. A cheat sheet with 400 words structured into a clean grid succeeds.

## Content Patterns — What Fits on a Cheat Sheet

1. **Decision matrix:** rows by condition, columns by action. Example: "If X, do Y. If Z, do W."
2. **Formula library:** a set of calculations with inputs and outputs. Example: customer economics formulas with definitions.
3. **Terminology glossary:** key terms with 1-to-2-line definitions.
4. **Framework diagram:** a flowchart or sequence diagram with labels.
5. **Comparison table:** rows by option, columns by attribute.
6. **Hook bank:** a collection of 15 to 25 proven hook patterns in a scannable layout.
7. **Objection response library:** 10 objections with one-line responses.
8. **Rate card or pricing reference:** tiers with features side-by-side.

Pick one pattern per cheat sheet. Mixing patterns in one sheet destroys scannability.

## Opt-in Page Copy Structure

- Hook headline: cheat sheet title.
- Subhead: "Print it. Pin it above your desk. Stop googling [topic]."
- Visual preview of the cheat sheet (a screenshot or mockup) — this is the highest-leverage element for opt-in rate on cheat sheets.
- Single CTA button: "Send me the cheat sheet."
- Two or three bullets naming what is on the sheet.

Keep opt-in page under 200 words. Visual preview is the sales copy.

## Delivery Automation

- Opt-in fires on submit.
- ESP tags: `lm-cheatsheet-[slug]`, `welcome-active`.
- Delivery email under 120 seconds.
- Delivery email subject: "Your cheat sheet is inside."
- Delivery body: two sentences plus link. Short.
- Welcome sequence triggered immediately.

## Design Specs

- One page only. Back-side acceptable for two-sided print.
- US Letter or A4, landscape orientation often works better for density.
- Font: body at 9 to 11pt. Cheat sheets accept smaller type than guides because the reader is scanning, not reading.
- Color: two to three colors. One primary for titles, one accent for callouts, plus a neutral.
- Visual grid: strong grid alignment. Misaligned elements kill scannability.
- Export at 300dpi, CMYK-safe if intended for print.
- Include a visual marker indicating it is printable (print icon in corner, "Print me" tagline).

Design weight is 50 percent for this format. A cheat sheet with weak design fails regardless of content.

## Benchmarks and What They Tell You

| Metric | Healthy Range | If Below | If Above |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opt-in rate on warm traffic | 40 to 60 percent | Title vague or visual preview missing | Fine |
| Opt-in rate on cold traffic | 20 to 30 percent | ICP mismatch or design not shown on opt-in page | Fine |
| Delivery email open rate | 70 to 90 percent | Sender issue | Fine |
| Bridge CTA click rate | 2 to 6 percent (lower than guides; cheat sheets are reference, not funnel) | Fine if quiet bridge | Healthy |
| Refer-share rate (if tracked) | 5 to 15 percent | Fine; cheat sheets get shared often | Excellent |

## Common Failure Modes

1. Cheat sheet is a half-empty page with three bullets. Fails format. Fix: increase density to at least 300 words of useful content.
2. Cheat sheet is a PDF guide in disguise (paragraphs of prose). Fails format. Fix: convert to grid, table, or diagram layout.
3. Design is a word-processor export. Perceived value drops. Fix: use design tool with grid, typography, and color.
4. No visual preview on opt-in page. Opt-in rate 15 percent lower than benchmark. Fix: add a mockup screenshot to the opt-in page.
5. Font too large. Content does not fit on one page, overflows to two. Fix: compress font or trim content.
6. Bridge-to-offer dominates the sheet. Looks like an ad. Fix: bottom strip only, quiet.
7. Framework or formulas are generic, not creator-specific. Reader gets no mechanism plant. Fix: wrap the reference in the creator's mechanism naming.

## Voice-Match and Compliance

- 2 or more `phrases_to_use` (cheat sheets are sparse; voice carries on titles and callouts).
- Banned-vocabulary sweep clean on all titles, callouts, and body text.
- No fabricated formulas or stats.
- Mechanism name appears at least once in the main grid and once in the footer.
- Permissions cleared on any attributed content.

## Next Step Routing

After delivery, welcome sequence begins. Bridge CTA in the bottom strip points to:

- High-ticket offer: a soft link to "more on this, here" pointing to an application or a VSL
- Mid-ticket offer: landing page for core offer
- Content brand: content hub or newsletter

Cheat sheets convert less aggressively downstream than guides or webinars. Their value is long-arc: persistent reference plants mechanism over months.
