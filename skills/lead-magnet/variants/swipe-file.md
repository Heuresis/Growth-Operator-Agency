# Swipe File Playbook

## Role In the Funnel

A swipe file is the highest-perceived-value lead magnet in the tactical-learning category. Readers treat it as an ongoing reference, not a one-time asset. They come back to it when they need a specific hook, a specific headline, or a specific angle.

Three jobs:

1. Deliver persistent tactical value. The swipe file gets opened dozens of times across months.
2. Position the creator as the curator who has seen the work across the field. The quality of the selections signals the creator's taste and expertise.
3. Bridge to the core offer by placing CTAs at the introduction and appendix, plus quietly throughout if the swipe file is hosted.

A swipe file is not a checklist and is not a cheat sheet. Checklists sequence actions. Cheat sheets reference concepts. Swipe files provide example artifacts that the reader adapts.

## Gate-in Conditions

Ship a swipe file only if:

- Compartment 2 (Audience) at 60 percent
- Compartment 3 (Offer) at 40 percent
- Creator has access to 20 or more high-quality, permission-cleared examples (owned, creator's own past work, or public-domain examples with attribution)
- Compartment 5 (Copy and Messaging) has the categorization framework ready
- Host platform selected: Notion, Airtable, Google Doc, or PDF
- Legal review complete for fair-use or permission status of every example included

## Structural Anatomy

A 40-example swipe file typically includes:

| Zone | Content |
|---|---|
| Cover or intro page | Title, credit, how to use this swipe file, bridge-to-offer #1 |
| Category index | 3 to 8 categories with example counts |
| Examples by category | 5 to 15 examples per category, each with 3 to 5 annotations |
| Appendix | "What to do next," bridge-to-offer #2, credits |

Example types by content focus:
- Hook bank: 30 to 50 opening-line examples
- Headline swipe: 50 to 100 headlines across categories
- Email subject line swipe: 50 to 100 subjects
- Ad creative swipe: 20 to 40 ad examples with screenshots
- Landing page swipe: 10 to 20 landing page examples
- Sales script opener swipe: 30 to 50 opening-line examples
- Objection response swipe: 20 to 40 responses

Pick one content focus per swipe file. Mixing focuses dilutes perceived value.

## Annotation Structure Per Example

Each example in the swipe file gets:

- **The example itself** (the hook, headline, subject line, or artifact). Reproduced verbatim where permission allows; reproduced in structure form where it does not.
- **Why it works:** one to three sentences naming the specific persuasion mechanism or structural choice.
- **When to use it:** one sentence naming the ICP or funnel stage it fits.
- **Adaptation template:** one sentence showing how to adapt the pattern with fill-in-the-blank variables.

Example (for a headline):
- **Headline:** "The 7-Day Lead Generation Map for Coaching Practices Stuck at $5K/mo"
- **Why it works:** Specificity stack (timeframe + ICP role + milestone) plus named mechanism anchor.
- **When to use it:** Lead magnet titles, landing page hero headlines, ad hook lines.
- **Adaptation template:** "The [N]-Day [Outcome] Map for [ICP Role] Stuck at [Specific Milestone]."

## Categorization Framework

Categorize examples by one primary dimension:

- **By funnel stage:** Awareness / Consideration / Decision
- **By asset type:** Ads / Landing pages / Emails / Sales calls
- **By ICP segment:** Enterprise / Mid-market / Small business / Creator solo
- **By persuasion principle:** Authority / Social proof / Scarcity / Reciprocity
- **By emotional lever:** Fear / Greed / Guilt / Pride / Curiosity

Pick one dimension. Stick with it. Cross-categorization creates overlap that confuses the reader.

1. "The [N] [Example Type] Swipe File"
2. "[Number] Proven [Examples] from [Specific Context]"
3. "The [Industry] [Example Type] Swipe File: [N] Examples, Fully Annotated"

8. "[N] [Examples] Ranked by [Specific Metric]"

## Opt-in Page Copy Structure

- Hook headline: title with specific count of examples.
- Subhead: "[N] [examples], fully annotated, organized by [categorization dimension]."
- Visual preview: screenshot of 2 or 3 example entries.
- Single CTA button: "Send me the swipe file."
- Three bullets naming the top categories.

Keep opt-in page under 350 words. Count and visual preview are the sales copy.

## Delivery Automation

- Opt-in fires on submit.
- ESP tags: `lm-swipe-[slug]`, `welcome-active`.
- Delivery email under 120 seconds.
- Delivery email subject: "Your swipe file is inside."
- Delivery body: short. One sentence, one link, one note on how to bookmark for repeat access.
- If hosted on Notion or Airtable, provide a tip on duplicating to the reader's own workspace.
- Welcome sequence triggered immediately.

## Design Specs

PDF swipe files:
- 10 to 40 pages depending on example count.
- Font: body at 10 to 11pt, headline font at 16 to 20pt for example titles.
- Each example on its own page or half-page for scannability.
- Screenshots (for ads or landing pages) at 150dpi minimum.
- Embedded fonts, file size under 25 MB.

Hosted (Notion, Airtable) swipe files:
- Clear table structure with filterable columns.
- Each example as a row or a linked sub-page.
- "How to use this" intro page with instructions for duplication or bookmarking.
- Updated quarterly with new examples; subscribers notified of additions via broadcast.

## Benchmarks and What They Tell You

| Metric | Healthy Range | If Below | If Above |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opt-in rate on warm traffic | 40 to 60 percent | Example count too low or title vague | Fine |
| Opt-in rate on cold traffic | 25 to 35 percent | Category mismatch with ICP | Excellent |
| Delivery email open rate | 75 to 90 percent | Sender issue | Fine |
| Hosted swipe file return visits (if tracked via analytics) | 3 to 8 visits per subscriber in first 90 days | Fine | High perceived value |
| Bridge CTA click rate | 4 to 10 percent | Fine for passive bridge | Fine |
| Refer-share rate | 10 to 25 percent | Fine; swipe files spread | Excellent |

## Common Failure Modes

1. Examples lack annotations. Reader gets the example but no insight. Fix: every example gets why-it-works, when-to-use, and adaptation template.
2. Example count under 15. Perceived value drops. Fix: ship at 20 or more examples, minimum.
3. Categorization unclear. Reader cannot find what they need. Fix: one clear dimension, strict adherence.
4. Permissions not cleared. Legal flag, file unusable. Fix: verify permissions before publish; swap out any contested examples.
5. All examples from the creator's own work, no external ones. Feels narrow. Fix: mix creator examples with public-domain or permission-cleared external examples (minimum 40 percent external).
6. Hosted on a platform the ICP does not use. Delivery friction. Fix: choose host platform based on ICP familiarity (Notion for creators, Airtable for operators, Google Doc for widest compatibility, PDF as fallback).
7. Bridge-to-offer absent or hidden. Reader studies swipe file for months without knowing about the core offer. Fix: bridge at intro plus appendix plus quietly inline in hosted versions.

## Voice-Match and Compliance

- 3 or more `phrases_to_use` across annotations.
- Banned-vocabulary sweep clean.
- Every example has explicit attribution where identifiable.
- No fabricated examples. If an example is modeled on the creator's own work, state that.
- No income claims in annotations unless the example's real performance data is cited with permission.
- Mechanism name appears in the intro and at least three annotations.

## Next Step Routing

After delivery, welcome sequence begins. Bridge CTA points to:

- High-ticket offer: application or calendar
- Mid-ticket offer: VSL or landing page
- Content brand: content hub or newsletter
- Community funnel: community join page

Swipe files frequently spawn downstream content: reader questions about specific examples become broadcast topics, reader adaptations become testimonial content for the core offer.
