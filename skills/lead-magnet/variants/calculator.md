# Calculator Playbook

## Role In the Funnel

A calculator is the utility-first lead magnet. Readers come for the answer, not for the teaching. They have a specific question (How much could I save? What should I charge? How long until I hit my goal?) and they want a number.

Three jobs:

1. Deliver a personalized number that the reader could not have calculated themselves easily.
2. Reveal the gap between the reader's current state and their goal, making the gap feel real and quantified.
3. Bridge to the core offer by framing the offer as the mechanism that closes the calculated gap.

A calculator is not a quiz and is not a spreadsheet. Quizzes diagnose archetypes. Spreadsheets are passive tools the reader fills in. Calculators produce an outcome number that the reader came specifically to get.

## Gate-in Conditions

Ship a calculator only if:

- Compartment 2 (Audience) at 60 percent; calculator topic must match a specific ICP pain quantifiable in numbers
- Compartment 3 (Offer) at 60 percent; offer must plausibly close the gap the calculator reveals
- Math is sound: formula tested on 20 or more sample input sets and produces reasonable outputs at edge cases
- Calculator platform selected (custom-coded, Typeform with logic, Outgrow, ScoreApp, or no-code builder)
- Compliance review complete: if outputs could be mistaken for financial advice, disclaimer language is drafted

## Structural Anatomy

A calculator has these components:

| Component | Spec |
|---|---|
| Hook page | Title, problem frame, CTA to start the calculation |
| Input fields | 3 to 8 fields on 1 to 3 screens |
| Email gate | Before result reveal |
| Result page | Personalized number plus interpretation plus CTA |
| Email report | Detailed breakdown with same numbers in PDF or richer-format email |
| Follow-up sequence | Result-tiered drip (different sequences for different result ranges) |

## Calculator Design Patterns

Pick one of these patterns per calculator:

1. **Savings calculator:** "How much would you save if you [specific action]?" Inputs drive a saved-dollars or saved-hours output.
2. **ROI calculator:** "What is your expected return on [specific investment]?" Inputs drive a projected-return output.
3. **Goal-timing calculator:** "How long until you reach [specific goal]?" Inputs drive a timeline output.
4. **Pricing calculator:** "What should you charge for [service]?" Inputs drive a recommended price range.
5. **Capacity calculator:** "How many [units] can you handle at [stage]?" Inputs drive a capacity limit.
6. **Gap calculator:** "What is the gap between your current [metric] and your target?" Inputs drive a gap-in-dollars or gap-in-time output.
7. **Cost-of-delay calculator:** "What does waiting six months cost you?" Inputs drive a cost output.
8. **Conversion-rate calculator:** "What conversion rate do you need to hit [revenue target]?" Inputs drive a required-rate output.

## Input Field Rules

- 3 to 8 fields total. Fewer than 3 fails to produce differentiated outputs. More than 8 creates friction.
- Each field has a clear label. "Current monthly recurring revenue in dollars" works. "MRR" confuses half the audience.
- Default values or examples shown in placeholder text. Reduces bounce when reader does not know their own number.
- Input validation: numeric fields reject non-numeric entries; required fields flag before submission.
- Progressive disclosure: split 8 fields across 2 or 3 screens if they feel heavy on one screen.

## Output Design Rules

The result must be:

- **Specific.** A single number or a tight range, not a vague descriptor.
- **Contextual.** "You could save $47,300/year" is specific. "$47,300" alone lacks context. Pair the number with what it represents.
- **Visualized.** A bar, gauge, chart, or comparison visual raises perceived value.
- **Interpreted.** Two to three sentences explaining what the number means for the reader's situation.
- **Actionable.** A specific next step tied to the number. "At this gap, the path to close it is [the core offer]."

## Opt-in Page Copy Structure

- Hook headline: calculator title with specific metric.
- Subhead: "Enter [N] numbers. Get your [specific output]. [Timeframe]."
- Visual: a preview of the result page (without real data) showing what the reader will see.
- CTA button: "Run the calculation."
- Social proof if available: count of past users.

Opt-in page under 300 words. The preview visual sells the calculator.

## Email Gate Placement

Gate before result, never before input. Reader who has filled in their numbers has already committed.

Email gate copy:
- "Where should we send your detailed report?"
- Email field plus first name field (optional).
- Privacy note.
- CTA: "Get my result."

Show the result on screen after gate submission, not after email confirmation. Instant gratification is part of the format's value.

## Delivery Automation

- Opt-in fires when email is submitted in the gate.
- ESP tags: `lm-calc-[slug]`, `calc-tier-[result-tier]`, `welcome-active`.
- Result page displays immediately.
- Email report fires within 2 minutes with the same numbers formatted as a shareable PDF or rich-text email.
- Result-tiered follow-up sequence begins 1 day after calculation.

## Result-Tiered Sequences

Calculator results fall into tiers based on the output. Different tiers route to different follow-up sequences:

- **High-gap tier:** reader has a large gap between current and goal. The core offer is the fastest path. Follow-up sequence leans toward offer and application CTA.
- **Medium-gap tier:** reader has a moderate gap. Follow-up sequence leans toward education plus a mid-tier offer or webinar.
- **Low-gap tier:** reader is close to goal or has a small gap. Follow-up sequence leans toward retention content, referral invitations, or ascension framing.
- **Edge-case tier:** reader's inputs produced an unusual result. Follow-up sequence includes a manual check-in or a direct reply prompt.

## Benchmarks and What They Tell You

| Metric | Healthy Range | If Below | If Above |
|---|---|---|---|
| Calculator start rate | 45 to 70 percent | Title unclear or visual missing | Fine |
| Calculator completion rate | 60 to 85 percent | Too many inputs or unclear labels | Fine |
| Email gate opt-in rate | 60 to 80 percent | Gate copy weak or timing wrong | Fine |
| Overall opt-in rate (visit to email) | 35 to 55 percent on warm; 20 to 30 percent on cold | Topic mismatch | Strong |
| Result page CTA click rate | 8 to 20 percent | Offer mismatch to result tier | Strong |
| Return usage (if tracked) | 15 to 30 percent return within 60 days | Not tracking or single-use tool | Excellent |
| Share rate | 3 to 10 percent | Share CTA buried | Fine |

## Common Failure Modes

1. Formula produces wrong results at edge cases. Reader catches it. Trust collapses. Fix: test formula on 20 or more sample input sets including extremes.
2. More than 8 input fields. Completion rate drops below 40 percent. Fix: cut to 6 or fewer.
3. Output is a vague descriptor, not a number. Reader feels shortchanged. Fix: output is always a specific number or range.
4. No interpretation with the number. Reader gets a number but no context. Fix: 2 to 3 sentences interpreting the number.
5. Result page CTA is generic. Tiers all route to same offer regardless of result. Fix: tier the result, tier the CTA.
6. No disclaimer when results could be mistaken for financial advice. Legal risk. Fix: compliance-reviewed disclaimer footer on result page and report email.
7. Calculator is a disguised form with no real math. Reader enters data and gets a canned response. Feels fake. Fix: real formula, real math, real variability in outputs.

## Voice-Match and Compliance

- 2 or more `phrases_to_use` on result page and email report.
- Banned-vocabulary sweep clean on titles, labels, result interpretation, and follow-up emails.
- No income guarantees. Projected outputs carry "projection not guarantee" disclaimer.
- If financial calculations, disclaimer language legal-reviewed.
- Privacy note at email gate and on result page.
- GDPR-compliant consent for input storage if EU subscribers in scope.

## Next Step Routing

After calculation, subscribers route by result tier:

- High-gap tier → application sequence or direct-to-offer landing page
- Medium-gap tier → nurture sequence with mechanism-focused emails leading to mid-tier offer or webinar
- Low-gap tier → retention or referral sequence
- Edge-case tier → manual review plus check-in email from the team
