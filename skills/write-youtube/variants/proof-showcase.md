# YouTube Variant — Proof Showcase (10-20 min)

> **Signature move.** A concentrated showcase of real results, real artifacts, real metrics — organized into a coherent narrative rather than a reel of testimonials. The video walks through 5-12 proof assets (case studies, dashboards, before/after screens, testimonial clips, revenue shots), each with context and takeaway. The viewer finishes with a pattern-level belief in the creator's capability. The video sells the creator's track record, not a single product.

## When to Use

- Creator has accumulated enough genuine proof to fill 10-20 minutes of specific cases. Not optional — the variant requires real depth.
- Pillar is Proof, Authority, or Pre-Launch Trust Stack.
- Conversion stage is Decision — the viewer is evaluating trust and needs aggregated evidence.
- Directly precedes a launch window or is the "why now" asset before a big offer moment.

## When NOT to Use

- Creator has fewer than 5 genuine proof assets. Don't pad. Use a case study breakdown instead.
- All proof is from the same niche / same stage / same outcome type. Lack of diversity reads as over-fitting.
- No permission from the featured clients. Proof showcase is fundamentally client-visibility-heavy.
- Creator is uncomfortable being the "guy who shows his own results" — voice becomes performative.

## Title Formula

Pattern: `[N] [Specific Results] in [Timeframe] — [Pattern or Angle]`

Examples (archetypal):

1. "12 Client Results in 6 Months — The Pattern That Keeps Repeating"
2. "$2M in Documented Client Outcomes — Walk-Through of Every One"
3. "8 Before/After Case Studies at Different Revenue Stages"

Title rules:

- 60-70 characters.
- Specific number of proofs — not "many clients" but "12."
- Specific timeframe.
- Angle that makes it more than a reel — "the pattern," "every one," "at different stages."

## Thumbnail SOP

Proof showcase thumbnails visualize aggregation:

- **Grid thumbnail.** A 3x3 or 4x3 grid of small dashboard screenshots / faces / numbers. Viewer sees volume at a glance.
- **Big-number thumbnail.** One massive summary number with small proof tiles below it.
- **Client-faces thumbnail.** Multiple client faces (with permission) in a grid. Very strong if clients are willing.

Text overlay: the headline number ("$2M," "12 Clients," "8 Cases").

## Script Structure

### Intro (0:00 - 1:30)

- **0:00 - 0:15 — Volume Hook.** The total count and timeframe. "Twelve client results in six months. Here's every one."
- **0:15 - 0:30 — Credibility.** Why the creator is showing this — the pattern, the moment, the reason now.
- **0:30 - 1:00 — Framework.** The lens through which the cases will be organized (stage, outcome type, mechanism, timeframe).
- **1:00 - 1:30 — Roadmap + Pre-Handle.** "I'll walk through each. You might be thinking 'is this a launch video.' It's adjacent. Here's what's useful regardless of whether you buy anything."

### Case 1 (1:30 - 3:00)

- Client context (role, stage, starting point) — 20 seconds.
- What they did — 30-40 seconds.
- Specific outcome + on-screen proof — 20-30 seconds.
- One takeaway line.

### Case 2 (3:00 - 4:30)

Same structure. Different stage / different outcome type.

### Case 3-12 (4:30 - 16:30)

Each case: 80-100 seconds. Varying length based on density of the case.

### Pattern Reveal (16:30 - 18:00)

- What all cases have in common.
- The through-line mechanism.
- Why the pattern works.

### Close (18:00 - 20:00)

- One line of synthesis.
- Soft CTA (if appropriate to timing).
- End-screen.

## CTA Placement

- No CTA in first 16 minutes — the proof is the point.
- Soft CTA at minute 17-18 if the video is timed to a launch: "If you want to see how this works for you, [funnel link]."
- No CTA if the video is trust-building only.
- End-screen: related case study breakdown or the offer funnel if launch-timed.

## Retention Curve

Target: AVD above 45% for 15-20 min proof videos.

Defense points:

- **Minute 0:15** — volume hook. The total count earns the watch.
- **Minute 1:30** — first case. Establishes the format.
- **Minute 5:00** — viewers have absorbed 3 cases and are either engaged with the pattern or bored. Variant #4-5 need to be the strongest case to reset.
- **Minute 12:00** — viewer fatigue peak. Case 8 or 9 should be the most surprising / memorable one.
- **Minute 16:30** — pattern reveal. This is what the whole video was building toward.

## End-Screen Strategy

- Next video: a full case study breakdown of the most dramatic case in this showcase.
- Playlist: the case study series.
- Subscribe element.
- Funnel link if launch-timed.

## Loop Management

- Loop 1 opened at 0:30 ("the pattern keeps repeating") — closed at 16:30.
- Loop 2 opened at 1:00 ("one of these is the counter-example") — closed when that specific case is shown.
- Loop 3 opened at 5:00 ("the hardest case comes up later") — closed at that case.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- 3+ `phrases_to_use` per 5-minute segment.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Voice balances excitement for client results with analytical distance. "Here's what happened" — not "AMAZING result!" The proof speaks.

## Specificity Floor

- Every case has a specific role, specific stage, specific outcome, specific timeframe.
- Every proof asset is visible on screen — dashboard, Stripe row, testimonial clip, contract.
- The pattern at the end is named specifically, not "they all succeeded."

## Script Word Count

- 10-min: ~1,400-1,500 words
- 15-min: ~2,100-2,250 words
- 20-min: ~2,800-3,000 words

## Production Notes

- B-roll heavy — every case needs visible proof on screen.
- Dashboard screenshots, redacted as needed for private info but keeping the shape of the number.
- Testimonial clips (30-60 seconds each where available) inserted for emotional texture.
- Creator on-camera framing each case — "This is Client N. They were at [stage]. Here's what happened."
- Graphics: small case cards at each transition ("Case #4 of 12 — Freelancer → Agency, 6 Months").

## Description Structure

```
[Hook line — the count and timeframe]

[2-3 paragraphs — why this video exists, what the audience gets, any FTC disclosure]

[FTC disclosure if any paid testimonials]

Chapters:
[as above — each case named]

Related videos:
- [Full teardowns of specific cases in this showcase]

[Creator's channels]

[Hashtags]
```

## Common Failure Modes

- **Padding.** Creator only has 6 good cases but pads to 12 with weaker ones. Viewers notice the drop in quality mid-video.
- **No diversity.** All cases are the same stage or the same outcome type. Viewers can't see themselves in every example.
- **Missing proof assets.** Claims without screens. One case without proof contaminates the credibility of the other 11.
- **Too much context per case.** Some cases get 3 minutes because the creator wants to tell the full story. Keep each to 80-100 seconds. For full stories, do a dedicated case study breakdown.
- **Hype voice.** Creator sounds like an infomercial. Viewers tune out. Keep the tone analytical.
- **Pattern reveal is generic.** "They all worked hard." The pattern must be specific — a specific decision, a specific mechanism, a specific variable.

## Worked Example — 12 Client Results in 6 Months

### Title
"12 Client Results in 6 Months — The Pattern That Keeps Repeating"

### Thumbnail
- 4x3 grid of small dashboard / Stripe screenshots
- Center: "12 in 6 months"
- Creator face, lower-right

### Script Outline (abbreviated)

```
INTRO (0:00 - 1:30)

0:00 - 0:15 VOLUME HOOK
VO: "Twelve client results in six months. Different stages, different niches, different starting points. But a pattern keeps repeating across every one. This video walks through every case plus the pattern."

0:15 - 0:30 CREDIBILITY
VO: "I'm sharing this because the pattern is operating-knowledge worth broadcasting. And because I want the next twelve to know what they're walking into."

0:30 - 1:00 FRAMEWORK
VO: "I'll organize the cases by starting stage. Four at early stage — under twenty-K monthly. Four at mid-stage — twenty to eighty. Four at upper-stage — eighty and above."

1:00 - 1:30 ROADMAP + PRE-HANDLE
VO: "Each case is about ninety seconds. You might be thinking this is a launch video. It's adjacent to one, honest disclosure. But you'll see twelve specific mechanisms whether you ever buy anything, and the pattern reveal at the end is useful on its own."

CASE #1 — Freelancer → $12K/mo (1:30 - 3:00)
Context: Freelancer at $4K/mo, mostly project-based.
Action: Productized 3 services, shifted to outcome pricing, hired a setter.
Outcome: $12K/mo by month 4.
[Dashboard shown on screen.]
Takeaway: "Freelancers don't scale by working more. They scale by structuring the work differently."

CASE #2 — Coach → $30K/mo (3:00 - 4:30)
Context: Coach at $8K/mo, 1:1 exclusively.
Action: Launched a $3K group program, retired 1:1 under $5K.
Outcome: $30K/mo by month 6.
[Stripe screenshot shown.]
Takeaway: "Group programs are not worse than 1:1. They're a different unit economics that compound differently."

CASE #3 — Consultant → Agency Model (4:30 - 6:00)
Context: Consultant at $15K/mo, solo.
Action: Hired 2 junior consultants, raised prices, repositioned as agency.
Outcome: $45K/mo by month 5.
[P&L shown.]
Takeaway: "Consultant-to-agency isn't a hire decision. It's a positioning decision first."

CASE #4 — SaaS Founder → 3x ACV (6:00 - 7:30)
Context: SaaS at $40K MRR, feature-led ads.
Action: Rewrote ads as outcome-first, added qualifier quiz.
Outcome: ACV 3x in 60 days.
[Dashboard shown.]
Takeaway: "SaaS funnels often leak at positioning, not at conversion."

CASE #5 — Coach → Cohort Model (7:30 - 9:00)
Context: Coach at $25K/mo, 1:1.
Action: Converted existing offer into a 12-week cohort.
Outcome: $80K launch. Ongoing cohorts.
[Launch dashboard shown.]
Takeaway: "Cohort is the retail layer on top of coaching."

CASE #6 — Freelancer → Productized (9:00 - 10:30)
Context: Freelancer at $6K/mo, wide service menu.
Action: Cut to one productized service, raised prices 3x.
Outcome: $18K/mo by month 3.
[Revenue chart.]
Takeaway: "Menu-killing is revenue-unlocking for most freelancers."

CASE #7 — Creator → Newsletter Offer (10:30 - 12:00)
Context: Creator at $40K/mo from courses.
Action: Launched $20/mo newsletter with offer wrap.
Outcome: $60K/mo by month 4.
[Subscriber chart + MRR.]
Takeaway: "Newsletter is not media. It's a reliably-billing offer with content embedded."

CASE #8 — Coach → Mastermind (12:00 - 13:30) [the "hardest case" — the most instructive]
Context: Coach at $80K/mo, burnt out.
Action: Retired cohort, launched $30K/year mastermind.
Outcome: $200K/mo by month 6. 1/4 the calendar load.
[Mastermind dashboard.]
Takeaway: "Price is the relief valve. The same clients will pay more for a format that honors the creator's time."

CASE #9 — Agency → Retainer (13:30 - 15:00)
Context: Agency at $50K/mo, project-based.
Action: Shifted to retainer only.
Outcome: $70K MRR by month 5. Higher retention.
[MRR chart.]
Takeaway: "Projects are revenue. Retainers are business."

CASE #10 — Creator → Community (15:00 - 16:30)
Context: Creator at $30K/mo, course-based.
Action: Built paid community, migrated buyers.
Outcome: $45K/mo recurring by month 6.
[Community metrics.]
Takeaway: "Community turns buyers into renewable revenue."

PATTERN (16:30 - 18:00)

VO: "Twelve cases. Different stages. Different niches. Different mechanisms. One through-line — every single one involved a pricing decision. Not marketing. Not hiring. Not content. Pricing."

[Diagram on screen.]

VO: "Every case required a price change in the first sixty days. The price change was the lever that made the rest of the strategy function. Without it, the other decisions didn't compound."

CLOSING (18:00 - 20:00)

VO: "That's the pattern. If you're stuck at your current stage and the strategy side feels right, check your pricing first. Most of the time that's the lever that's been waiting."

End-screen + funnel link.
```

### Retention Defenses

- 0:15 volume hook earns the watch.
- Case #4 (SaaS) is the mid-video surprise — different category than coaches/freelancers.
- Case #8 is the "hardest case" — tease it early, deliver at minute 12.
- 16:30 pattern reveal is what all 15+ minutes built to.

### Description (excerpt)

```
12 client results across 6 months — different stages, different niches, different starting points. But a pattern repeats across every one. This video walks through every case plus the pattern at the end.

FTC disclosure: All cases have client permission on file. Some clients remain anonymous by request.

Chapters:
[as above]

[Creator's channels]

[Hashtags]
```

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- Client permission on file for every case.
- FTC disclosure for any paid testimonials or compensated endorsements.
- Private data redacted while keeping the shape of numbers visible.
- No income guarantees — "this client did X" is a claim; "you can do X" is a guarantee.
- Truth Gate: every specific number survives fact-check.

## Hand-off

Proof Showcase deliverables:

- Full script (1,400-3,000 words).
- Title + 2 variants.
- Thumbnail concept + 1 variant (grid vs. big-number).
- Chapter markers with each case named.
- B-roll / screenshot list per case.
- Testimonial clip list with timestamps.
- Client permission forms signed.
- Description with FTC disclosure.
- Compliance check signed off.
