# YouTube Variant — VSSL / Hidden-Pitch Long-Form Video (20-45 min)

> **Signature move.** A long-form video that delivers genuinely useful content for 80% of its runtime, then reveals an offer in the final 20% — so naturally that viewers who converted often say they "forgot they were watching a sales video." This is the YouTube-native sales letter. The video sells without triggering the "this guy is selling me" defense. The reveal happens at the moment the viewer is most invested in the mechanism the creator is teaching.

## When to Use

- Distribution is YouTube organic — not paid traffic. For paid traffic, use a traditional VSL (see `build-vsl` SKILL, Variants A-D).
- Pillar is Authority Build combined with Direct-Response Conversion.
- Conversion stage is Consideration or Decision — the viewer is product-aware or most-aware.
- The offer is $1K-$30K and has a mechanism that can be partially taught on video.
- Creator is content-first and the audience expects teaching.

## When NOT to Use

- Paid traffic. VSSLs convert poorly on paid traffic because the watch-time economics don't hold up when viewers are cold. Use a traditional 15-step or Pull-Push-Persuade VSL for paid.
- Offer is under $500 — the 80% value / 20% pitch ratio is too much runtime for a low-ticket direct-cart.
- Offer is enterprise $100K+ — the asynchronous format doesn't map to enterprise sales cycles.
- Creator has no existing audience to surface the video to. VSSL needs subscriber velocity or related-video surfacing.

## Title Formula

Pattern: `[Mechanism or Big Idea] — [Outcome] — [Specificity]`

Examples (archetypal):

1. "The 3-Variable Framework Behind $1M-a-Year Coaching Businesses"
2. "How to Build a Creator Business That Pays You Monthly (Even When You're Not Working)"
3. "The Real Reason Most Offers Fail at the Guarantee (And How to Write One That Actually Works)"

Title rules:

- Does not signal "sales video" — reads as educational.
- Names a specific mechanism or big idea.
- Promises a concrete outcome.
- Never includes "watch this entire video" or "stick around until the end" — these signal sales content.

## Thumbnail SOP

VSSL thumbnails are educational-first:

- **Concept-visualization thumbnail.** A diagram, a framework, a whiteboard sketch that visualizes the mechanism.
- **Creator + idea thumbnail.** Creator face (40-50%) plus a concept label ("The 3 Variables" or "The Hidden Cost").
- **No "watch this" thumbnail.** No finger-pointing, no shocked face, no "DO THIS" yellow text. VSSL lives on credibility, not clickbait.

## Script Structure (5 Chapters)

### Chapter 1 — "What Is [Mechanism]?" (0:00 - 6:00)

- **0:00 - 0:30 — Hook.** Name the mechanism or big idea. "There's a three-variable framework that sits under every coaching business that clears a million a year. Almost nobody names it explicitly. In this video I will."
- **0:30 - 1:30 — Analogy.** Use an analogy from outside the domain that makes the mechanism click. Analogies earn trust because they show mastery.
- **1:30 - 3:30 — How You Got Into It.** Tell the specific story of how the creator discovered / learned / worked with this mechanism. 3-A structure.
- **3:30 - 5:30 — Why It's Trending Up.** Name the market-timing reason this mechanism matters now.
- **5:30 - 6:00 — Chapter Close.** Tease Chapter 2.

### Chapter 2 — Who It's For / Requirements (6:00 - 12:00)

- **6:00 - 7:00 — Pre-handle Experience Objection.** "You might be thinking this only works if you already have X. That's not true. Here's the real prerequisite."
- **7:00 - 9:00 — Who This Works For.** Specific stage, specific audience, specific starting conditions.
- **9:00 - 11:00 — Who This Doesn't Work For.** Specific exclusions — mindset-based, work-ethic-based, stage-based.
- **11:00 - 11:30 — Pre-handle Money Objection.** "You might be thinking this takes a bunch of money to start. Here's what it actually costs."
- **11:30 - 12:00 — Chapter Close.** Tease Chapter 3.

### Chapter 3 — How It's Different (12:00 - 20:00)

- **12:00 - 13:30 — What Most People Do.** Name the conventional approach neutrally. Do not straw-man.
- **13:30 - 16:00 — Why That Fails.** The specific failure mode of the conventional approach.
- **16:00 - 18:30 — What This Does Instead.** The specific difference. Named. Diagrammed.
- **18:30 - 19:30 — Why That Matters.** The compounding effect of the difference.
- **19:30 - 20:00 — Chapter Close.** Tease Chapter 4.

### Chapter 4 — Social Proof (20:00 - 30:00)

- **20:00 - 23:00 — Student / Client 1.** Specific starting situation, specific mechanism applied, specific outcome. Visible artifact if possible (dashboard, Stripe, testimonial clip).
- **23:00 - 26:00 — Student / Client 2.** Different starting situation — isomorphism matters. If all three are the same, the pattern narrows.
- **26:00 - 29:00 — Student / Client 3.** Third starting situation. Ideally spans beginner, intermediate, advanced.
- **29:00 - 30:00 — Pattern Recognition.** Name what all three have in common. The shared mechanism is the proof.

### Chapter 5 — Two Options / The Reveal (30:00 - 45:00)

This is where the pitch lives, but it does not feel like a pitch.

- **30:00 - 32:00 — The Fork.** "You have two options from here. Option one: take what you've learned in this video and go figure it out yourself. That's a valid path. Here's what it usually looks like — [realistic timeline and struggles]."
- **32:00 - 34:00 — Option 2.** "Option two — I've built the whole thing into a proven system. Here's what it is." Name the offer.
- **34:00 - 38:00 — What's Inside.** The deliverables, briefly. Not a value-stack dump — a focused walkthrough.
- **38:00 - 40:00 — Who It's For / Guarantee.** Re-qualify. Reverse risk.
- **40:00 - 42:00 — Proof Again.** One final case study in detail, because the reveal just happened and the viewer needs a re-install of belief.
- **42:00 - 44:00 — How to Get It.** Where to go, what happens when you click. Assumptive, not ask-forward.
- **44:00 - 45:00 — The Frame Reset.** "And if option one is where you land, that's great — the video you just watched has real value on its own. This part was just for people who want the full thing."

## CTA Placement

- No CTA until minute 30 (the 80% / 20% rule).
- Soft lead-magnet mention at minute 2-3 in the description ("template in the description") — not in the video.
- The CTA at minute 42-44 is to the offer's funnel — application, book-a-call, or checkout depending on offer structure.
- End-screen: the offer's funnel page AND the related content video. Give viewers both options.

## Retention Curve

Target: AVD above 40% for 30-45 min VSSL.

Defense points:

- **Minute 0:30** — hook. Viewers decide if this is worth 30+ minutes. The mechanism name and the promise do the work.
- **Minute 3:30** — story chapter. Retention usually rises here because story increases investment.
- **Minute 12:00** — Chapter 3 opens. The "how it's different" is the sharpest teaching section. Retention often spikes.
- **Minute 20:00** — proof section begins. This is the highest-retention chapter in most VSSLs.
- **Minute 30:00** — the reveal. Retention drops here. That drop is expected — some viewers leave at the "option 2" moment. That's fine; the ones who stay are converting.
- **Minute 42:00** — how-to-get-it. Retention rises again for converting viewers.

If retention drops 20+% at the minute 30 reveal, the problem is usually a reveal that feels abrupt. Smooth the transition from Chapter 4 to Chapter 5.

## Chapter Markers

```
0:00 - Intro
0:30 - Chapter 1: What Is [Mechanism]
6:00 - Chapter 2: Who It's For
12:00 - Chapter 3: How It's Different
20:00 - Chapter 4: Student Results
30:00 - Chapter 5: Your Two Options
45:00 - Outro
```

## End-Screen Strategy

VSSL end-screen is different from other YouTube formats because it has a direct-response goal:

- Primary: the offer's funnel page (external link via pinned comment + description if link-in-YouTube is not supported for that account).
- Secondary: the next video in the series (for viewers who chose option 1).
- No "subscribe" element as primary — the conversion is the point.

## Loop Management

VSSL loops are structured to close at the reveal, not earlier:

- Loop 1 opened at 0:30 ("the three-variable framework") — closed progressively through Chapters 1-3.
- Loop 2 opened at 1:00 ("one specific student went from X to Y") — closed at Chapter 4.
- Loop 3 opened at 6:30 ("who this works for might surprise you") — closed at Chapter 2.

All educational loops close by minute 30. After minute 30, the only open loop is "what do I do next" — which the reveal closes.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- 3+ `phrases_to_use` per 5-minute segment.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- VSSL voice is conversational and generous — the creator gives freely for 30 minutes before asking. Voice must sound like teaching, not selling. Any hint of "sales voice" kills the trick.
- Read the entire script aloud. If any section sounds like an infomercial, rewrite.

## Specificity Floor

- Every mechanism named specifically.
- Every case study quoted with specific starting situation, specific decisions, specific outcomes.
- The offer reveal uses specific numbers — specific price, specific deliverables, specific guarantee.

## Script Word Count

- 30-min VSSL: ~4,200-4,500 words
- 45-min VSSL: ~6,300-6,750 words

## Production Notes

- Creator on-camera primary.
- Whiteboard or diagram segments welcomed — they reinforce the "teaching" frame.
- Studio or home-studio setup. Consistent lighting across chapters.
- Audio: professional mic mandatory. VSSL listening experience is the primary mode (many viewers listen in-app at 1.25x-1.5x speed).
- B-roll: student / client dashboards, screenshots, testimonial clips. Redact private information.
- Edit pace: slower than Deep-Dive. VSSL is a long watch — viewers need space to process.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Reveal feels abrupt.** The jump from Chapter 4 to Chapter 5 feels jarring. Smooth with: "Before I tell you what's next, here's the pattern across those three cases..."
- **Too much pitch.** The offer reveal runs 20+ minutes. That's a VSL, not a VSSL. Keep the reveal to 12-15 minutes max of 45-min total.
- **Weak teaching in first 30 min.** If the first 80% isn't genuinely useful, the reveal feels like a bait-and-switch. The teaching must be valuable enough to stand alone.
- **Missing pattern recognition.** Chapter 4 shows three students but doesn't name what unites them. The unity is the whole proof.
- **Offer reveal that doesn't match the teaching.** The 30 minutes of teaching was about X, but the offer is Y. Viewers feel deceived. The offer must be the productized version of the teaching.
- **Forgetting the Option 1 dignification.** Viewers who choose option 1 feel rejected if the reveal doesn't validate that choice. The "option 1 is valid" line at 30:00 and the reinforcement at 44:00 preserve trust with non-converters.

## Worked Example — The 3-Variable Pricing Framework

### Title
"The 3-Variable Framework Behind $1M-a-Year Coaching Businesses"

### Thumbnail
- Whiteboard with 3 boxes labeled (variables unnamed in thumbnail)
- Creator at whiteboard, pointing at middle box
- Text: "$1M/year"

### Script Outline (abbreviated)

```
CHAPTER 1 — What Is the 3-Variable Framework (0:00 - 6:00)

0:00 - 0:30 HOOK
VO: "There's a three-variable framework that sits under every coaching business that clears a million a year. Almost nobody names it explicitly. I'll name it, and I'll show you how to see it in your own business."

0:30 - 1:30 ANALOGY
VO: "Pricing a coaching program is like tuning a piano. Three strings have to match — if any one is off, the chord sounds wrong. Most coaches tune one string. That's why they sound wrong to the buyer."

1:30 - 3:30 HOW I FOUND IT
VO: "I priced coaching programs for four hundred engagements before I saw it. The pattern was always the same — when all three variables were in sync, the offer sold fast. When any one was off, the offer stuck. It took me two years to figure out what the three were."

3:30 - 5:30 WHY IT MATTERS NOW
VO: "This matters more in twenty-twenty-six than it did five years ago. The buyer is more skeptical. The market is more crowded. Getting the three variables right is how you separate from everyone else pricing by feel."

CHAPTER 2 — Who This Works For (6:00 - 12:00)

[Pre-handle experience objection, specific stage audience, exclusions, pre-handle money objection, chapter close.]

CHAPTER 3 — How the 3-Variable Framework Is Different (12:00 - 20:00)

12:00 - 13:30 WHAT MOST COACHES DO
VO: "Most coaches price based on what their competitors charge. Look at three other coaches, price in the middle, tweak up or down."

13:30 - 16:00 WHY THAT FAILS
VO: "That fails because pricing at the market median is pricing at the quality median. You are not at the market median. You have a specific mechanism your competitors don't have. Pricing like them is pricing away from that mechanism."

16:00 - 18:30 THE THREE VARIABLES
VO: "Variable one — outcome value. What the outcome is worth to the buyer in dollars. Variable two — mechanism scarcity. How replaceable your mechanism is. Variable three — proof density. How many case studies you have at the stage your next buyer is at."

18:30 - 19:30 WHY THIS MATTERS
VO: "Price becomes a math problem. Three variables multiplied. The output is a range, not a number. Your job is to pick a point inside the range and ship."

CHAPTER 4 — Three Students (20:00 - 30:00)

[Three case studies — coach at $20K MRR, coach at $50K MRR, coach at $80K MRR. Specific starting conditions, specific variable analysis, specific outcomes.]

29:00 - 30:00 PATTERN RECOGNITION
VO: "Three coaches. Three stages. Three price moves. What they share — all three named their mechanism scarcity variable before they moved the price. That is the unlock."

CHAPTER 5 — Your Two Options (30:00 - 45:00)

30:00 - 32:00 OPTION 1
VO: "You have two options from here. Option one — take the three-variable framework from this video and apply it yourself. That's a real path. Here's what it usually looks like — three to six months of experimenting, one or two uncomfortable price changes, maybe some lost deals while the new positioning lands. Some coaches do this and get there."

32:00 - 34:00 OPTION 2
VO: "Option two — I've built the three-variable framework into a coaching program. Nine weeks. I walk through each variable in detail, then help you calculate your range for your specific business, then help you announce the price change to your list in a way that doesn't lose the audience that was paying the old price."

34:00 - 38:00 WHAT'S INSIDE
[Nine weeks, specific modules, live sessions, community, templates.]

38:00 - 40:00 WHO IT'S FOR + GUARANTEE
[Re-qualify, specific stage, specific traits. Guarantee with specific conditions.]

40:00 - 42:00 ONE MORE CASE
[One specific client who went through the program — full story, full numbers, full mechanism.]

42:00 - 44:00 HOW TO GET IT
[Where to go, what happens when you click, the sequence from click to enrolled.]

44:00 - 45:00 FRAME RESET
VO: "If option one is where you land, that's a real path. Use the three variables. You'll get there. This was for the people who want the full system. Either way, the framework is yours now."
```

### Retention Defenses

- 0:30 hook earns the 45-minute commitment.
- 3:30 story moment deepens investment.
- 16:00 three-variable reveal is the mid-video payoff.
- 20:00 proof section begins — highest retention chapter.
- 30:00 reveal transition is smooth due to pattern-recognition line.
- 42:00 how-to-get-it holds converting viewers.

### Loop Map

- Loop 1 opened at 0:30 (the three variables) — closed at 16:00.
- Loop 2 opened at 3:30 (the discovery story) — closed at 5:30.
- Loop 3 opened at 18:30 (the compounding effect) — closed at 29:00.
- Loop 4 opened at 6:30 (the surprising who-it-works-for) — closed at 12:00.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- No income guarantees.
- Full FTC disclosure at minute 0-2 if the video contains sponsored content OR if it's a VSSL selling a product (YouTube and FTC both expect clear disclosure).
- Every case study has client permission on file.
- Truth Gate: every specific claim survives a 30-second fact-check.
