# YouTube Variant — Challenge Progression (15-30 min)

> **Signature move.** A multi-episode or single long-form video that documents the creator (or a subject) attempting a specific, time-bound challenge with a measurable outcome. The viewer watches the progression from start to end, with clear milestones, setbacks, and the final result. The video sells process-credibility and entertainment-value, not a product directly.

## When to Use

- Creator has a genuinely interesting, measurable challenge to attempt — with an unpredictable outcome.
- Pillar is Challenge, Documentary, or Entertainment-Authority Fusion.
- Conversion stage is Awareness (attracting broad audience through entertainment) or Consideration (demonstrating capability under constraint).
- The challenge is publicly verifiable. Private challenges feel fabricated.

## When NOT to Use

- Outcome is guaranteed. If the creator is not actually risking failure, the challenge has no tension.
- Challenge is outside the creator's domain in a way that looks stunt-y. Challenge-for-challenge's-sake is low-signal content.
- Challenge involves safety risks, financial irresponsibility, or client-facing commitments that shouldn't be public experiments.
- Creator cannot sustain the multi-episode arc. Single-episode retrospectives work, but the ongoing challenge format requires creator availability.

## Title Formula

Pattern: `[Challenge] in [Timeframe] — [Constraint or Angle]`

Examples (archetypal):

1. "I'm Building a Business to $10K/mo in 30 Days From Scratch"
2. "Can I Get 10,000 Subscribers in 60 Days? The Full Attempt"
3. "$0 to $1M ARR in One Year — Month 3 Update"

Title rules:

- 60-70 characters.
- Specific challenge, specific timeframe, specific measurable.
- If it's an episode in a series, the episode number or month should be in the title.
- Honest about unpredictability — no "how I built" if the video is mid-attempt.

## Thumbnail SOP

Challenge thumbnails visualize stakes:

- **Countdown thumbnail.** A specific number ("DAY 12 OF 30" or "$4,200 / $10,000"). Creates visible stakes.
- **Outcome-at-risk thumbnail.** Creator face + a large number + a question mark. "10,000 subs?"
- **Progress-bar thumbnail.** A visual bar showing how far the creator is from the goal.

Text overlay: the current progress state. Updates across the series.

## Script Structure

### For the Pilot / First Episode:

#### Intro (0:00 - 2:00)

- **0:00 - 0:15 — Challenge Declaration.** "Thirty days. Ten thousand dollars a month. From scratch. Starting today."
- **0:15 - 0:45 — Stakes.** Why this challenge. What's at risk. Why now.
- **0:45 - 1:15 — Rules.** What counts. What doesn't. How the measurement works.
- **1:15 - 1:45 — Roadmap.** Weekly check-in schedule. Where the creator is starting from.
- **1:45 - 2:00 — Pre-Handle.** "You might be thinking this is a stunt. Here's what I'm actually trying to test."

#### The Setup (2:00 - 7:00)

- Starting state documented in detail.
- First plan outlined.
- Resources / constraints named.

#### Days / Weeks 1 Unfolding (7:00 - 20:00)

- Vignettes of the first check-in periods.
- Milestones hit and missed.
- Course corrections.

#### Current State (20:00 - 25:00)

- Where the creator is now.
- What's working / not working.
- What comes next.

#### Call to Follow (25:00 - 30:00)

- Viewer invitation to follow the rest of the series.
- Subscribe ask tied to the challenge arc.
- End-screen: first full update episode.

### For Update Episodes:

Same structure but the intro summarizes the story so far in 60-90 seconds, and the body focuses on the new period (week, month).

### For a Retrospective (single long-form after completion):

- Intro: challenge + outcome teased.
- Act 1: setup.
- Act 2: middle third of challenge — where things went sideways.
- Act 3: final third — resolution.
- Synthesis: what the creator learned.
- Final state + takeaways.

## CTA Placement

- No offer CTA in early episodes. The challenge is the content.
- Subscribe ask strongly tied to the serial format: "The next update drops in 7 days. Subscribe so you don't miss it."
- Comment prompt mid-video: "What would you do in this situation? Drop it in comments, I'll address the best ones in the next update."
- End-screen: next episode if available, otherwise a related challenge video.

## Retention Curve

Target: AVD above 45% for 15-30 min challenge videos. Retention skews higher than Deep-Dive because the entertainment value pulls viewers through.

Defense points:

- **Minute 0:15** — challenge declaration. Viewer decides if the stakes interest them.
- **Minute 2:00** — setup phase. Low retention floor — setup is the driest part.
- **Minute 7:00** — the first real event. If nothing has happened yet, viewers leave.
- **Minute 15:00** — mid-video pivot. The biggest surprise or setback.
- **Minute 25:00** — current state. Viewers want to know where things stand.

## Chapter Markers

```
0:00 - Challenge Declaration
2:00 - The Setup
7:00 - Days 1-7
12:00 - Days 8-14
17:00 - Days 15-21
22:00 - Days 22-28
25:00 - Current State
28:00 - What's Next
```

Adjust based on the specific timeframe (weeks, months, days).

## End-Screen Strategy

- Next video: the next episode in the series.
- Playlist: the full challenge series.
- Subscribe element emphasized — serial format depends on retention across episodes.

## Loop Management

Challenge videos have naturally open loops — the challenge outcome is the biggest loop.

- Loop 1 opened at 0:15 (the challenge outcome) — only closes in the final episode.
- Loop 2 opened at 7:00 (this episode's specific obstacle) — closes by end of episode.
- Loop 3 opened at 15:00 (mid-episode surprise) — closes at episode-end.

Serial loops are features, not bugs — viewers subscribe to find out.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- 3+ `phrases_to_use` per 5-minute segment.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Voice is excited but honest. "This worked" and "this broke" both delivered with the same credibility. No performative excitement.

## Specificity Floor

- Challenge timeframe is specific.
- Measurement criteria are specific and auditable.
- Milestones are specific dollar amounts, subscriber counts, completions.
- Setbacks are specific — "ad account flagged on day 4" not "something went wrong."

## Script Word Count

- 15-min: ~2,100-2,250 words
- 20-min: ~2,800-3,000 words
- 30-min: ~4,200-4,500 words

## Production Notes

- Documentary-style. Handheld or light-tripod footage.
- Daily or weekly self-shot vlogs compiled into the episode.
- Screen recordings of dashboards, email platforms, ad accounts as evidence.
- Interviews with team members or advisors for variety.
- Natural light + ambient sound. Over-produced footage breaks the authenticity frame.
- Music: minimal, rises during setback/breakthrough moments.

## Description Structure

```
[Challenge declaration — the outcome and timeframe]

This is episode [N] of a [total] episode series.

- [Episode 1 link]

[as above]

[Hashtags]
```

## Common Failure Modes

- **Guaranteed-outcome challenge.** Creator sets up a challenge they already know will succeed. Viewers sense the lack of tension.
- **Undocumented progress.** Claims about progress aren't backed by on-screen evidence. Challenge videos require dashboard screens, transaction records, subscriber counts.
- **Stunt-flavor.** The challenge doesn't relate to anything the creator normally does. Feels like content-for-content's-sake.
- **Missing setbacks.** Every episode is "everything's going great." Viewers need friction. If the challenge really is going perfectly, share the near-misses that could have gone wrong.
- **Abandoned series.** Challenge series started but never finished. Kills trust for future challenge content.

## Worked Example — $0 to $10K/mo in 30 Days

### Title
"I'm Building a Business to $10K/mo in 30 Days From Scratch — Day 1"

### Thumbnail
- Countdown: "DAY 1 / 30"
- Current state: "$0 / $10,000"
- Creator at desk, laptop open

### Script Outline (Episode 1, abbreviated)

```
INTRO (0:00 - 2:00)

0:00 - 0:15 CHALLENGE DECLARATION
VO: "Thirty days. Ten thousand dollars a month. From zero. Starting today."

0:15 - 0:45 STAKES
VO: "I've run multiple businesses past ten thousand a month. But most people think it takes a year or more to get there. I want to test — with everything I know now, can I compress it to thirty days?"

0:45 - 1:15 RULES
VO: "Rules — no existing audience. No reliance on my personal brand. Start with five hundred dollars in total spend capital. Anything above ten thousand in recurring at day thirty counts as success."

1:15 - 1:45 ROADMAP
VO: "Weekly update videos. Every seven days I publish where I am. You see the dashboards. You see the mistakes. You see if this works or fails."

1:45 - 2:00 PRE-HANDLE
VO: "This might seem like a stunt. I'd argue the test matters — if it works, it proves the process compresses. If it fails, I'll tell you exactly why."

THE SETUP (2:00 - 7:00)

2:00 - 3:30 Starting state
[Screen: blank email list, zero social accounts for the test persona, $500 in ad-account credit.]
VO: "Here's what I'm starting with. New email provider, zero subscribers. New LinkedIn account, zero connections. Five hundred dollars of ad credit in a fresh Meta account. That's the full starting kit."

3:30 - 5:00 First plan
VO: "The plan — sell one outcome-led service at three thousand dollars. Use five hundred dollars of ads to test the offer. Iterate on the ads based on first-week response."

5:00 - 7:00 Resources / constraints
VO: "What I have — operating knowledge. What I don't have — audience, pre-existing client list, or any unfair advantage in this specific niche."

WEEK 1 UNFOLDING (7:00 - 20:00)

7:00 - 10:00 Day 1-3
[Vignettes of setting up funnel, first ads launched, first responses.]
VO: "Day three. First ten conversations. Seven are not qualified. Three are close. No close yet."

10:00 - 14:00 Day 4-5
[The setback — ad account flagged.]
VO: "Day four. Meta flagged the ad account. I have to resubmit. Lost forty-eight hours and two hundred dollars of spend momentum."

14:00 - 17:00 Day 6-7
[The first close.]
VO: "Day seven. First close. Three thousand dollars. The offer works. The ad that produced the lead is back up. Everything else is on track."

17:00 - 20:00 Week 1 recap
[Dashboard shown: first close, current MRR projection, budget remaining.]
VO: "Week one — three thousand closed, two thousand two hundred in ad spend used, remaining budget three hundred. Twenty-three days left."

CURRENT STATE (20:00 - 25:00)

VO: "Where I am now. Three thousand of a ten-thousand target. Twenty-three days left. Ad account stable. Offer validated. Next week — scaling spend and pushing for two more closes by day fourteen."

CALL TO FOLLOW (25:00 - 30:00)

VO: "Day fourteen update next week. Subscribe so you see the mid-point. Comment what you'd do from here — I'll address the best ideas in the next episode."

[End-screen + subscribe prompt.]
```

### Retention Defenses

- 0:15 challenge declaration earns the watch.
- 7:00 first real event — the first 10 conversations.
- 10:00 ad account flagged — the setback is the highest-retention chapter.
- 14:00 first close — the payoff.
- 17:00 recap — the "where am I" moment.

### Description (excerpt)

```
30 days. $10K/mo. From zero. This is Day 1.

I've run multiple businesses past $10K/mo. The test: can I compress that to 30 days with only $500 of starting capital, a new Meta account, no audience, no pre-existing client list?

Weekly updates. Every 7 days I publish where I am, including the dashboards.

This is episode 1 of a 4-5 episode series.

Chapters:
[as above]

[Creator's channels]

[Hashtags]
```

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- No income guarantees — the challenge frame explicitly includes failure as an outcome.
- Honest disclosure if the challenge is for content + real business test — FTC-aware.
- Dashboard screens genuinely from the challenge attempt, not from prior business.
- Any team members featured have consented.

## Hand-off

Challenge Progression deliverables:

- Full script per episode (2,100-4,500 words).
- Title + variant per episode (numbered appropriately).
- Thumbnail concept + update-visible progress state.
- Chapter markers per episode.
- B-roll / screen-recording list with timestamps.
- Challenge rules document (separate from script, for creator's own tracking).
- Series arc outline (all 4-6 episodes mapped).
- Description with FTC-aware framing.
- Compliance check signed off.
