# YouTube Variant — Lifestyle / Authority (12-25 min)

> **Signature move.** A video that shows the creator's actual life, routine, or moment — not as a day-in-life reel, but as a long-form window into how the creator operates. The audience receives identity association and human texture they cannot get from short-form. The video sells the creator as a person worth following, not a product.

## When to Use

- Creator has a distinct way of operating that the audience is curious about (routine, workspace, family balance, creative process, travel-as-work, specific work rhythm).
- Pillar is Personality, Cult-of-Creator, or Identity Association.
- Conversion stage is Awareness (attracting new audience who identify with the creator's lifestyle) or Consideration (building parasocial trust before the pitch).
- The creator has enough existing audience to absorb a non-search-optimized video. Lifestyle videos don't SEO.

## When NOT to Use

- Creator is early-stage and needs top-of-funnel search traffic. Lifestyle videos depend on subscriber surfacing.
- Creator's lifestyle is already over-exposed on short-form. Audience gets fatigue.
- Creator is in a regulated or enterprise niche where personal content dilutes brand.
- The lifestyle content would expose proprietary business info, family members without consent, or sensitive locations.

## Title Formula

Pattern: `[Lifestyle Framing] [Specific Outcome or Texture]`

Examples (archetypal):

1. "A Week in My Life Running a Seven-Figure Creator Business From Mexico"
2. "How I Actually Spend My Day Running a Five-Person Team"
3. "My Workspace Tour (And the 4 Things That Changed My Output)"

Title rules:

- 60-70 characters.
- Reads as personal, not promotional.
- Specific anchor (revenue stage, location, team size, outcome).
- No "you won't believe" or clickbait.

## Thumbnail SOP

Lifestyle thumbnails are aesthetic-forward:

- **Environment thumbnail.** The creator in their workspace, home, or travel location. Wide framing.
- **Object thumbnail.** A specific object from the video (desk, camera, view, plant, piece of tech) with creator in corner.
- **Moment thumbnail.** A candid moment from the video — working, walking, thinking.

Text overlay: minimal. Often just the location or a key label ("Mexico," "The Studio," "My Daily Routine"). Lifestyle videos don't need click-bait text because the environment itself is the hook.

## Script Structure

Lifestyle videos use a looser script than Deep-Dive or Tutorial. The structure is vignette-based, not chapter-based.

### Intro (0:00 - 1:00)

- **0:00 - 0:15 — Opening moment.** Often the creator in a specific setting doing something specific. "It's seven AM in Mexico City. I'm about to do what I do every Tuesday."
- **0:15 - 0:30 — Frame the video.** What this video is. Not "hey guys welcome back" — direct framing.
- **0:30 - 0:45 — Why this matters.** What the audience gets from spending 20 minutes with the creator.
- **0:45 - 1:00 — Soft roadmap.** "I'll walk through [thing], [thing], and [thing]."

### Vignette 1 — Morning / Setup (1:00 - 5:00)

- Specific routine, specific setting, specific objects.
- Creator voice-over narrates what's happening and what it teaches.
- 3-5 minute segment.

### Vignette 2 — Core Work Block (5:00 - 10:00)

- The actual work the creator does.
- One specific project, email, conversation, or output.
- Voice-over reflects on why this work matters.

### Vignette 3 — A Moment (10:00 - 15:00)

- Not staged. A real moment — a decision, a conversation with a team member, a problem that emerged, a workout, a meal.
- 5 minutes of texture.

### Vignette 4 — End of Day / Reflection (15:00 - 20:00)

- Closing the day.
- What worked, what didn't.
- One specific lesson from the day.

### Outro (20:00 - 22:00 or 25:00)

- Soft close — not a pitch, not a hard CTA.
- "If you liked this, I post one of these every week" or similar.
- End-screen with related video.

## CTA Placement

- No offer CTA. Lifestyle videos are long-term trust, not short-term conversion.
- Soft "subscribe if you like this" at minute 12-15.
- Comment prompt at minute 18-20: "What part of my week did you most relate to?"
- End-screen: next lifestyle video or a day-in-life from a different week.

## Retention Curve

Target: AVD above 40% for 15-25 min lifestyle. Lower than Deep-Dive target because lifestyle viewers are there for duration, not density.

Defense points:

- **Minute 0:15** — the frame decision. Viewer decides if this is interesting enough.
- **Minute 5:00** — first vignette ends. If the first five minutes are boring, viewers leave.
- **Minute 10:00** — mid-video pivot. The "moment" vignette should be the most texture-rich.
- **Minute 15:00** — final third. Some viewers leave; those who stay are the core audience.

Lifestyle videos tolerate lower retention in absolute terms but reward creators with higher subscriber conversion rates among the retention that does stay.

## Chapter Markers

```
0:00 - Opening
1:00 - [Vignette 1 Name]
5:00 - [Vignette 2 Name]
10:00 - [Vignette 3 Name]
15:00 - [Vignette 4 Name]
20:00 - Closing Reflection
```

Name each vignette after its content — "Morning in the Studio," "Writing Block," "Team Call," "Evening Walk." Specific, not generic.

## End-Screen Strategy

- Next video: another lifestyle video or a day-in-life from different week.
- Playlist: the lifestyle series.
- Subscribe element.

Lifestyle end-screens do not push to offer funnels. The trust-building is the point.

## Loop Management

Lifestyle videos use fewer curiosity loops. The "loop" is often "will the creator finish this day" or "how does this specific moment resolve."

- Loop 1 opened at 0:15 (the frame claim) — closed throughout.
- Loop 2 opened at the start of Vignette 3 (a decision or problem) — closed at the end of Vignette 4.

Do not force curiosity loops — lifestyle videos live on texture, not suspense.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- 3+ `phrases_to_use` per 5-minute segment.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Voice is reflective, slower, less produced than Deep-Dive or Tutorial. Pauses are allowed. Thinking-out-loud is fine.

## Specificity Floor

- Every vignette has a specific time, location, and object.
- Every lesson at the end of a vignette is specific, not generic.
- The "week" or "day" framing is anchored — not "a typical week" but "this week, specifically."

## Script Word Count

Lighter than Deep-Dive. Lifestyle voice-over runs ~100-130 words per minute because of pauses and ambient moments.

- 12-min: ~1,200-1,500 words
- 20-min: ~2,000-2,500 words
- 25-min: ~2,500-3,200 words

## Production Notes

- B-roll heavy — 40-60% b-roll of environments, objects, processes.
- Creator on-camera less than Deep-Dive — 20-30% of runtime.
- Natural light preferred over studio light.
- Audio: ambient sound layered with voice-over. Lifestyle videos sound different from Deep-Dive.
- Edit pace: slower. Long takes of b-roll (5-10 seconds) are welcomed.
- Music: subtle, often ambient or instrumental. Lifestyle videos have a soundtrack more than Deep-Dive does.

## Description Structure

```
[Opening line — a reflective framing of what this video is]

[1-2 paragraphs — context for this specific week or day, why you chose to film it]

[Personal channels / socials]

Chapters:
[as above]

Related videos:
[3-5 lifestyle videos or related content]

[Hashtags — minimal, personal]
```

## Common Failure Modes

- **Over-produced.** The lifestyle content reads as staged. Viewers detect staging and trust drops. Keep the production rough.
- **No specific moments.** The video is a montage of generic-creator-living. Every vignette needs a specific anchor — a specific decision, a specific object, a specific conversation.
- **Forced lesson at every vignette.** The creator tries to extract a lesson from every moment. Feels lecturing. Let some moments be moments.
- **Missing texture.** The video shows the polished version of the life. Viewers want the unpolished version. Include the boring, the annoying, the real.
- **Length-for-length's-sake.** Stretching to 25 minutes when there's 15 minutes of actual content. Cut to the length the content supports.

## Worked Example — A Week From Mexico

### Title
"A Week in My Life Running a Seven-Figure Creator Business From Mexico"

### Thumbnail
- Wide shot of creator at outdoor workspace, laptop visible, Mexico City skyline
- Text: "Mexico. Week 4."
- Minimal face visibility

### Script Outline (abbreviated)

```
OPENING (0:00 - 1:00)

0:00 - 0:15 OPENING MOMENT
[B-roll of coffee, sunrise, creator at desk outdoor.]
VO: "It's seven AM in Mexico City. Week four of being based here. I'll show you what a work week looks like from this setup."

0:15 - 0:30 FRAME
VO: "This isn't a digital-nomad video. I'm not selling a lifestyle. I'm running a seven-figure content business from here for the next six months. This is what that actually looks like."

0:30 - 0:45 WHY THIS MATTERS
VO: "People ask — does the work actually get done when you're not in the usual setup. Short answer yes. Longer answer is the point of this video."

0:45 - 1:00 ROADMAP
VO: "I'll walk through my morning, a writing block, a team call, an evening that I almost blew up, and what I changed."

VIGNETTE 1 — Morning (1:00 - 5:00)

[B-roll of creator making coffee, reviewing inbox, stretching. Voice-over throughout.]
VO: "The first ninety minutes is protected. No calls, no Slack. I've protected this block for three years regardless of where I've been. Mexico didn't change that; it just moved the view."

[Specific moment: creator reviews a Loom from an editor, makes a note.]
VO: "This is the Loom from my editor this morning. She flagged three changes to a video we thought was done. The changes are right. That's a five-minute decision that would have cost the week if I'd ignored it."

VIGNETTE 2 — Writing Block (5:00 - 10:00)

[Creator at desk, laptop visible. Long takes.]
VO: "Ten AM. Writing block. The only rule is the doc has to be different at the end of the block than at the start."

[Specific moment: creator steps away for three minutes, returns.]
VO: "Stepping away is part of the writing. Some people think it breaks the flow. The flow comes back faster if you honor the step-away."

VIGNETTE 3 — Team Call That Went Sideways (10:00 - 15:00)

[Zoom call visible over shoulder, face of team member blurred. Real conversation.]
VO: "Two PM. Weekly review with the team. Today it went sideways — one of the projects we thought was shipping Thursday has a dependency we missed. We had to cut scope."

[Specific moment: the decision to cut scope.]
VO: "Cutting scope in the call is hard because everyone has been working against the original. But cutting late is worse. The rule — if a dependency is missed, cut scope in the call. Don't carry the promise into the next week."

VIGNETTE 4 — Evening Reset (15:00 - 20:00)

[Walk outside, sunset, creator reflective.]
VO: "Six PM. I almost blew up the evening because of the team call. Carried the frustration into dinner. Had to actively reset. Walk. Ten minutes. Solved it."

[Specific moment: creator sitting at a cafe.]
VO: "The rule I keep having to re-learn — work hiccups are work-hour problems. They don't belong in the evening. Mexico doesn't teach that; it just reveals when you haven't learned it."

CLOSING (20:00 - 22:00)

VO: "That was the week. Some decisions went well. One conversation went sideways. The work got done. The view was better than usual. That's honestly the whole point of the location change."

[End-screen prompt.]
VO: "If you liked this, I post one of these every three or four weeks. Next week I'm in a different city. Subscribe if you want to see how that one lands."
```

### Retention Defenses

- 0:15 frame line — "this isn't a nomad video" — separates from the category.
- 5:00 writing-block vignette defends the "is anything actually happening in this video" skepticism.
- 10:00 team-call-that-went-sideways vignette is the highest-retention chapter.
- 15:00 evening reset is unexpected texture.

### Description (excerpt)

```
A week in my life running a seven-figure creator business from Mexico City. Week 4. Not a nomad lifestyle video — a work-actually-happens video.

Chapters:
0:00 - Morning Coffee, First Ninety Minutes
5:00 - Writing Block

15:00 - Evening Reset
20:00 - Closing

[3-5 related lifestyle videos]

[Personal channels]

[Hashtags — minimal]
```

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept.
- No income guarantees implied.
- Team members and clients blurred or off-camera unless permissioned.
- Sensitive locations, home addresses not shown.
- No proprietary business information on screen.

## Hand-off

Lifestyle deliverables:

- Full script / vignette outline (1,200-3,200 words).
- Title + 1 variant.
- Thumbnail concept.
- Chapter markers named after vignettes.
- B-roll shot list with durations.
- Audio plan — ambient + voice-over levels.
- Music direction.
- Description with personal framing.
- Compliance check signed off.
