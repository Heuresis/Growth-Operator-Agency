# YouTube Variant — Case Study Breakdown (15-25 min)

> **Signature move.** A single specific client outcome reverse-engineered in full over 15-25 minutes. The video walks through the client's starting situation, the exact decisions made, the real metrics at each stage, the obstacles faced, and the generalizable lesson. The viewer finishes with a mental template they can apply to their own work. The video sells authority through visible cause-and-effect, not generic teaching.

## When to Use

- Creator has a specific, documented client outcome with permission for full disclosure.
- Pillar is Case Study, Proof, or Conversion-forward Authority.
- Conversion stage is Decision — viewers are evaluating whether the creator can produce outcomes like this for them.
- The case study is recent (within 18 months) and can be told with current-state metrics.

## When NOT to Use

- No permission from the client to disclose specifics. Anonymized case studies work for reels but not for 15-25 min videos — the audience needs texture.
- The outcome is too good to be believable without live customer presence. If the client can't appear, this variant is not the right tool.
- The creator's contribution to the outcome is small enough that the full breakdown exposes it.
- The audience is Awareness-stage and doesn't yet believe case studies.

## Title Formula

Pattern: `[Client Role / Stage] → [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe]`

Examples (archetypal):

1. "SaaS Founder Went From 2 Calls/Week to 30 Applications/Week in 9 Days — Full Teardown"
2. "How a Consultant Went From $8K/mo to $47K/mo in One Quarter (Full Breakdown)"
3. "The 90-Day Repositioning That Turned a $30K Service Business Into $120K/mo"

Title rules:

- 60-70 characters.
- Specific number in the outcome — not "dramatically increased" but the actual delta.
- Specific timeframe.
- Named client role or stage ("SaaS founder," "consultant," "coach").

## Thumbnail SOP

Case-study thumbnails visualize the gap — the before and after.

Options:

- **Split-screen thumbnail.** Left: the "before" metric. Right: the "after" metric. Arrow between. Face of creator in corner.
- **Chart thumbnail.** A visible line chart showing the transformation, with a dramatic slope. Creator face in corner.
- **Client-visible thumbnail.** If the client is comfortable, the client's face + name + role, with the delta number. Requires permission.

Text overlay: specific number (e.g., "$8K → $47K" or "2 → 30/week").

## Script Structure

### Intro (0:00 - 1:30)

- **0:00 - 0:15 — Hook.** The specific outcome in one sentence. "Nine days. Thirty applications a week. Here's the full breakdown."
- **0:15 - 0:30 — Who this client is.** Role, stage, prior context. No name if permissioned-out.
- **0:30 - 0:45 — Credibility.** Why this case study matters — the pattern, the stakes, the replicability.
- **0:45 - 1:15 — Roadmap.** "I'll walk through: starting state, the three decisions, the metrics at each stage, the obstacles, and the rule."
- **1:15 - 1:30 — Pre-handle + tease.** Pre-handle the "but this worked because the client already had X" objection.

### Chapter 1 — Starting State (1:30 - 4:30)

- The client's situation in detail.
- Specific metrics at the start.
- What the client had already tried.
- Why previous attempts failed.

### Chapter 2 — The First Decision (4:30 - 8:30)

- The specific decision — not "we improved the funnel" but "we replaced the ebook with a quiz that routes to three tiers of follow-up."
- The reasoning behind the decision.
- What happened in the first week of implementing.
- The metric shift at 7 days.

### Chapter 3 — The Second Decision (8:30 - 12:30)

- Same depth. Specific decision, reasoning, implementation, metric shift.

### Chapter 4 — The Third Decision (12:30 - 16:30)

- Same depth.

### Chapter 5 — Obstacles & Correction (16:30 - 19:30)

- What went wrong mid-way.
- What had to be corrected.
- The one decision that nearly derailed the outcome.

### Chapter 6 — The Final Metrics (19:30 - 21:30)

- Final numbers, shown on screen.
- The month-over-month breakdown.
- The compounding effect if applicable.

### Synthesis (21:30 - 23:30)

- The generalizable rule.
- What this pattern looks like in other cases.
- When this case study doesn't apply.

### Outro / Binge Loop (23:30 - 25:00)

- Link-back to the rule.
- New problem raised (often: "okay but how do you replicate this without my involvement").
- Next video tease.
- End-screen.

## CTA Placement

- Soft CTA at minute 12-15: "If you want the full template the client used, link in description."
- No offer CTA mid-video — Case Study Breakdown trades short-term conversion for long-term authority.
- Comment prompt at minute 20: "What stage are you at? Drop it in the comments and I'll do a case study at your stage."
- End-screen: next video in the case study series.

## Retention Curve

Target: AVD above 50% for 15-25 min case study videos.

Defense points:

- **Minute 1:00 - 1:30** — the viewer is asking "is this actually a teardown or a brag." Roadmap + pre-handle earn the stay.
- **Minute 4:30** — first chapter turn. The real teardown starts. First metric reveal.
- **Minute 12:30** — third decision reveal. Deepest chapter. Retention floor.
- **Minute 16:30** — obstacle reveal. This chapter is underrated — viewers love the "things went wrong" section because it feels honest.
- **Minute 21:30** — synthesis. The whole video earns out here.

## End-Screen Strategy

- Next video: another case study in the same series, or a teardown of a related mechanism.
- Playlist: "Case Study Breakdowns" playlist.
- Subscribe element.

## Loop Management

- Loop 1 opened at 0:45 ("the one obstacle that nearly broke this") — closed at 16:30.
- Loop 2 opened at 4:30 ("the first decision was the most controversial") — closed at 8:30.
- Loop 3 opened at 11:00 ("the metric that actually matters here") — closed at 21:30.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- 3+ `phrases_to_use` per 5-minute segment.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Tone is analytical and narrative, not hype. "Here's what we did, here's why it worked" — not "transformed their business."

## Specificity Floor

- Every decision named specifically.
- Every metric quoted precisely.
- Every obstacle described concretely.
- The client's role and stage identified precisely.

## Script Word Count

- 15-min: ~2,100-2,250 words
- 20-min: ~2,800-3,000 words
- 25-min: ~3,500-3,750 words

## Production Notes

- On-camera creator + b-roll of the client's category (dashboards, workspaces, screenshots).
- Real dashboard / Stripe / metric screenshots shown on screen for 5-10 seconds each at reveals.
- Redaction of private information (names, exact bank, private emails) — keep the shape of the number, redact identifying detail.
- If the client appears on camera: separate shoot, 2-3 minute interview clips cut in at reveal moments.
- Graphics: before/after comparison at intro, metric chart at chapter 6, rule diagram at synthesis.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Brag, not teardown.** The creator hypes their own contribution. Viewers detect and turn. Case Study Breakdown should feel like a forensic report, not a victory lap.
- **Anonymization over-friction.** The creator anonymizes so heavily that the case study loses texture. Better: get real permission + use real detail.
- **Mechanism hidden.** Creator doesn't fully reveal the decisions because they "want to save something for the offer." Wrong tradeoff. Case Study Breakdown is the authority moat — full disclosure here earns long-term trust.
- **Over-attribution.** Creator takes credit for outcomes the client produced themselves. Share credit honestly.
- **No obstacle chapter.** The video reads too clean. Real case studies have friction. Include chapter 5 (obstacles) or the video feels sanitized.

## Worked Example — SaaS Founder Case Study

### Title
"SaaS Founder Went From 2 Calls/Week to 30 Applications/Week in 9 Days (Full Teardown)"

### Thumbnail
- Split-screen: "2 calls/week" on left, "30 apps/week" on right
- Center: arrow with "9 days" in bold
- Creator face, lower-right corner

### Script Outline (abbreviated)

```
INTRO (0:00 - 1:30)

0:00 - 0:15 HOOK
VO: "A SaaS founder went from two calls a week to thirty applications a week in nine days. Same product, same traffic budget. This video is the full teardown."

0:15 - 0:30 CLIENT CONTEXT
VO: "Product was a mid-market SaaS at forty thousand monthly recurring. The founder had been flat for six months. Running ads. Getting leads. Not getting calls."

0:30 - 0:45 CREDIBILITY
VO: "I'll show every metric — before, during, after. I'll name every decision. This is what nine days of correct decisions looks like."

0:45 - 1:15 ROADMAP
VO: "We'll cover the starting state, the three decisions we made, the metric shift at each decision, the one thing that nearly broke this, the final numbers, and the rule."

1:15 - 1:30 PRE-HANDLE + TEASE
VO: "You might say the founder was lucky, or already had an audience. I'll show the numbers — audience was modest, traffic was ads. Everything that shifted was what we decided."

CHAPTER 1 — Starting State (1:30 - 4:30)
- Monthly recurring: $40K
- Traffic: $3,000/mo Meta spend
- Leads: 120/week
- Calls booked: 2/week
- The product was good. The funnel was broken at stage 3 — leads to qualified demo.

CHAPTER 2 — Decision #1 — Quiz (4:30 - 8:30)
- Replaced the ebook lead magnet with a 7-question quiz.
- Quiz output: one of three tiers (ready, close, not-yet).
- Ready tier routed to calendar immediately.
- Not-yet tier routed to nurture sequence.
- Week-1 metric: qualified routing from 2% to 11%.

CHAPTER 3 — Decision #2 — Outcome Ads (8:30 - 12:30)
- Rewrote 8 ad variants from feature-first to outcome-first.
- Headline shift: "the best tool for X" → "30 qualified demos a week without an SDR."
- CPC rose slightly. CTR doubled. Application completion rate rose.
- Week-1 metric: applications per $1,000 of spend from 3 to 9.

CHAPTER 4 — Decision #3 — Founder Closing (12:30 - 16:30)
- The founder was handing calls to an SDR.
- We moved the founder back into the closer seat for 60 days.
- Every qualified application routed directly to founder's calendar.
- Close rate went from 18% to 42%.

CHAPTER 5 — What Went Wrong (16:30 - 19:30)
- Day 4, the quiz was over-filtering — the "ready" tier was too strict.
- Only 1 in 100 was qualifying as "ready."
- We loosened criteria on day 5.
- Retrospectively, losing 4 possible apps to over-filter.

CHAPTER 6 — Final Metrics (19:30 - 21:30)
- Day 1: 2 applications, 0 calls.
- Day 4: 11 applications, 4 calls.
- Day 9: 31 applications, 14 calls.
- Close rate on calls: 42%.
- Revenue effect: $58K in new ACV in the 9-day window.

SYNTHESIS (21:30 - 23:30)
The rule: the funnel was not broken on traffic. It was broken on routing. A qualifier plus outcome ads plus founder-as-closer is the fix when traffic exists but calls don't.

OUTRO (23:30 - 25:00)
Binge loop: "Next video — how this founder replaced himself as the closer after 60 days without losing close rate."
End-screen: subscribe + next video.
```

### Retention Defenses

- 0:45 roadmap earns the stay.
- 4:30 first-decision reveal is the first real payoff.
- 12:30 third-decision reveal is the deepest chapter.
- 16:30 obstacle chapter is a texture moment that surprises.
- 21:30 synthesis is the rule.

### Loop Map

- Loop 1 opened at 0:45 ("the one thing that nearly broke this") — closed at 16:30.
- Loop 2 opened at 4:30 ("the decision most founders refuse to make") — closed at 12:30.
- Loop 3 opened at 11:00 ("the metric that actually mattered here") — closed at 21:30.

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept through script.
- FTC disclosure if paid relationship or compensated endorsement.
- Client permission on file for all disclosed metrics.
- Private information redacted — no identifying client information unless permissioned.
- No income guarantees — "this client did X" is a claim, "you can do X" is a guarantee.
