# YouTube Variant — Educational Deep-Dive (25-45 min)

> **Signature move.** A single concept taught thoroughly in 25-45 minutes with heavy examples, diagrams, and a clear conceptual arc from opening claim to final synthesis. The viewer finishes feeling they have a working mental model they didn't have at the start. The video sells authority at the highest density — the creator is the person who understands this one thing best.

## When to Use

- Creator has operator-grade depth on one specific topic and can sustain 25+ minutes of substance without filler.
- Pillar is Authority Build or SEO-seed Evergreen.
- Conversion stage is Awareness (attracting new audience via search) and Consideration (nurturing existing audience toward trust).
- The topic is searchable — people type the question or concept into search and need a long-form answer.

## When NOT to Use

- The creator is not actually the deepest expert on the topic and is synthesizing from other sources. Deep-dive from shallow expertise fails fast.
- The topic is trend-dependent (goes stale in 60 days). Deep-dives should evergreen.
- The creator is on-camera-reluctant and can't sustain presence for 25+ minutes.
- The audience is early-stage problem-aware and needs short-form first.

## Title Formula

Pattern: `[Specific Outcome / Topic] — [Angle] — [Authority Signal or Number]`

Examples of the pattern (archetypal, not real):

1. "How to Price a Coaching Program (the 3-Variable Framework I Use After 400 Engagements)"
2. "The Unit Economics of a Creator Business (Full Teardown, 2026 Edition)"
3. "Why Most Offers Fail at the Guarantee — and How to Write One That Actually Works"
4. "The Complete Guide to Cold Email for High-Ticket Offers (Last 18 Months of Data)"

Title rules:

- 60-70 characters maximum for mobile preview.
- Primary keyword in the first 40 characters.
- A specific number or authority signal ("from 400 engagements," "last 18 months of data") when honestly available.
- No clickbait — the title must pay off inside the first 60 seconds of the video.

## Thumbnail SOP

Deep-dive thumbnails follow the complementary principle — the thumbnail says something the title doesn't, so the combined information from title + thumbnail is 2x either alone.

- Face: 40-60% of frame, one primary emotion, eyes toward camera or toward the subject on screen.
- Text overlay: 3-5 words, large, high contrast. Usually a specific payoff number or a challenge statement.
- Background: context-setting (whiteboard, workspace, or abstract graphic related to the concept).
- Color: consistent with channel palette but with one accent color for the accent graphic.

Deep-dive thumbnails win on: legibility at mobile size, emotional specificity (not generic grin), and the "complementary" payoff (title says what, thumbnail says why or how).

Test: cover the title. Does the thumbnail still provoke a click? If not, revise.

## Script Structure

### Intro (0:00 - 1:15)

- **0:00 - 0:15 — Hook.** State the specific outcome or reframe the video will deliver. Use a hook from the 20-formula library (Bold Promise, Challenge Belief, Curiosity, Stakes, Proof, Urgency, or Emotion category).
- **0:15 - 0:30 — Social proof.** One specific credential that earns the 25+ minutes. "Based on X engagements" or "after Y years of doing this" or "one specific result I'll show you."
- **0:30 - 0:45 — Audience callout.** Who this is for, who it's not for. Named stage of business / career / skill.
- **0:45 - 1:00 — Content roadmap.** Table of contents. 4-7 sections. "In this video we'll cover A, B, C, D."
- **1:00 - 1:15 — Pre-handle + tease.** Pre-handle the top objection viewers will raise ("you might be thinking X, here's why X doesn't apply"). Tease the end-of-video payoff.

### Body (1:15 - 38:00)

The body is 4-7 chapters, each of which follows the Three-Part Formula:

- **Re-Hook** — 30-60 seconds. Problem statement for the chapter, agitation, delay before the lesson.
- **Story** — 2-4 minutes. 3-A structure (Arrival / Adversity / Aftermath) with BUT/THEREFORE progression. Story earns the lesson.
- **Lesson** — 2-4 minutes. The specific teaching. Backed by a concrete example.

Between chapters: a Bridge — 15-30 seconds that closes one chapter's loop and opens the next.

### Synthesis (38:00 - 42:00)

- Restate the 4-7 teachings in one sentence each.
- Show how they connect — the meta-insight.
- A worked example that integrates all teachings.

### Outro (42:00 - 45:00)

For Educational Deep-Dive (non-VSSL), the outro is the Binge Loop:

- **Link-Back** — one sentence summary of the key insight.
- **New Problem** — what remains unresolved from this video.
- **Next Video Promise** — end-screen CTA to the next logical video.
- **Subscribe ask** — soft, positioned as "subscribe so the next video appears."

No external funnel CTA in Educational Deep-Dive outro — that's VSSL-only.

## CTA Placement

- No paid offer CTA. Deep-dives are authority moats, not direct-response.
- Soft CTAs: lead magnet mentioned at minute 3-5, again at minute 25-28.
- Comment prompt: at minute 18-22, a specific question viewers can answer in comments.
- End-screen: next video, not lead magnet, not funnel.

## Retention Curve

Target: AVD above 45% for 30+ minute videos (YouTube benchmark for long-form deep-dives).

Where retention drops happen and how to defend each:

- **Minute 0:45 - 1:00** — viewers decide whether to stay. Defend with specific roadmap.
- **Minute 4:00 - 5:00** — first-chapter completion. Defend with the first strong payoff.
- **Minute 12:00 - 15:00** — mid-video drop. Defend with a fresh hook or a major reframe.
- **Minute 22:00 - 25:00** — second-half decision. Defend with tease of the synthesis.
- **Minute 35:00 - 38:00** — pre-outro drop. Defend with the integrated worked example.

If retention drops more than 15% at any 60-second window, the drop point is the weak link. Rewrite or re-cut.

## Chapter Markers

Deep-dives require chapter markers in the YouTube description. Typical structure:

```
0:00 - Intro
1:15 - Chapter 1: [Named Concept]
6:30 - Chapter 2: [Named Concept]
12:45 - Chapter 3: [Named Concept]
18:20 - Chapter 4: [Named Concept]
24:10 - Chapter 5: [Named Concept]
30:00 - Chapter 6: [Named Concept]
35:15 - Synthesis
42:00 - Outro
```

Each chapter is named after the concept it teaches, not a generic "Part 1." The names are searchable and show up in YouTube's chapter list.

## End-Screen Strategy

End-screen is 20 seconds (minute 42:00 - 42:20) showing:

- Subscribe element
- Next video — the logical sequel in the Binge Loop
- Playlist — the related deep-dive series

No sponsor card. No affiliate link. No external website. Educational Deep-Dive end-screens funnel within YouTube only.

## Loop Management

Open 3-5 major curiosity loops in the first 50% of the video. Close each by the 80% mark.

Example loop map:

- Loop 1 opened at 0:45 ("the one variable most creators miss") — closed at 22:00.
- Loop 2 opened at 4:30 ("the counter-example that breaks this") — closed at 28:00.
- Loop 3 opened at 11:00 ("the data on this that I've never shared") — closed at 35:00.

No loop is left open at the outro. Open loops at outro = broken trust.

## Voice-Match Requirements

- 3+ `phrases_to_use` per 5-minute segment from Compartment 1.
- Zero items from `spec/BANNED-VOCABULARY.md`.
- Deep-dive voice is slower, more measured than short-form. Sentence length is longer. Pauses are allowed.
- Read each chapter aloud before recording. If it sounds like recitation, rewrite.

## Specificity Floor

- Every claim backed by a specific example, number, or case.
- "I've seen this hundreds of times" is weak. "I saw this in 23 of the last 40 engagements" is specific.
- Every framework has named sub-parts. "There are three things" with named sub-parts beats "there are many things."

## Script Word Count

At 140-150 words per minute spoken average:

- 25-minute video: ~3,500-3,750 words
- 35-minute video: ~4,900-5,250 words
- 45-minute video: ~6,300-6,750 words

## Production Notes

- On-camera primary. Deep-dive is a face-and-voice format. If creator is off-camera, the video should be Tutorial or VSSL, not Deep-Dive.
- B-roll heavy — 20-30% of runtime covered by b-roll, graphics, or screen-recordings. Pure creator-on-camera for 45 minutes loses retention.
- Graphics / animations: reserved for concepts that need visualization (frameworks, diagrams, comparisons). Minimum 5 graphic assets per deep-dive.
- Audio: professional mic required. Deep-dives are listen-while-commuting content — audio fidelity matters more than visual.
- Edit pace: 5-10 cuts per minute for on-camera, faster during graphic/b-roll segments.

## Common Failure Modes

- **Filler-padded runtime.** 25-minute target, but the creator has only 15 minutes of substance. Every extra minute below target substance drops retention. Cut to substance length.
- **Shallow expertise exposed.** 20 minutes in, the creator hits a question they can't answer. Viewers sense the ceiling. Don't deep-dive topics where expertise is imported.
- **Missing synthesis.** 7 chapters taught, then outro. Viewers want the meta-insight. Synthesis is the whole payoff for watching 35 minutes.
- **Open loops at outro.** Any curiosity loop left open at the end reads as forgotten, not deliberate. Close every loop.
- **No searchable keyword in title.** Deep-dives are evergreen SEO engines. If the title isn't searchable, the video is a one-week release with no 12-month tail.

## Worked Example — Pricing a Coaching Program

### Title
"How to Price a Coaching Program (the 3-Variable Framework, 400+ Engagements)"

### Thumbnail
- Face: creator at whiteboard, pointing at a three-box diagram
- Text overlay: "$3K, $10K, $30K?"
- Background: clean whiteboard with three labeled boxes visible

### Content Brief
- **Essential Question**: At what price should a coaching program sit, and how do you know when to move it?
- **ICP**: Coaches at $20K-$80K MRR, selling 1:1 coaching, considering raising prices.
- **UVP**: A specific 3-variable framework that replaces the "price-what-feels-right" default.
- **Three-Answer Framework**:
  - Answer 1: Price based on what competitors charge — breaks because no competitor has your unique mechanism.
  - Answer 2: Price based on your cost of delivery — breaks because it prices against you, not the outcome.
  - Answer 3 (ultimate): Price based on a 3-variable equation — outcome value to buyer, scarcity of mechanism, proof density you've accumulated.

### Script Outline (abbreviated)

```
INTRO (0:00 - 1:15)

0:00 - 0:15 HOOK
VO: "I've priced coaching programs for four hundred engagements. Most coaches get this wrong three different ways. This video is the framework that gets it right."

0:15 - 0:30 SOCIAL PROOF
VO: "Four hundred engagements, offer prices from five hundred to a hundred and fifty thousand. Three patterns that break. One framework that works."

0:30 - 0:45 AUDIENCE CALLOUT
VO: "This is for coaches between twenty and eighty K monthly. If you're under twenty, you have a different problem — not pricing, proof. Go make the first ten customers first, then come back."

0:45 - 1:00 ROADMAP
VO: "I'll cover: the three mistakes coaches make when pricing, the three-variable framework, how to tell when the framework says raise, and three specific case studies of prices moving."

1:00 - 1:15 PRE-HANDLE + TEASE
VO: "You might be thinking pricing is just a confidence game. It's not. Pricing is a math problem with a confidence layer. Both matter. Stay through the synthesis — I'll show you the exact number I'd quote for someone at your stage."

CHAPTER 1 — The Three Mistakes (1:15 - 8:30)
Re-hook, 3-A story of a coach who priced wrong, lesson on the three mistakes.

CHAPTER 2 — Variable 1: Outcome Value (8:30 - 14:45)
Re-hook, story of a specific engagement, lesson on measuring outcome value.

CHAPTER 3 — Variable 2: Mechanism Scarcity (14:45 - 20:30)
Re-hook, story of a coach whose mechanism was cloned, lesson on mechanism scarcity as a price floor.

CHAPTER 4 — Variable 3: Proof Density (20:30 - 26:00)
Re-hook, story of a coach with strong mechanism but zero proof, lesson on proof density.

CHAPTER 5 — The Integration (26:00 - 32:15)
Re-hook, putting the three variables together, lesson on the multiplication effect.

CHAPTER 6 — When to Raise (32:15 - 37:45)
Re-hook, story of a coach who raised at the right time, lesson on timing.

SYNTHESIS (37:45 - 42:00)
Meta-insight: pricing is a function, not a guess. Worked example: three coaches at different stages, each quoted the exact price.

OUTRO / BINGE LOOP (42:00 - 45:00)
Link-back, new problem (the delivery model that supports the higher price), next video tease, end-screen.
```

### Retention Defenses

- 0:45 roadmap defends the "stay for 45 min" decision.
- 8:30 chapter turn defends the "cross into real content" threshold.
- 20:30 third-variable reveal is the major mid-video payoff.
- 26:00 integration chapter is the "why you came" moment.
- 37:45 synthesis is the closing reward.

### Loop Map

- Loop 1 opened at 1:00 ("the exact number I'd quote for your stage") — closed at 37:45.
- Loop 2 opened at 4:30 ("the third mistake is the invisible one") — closed at 6:45.
- Loop 3 opened at 14:00 ("most coaches miss variable two entirely") — closed at 19:00.
- Loop 4 opened at 26:30 ("the multiplication effect that makes this non-obvious") — closed at 31:00.

### Description (excerpt)

```

In the first 400 engagements, I noticed 3 patterns that break — and one framework that works across every price tier.

Chapters:

1:15 - Chapter 1: The 3 Mistakes
8:30 - Chapter 2: Variable 1 — Outcome Value
14:45 - Chapter 3: Variable 2 — Mechanism Scarcity
20:30 - Chapter 4: Variable 3 — Proof Density

[3-5 related deep-dives]
```

## Compliance Check

- Banned vocabulary swept through full 4,000-6,000 word script.
- No income guarantees — "coaches can earn X" is a guarantee; "coaches in my engagements earned X" is a claim.
- FTC disclosure if any testimonial is referenced.
- Truth Gate: every specific claim survives a 30-second fact-check.
- No fabricated case studies. No fabricated numbers. No invented clients.

## Hand-off

Deep-dive deliverables:

- Full script (4,000-6,000 words).
- Title + 3 variants for A/B testing.
- Thumbnail concept + 2 variants.
- Chapter markers formatted for YouTube description.
- Retention defenses mapped to each chapter turn.
- Loop map with open/close timestamps.
- B-roll shot list with timestamps.
- Graphics list with concept descriptions.
- End-screen layout (subscribe + next video + playlist).
- Description with SEO-forward copy.
- Compliance check signed off.
