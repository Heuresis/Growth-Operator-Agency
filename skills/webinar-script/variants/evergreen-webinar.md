# Evergreen Webinar Playbook

## Role In the System

An evergreen webinar is the creator's 24/7 conversion engine. Once recorded and wired into an automated funnel, it sells the offer continuously without the creator's real-time presence. Trade-off: no live-multiplier effect, so conversion is lower than live. But scale is unbounded; the webinar runs every day of the year.

Three jobs:

1. Convert cold-to-warm traffic at predictable rates that justify paid ad spend.
2. Remove the creator from the live-delivery bottleneck. Once shot, creator involvement is zero.
3. Feed buyers and non-buyers into downstream sequences automatically.

An evergreen webinar is not a live webinar and is not a VSL. Live webinars capture real-time authority and chat dynamics. VSLs are 10 to 45 minutes of tighter direct-response script. An evergreen webinar is longer (60 to 90 min), teaches more, and delivers a pitch inside a teaching frame.

## Gate-in Conditions

Ship an evergreen webinar only if:

- Compartment 2 (Audience) at 70 percent
- Compartment 3 (Offer) at 70 percent with finalized components
- Compartment 5 (Copy and Messaging) at 50 percent with top 10 objections scripted
- Creator has completed and iterated on at least one live webinar of this offer (evergreen works better after live proves conversion)
- Evergreen platform configured (EverWebinar, WebinarNinja evergreen, Demio automated, or custom video-funnel setup)
- Registration flow tested: time-slot selection, reminder sequence, replay delivery
- Offer page live and tested end-to-end
- Fast-action bonus countdown timer tested (timer must expire per individual viewer, not globally)

## The 5-Section Structure — Evergreen Runtime Breakdown

### Section 1 — Intro (12 to 20 min)

Similar to live but tighter. No live chat to engage, so commitments shift to on-screen interactions.

**0:00 to 0:03 — Hook**

Same rules as live: pattern interrupt, contrarian claim or specific outcome declaration. The first 30 seconds decide whether the viewer keeps watching or tabs away.

**0:03 to 0:07 — Authority Positioning**

Tighter than live. Results-first, relevance-second. Skip the resume. Three testimonial types compressed into 3 to 4 minutes max.

**0:07 to 0:11 — Unique Mechanism Sell**

Same as live. Mechanism named, logical frame, emotional frame. The mechanism is still the spine.

**0:11 to 0:15 — Commitments + Open Loops**

Evergreen commitments shift format:
- Instead of "drop a Y in chat," use on-screen action prompts. "Pause the video for 10 seconds and grab a notebook."
- 3 open loops, same as live. Stay-rate depends on loops.
- Pre-plan loops that close specifically in Content, not in Q&A (there is no Q&A).

**0:15 to 0:20 — Pre-Handle Top 3 Objections**

Same rules as live. State the objection, validate, reframe. Pre-handling compounds when the objection re-surfaces during Pitch.

### Section 2 — Content (30 to 45 min)

Evergreen Content is tighter than live. No live-chat momentum to sustain a longer teaching phase.

**Per-Idea Structure (8 to 12 minutes each)**

Teach 3 main ideas (not 5 — evergreen audiences tolerate less content density than live).

| Component | Minutes | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Context | 1 to 2 | Why this idea matters |
| Teaching | 4 to 6 | The concept at depth, one example |
| Pro Tips | 1 to 2 | Objection pre-handling, bonus setup |
| Transition | 1 | Bridge to next idea |
| On-screen prompt | 30 sec | "Pause here and write down [specific action]." |

**Teaching Rule**

Same as live: teach WHAT not HOW.

**Mechanism Thread**

Each idea references the mechanism. By end of Content, viewer can describe the mechanism unassisted.

### Section 3 — Transition to Pitch (5 to 8 min)

Same structure as live: Recap, 3 to 5 yes questions, Crossroads Close, permission to pitch.

Evergreen yes questions are not interactive; they are rhetorical. Frame them as "If you're asking yourself [question]..." rather than "Drop a Y if you agree."

Crossroads Close is identical to live. Sacred component.

### Section 4 — Pitch (12 to 18 min)

Same structure as live: core offer, bonus stack, price anchor, guarantee, CTA.

Differences from live:

- **Urgency via individual countdown timer.** The timer starts when the viewer's session started, not globally. Fast-action bonus expires per viewer.
- **CTA link shown on screen and in session description.** No chat drop, so the link needs on-screen persistence.
- **Simulated chat if enabled.** Some evergreen platforms simulate chat with pre-seeded questions and answers during Pitch. Use carefully; simulated chat that feels fake damages trust more than no chat.

### Section 5 — Q&A or Scripted Close (10 to 15 min)

Evergreen has no live Q&A. Replace with scripted objection destruction.

**Scripted Objection Destruction (10 to 15 min)**

Record 5 to 8 scripted objection responses, each 1 to 2 minutes:

- "I can't afford it" with payment plan response
- "I don't have time" with isomorphic busy-implementer case
- "I'm not ready" with foundational-stage case
- "Will this work for my [niche]" with industry-specific case
- "I've tried things like this before" with differentiation case
- "Why should I trust you" with authority re-stack
- "What if it doesn't work" with guarantee re-stack
- "I need to check with [partner]" with follow-up framing

Each response ends with "If this is clearing things up, the link is [on-screen / in your email / below the video]."

**Final Urgency + Close (2 to 5 min)**

- Restate fast-action bonus deadline (timer visible on screen).
- Restate offer components, total value, price.
- Final CTA.

## Production Notes

### Pre-Production

- Script dry run twice before recording.
- Slide deck designed for video capture (not live screen-share).
- Audio environment tested (treated room, no background noise).
- Camera setup: multi-angle if desired (A-roll plus B-roll cutaways), or single-angle at minimum.
- Teleprompter optional; reads as scripted unless creator is practiced in authenticity.

### Recording

- Record sections separately and edit together. Section-level re-shoots are cheaper than full re-shoots.
- Record extra footage for B-roll (whiteboard diagrams, demo screen captures, case-study visuals).
- Capture clean audio with a lavalier or condenser microphone.
- Plan for 2 to 3 days of recording plus 1 to 2 weeks of edit.

### Post-Production

- Cut ruthlessly. Evergreen viewers tolerate less filler than live.
- Captions or subtitles strongly recommended (50+ percent of viewers watch muted at some point).
- Chapter markers inserted for the 5 sections.
- Export in 1080p with audio-normalized levels.
- Upload to evergreen platform with the registration and time-slot flow.

### Automation Wiring

- Registration form captures name, email, phone optional, time slot.
- Confirmation email fires within 2 minutes with session details.
- Reminder sequence: T-24h, T-1h, T-5min.
- At session time, auto-enrollment into the viewer's scheduled replay.
- Post-session sequence based on watch-behavior (viewed, partial-viewed, no-showed).

## Benchmarks and What They Tell You

| Metric | Healthy Range | If Below | If Above |
|---|---|---|---|
| Registration rate | 25 to 45 percent from opt-in page | Title or page weak | Fine |
| Show-up rate | 45 to 65 percent | Time-slot system not working or reminders weak | Excellent |
| 30-min stay rate | 55 to 75 percent | Hook or Content too soft | Fine |
| Viewer to buyer conversion | 3 to 8 percent | Offer or pitch misaligned | Fine |
| Fast-action bonus take rate | 40 to 70 percent of buyers | Timer not salient | Excellent |
| Replay viewer conversion (re-watchers) | 1 to 3 percent | Fine | Strong long-tail |

## Common Failure Modes

1. Evergreen shot without first running a live webinar. Conversion is 30 to 50 percent below potential because pitch has not been iterated on live audiences. Fix: run 2 to 3 live sessions before shooting evergreen.
2. Simulated chat feels fake. Trust drops. Conversion drops. Fix: disable simulated chat or use it only for legitimate Q&A pre-seeded from prior live sessions.
3. Fast-action timer is global, not per-viewer. Late registrants see expired timer immediately. Fix: per-viewer timer, starting at session start for that viewer.
4. Content teaches HOW. Viewers feel done, do not need offer. Fix: teach WHAT, tease HOW.
5. No chapter markers or captions. Mobile viewers cannot scrub. Drop-off rises. Fix: captions and chapters standard.
6. Post-session email sequence not tied to watch-behavior. All viewers get the same emails regardless of whether they watched 5 minutes or 60. Fix: behavior-based email triggers.
7. Offer page not updated to match evergreen pitch. Pitch promises X, offer page shows Y. Cart abandonment rises. Fix: offer page mirrors pitch exactly.

## Voice-Match and Compliance

- 3 or more `phrases_to_use` per section in the script.
- Banned-vocabulary sweep clean on script, slides, registration page, and email sequence.
- No income guarantees.
- Testimonial numbers carry "results vary" disclosure.
- Refund and guarantee language matches sales page identically.
- Evergreen platform disclosures: some jurisdictions require disclosure that the webinar is pre-recorded. Check legal.
