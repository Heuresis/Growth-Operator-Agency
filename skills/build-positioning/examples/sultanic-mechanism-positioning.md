## Context

Sultanic's Phase 2 names Mechanism Identification as the load-bearing move for positioning. His rule: mechanism must pass the 30-second test using [Adjective]+[Noun]+[Result]. This example walks the discipline through a B2B copy consultancy case. The founder writes for enterprise SaaS and wants to productize the IP into a $15K cohort coaching offer. Below is the positioning produced by running Phase 2 against the case.

## The Encoded Positioning

### Section 1 - Positioning Statement

> **For** B2B SaaS founders with $1M-$20M ARR **whose** marketing copy is written by senior marketers (not senior copywriters) and **who** are losing deals at the demo stage because the copy doesn't match the buying-committee's language, **[Brand Name]** is the **B2B copy system for founder-led sales-cycles** **that** rewrites your category-creation copy, demo decks, and sales-call scripts using The Committee-Language Method™ **because** it's the only framework that lets founders pitch to procurement, IT, and finance simultaneously without losing either audience.

### Section 2 - Category Design

**Category Choice:** Subcategory creation. The existing market for "B2B copywriting" is commoditized ($200-$2K per page from Upwork/Contra). The existing market for "go-to-market consulting" is premium ($50K-$250K per engagement from First Round / Reforge). The subcategory being built sits between them: **B2B Copy System for Founder-Led Sales-Cycles** - productized copy IP delivered through cohort + 1:1 coaching at $15K per engagement.

**Old Vehicles Being Replaced:**
1. Senior marketers doing their own copy - strong on brand, weak on committee-language
2. $2K-per-page freelance copywriters - strong on output, no IP or system
3. GTM consultants at $100K+ - strong on strategy, no copy execution
4. ChatGPT + GTM playbooks - strong on speed, weak on committee specificity

### Section 3 - Core Belief Statement

**The Core Belief:** "You don't lose B2B deals at the demo. You lose them in the 48 hours after, when the champion forwards your materials to the buying committee, and the committee reads language written for the champion, not for them."

**Why It Must Be True:** B2B purchases at $50K-$500K+ ACV are committee decisions. Procurement evaluates vendor criteria. IT evaluates security. Finance evaluates unit economics. A deck written for the champion is systematically misread. The loss shows up as "we decided to go a different direction."

**Evidence:** 77% of B2B buyers report their latest purchase as "difficult or complex," with 6.8 stakeholders on average (Gartner). Up from 5.4 five years ago. The copy hasn't kept pace.

### Section 4 - Unique Mechanism

**Name:** The Committee-Language Method™

**Mechanism Formula:** Committee-Language + Method + Procurement-Pass ([Adjective]+[Noun]+[Result]).

**What It Is:** A 5-phase engagement rewriting three asset classes (category copy, demo deck, sales-call script) against a committee-language rubric. Phase 1 Committee Audit (interview last 5 wins and 5 losses). Phase 2 Lens Mapping (tag every line against procurement/IT/finance/champion). Phase 3 Rewrite. Phase 4 Install into demo flow. Phase 5 Governance (quarterly refresh).

**Why It's Different:** Every other B2B copy approach writes to a single audience. The Method writes to four simultaneously using a published rubric and tagging protocol.

**Why It Works:** B2B losses at $50K-$500K+ ACV are misidentified as "product fit" losses when they're actually "committee-readability" losses. The champion is sold. The committee is not. Committee-readable copy moves the conversion point from the demo (you already win) to the 48-hour post-demo window (you currently lose).

### Section 5 - Competitive Differentiation

**1. Multi-Lens Rubric, Not Single-Audience Copy.** Every competing B2B copywriter writes to one audience. The Method writes to four simultaneously and tags each sentence with its primary lens.

**2. Committee-Audit-Driven, Not Brand-Guideline-Driven.** Generic B2B copy starts with brand voice. The Method starts with the last 10 buying committees (5 yes, 5 no) and reverse-engineers the rubric from actual decision criteria.

**3. Install & Govern, Not Deliver & Leave.** A freelance copywriter delivers and leaves. The Method installs into the demo flow and governs quarterly against new committee data.

**4. Capability Removal.** Sultanic's principle: founder doesn't need to become a copywriter, hire a senior marketer, or retrain the sales team. The Method handles all three.

### Section 6 - Brand Narrative

**The Struggle:** 7 years inside a B2B SaaS that scaled $2M to $90M ARR, watching copy evolve from founder-written clarity to marketing-manager generalities to enterprise-mush. Every deal post-mortem: "champion loved us, committee didn't get us."

**The Discovery:** The pattern was the copy's inability to carry across the four committee lenses. Reverse-engineering the wins revealed the rubric.

**The Mission:** Productize the rubric. Give B2B SaaS founders the committee-language system that turned 28% close rates into 47%, without requiring them to become copywriters.

### Section 7 - Messaging Hierarchy

**Primary Message:** "Your champion sold the demo. Your copy lost the committee. Here's the system for both."

**Supporting Messages:**
1. "B2B purchases have 6.8 stakeholders. Your copy is written for 1. That's the gap." (Pre-handles the category belief.)
2. "You don't need a senior copywriter. You need a committee-language rubric." (Capability removal.)
3. "Your last 10 buying committees hold the answer to your next 100." (Audit-driven.)

### Section 8 - Anti-Positioning

**What We Are NOT:** Not a B2B copywriting shop (no per-page work). Not a GTM consultancy (no positioning without copy rewrite). Not a fractional CMO (we don't manage your marketing team).

**Who We Don't Serve:** B2B companies under $1M ARR (no committee complexity yet). Founders who delegate all copy decisions (the Method requires founder presence in Phases 1 and 3). Companies selling to a single buyer.

**What We Refuse to Do:** No audit, no rewrite. No deliver-and-leave (install + govern is the engagement). No more than 4 companies per partner per quarter (cap in the engagement letter).

## Why This Is A Good Example

- **Mechanism passes Sultanic's 30-second test.** "Committee-Language Method, a 5-phase engagement rewriting category copy, demo decks, and sales scripts against a 4-lens rubric built from audits of the last 10 buying committees." Clear in one sentence.
- **Positioning leads with mechanism, not benefit.** Sultanic's Phase 2 discipline: mechanism identification before benefit articulation. The outcome (close-rate improvement) shows up as supporting evidence, not the lead.
- **Anti-positioning is structurally defensible.** "No more than 4 companies per partner per quarter" is in the engagement letter. "No audit, no rewrite" is a refusal, not a preference. Each refusal has structural backing.
