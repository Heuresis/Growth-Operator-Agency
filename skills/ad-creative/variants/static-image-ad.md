# Static Image Ad — Archetype Playbook

> **When it wins:** Scroll-stopper. Single-image ads still convert, especially on LinkedIn and Meta newsfeed. Lowest production cost per ad variant.

## When to pick this ad type

- Platform: LinkedIn (static-image still dominates feed for B2B), Meta feed, Display networks.
- Budget for rapid testing — static-image ads can be produced 10× per day.
- You want to isolate the variable of copy vs creative.
- Your offer doesn't require video demo.

## Structure

### Image composition
- **One subject.** Not 3 things competing.
- **Three-word scan.** 3 biggest words must register in < 2 seconds at mobile scale.
- **Eye direction / weight:** draws attention to the CTA or key copy.
- **Mobile-first:** what does this look like at 4" screen size?

### On-image copy (if any)
- ≤ 40 words on the image itself
- Large typography; readable at mobile scale
- High contrast text on image

### Caption copy (below image in feed)
- Hook in first line — people see first 120 chars before "see more"
- 120–200 words typical for Meta; 80–180 for LinkedIn
- One clear CTA at end

## Design principles

- **Contrast:** high-contrast color choice; off-brand if needed for scroll-stop
- **Face:** human face in frame often lifts CTR (depending on ICP)
- **Numerical anchor:** specific number, stat, or outcome creates memorability
- **Negative space:** not everything needs decoration; breathing room feels premium
- **Brand consistency:** palette + typography + style match across campaigns

## Copy frameworks

- **Benefit-led:** "[Specific outcome] without [common friction]"
- **Social-proof-led:** "Over [N] [ICP type] have [action]"
- **Curiosity-led:** "The [unexpected thing] that [desired outcome]"
- **Contrarian:** "Most [ICP] think X. The truth is Y."
- **Direct CTA-led:** "Get the [specific thing] → link"

## Variants to test

- Same copy + 3 image variants
- Same image + 3 copy variants
- Colored vs monochrome
- Face-forward vs product-forward
- Stat-heavy vs story-heavy

## CVR benchmarks

| Platform | CTR | CPA |
|---|---|---|
| Meta feed | 0.5–1.5% | $30–$150 |
| LinkedIn feed | 0.3–0.8% | $80–$350 (higher B2B rates) |
| Display | 0.1–0.4% | $15–$80 |

LinkedIn is usually the highest-performing platform for static-image in B2B; Meta dominates B2C.

## Common failure modes

1. **Too many elements.** Image feels cluttered; eye doesn't know where to land. Fix: one subject.
2. **Illegible text.** Mobile scale failure. Fix: 3-word scan test before shipping.
3. **Brand-inconsistent.** Every ad looks like a different company. Fix: template + brand guidelines.
4. **Wrong aspect ratio.** Square on LinkedIn wastes feed real estate. Fix: platform-specific.
5. **Caption mismatch.** Image says X, caption pitches Y. Fix: image and caption reinforce each other.
6. **No testing discipline.** Ship one ad and hope. Fix: 3–5 variants minimum in rotation.
