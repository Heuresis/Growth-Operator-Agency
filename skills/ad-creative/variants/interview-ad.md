# Interview Ad — Archetype Playbook

> **When it wins:** Conversational authority. Two-person dialogue makes an ad feel like podcast content or expert interview. Longer-form engagement (30–90 sec) without feeling like an ad.

## When to pick this ad type

- Warm audience / retargeting (cold conversion slower on interview ads).
- Creator is confident on camera + another voice is available (interviewer / partner / customer).
- Offer benefits from expertise framing (not a basic product pitch).
- You have 30–90 seconds of airtime — longer-form ads.

## Structure (30–90 sec)

### Hook (0:00–0:05)
- Interviewer opens with a specific, compelling question: *"So you went from $0 to $50K/month in 18 months — how?"*
- OR: setup + question: *"I asked [creator] the one question every coach gets wrong. Here's what they said."*

### Body (0:05–0:70)
- Creator answers with specific story / number / insight
- Interviewer follows up 1–2 times to deepen or clarify
- Natural pacing — feels like real conversation, not scripted
- One big idea delivered + one piece of proof

### Close (0:70–0:90)
- Interviewer CTA: *"Where can people learn more about [method]?"* → creator directs to link
- Or: creator wraps with soft CTA
- On-screen URL / @handle visible at close

## Creative requirements

- Two people visible OR podcast-style audio + face cards
- Podcast-quality audio (this platform is about conversation; bad audio kills)
- Natural lighting; not polished-studio production
- Minimal cuts — let the conversation breathe
- Captions for both speakers (differentiated)

## Variants to test

- **Interview ad (operator + interviewer):** creator is the expert being interviewed
- **Customer interview:** interviewer + real customer (crossover with testimonial ad)
- **Peer conversation:** two creators discussing (partnership cross-promotion)
- **Podcast snippet:** repurpose 30–60 sec from actual podcast episode as ad

## Casting the interviewer

- **Trained interviewer:** professional podcast host or video producer
- **Internal team member:** employee with on-camera confidence
- **Peer creator:** mutually promotes each creator
- **Customer interviewing creator:** unconventional but powerful for trust

## CVR benchmarks

| Metric | Good | Great |
|---|---|---|
| CTR | 0.6–1.5% | 1.8–3.5% |
| CPA | $50–$180 | $25–$80 |
| View-through-rate (15 sec) | 40% | 60%+ |
| Full-watch rate (90 sec ad) | 15% | 30%+ |

Interview ads trade lower CTR for higher downstream conversion rate per click (warmer clicks).

## Common failure modes

1. **Scripted-feeling delivery.** Viewer senses rehearsal. Fix: bullet points + improvise.
2. **Audio quality poor.** One mic, one bad mic = ad dead. Fix: both speakers with decent mics.
3. **Over-long without payoff.** 90 sec ad with no punchy moment. Fix: cut to the best 30 sec.
4. **No explicit CTA.** Viewer leaves without direction. Fix: on-screen URL + verbal CTA at close.
5. **Wrong interviewer.** Peer creator who talks more than the creator → viewer confused about whose expertise. Fix: interviewer asks + listens; creator answers + teaches.
6. **Podcast episode too long.** Raw episode can't become an ad. Fix: edit to the 30–90 sec moment; add ad-specific hook + CTA.
