# Testimonial Ad — Archetype Playbook

> **When it wins:** Proof-heavy audience. Buyers who need social validation before committing. Highest trust-building single ad format.

## When to pick this ad type

- Offer price $2K+ (high-ticket; social proof matters more as price rises).
- You have verifiable testimonials + written releases from 3+ buyers (`reference/legal-templates/photo-video-release.md`).
- ICP is skeptical / has been burned by similar offers before.
- Warm retargeting layer especially (testimonials cold-work but shine retargeting).

## Structure (30–90 sec typical)

### Hook (0:00–0:05)
- Lead with the outcome: *"I went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]."*
- OR: the customer's name + specific result: *"[First name] closed $47K in 30 days. Here's what changed."*
- OR: visual of the customer + outcome number on-screen

### Body (0:05–0:60)
- Customer tells the story:
  - Where they were before (specific friction)
  - What they tried that didn't work
  - The decision to join / buy
  - What changed after
  - Specific outcome / number
- 2–3 cut-away shots to stack visual proof (dashboard, testimonial text, venue, screenshot)

### Close (0:60–0:90)
- Customer direct CTA: *"If you're where I was, check out [offer name]"*
- OR: operator voiceover CTA: *"If you want results like [customer], [URL]"*

## Creative requirements

- **Real customer**, not actor. Verifiable on LinkedIn or social.
- **Written release** on file before filming.
- **Uncoached** where possible — prompts but not scripts ("tell me what changed" not "say X").
- **Visible proof stack:** dashboard screenshots, revenue graphs, before-after if health/fitness.

## Variants to test

- **Single-customer 60-sec:** one voice, one story, one outcome
- **Multi-customer montage (30-sec):** 3–5 customers each showing one specific result
- **Screen-record + voiceover:** customer narrates over screen-capture of their dashboard / proof
- **DM screenshot compilation:** real customer DMs cycled through with minimal voiceover

## Compliance (non-negotiable)

- FTC disclosure: "I was compensated" or "I paid for this" where applicable
- No fabricated results ever
- Income claims require: disclaimer ("not typical"), how-earned disclosure, testimonial-vs-average distinction
- Medical / health testimonials require their own compliance (FDA / health-claims)
- Results verifiable on request

## CVR benchmarks

| Audience | CTR | CPA |
|---|---|---|
| Cold broad | 0.8–1.5% | $80–$250 |
| Retargeting 14-day | 3–6% | $30–$80 |
| Lookalike of past buyers | 1.5–3% | $40–$120 |

Testimonial ads often have lower CTR than hook ads but 2–3× higher close rate on the clicks they do produce.

## Common failure modes

1. **Over-produced.** Looks like an ad, not a testimonial. Fix: intentionally less polished.
2. **Vague testimonial.** "This changed my life." Fix: specific numbers, specific before-after.
3. **No release on file.** Legal risk. Fix: signed photo-video release before filming.
4. **Fake testimonial.** Real or ban. Fix: no synthetic / paid-actor testimonials without disclosure.
5. **Unverifiable claim.** "Made $500K in 30 days" with no proof. Fix: dashboard / invoice / verifiable.
