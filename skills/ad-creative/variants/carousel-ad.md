# Carousel Ad — Archetype Playbook

> **When it wins:** Swipeable education. Mid-attention consumption. Lower-CPM alternative to video on feeds. Strong for teaching-led offer categories.

## When to pick this ad type

- Platform: Instagram feed, Facebook feed, LinkedIn feed.
- Offer benefits from educational teaching before pitch.
- You have strong design capacity (carousels live or die on slide-1 + slide-2).
- Audience consumes slow, reads, swipes intentionally (B2B, professional, thinking-heavy niches).

## Structure (10 slides typical; can be 5–12)

### Slide 1 — Cover
- Big, scannable title
- Hook that earns the tap
- Minimal design clutter
- Often the most-designed slide in the set

### Slide 2 — Hook extension
- Sets up what the carousel delivers
- Promises the payoff
- Swipe-forward motivation

### Slides 3–8 — Content
- One idea per slide
- ≤ 40 words per slide
- Visual element per slide (icon, illustration, screenshot)
- Readable without reading every previous slide

### Slide 9 — Summary / Recap
- Short list of the main takeaways
- Makes the carousel scannable for return visits

### Slide 10 — CTA
- One clear next step: follow, save, visit link, DM keyword
- NOT multiple CTAs stacked

## Design requirements

- **Consistent branding:** same palette, typography, iconography across all slides
- **Mobile-first:** text readable at 5"/medium feed size
- **Dense but not crowded:** 40 words max per slide
- **Native aspect ratio:** 1:1 or 4:5 (Instagram/Facebook); 1.91:1 (LinkedIn)
- **Accessibility:** alt text for screen readers

## Content pillars that work well

- **Frameworks:** "The [N]-step framework I use for X"
- **Lists:** "[N] things I wish I knew about Y before [age/stage]"
- **Before-after:** "What I used to do vs what I do now"
- **Case study teardown:** "How [outcome] happened in [timeframe]"
- **Myth-busting:** "[N] things people get wrong about X"

## CVR benchmarks

| Metric | Good | Great |
|---|---|---|
| CTR (to landing from carousel) | 0.7% | 1.8%+ |
| Save rate (organic indicator) | 3% | 8%+ |
| Comment rate | 0.5% | 2%+ |
| CPA (paid) | $25–$120 | $12–$60 |

## Common failure modes

1. **Slide 1 too busy.** Fails to earn the tap. Fix: minimalist cover, bigger type.
2. **Text-heavy slides.** 80+ words per slide = scroll-past. Fix: 40 words max.
3. **Inconsistent visuals.** Looks DIY / unbranded. Fix: template-driven consistency.
4. **No CTA slide.** Viewer leaves nowhere. Fix: explicit CTA on last slide.
5. **Design over content.** Pretty but no substance. Fix: content first, design serves content.
6. **Wrong aspect ratio.** Square on LinkedIn feels small; 1.91:1 on IG feels cramped. Fix: platform-specific.

## Templates + tools

- **Canva:** template libraries exist; fastest time-to-ship
- **Figma:** highest-flexibility design; team-shareable
- **Custom:** in-house design team for brand-owned templates

For most creators, Canva with a brand-locked template (fonts + colors + icon style locked) is the right trade-off between speed and quality.
