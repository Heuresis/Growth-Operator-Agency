# UGC-Style Ad — Archetype Playbook

> **When it wins:** Native-feeling authenticity. Feels like a customer recorded a phone review — not a polished talking head. Often outperforms producer-grade ads 2–4× on CPM.

## When to pick this ad type

- Meta / TikTok / Instagram feeds — platforms where UGC and content blur.
- Ad fatigue on polished creative — UGC refresh restores performance.
- Creator doesn't want face-front (UGC-actor does).
- Budget for 5–20 UGC creators per rotation.
- Volume testing: UGC is where you scale tests cheaply.

## Structure (15–45 sec)

### Hook (0:00–0:03)
- Casual phone-camera opener: *"Ok so I just need to tell you about this…"*
- Or: specific claim + phone-record feel: *"So I bought this and [outcome]…"*
- Avoid production sheen: no intro music, no graphics, no logo

### Body (0:03–0:30)
- UGC actor tells the story like a friend recommending
- Specific detail + emotional anchor
- 1–2 cutaway shots (product / screen / dashboard)
- Platform-native cadence

### Close (0:30–0:45)
- Soft: "Here's the link, check it out"
- OR: "Saved my life / business — go check them out"
- Disclosure: "paid partnership" / "#ad" / "I was compensated" (FTC-compliant placement)

## Creative requirements

- Shot on phone (not pro camera)
- Natural lighting if possible
- Single cut — minimal edits
- Background realistic (bedroom, car, office, coffee shop)
- Actor's real voice, minor nervousness/imperfection intact

## Casting

Source UGC actors via:
- UGC marketplaces (Billo, TrendHero, JoinBrands, Insense)
- Your own customer base (highest conversion — real users)
- Creator agencies with UGC rosters
- DMs to people who've commented on your content organically

Typical rate: $50–$500 per script + scene.

## Compliance

- Disclosure non-negotiable: "#ad" / "Paid partnership" / "I was compensated" in captions + mentioned verbally where required.
- No fabricated claims.
- Legitimate customer UGC: treat like a testimonial (release on file).
- UGC-actor UGC: clearly not posing as a customer unless they actually are.

## Variants to test

- **Personal-use UGC:** "I bought this for myself and..." (highest trust)
- **Recommendation UGC:** "A friend recommended this, now I'm telling you..."
- **Before-after UGC:** visual transformation over the ad's duration
- **Unboxing UGC:** physical product focus (for book funnels or physical tripwires)
- **Tutorial UGC:** "Here's how to use this..." (for software / tool offers)

## CVR benchmarks

| Metric | Polished ad | UGC ad |
|---|---|---|
| CPM | $15–$45 | $8–$25 |
| CTR | 0.8–2% | 1.2–3.5% |
| Cost per click | $1.50–$5 | $0.40–$2 |
| Cost per conversion | $80–$250 | $40–$150 |

UGC outperforms on reach/CTR but often has slightly lower per-click close rate (less qualification upfront). Net: 1.5–2.5× more revenue per ad dollar at scale.

## Common failure modes

1. **Too polished.** Looks produced → viewer skeptical. Fix: intentional imperfection.
2. **Scripted delivery.** Actor reading → wooden. Fix: bullet points + let them improvise.
3. **No disclosure.** FTC risk. Fix: "#ad" or "paid partnership" prominently.
4. **Actor looks wrong for ICP.** 22-year-old promoting offer to 45-year-olds → mismatch. Fix: cast close to ICP.
5. **Script-written claims.** Actor says things they didn't experience. Fix: actors speak truthfully or use carefully scripted language ("My friends who've tried this said...").
6. **Running same UGC for 30+ days.** Fatigue. Fix: 3–5 new UGC variants per week at scale.
