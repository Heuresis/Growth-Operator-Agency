# Hogendoorn 11-Step Pull-Push-Persuade VSL - Faceless Clipping Offer

> **Framework:** Lucas Hogendoorn's Pull-Push-Persuade 11-Step - `reference/frameworks/vsl/hogendoorn-11-step.md`
> **Runtime target:** 10-12 minutes (within the 8-12 min window)
> **Best for:** Faceless / no-camera / paid-traffic cold audience
> **Applied to:** ClipScale Academy - Clipping Agency Accelerator, Meta Ads → VSL page (no creator face)

## Context

The Hogendoorn 11-Step is the paid-traffic VSL framework optimized for faceless, text-and-slides execution. Across 50+ funnels each generating $1M+ in pipeline, Hogendoorn's rule set holds: specificity beats creativity, every sentence earns the next, PULL earns attention before PUSH creates pressure, and the sale doesn't begin before Step 9. The runtime is much shorter than Pampa's 15-step (8-12 min vs 20-45 min) because paid cold traffic has a ~1,800-word tolerance window before bounce. Every word pays rent. This example applies the framework to a faceless variant of ClipScale Academy - Meta Ads → VSL page → application funnel, no creator face, voice-over and screen recordings only.

## The Encoded 11-Step VSL (10-11 minutes, ~1,800 words)

### Phase 1: PULL (Steps 1-4, ~2.5 min / 25% of runtime)

#### Step 1 - Promise (Hook)

"If you're making $15/clip on Fiverr or $1,500/month on Whop, and you want $10,000/month running a clipping agency with retainer clients and a team, this video shows the 3-phase system that got 47 of our members to $5K+/month in the last 90 days."

Specific promise, specific person, specific outcome.

#### Step 2 - Profile

"You've already tried clipping. Maybe a Whop account with 40 clips and $487 earned. Maybe Fiverr gigs at $15 a pop, 50-hour weeks for $1,800/month. You're in a Discord with 3,000 other clippers asking: 'how do I find streamer clients who pay $2,500/month?' Nobody in the Discord has the answer because nobody in the Discord built an agency."

Self-selection via specificity.

#### Step 3 - Proof

"Fujiwara Clipping did $7.7M in 2025 running exactly this model [Bloomberg]. Our cohort has collectively closed $340,000 in retainer revenue over 6 months. Jalen: $600/month on Fiverr to $8,500/month, 4 retainer clients. Priya: corporate 9-to-5 to $6,200/month. Marcus: college student, zero experience, $5,000/month."

Market proof, aggregate proof, pattern proof.

#### Step 4 - Presence

"I'm the founder of ClipScale Academy. 4 years in the streaming space, first as a $15-clip clipper, then an agency running 7 accounts. 200+ members onboarded. This is what I do."

### Phase 2: PUSH (Steps 5-8, ~5 min / 50% of runtime)

#### Step 5 - Pain Points

"At $1,500/month freelancing, your yearly income ceilings at $18K. A single agency client at $2,500/month is $30K/year. One client beats your entire freelance year.

It gets worse the longer you wait. AI clip tools (Opus Clip at 16M users, $29/month) are collapsing the floor of freelance. A year from now, generic clipping will be free. The clippers who jumped to agency will be fine, because agency is about sales, strategy, and team management. Those aren't AI-replaceable. Every month you delay is a month of income gap and competitive degradation."

#### Step 6 - Case Studies

"Jalen, 22, Fiverr clipper in Atlanta, $600/month. Joined in September. Week 3 he sent our DM script to a mid-tier Kick streamer. Streamer replied in 11 minutes. Retainer call Friday. $2,500/month close. Today 4 clients, 2 editors, $8,500/month.

Marcus, 19, college student, zero experience. Week 5 first client at $1,800/month. Week 9 client #2 at $2,500. Today $5,000/month with one editor. The system doesn't care about your experience, it cares about whether you send the outreach."

#### Step 7 - Process

"The Clip-to-Agency Method. Three phases. Phase 1 Production Mastery: AI-plus-human workflow. Opus Clip scans the VOD, human editor polishes the top 10%, you produce 20+ clips/day. Phase 2 Client Engine: DM scripts, proposals, pricing frameworks for Twitch/Kick/YouTube streamers. The part every clipper is missing. Phase 3 Scale System: at 2-3 clients, hire your first editor overseas at $500-$1,200/month. Margin triples. Solo clipper becomes agency owner."

#### Step 8 - Future Pacing

"Picture the Monday you land your first retainer. 2 PM. Your phone buzzes. 'Let's do it. How do I pay you?' Stripe lights up for $2,500 monthly recurring. You post the win in the community. Three months later, 4 clients. First editor hired. Monthly income triples, weekly hours drop from 50 to 20. That's 90 days from today if you start now."

### Phase 3: PERSUADE (Steps 9-11, ~2.5 min / 25% of runtime)

#### Step 9 - Objection Handling (4 in order)

**Price:** "Accelerator tier is $2,997. One retainer at $2,500/month pays for it in 30 days."

**Fit:** "Marcus was a college student with no experience. Jalen was at $600/month. Edit a basic clip, send 50 DMs, the math works. Not personality. Volume."

**Decision-avoidance:** "Every month you wait is $2,500-$10,000 of retainer revenue unearned. 12 months of waiting is $30K-$120K left on the table."

**Trust:** "If you've been burned by a course that taught editing without sales, this is different. The full system includes client acquisition, not just production. The guarantee protects you."

#### Step 10 - The Close (5-layer)

**Core:** The Clip-to-Agency Method: 8-module curriculum + DM scripts + proposals + pricing + SOPs + hiring playbooks.

**Value Stack:** Course + 12 coaching sessions + niche playbooks (5 verticals) + DWY first client + accountability partner + community.

**Price Anchor:** "Assembled separately: SMMA course $3K-$7K + 12 sessions at $500 = $6K + playbooks $1,500-$3,000. Retail $10,900-$16,600. Accelerator is $2,997."

**Guarantee:** "Complete the modules, attend 8/12 calls, send 50 messages. If no client in 90 days, another 90 days coaching free plus a strategy session."

**CTA:** "Application below. Cohort capped at 25."

#### Step 11 - Postscript

"The clipping education space doesn't have a category leader yet. In 12 months, it will. Building while the category is still forming is worth more than any guarantee. See you inside."

## Why This Is A Good Example

- **Word-rent discipline.** ~1,800 words for a 12-minute VSL means every sentence must pay rent. Hogendoorn's invariant is strict - this draft cuts adjectives, kills transitions, and refuses redundancy. A 3,500-word draft would bounce paid cold traffic.
- **PULL phase earns the PUSH phase.** Steps 1-4 (25%) establish specificity, self-selection, and proof before the pain amplification begins. If you skip PULL and open with pain, the viewer reads it as manipulative and bounces.
- **Never sells before Step 9.** The price is hidden until Step 9 Objection Handling - same invariant as Pampa's 15-step, different total runtime. The first 8 steps install all 8 beliefs before the price is introduced.
