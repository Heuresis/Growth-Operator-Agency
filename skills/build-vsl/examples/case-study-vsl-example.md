# the VSL director Case-Study VSL Variant - Fitness Coaching

> **Framework:** 15-step VSL restructured into case-study format - a real founder's journey carries the full VSL arc
> **Runtime target:** 28-34 minutes
> **Best for:** Warm traffic, Solution Aware audience, high-ticket ($3,997+) coaching
> **Track record:** Case-study VSLs tend to out-convert direct VSLs on warm traffic because the surface reads as teaching, not selling

## Context

The case-study VSL is a variant of the 15-step structure where the Hero Story (Step 5) is expanded into the central spine of the entire VSL - a specific client's 90-day transformation becomes the primary narrative thread, and the 15 framework beats weave through that story rather than running as a separate sequence. This variant works best when the audience is Solution Aware and already skeptical of direct VSL patterns. This example applies the variant to the Fitness Coaching offer, using an illustrative client ("Marcus, Series B SaaS CEO") as the case-study spine.

## The Encoded Case-Study VSL

### Opening Frame (0-2 min) - Hook + Lead merged [Beliefs #1, #2]

"Marcus was a Series B SaaS CEO. 18 months ago, he was 207 pounds, operating at 5 of 10, and sleeping 5 hours a night. 90 days later - without leaving his company or canceling a single board meeting - he was 22 pounds lighter, sleeping 7+ hours, and his CTO told him it was 'the most noticeable performance jump we've seen in a year.' In the next 28 minutes I'm going to walk you through exactly what he changed. Not what he tried. What actually worked. Including the weeks where his usual approach would have broken, and why this system didn't."

Hook does WTF (specific numbers), authority (Series B founder), and teases the mechanism (a system that didn't break).

### The Setup (2-5 min) - Qualification + Problem [Beliefs #1 reinforced, #4 start]

"If you've been running a company for 2+ years, and in those 2 years you've gained 10+ pounds, lost sleep quality, and tried at least two fitness programs that didn't stick - Marcus's story is going to look familiar. If you're pre-revenue, or if you're looking for bodybuilding, or if you want to be handed a PDF and never speak to another human - stop watching. This is about a specific kind of founder with a specific pattern of failure."

Qualification: self-select in and out. Problem introduction: names the specific pattern.

### The Client's Dark Night (5-9 min) [Belief #7]

"Marcus tried a behavior-change app, an in-person trainer at $300/session, a keto coach on Twitter. Each worked 3-5 weeks and collapsed during the first startup emergency. Breaking point: March 14, hotel bathroom in San Francisco, 9:42 PM, accepting a design award the next morning. Shirt didn't button. Texted his wife: 'I don't know what to do.' His CTO, who'd lost 30 pounds, said: 'You solve complex systems problems every day. Why are you approaching your body differently?'"

Specificity: exact time, exact place, exact text.

### The Mechanism Reveal (9-13 min) [Beliefs #3, #5]

"Marcus stopped treating fitness as willpower and started treating it as operations. Four elements. One, he delegated decisions. Coach handled weekly programming. Two, he installed metrics. a wearables brand. Coach reviewed weekly. Three, he built infrastructure for his worst weeks. Hotel-room version of every protocol. Restaurant-compatible meal plans. Four, he joined other founders doing the same thing. That's the Founder Fitness Operating System, FFOS."

### The Evidence Stack (13-18 min) [Beliefs #3, #6, #7, #8]

"Marcus's 90-day a wearables brand export: sleep 5.1 hours to 7.2. HRV up 22%. Recovery moved from yellow to green. Weight 207 to 185. Week 6 calendar: three hotel nights, two board meetings, one launch prep. Did he skip workouts? No. Hotel-room version. The program adapted to Wednesday's fire.

He's not unusual. Jason, YC founder, 18 pounds during a Series A close. Priya, fintech CEO, fixed her HRV while her company 3x'd. Same protocol. Same 90 days."

### Price Reveal (18-22 min) [Beliefs #5, #6]

"Marcus asked: what's the cost of staying at 5 of 10 for another year? Company at $5M ARR, 5% growth-trajectory hit is $250K. Equinox Optimize is $40K/year. the ultra-premium science-based founder-fitness program is $20K+. Assembled separately, $11,900-$18,500/quarter. **FFOS Group Coaching is $3,997/quarter.** Less than one month of the cognitive performance Marcus was leaving on the table."

### Guarantee (22-24 min) [Beliefs #3, #7]

"Complete 90 days. 80% of calls, 5 days training, weekly check-ins. If no 10-pound loss and measurable energy improvement, another 90 days free until you get it. The condition is that you did the work. The protocol is proven across 100% of adherent founders."

### Urgency (24-25 min)

"Four cohorts per year, 20 founders each, because group coaching requires hot-seat time. If this cohort is full, waitlist for 90 days."

### Crossroads + Takeaway (25-28 min)

"Marcus is 12 months past his 90 days. 187 pounds now, company at $12M ARR, grew 2.4× while fixing his health. Founders who don't apply stay 5-10 pounds heavier, operating at 5 of 10, burning $8-15K a year on programs that don't stick. Which version of yourself do you want to be a year from now?

Not for everyone. Not willing to train 5 days at 30 minutes? Don't apply. Not willing to attend 80% of calls? Don't apply. If you watched this full video, you're probably not a tire-kicker."

### Help Them Buy + Future Pace (28-30 min)

"Application button below. 7 minutes. If you qualify, a 30-minute call. Once you're in, Week 1 your sleep improves before any weight moves. Week 2 afternoon energy returns. Week 6 shirts fit differently. Day 90 your 10-page performance report shows body metrics alongside business metrics. You become the founder other founders ask how you stay in this shape while running a company."

## Why This Is A Good Example

- **Shows the case-study variant's structural difference from the VSL director's direct 15-step.** The client story (Marcus) is the spine; the 15 beats thread through the story instead of running as discrete sections. This works on warm traffic because it reads as teaching-through-example rather than selling.
- **Honors the invariants.** Never sells before Step 9 (first price mention at min 18+). Mechanism threads all 5 required places (Hook, Problem, Solution, Testimonials, Crossroads). Testimonials are isomorphic. Crossroads Close is present and not-optional. Future Pace is grounded, not fantasy.
- **Specificity installs belief.** "March 14, 9:42 PM," "207 to 185 pounds," "HRV up 22%," "$5M ARR, 5% trajectory hit, $250K." the VSL director's invariant: specificity beats creativity. Generic "Marcus felt better" would kill the asset.
